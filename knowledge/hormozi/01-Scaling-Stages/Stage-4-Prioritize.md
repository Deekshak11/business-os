# Stage 4 — Prioritize

## Snapshot

| Field | Value |
|-------|--------|
| Theme | **Prioritize** |
| Headcount | **5–9** |
| Your role | **Manager** — manage team output |
| Leadership | First real layer / first team |
| Context | Only ~3M companies at 5–9 people |

## Primary constraint

**You said yes to everyone.** Feedback pulls product in too many directions. Volume without specialization creates unqualified leads and unfocused delivery.

## Function constraints & graduation

### Product
- **Constraint:** Too many product directions from heterogeneous customers.
- **Graduate:** **Specialize** the product into **V2**; **price/position to serve a niched-down avatar**.  
  **Rule of thumb:** Go **deep**, not wide. Most people go wide here and stall.

### Marketing
- **Constraint:** Too many **unqualified** leads (especially as product specializes).
- **Graduate:**
  - Better free stuff + more creative to boost volume
  - Add **qualification friction** so extra volume self-selects
  - Don’t open a second channel until the first is excellent

### Sales
- **Constraint:** Speed-to-contact drops; no metrics; performance drifts.
- **Graduate:** Track **basic KPIs manually** (spreadsheet is fine).

### Customer success
- **Constraint:** Notes and history scattered.
- **Graduate:** **Centralize notes in a CRM** for each customer; basic metrics + single source of truth.

## Founder behaviors that win

- Explicit avatar + explicit “who we don’t serve.”
- One primary lead channel optimized hard.
- Weekly KPI review (even if manual).
- CRM discipline for every deal and customer.

## Founder behaviors that fail

- New product lines for every customer request.
- Second/third ad channel before first works.
- “Vibes-based” sales management with no numbers.
