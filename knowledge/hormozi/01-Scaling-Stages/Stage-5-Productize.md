# Stage 5 — Productize

## Snapshot

| Field | Value |
|-------|--------|
| Theme | **Productize** |
| Headcount | **10–19** |
| Your role | **Director** |
| Leadership | ~1.5 layers (e.g. a couple managers + some direct reports) |
| Context | ~2.4M companies at this size band |

## Primary constraint

**Customers have nothing else to buy** after the core offer — LTV caps what you can spend to acquire. Also: promises and delivery can diverge as the team grows.

## Function constraints & graduation

### Product
- **Constraint:** After niching the core offer, customers ask “what else?” — nothing to sell next.
- **Graduate:** Connect **Customer Success → Product** to learn what else they want; **make something new to sell** (second product). This is why the theme is *productize*.

### Marketing
- **Constraint:** Qualified leads are too expensive; ad spend caps growth.
- **Graduate:** With second product / higher LTV you can afford more CAC. Install:
  - Lead scoring
  - Attribution
  - CRO (conversion rate optimization) process  
  (Deep how-to is in stage training; use ads/hooks/proof playbooks for creative.)

### Sales
- **Constraint:** Customers sold with **unrealistic expectations** → refunds and bad reviews.
- **Graduate:**
  - Create **sales aids** (VSLs, webinars, decks)
  - Weekly **game tape** between CS, sales, and marketing (what we promise vs deliver)
  - Feedback metrics: **NPS / CSAT**

### Customer success
- **Constraint:** Must feed product and sales truth.
- **Graduate:** Systematic feedback + satisfaction metrics tied to the loop above.

## Founder behaviors that win

- Design **money model** sequences (upsells, continuity) not only a second random SKU.
- Align sales language with fulfillment reality.
- Invest in conversion systems once LTV supports CAC.
- Treat CS as R&D for next offers.

## Founder behaviors that fail

- Raising ad spend with flat LTV and weak close rate.
- Second product that confuses the niche (random adjacent junk).
- Sales and CS fighting with no shared “game tape.”
