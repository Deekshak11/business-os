# Stage 1 — Monetize

## Snapshot

| Field | Value |
|-------|--------|
| Theme | **Monetize** |
| Headcount | **1** (still just you) |
| Your role | **Starter** (was Researcher) |
| Leadership | You alone |
| Milestone | First revenue → official company (~30M+ US companies generate ≥$1) |
| Context | Only ~9% of people start a business — first dollar already puts you ahead |

## Primary constraint

**Product isn’t good enough to sell yet** — or you haven’t asked for money. Graduation is **V1 paid** + first buck, not a perfect company.

## Function constraints & graduation

### Product
- **Constraint:** Not good enough to charge.
- **Graduate:** Fix until **V1** is sellable. Use free-stage feedback.
- **Tactics:** Find **hidden costs** of the product (non-monetary reasons people say no): risk, time, complexity, status, switching cost. Ask: *What makes this a no? What would make it a yes?*

### Marketing
- **Constraint:** Nobody knows you have **paid** stuff.
- **Graduate:** Tell people about paid offer; use free/cheap ad credits where available.

### Sales
- **Constraint:** You still barely know how to sell.
- **Graduate:** Make **first sale** using good results from free users → sell paid problem-solving. No need for full process/scripts yet — get the first dollar.

### Customer success
- **Constraint:** Free customers aren’t using it (or paid not activated).
- **Graduate:** Drive usage so results exist to sell next customers.

## Founder behaviors that win

- Charge as soon as free feedback says “this helps.”
- Turn free outcomes into proof for paid.
- Prefer simple offers over multi-SKU complexity.
- Apply **Grand Slam Offer** thinking even to first paid product.

## Founder behaviors that fail

- Endless free forever (fear of charging).
- Building features instead of fixing the #1 reason people won’t pay.
- Hiring before consistent paid demand.
