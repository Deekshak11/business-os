# Stage 3 — Stabilize

## Snapshot

| Field | Value |
|-------|--------|
| Theme | **Stabilize** |
| Headcount | **1–4 full-time employees** (at least one besides founder) |
| Your role | **Trainer** — people don’t know what they’re doing yet |
| Leadership | First FT hires (often friends/network) |
| Context | Only ~24% of businesses have ≥1 employee outside founder |

## Primary constraint

**You no longer have time to do everything yourself, and the easy warm market is drying up.** Systems for people, nurture, and qualification matter more than more random hustle.

## Function constraints & graduation

### Product
- **Constraint:** No time to fix everything that’s wrong; pace is picking up.
- **Graduate:** Fix the **one thing customers complain about most**.

### Marketing
- **Constraint:** Leads aren’t buying fast enough; friends/family warm well is used up; newer audiences are colder (“I don’t know who you are”).
- **Graduate:** Add **long-term nurturing** via email and/or content.

### Sales
- **Constraint:** Waste time with bad leads; not enough time for good ones.
- **Graduate:** Define **basic qualifications** (who is supposed to buy) and **outsource setting and/or calendaring** so you spend time on the phone with good prospects—not texting times back and forth.

### Customer success
- **Constraint:** New customers feel lost (old ones “knew you”; new ones didn’t get the same experience).
- **Graduate:** Build **onboarding** so new customers aren’t lost.

## Founder behaviors that win

- Train obsessively; document what “good” looks like.
- One product complaint priority list.
- Nurture assets (emails, content) for colder buyers.
- Simple qualification checklist before sales calls.

## Founder behaviors that fail

- Hiring more people into chaos without training.
- Still taking every lead personally without qualifiers.
- Ignoring onboarding while buying more ads.
