# Stage 9 — Capitalize

## Snapshot

| Field | Value |
|-------|--------|
| Theme | **Capitalize** |
| Headcount | **250–500** |
| Your role | **Chairman** (was president) |
| Leadership | Division / product-line leaders / mini-CEOs; you **allocate resources** across revenue lines |
| Context | ~300k companies (~0.001% of starters reach here) |
| Note | **Last stage** in the $100M Scaling Roadmap series |

## Primary constraint

**You’re not sure where the next big growth comes from.** Incremental tweaks to the cash cow often make it worse. You must **capture another market / trend** with a big bet that capitalizes on the resources you already built.

## Function constraints & graduation

### Product
- **Constraint:** Changes to successful products (**cash cows**) make them worse — but you still need new growth.
- **Graduate:** Find the next **rising star** via **M&A** or **R&D**. Think 1–3 years out; at this scale only capturing a real market/trend moves the needle.

### Marketing
- **Constraint:** Multi-product multi-channel **attribution** breaks; platform/regulatory **compliance** matters.
- **Graduate:** Shift toward **brand-first**, **omni-channel** advertising with **blended ROAS** across products; install a **compliance process**. Budget mix includes awareness, brand/sponsorships, collaborations, customer-love investments — not only pure DR arbitrage.

### Sales
- **Constraint:** Too many leads and products for any one salesperson to master.
- **Graduate:** Salespeople **specialize** by product, lead type, process, and sometimes **territory** (e.g. DTC vs wholesale teams).

### Customer service
- **Constraint:** Support for varied offerings breaks under volume.
- **Graduate:** **Triage** to specialized CS reps (shorter training, higher efficiency) — same specialization theme as sales.

### IT
- **Constraint:** Prior tech stack is outdated; departments demand more specialized solutions.
- **Graduate:** Specialized tech roles per major function; updated/segmented stacks; possibly new **ERP** / enterprise layer to connect systems. IT becomes multi-team support, not one person.

### Recruiting
- **Constraint:** Best talent ignores standard recruiting — only founder/CEO can open doors.
- **Graduate:** Founder **directly attracts and sells** highest-level roles (often 6–12+ months of courting). Normal recruiting continues for IC/manager layers.

### HR
- **Constraint:** At 250 people, even modest turnover is huge (e.g. 5% ≈ many people/month to replace just to break even); skill gaps block growth.
- **Graduate:** **Strategic workforce planning** — forecast capacity, backfills, skill gaps, redundancies; shift toward **hyper-specialization** of labor where volume allows.

### Finance
- **Constraint:** Finances not “legit” enough for outside capital (raise, borrow, sell equity) to fund the rising-star bet.
- **Graduate:** **Prep for sale** discipline even if not selling: quality of earnings, bankers’ issues list, capital structure audit, entity/legal cleanup, optional hedging of big investments. Goal: credit lines / institutional readiness and a cleaner house.

## Bottom line

**Make the big bet on the future** — capitalize on brand, people systems, and balance-sheet strength to fund the next rising star without destroying the cash cow.
