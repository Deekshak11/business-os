# Stage 0 — Improvise

| Field | Value |

| Your role | **Researcher** |
| Leadership | You alone |

## Primary constraint

**You have nothing to sell.** Clarity is missing. Solving it is research + free distribution of something people will try.

## Function constraints & graduation

### Product
- **Constraint:** Nothing to sell.
- **Graduate:** Make something **free** to give away (software, media/content, service time, physical sample, etc.).
- **Why free first:** Feedback + proof path into paid later (Stage 1).

### Marketing
- **Constraint:** Nobody knows you exist / you just created the free thing.
- **Graduate:** Tell people about the free stuff (any channel).

### Sales
- **Constraint:** You don’t know how to sell.
- **Graduate:** Get people to **try free** (not polished closing yet).

### Customer success
- **Constraint:** Learning from free users (usage, complaints, requests).
- **Graduate:** Observe what free users actually use and ask for.

## Founder behaviors that win

- Research markets, problems, competitors, and demand signals.
- Ship free offer fast; do not polish forever.
- Collect qualitative feedback from every free user.
- Resist hiring, paid ads scale, and complex funnels at this stage.

## Founder behaviors that fail

- Building in secret for months with zero free users.
- Charging before anyone will take free.
- Confusing “content for vanity” with “free thing that solves a narrow problem.”
