# Playbook — Closing

## When to use

- Advertising works; sales team can’t convert  
- High “I need to think / talk to spouse / too expensive”  
- Stage 2 script requirement  
- Need ethical objection handling system  

## Why closing matters

Advertising splits audiences into three buckets:

| Bucket | Meaning | Goal |
|--------|---------|------|
| **YES** | Lay-downs | Don’t mess it up; take payment |
| **NO** | Unqualified / bad fit | Filter out ASAP |
| **MAYBE** | On the fence | **This is why you train** |

Most money is in **maybes**. Champions close the points that matter.

## Power model

Maybes **avoid deciding** (not always true “no”). Closing’s job is **not** “force buy” — it is to give **decision-making power** so they can choose yes **or** genuine no.

Three closing jobs:

1. Understand what they want (earlier in conversation)  
2. Put what they want next to options to get there  
3. Ensure a **genuine decision**

**Ethical assumption:** product can get them the goal; you will support them; it can work for them. Help vs manipulation = **intention**.

## Onion of blame (where power goes)

People avoid decisions by **blaming**:

1. **Circumstances** — time, money, “when I want it”  
2. **Other people** — spouse, boss, past vendors  
3. **Self** — “I’m not ready,” perfection, rushed decisions  

Expect multiple layers. Peeling more objections means you’re closing correctly — keep going patiently.

### How blame presents in sales

| Layer | Common lines |
|-------|----------------|
| Circumstances | “Too busy,” “Too expensive” |
| Other people | “Talk to my X,” “Burned before” |
| Self | Preferences, fear of rushing |

## Rules of closing (operating)

- Train **daily** on the current #1 obstacle (stall → then price → etc.).  
- Connect life **now** to life **after** purchase (outcome, not features).  
- Explain value on price (Ferrari analogy: if value clear, price fights differ).  
- Prefer genuine no over soft maybe (maybes waste pipeline).  
- Pair with `CLOSER-Sales-Framework.md` for full call structure.

## Training loop

1. Collect objection frequencies from call notes.  
2. Drill the top objection this week.  
3. Role-play; review recordings.  
4. Update playbook snippets.  
5. Measure close rate by objection type.
