# Playbook — Fast Cash

## When to use

- Need cash in days, not months  
- Customers love you but only pay base plan  
- Quarterly “winning lottery ticket” on warm list  
- Fund ads/taxes/payroll spikes  

## Maxim

**Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Cash is reality.**

## What a Fast Cash Play is

- **Limited-time** offer  
- Advertised to **warmest** audiences: current customers, past customers, engaged leads  
- **Not** the same as everyday nurture content  
- Designed for **max money, shortest time**, from people most likely to buy  
- Usually: **scarce seats**, **high touch/exclusive**, **big ticket**, **urgent deadline**  
- Suggested cadence: about **every 90 days** (on top of normal billing)

## Why it prints cash

1. **Zero incremental CAC** — you already paid to acquire the audience.  
2. **High ticket margins** — fewer sales, more dollars.  
3. Uncaps spenders who want to buy more if you give them something to buy.

### 10× the 10% rule

If ~1 in 10 customers buy something at **~10×** price, you can **~double revenue** from that cohort math — ultra-premium add-ons matter.

## Structure

Two common paths (playbook outline):

1. **Push to consult** sequence (sell on call)  
2. **Push to automated checkout** sequence  

Include: offer, scarcity, urgency, sales enablement if team is new to high ticket, email/SMS schedule, start-same-day execution bias.

## Operator checklist

- [ ] Define ultra-premium offer with clear outcome  
- [ ] Cap seats honestly  
- [ ] Write short campaign schedule (email/SMS)  
- [ ] Train closes for this offer only  
- [ ] Invite actives + recent cancels (win-back angle)  
- [ ] Optional: prepay annual for residual cash  
- [ ] Track cash collected day 0–7 and retention impact
