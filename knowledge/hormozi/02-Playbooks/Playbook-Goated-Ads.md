# Playbook — GOATed Ads

## When to use

- “We’ve saturated the market” at low daily spend  
- Creative refresh is monthly/handful of ads  
- Stage 5+ CAC pressure on colder traffic  
- Need a repeatable ad factory  

**Prerequisite:** Paid ads chapters in $100M Leads — this playbook **amplifies**, does not replace.

## Why ads hit a wall

You start with warmest/highest-converting eyeballs, then expand to colder/larger audiences. As you scale, ads must convert **more varied, colder** people.

Awareness continuum (adapted from classic awareness models):

1. Most aware → only need the deal  
2. Product-aware → know you, unsure fit  
3. Solution-aware → want result, don’t know your product  
4. Problem-aware → feel problem, don’t know solutions  
5. Completely unaware  

**Diagnosis:** Usually **ad quality / creative volume**, not absolute market size.

## Ad assembly process

Stop “writing whole ads.” **Assemble**:

| Part | Role | Volume target (example weekly factory) |
|------|------|----------------------------------------|
| **Hooks** | Attention | ~50 |
| **Meat** | Proof / story / education | 3–5 strong scripts |
| **CTA** | Ask | 1–3 versions |

**Math:** 50 hooks × 3–5 meats × 1–3 CTAs ≈ **150–750 ads/week** potential combinations.

Practical operating cadence (example from playbook story): dedicate focused creative days; chop winners into hook/meat/CTA; kaleidoscope variations.

## Scaling logic

- Better ads convert larger/colder slices of the pyramid.  
- Volume of quality variants → more winners + harder for copycats to clone the winner set.  
- Prep time > pure recording time; assembly beats ad-hoc inspiration.

## Operator checklist

1. Pull current winners; split hook / meat / CTA.  
2. Generate many hooks (see Hooks playbook).  
3. Record few high-quality meats.  
4. Record few CTAs (lead magnet vs call vs buy).  
5. Launch matrix; kill losers; feed winners back into kaleidoscope.  
6. Revisit offer if even great creatives can’t convert (Grand Slam / Value Equation).
