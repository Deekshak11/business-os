# Playbook — Proof

## When to use

- “Sounds too good to be true”  
- Cold traffic ads underperform  
- Sales needs ammunition  
- Building Grand Slam perceived likelihood  

## Why proof matters

Value Equation: **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** is a top-side multiplier. Same delivery can be worth far more with better proof (10,000th patient vs first).

## Proof asset types (build a stack)

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Results | Metrics, before/after, revenue screenshots |
| People | Testimonials, faces, roles matching avatar |
| Process | Walkthroughs, curricula, SOPs shown |
| Third-party | Press, certifications, known brands |
| Volume | # clients, # days, # deliverables |
| Specificity | Named outcomes > vague “amazing” |
| Risk reversal | Guarantees (Offer Enhancers) |

## Operator checklist

- [ ] Collect proof continuously (not once a year)  
- [ ] Match proof to avatar and objection  
- [ ] Put strongest proof early in ads/sales aids  
- [ ] Use specific numbers and timeframes  
- [ ] Stage 5: sales aids (VSL, deck) carry proof consistently  
- [ ] Never fabricate — false proof destroys long-term
