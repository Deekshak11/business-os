# Playbook — Lead Nurture

## When to use

- Warm market dried up; colder audiences need time  
- High opt-in, low show/close  
- “I’ll think about it” after call — no follow-up system  
- Stage 3: add long-term nurturing via email/content  

## Core idea

Most engaged leads are not ready the day they enter. Nurture keeps you present, builds trust/proof, and re-opens buying windows without burning the list.

## Distinct from Fast Cash

| Nurture | Fast Cash |
|---------|-----------|
| Ongoing value + relationship | Short, intense promo |
| Educate, proof, soft CTA | Hard scarcity/urgency offer |
| Always on | ~Quarterly events |

Both use warm audiences; different intent.

## Operator system

1. **Segments:** new leads, engaged non-buyers, buyers, past buyers, cancels.  
2. **Cadence:** multi-touch sequence after opt-in + long-term broadcast.  
3. **Content pillars:** proof, how-to micro-wins, offer education, objection content, CTAs.  
4. **Speed-to-lead** still matters for hot intent (Stage 4 KPI).  
5. **Call follow-up:** structured touches after every maybe.  
6. **Metrics:** reply rate, show rate, close rate by age of lead, unsubscribe.

## Stage link

Stage 3 marketing graduation explicitly: friends/family done → **long-term nurturing** via emails and/or content.
