# Playbook — Retention

## When to use

- Customers leave as fast as they arrive  
- Month-over-month churn high  
- LTV too low to advertise  
- “Sales solves everything” has stopped working  

## Core problem: leaky bucket

Example math (gym case): **15% MoM churn** ≈ losing most of the base in a year → endless new sales just to stand still. Thin margins make small shortfalls fatal.

Naval framing used in playbook: sales because marketing failed; marketing because product failed — retention forces product + CS excellence.

## Method: solve like consultants

1. Ask many experts who already solved it  
2. Gather all answers  
3. Find **common threads**  

Applied: study low-churn operators → interview → extract shared factors.

## The 5 Horsemen of Retention (gym-origin, transferable)

| # | Practice | Point |
|---|----------|-------|
| 1 | **Track usage/attendance** | Drop in engagement predicts cancel; intervene early |
| 2 | **Reach out 2×/week** | Praise progress; solve small problems |
| 3 | **Handwritten cards** | Onboarding, milestones, birthdays/holidays |
| 4 | **Member events** | Regular calendar (e.g. every 21/42/63 days); invites |
| 5 | **Exit interviews** | Required at cancel; save large share of cancels; learn |

### Expected “shake the tree”

Churn may **rise first** (ghosts finally cancel when you re-engage), then fall hard if practices stick. Example path cited: ~10–15% → ~7% → ~3%. Dropping 9%→3% is **~3.3× LTV** effect in that framing.

## Generalization beyond gyms

- Define the **usage metric** that predicts retention for *your* product  
- Proactive outreach cadence  
- Human touch at milestones  
- Community/events if relevant  
- Structured save conversations before cancel completes  
- Price/value relationship: under-delivery at high price = churn (`Value-Equation`)

## Operator checklist

- [ ] Churn rate defined and measured weekly  
- [ ] Leading indicator of engagement defined  
- [ ] At-risk trigger + outreach script  
- [ ] Onboarding ensures early win (Stage 3)  
- [ ] Exit interview process live  
- [ ] Continuity offer still delivers ongoing value
