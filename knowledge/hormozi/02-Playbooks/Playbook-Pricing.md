# Playbook — Pricing

## When to use

- Healthy volume, thin profit  
- Considering raise vs acquire more vs sell more often  
- Membership/subscription businesses  
- Implementing annual, fees, anchors, continuity  

## Guiding principle

**Profit is unnatural. You must force it into existence.**

## Genie frame (what to double)

All three double *potential* revenue mathematically, but economics differ:

1. 2× new customers (halve CAC)  
2. 2× purchase frequency  
3. 2× price  

Work the math on **your** stats (clients, churn, price, margins) before choosing. Price levers often improve profit faster than acquisition when margins are low.

## Instant profit pricing plays (catalog)

Use as a menu — implement what fits legally/operationally:

| # | Play | Idea |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | Monthly → **28-day** billing | More bill cycles/year |
| 2 | Processing fees + 2nd payment form | Protect margin; card backup |
| 3 | Sales tax handling | Don’t eat tax as margin |
| 4 | **Annual price increases** | Systematic raises |
| 5 | **Annual billing** | Cash + commitment |
| 6 | **Round up** | Psychological price ends |
| 7 | Annual renewal fee + monthly | Extra cash event |
| 8 | **Automatic continuity** | Default recurring |
| 9 | **Ultra high-ticket anchor** | Make core look reasonable |
| 10 | Guarantee / warranty **upsells** | Monetize risk reversal |

## Implementation rules

1. Model profit impact before/after on real numbers.  
2. Communicate changes with value narrative (Value Equation).  
3. Grandfather strategically; don’t create chaos for no lift.  
4. Pair structural plays with offer strength — weak offers + higher price = more nos.  
5. For pure raise campaigns see `Playbook-Price-Raise.md`.
