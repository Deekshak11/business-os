# Attraction Offers

## When to use

- Need more people in the door  
- High CAC relative to first purchase  
- Building top of money model  
- Fast cash campaigns (with planned upsell)  

## Job

**Generate leads and customers** by offering something free or at a discount so the **price friction** drops and action rises.

## Discount continuum

Anytime “discount” appears, you may use **free**, **$1**, or partial discount — all reduce price friction to some degree. Steeper discount → more compelling, but must be recovered in the money model.

## Common attraction patterns (from Money Models)

- Free gift / free training  
- Giveaways  
- Decoy structures (buy X get Y framing)  
- Challenges (e.g. free or low-ticket challenge → core)  
- Trials  
- Limited-time promotional pricing that leads to prepaid / continuity  

## Rules of thumb

1. Attraction offer should still feel like a **Grand Slam** at its price point.  
2. Have **upsell ready** when they buy or complete the attraction path.  
3. Track: cost per engaged lead, cost per buyer, cash collected day 0–30.  
4. Don’t attract buyers you cannot fulfill or upsell ethically.
