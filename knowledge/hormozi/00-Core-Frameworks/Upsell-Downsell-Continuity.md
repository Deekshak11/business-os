# Upsell, Downsell, Continuity

## When to use

- Front-end sells but business still cash-poor  
- High no-rate with nothing behind the main offer  
- Need recurring base  
- Designing Stage 5 product ladder  

## Upsell offers — get them to spend more

**Job:** More cash / higher ticket after a yes (or during checkout / onboarding).

Patterns include:

- Order bumps / immediate complementary offers  
- Rollover upsells (apply payment toward higher tier)  
- Success-path upgrades (done-with-you → done-for-you)  
- Quantity / term prepaid discounts (e.g. prepay 6–12 months)  

**Rule:** Upsell should increase **likelihood of dream outcome** or speed (Value Equation), not random junk.

## Downsell offers — still get a yes

**Job:** When they refuse the main offer, present a smaller commitment that still moves them forward.

Patterns include:

- Payment plans  
- Lower tier / fewer bonuses  
- Extended time / DIY version  
- Trial continuity  

**Rule:** Downsell is not begging — it’s a different risk/price package for the same direction of travel.

## Continuity offers — keep them buying

**Job:** Recurring revenue; stabilize cash; raise LTV.

Patterns include:

- Memberships / subscriptions  
- Retainers  
- Replenishment  
- Ongoing advisory  

**Rule:** Continuity must deliver ongoing value or churn kills the model. Pair with retention playbook.

## Sequencing

Typical flow:

1. Attraction → first conversion  
2. Upsell while intent is hot  
3. Downsell on no  
4. Continuity for ongoing  

Measure **day-0 cash**, **30-day cash**, **LTV**, **churn**, **refund rate**.
