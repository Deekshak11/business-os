# Money Models Overview

## When to use

- Customers cost more to acquire than first invoice  
- Need cash to fund ads  
- Only one SKU; LTV capped  
- Designing Stage 5 second product / continuity  

## Definition

A **money model** is a **sequence of offers** that turns strangers into customers who:

1. Buy (attraction)  
2. Buy more / higher (upsell)  
3. Still buy something if they say no (downsell)  
4. Keep buying (continuity)  

Bad money models kill businesses (can’t fund advertising). Good money models make advertising an investment.

## Four offer types

| Type | Job |
|------|-----|
| **Attraction offers** | Generate leads + first purchase efficiently (often free/$1/discount) |
| **Upsell offers** | Get more cash now / higher ticket after yes |
| **Downsell offers** | Capture people who reject main offer (still monetize) |
| **Continuity offers** | Recurring revenue; keep them buying |

Typical build order taught in Money Models: **attraction → upsell → downsell → continuity**.

## Design principles

- Plan the **next offer before** you scale the first.  
- Attraction without upsell can bankrupt you.  
- Continuity stabilizes cash but still needs strong front-end.  
- Align with Grand Slam standards on each step.  
- Stage 5 “second product” is often an upsell/continuity design, not random SKU spam.
