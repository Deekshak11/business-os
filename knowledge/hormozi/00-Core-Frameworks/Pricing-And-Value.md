# Pricing and Value

## When to use

- Afraid to charge  
- Discounting to close  
- Need margin to fund advertising  
- Stage 1 first price or Stage 5 price architecture  

## Core rules

1. **Value-driven vs price-driven**  
   - Commodity → buyer compares price  
   - Grand Slam category-of-one → buyer compares **outcomes / risk / speed**

2. **Charge premium after value is obvious**  
   Raise Value Equation drivers **before** arguing price.

3. **Price communicates**  
   Too low can lower perceived likelihood (“this can’t be real / can’t be good”).

4. **Profit funds growth**  
   Thin margins → can’t buy leads, talent, or forgiveness for mistakes.

## Practical pricing process

1. Define dream outcome in economic or emotional dollars when possible.  
2. Build stack until saying no is stupid (`Grand-Slam-Offer.md`).  
3. Add guarantee to shift risk (`Offer-Enhancers.md`).  
4. Set premium price; resist matching competitors.  
5. If resistance: improve value/perception, not default to discount (or use structured downsells in Money Models).  
6. For raises later: `Playbook-Price-Raise.md`.

## Discount warning

Any fool can sell with a discount. Premium requires marketing + offer skill. Discounts train price-driven buyers and attract worse customers unless part of a deliberate attraction offer with a planned upsell path.
