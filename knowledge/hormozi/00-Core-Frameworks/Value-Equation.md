# Value Equation

## When to use

- Offer feels “expensive” or commoditized  
- Need to raise price without changing core delivery much  
- Designing bonuses, guarantees, or product experience  
- Any stage where product or marketing value communication is weak  

## Core formula

\[
Value = \frac{Dream\ Outcome \times Perceived\ Likelihood\ of\ Achievement}{Time\ Delay \times Effort\ \&\ Sacrifice}
\]

| Driver | Direction | Meaning |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Dream Outcome** | ↑ Increase | The result / status / life change they want |
| **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** | ↑ Increase | Certainty it will work **for them** |
| **Time Delay** | ↓ Decrease | Time from purchase to value (and perceived wait) |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | ↓ Decrease | Work, pain, risk, lifestyle cost on them |

**Why division:** If the denominator approaches zero (immediate + effortless), value approaches infinity. Elite companies obsess over the **bottom** (speed, seamlessness), not only louder claims on top.

## Perception is reality

Prospects don’t buy “true” physics of your product — they buy **perceived** likelihood, wait, and effort. Example pattern: reducing *perceived* wait (status maps, progress indicators) can beat expensive “true speed” investments.

## The four drivers (operator notes)

### 1. Dream Outcome (↑)
- Channel existing desires; don’t invent desire.
- Mirror the gap between current reality and dream.
- Frame benefits in **status** terms (how others will see them).
- When two offers satisfy the **same** dream, differentiate with the other three drivers.

### 2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (↑)
- People pay for **certainty**.
- Track record, proof, case studies, guarantees, demos, “10,000th patient vs first.”
- Messaging + what you include/exclude in the offer + guarantees all move this lever.

### 3. Time Delay (↓)
- Shorter distance purchase → promised benefit = higher value.
- Two layers: **long-term outcome** (what they buy) + **short-term experiences** (why they stay).
- Design **fast emotional wins** early (days, not months) so they trust the path.
- **Fast beats free** in many markets (pay to skip lines, overnight, on-demand).

### 4. Effort & Sacrifice (↓)
- Make it done-for-you, simpler, less lifestyle cost, less risk.
- Hide complexity; remove steps; provide templates, setup, support.
- Beginner marketers only inflate claims; pros shrink friction.

## Logical vs psychological solutions

Logical fixes are often already tried. Prefer **psychological** solutions that change perception of wait, risk, and status (not only cheaper/faster in raw engineering terms).

## Application checklist

1. Write the dream outcome in their words (status + concrete result).  
2. List proof assets that raise likelihood.  
3. List every day of delay you can remove or reframe.  
4. List every step they must do — delete, automate, or DFY.  
5. Rewrite offer stack so top rises and bottom falls.  
6. Only then set premium price (`Pricing-And-Value.md`).
