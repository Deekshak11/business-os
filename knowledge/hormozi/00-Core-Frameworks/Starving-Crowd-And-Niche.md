# Starving Crowd and Niche

## When to use

- Choosing who to serve  
- Offer works in demos but market is dead  
- Stage 4 “said yes to everyone” cleanup  
- Pricing ceiling feels permanently low  

## Core idea

Even a mediocre product sold to a **starving crowd** beats a perfect product sold to people who aren’t hungry. Demand first.

## Market filters (practical)

Prefer markets with:

1. **Pain** — urgent, costly problem  
2. **Purchasing power** — can pay premium  
3. **Easy to target** — reachable with Core Four  
4. **Growing** — wind at your back (helpful, not mandatory)  

## Niche messaging ladder

- Broad: “I help businesses grow.”  
- Better: “I help [person] solve [problem].”  
- Strong: “I solve [specific problem] for [specific person] in [context].”

Speak to **one** person and one painful problem at a time. Niche for **clarity of message and product**, not for shrinking ambition forever.

## Starving vs “kind of interested”

| Weak market signal | Strong market signal |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Nice-to-have | Must-solve now |
| No budget conversation | Already spending on alternatives |
| Hard to find | Congregate in known channels |
| Low emotion | High emotion / identity / status |

## Anti-patterns

- “Niche hopping” every time sales are hard (skill/offer problem misdiagnosed as market).  
- Serving broke avatars for “noble” problems without a paid path.  
- Staying forever broad at Stage 4+ when product specialization is required.
