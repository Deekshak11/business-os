# Scaling Roadmap Overview (Stages 0–9)

**Organizing principle:** Stage by **headcount** (not vanity revenue). Each stage has a **theme**, **founder role**, **constraints** by function, and a **graduation** action.

Source: Acquisition.com $100M Scaling Roadmap (complete transcript). Stages **0–9** are the full published ladder; **9 Capitalize** is the last stage.

## Stage table

| Stage | Theme | Headcount | Founder role | Leadership | Core graduation idea |
|-------|--------|-----------|--------------|------------|----------------------|
| **0** | Improvise | 0–1 (not yet a company) | Researcher | You only | Make **free** stuff + get people to try it |
| **1** | Monetize | 1 | Starter | You only | First **paid** dollar; V1 good enough to sell |
| **2** | Advertise | 1 (+ freelancers OK) | Doer | Freelancers, not FT employees | **Consistent** lead flow (Rule of 100) + basic close script |
| **3** | Stabilize | 1–4 FT employees | Trainer | First FT hires | Fix top complaint; nurture; qualify; onboarding |
| **4** | Prioritize | 5–9 | Manager | First layer | Specialize product to niche avatar; qualify volume; basic KPIs/CRM |
| **5** | Productize | 10–19 | Director | ~1.5 layers | Second product for LTV; attribution/CRO; sales aids; CS↔product loop |
| **6** | Optimize | 20–49 | Leader | Two full layers | Better processes: product pipelines, ad assembly, sales systems, cohort CS |
| **7** | Categorize | 50–99 | Executive | Three layers | Organize buckets: 2nd channel, proactive CS, ATS/HRIS, budgets |
| **8** | Specialize | 100–249 | President | Four layers + exec team | Prune product; brand; route best leads; dedicated stacks/roles |
| **9** | Capitalize | 250–500 | Chairman | Division leaders | Big bet (M&A/R&D); brand-first omni; specialized GTM; capital readiness |

## Constraints cheat sheet

### Stage 0 — Improvise
- Product: nothing to sell → give free thing away  
- Marketing: nobody knows → tell people about free stuff  
- Sales: don’t know how to sell → get free trials/users  
- CS: N/A / learn from free users  

### Stage 1 — Monetize
- Product: not good enough to charge → fix to V1  
- Marketing: nobody knows paid offer → announce paid + free ad credits  
- Sales: first sale only → use free results to sell paid  
- CS: free users not using → activate usage  

### Stage 2 — Advertise
- Product: unreliable for paid expectations → unscalable excellence  
- Marketing: inconsistent leads → **Rule of 100** daily advertising  
- Sales: inconsistent closes → set/close script (CLOSER)  
- CS: paid customers higher standards → fix fulfillment issues  

### Stage 3 — Stabilize
- Product: no time to fix everything → fix **#1 complaint**  
- Marketing: friends/family dry; colder market → long-term nurture (email/content)  
- Sales: bad leads waste time → qualifications + outsource setting/calendar  
- CS: new customers lost → onboarding  

### Stage 4 — Prioritize
- Product: said yes to everyone → niche avatar + V2 specialized product  
- Marketing: unqualified volume → better free stuff + friction/qualification  
- Sales: speed-to-lead / no metrics → manual KPIs spreadsheet  
- CS: notes scattered → CRM notes  

### Stage 5 — Productize
- Product: nothing else to buy → second product from CS feedback  
- Marketing: qualified leads expensive → scoring, attribution, CRO (enabled by higher LTV)  
- Sales: overpromising → sales aids + weekly “game tape” sales/CS/marketing  
- CS: measure NPS/CSAT → feedback loop  

### Stage 6 — Optimize
- Product: two products slow each other → separate improvement pipelines + owners  
- Marketing: colder traffic CAC up → **ad assembly** volume  
- Sales: inconsistent closes → training systems + team cadence  
- CS: churn up → cohort segmentation + activation  
- Also: security onboarding/offboarding; headhunt senior roles; benefits packages; AR/AP + granular finance  

### Stage 7 — Categorize
- Product: outdated / slow ship → focused feature teams, one problem at a time  
- Marketing: one-channel volatility → **second channel** funded by referrals + scored nurture  
- Sales: wasted leads → coverage, nurture checklist, work-the-leads culture  
- CS: reactive only → proactive journey, renewals, ascensions  
- Also: ATS + recruiting firms; HRIS; department budgets/expense controls  

### Stage 8 — Specialize
- Product: feature bloat → survey, **prune**, turn cuts into add-ons  
- Marketing: diluted brand → standardized messaging + brand associations + give value  
- Sales: premium closes drop → best leads → best closers + automated metrics  
- CS: conflicting journeys → blended journey + proactive cross-sell  
- Also: specialized tech stacks + SLAs; selection standards; group onboarding; renegotiate rates + audits  

### Stage 9 — Capitalize
- Product: can’t break cash cow → next rising star via **M&A or R&D**  
- Marketing: attribution/compliance → brand-first omni-channel + blended ROAS  
- Sales/CS: too much variety → specialize by product/territory/rep type  
- Talent: stars only take founder sell → court A-players; workforce planning  
- Finance: not capital-ready → prep-for-sale quality (QoE, structure, credit)  

## Which frameworks by stage

| Stage | Default frameworks / playbooks |
|-------|--------------------------------|
| 0 | Lead magnet (free), problem–solution cycle, niche signals |
| 1 | Grand Slam Offer, Value Equation, Pricing, Attraction offers |
| 2 | Core Four, Rule of 100, CLOSER / Closing playbook, Lead magnet |
| 3 | Lead nurture, qualification, onboarding, Retention basics |
| 4 | Niche, proof, hooks/ads quality, Pricing, KPIs |
| 5 | Money Models (upsell/continuity), LTV, Retention, Pricing raise |
| 6 | Goated Ads assembly, Closing systems, Retention cohorts, LTV |
| 7 | Second Core Four channel, Lead Nurture, Marketing Machine, Retention renewals |
| 8 | Branding, Proof, lead routing/Closing, Money Models add-ons |
| 9 | Branding, Marketing Machine, LTV, capital/M&A-style product bets |
