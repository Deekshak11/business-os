## **SECTION 6.3 — DYNAMIC SEGMENTATION (LADEIRA'S VTSD PRACTICES)**

---

**Ladeira** (VTSD — advanced segmentation in Brazil):

> *"A single list (everyone receives the same emails) = a waste.*
>
> *Segmentation (emails personalized to behaviors/interests/stage) = 2-4x the conversion.*
>
> *Example: An email 'Get your first client' (sent to everyone — irrelevant to customers who already have clients, it's irritating, they unsub).*
>
> *vs. An email 'Get your first client' (sent ONLY to those with the 'Prospect' tag + 'Goal: Clients' — relevant to 95%, 38% opens vs. 14% for a blast, 12% conversion vs. 3%).*
>
> *Segmentation is about respecting the subscriber (only relevant content, not irrelevant spam)."*

---

### **SEGMENTATION TYPES (5 Strategies):**

---

**TYPE #1 — LIFECYCLE SEGMENTATION (Stage of the Customer Journey):**

**Segments:**

- **Prospects** (subscribers, not customers — emails that sell, educate, and nurture)
- **Tripwire Customers** (bought for R$47-297 — emails that upsell the core, and deliver value for the tripwire)
- **Core Customers** (bought for R$997-2,997 — emails that deliver, upsell high-ticket, and for retention)
- **High-Ticket Customers** (bought for R$5k+ — VIP emails, exclusive, with continuity offers)

**Each segment:** Different emails (prospects ≠ customers, tripwire ≠ high-ticket — personalized to the lifecycle).

---

**TYPE #2 — ENGAGEMENT SEGMENTATION (Activity Level):**

**Segments:**

- **Hot** (opens 50%+ of emails in the last 30d — highly engaged, pitch emails work well)
- **Warm** (opens 20-49% — moderately engaged, education + soft pitches)
- **Cold** (opens <20% in the last 30d — needs re-engagement OR removal)

**Each segment:** Different frequency/content (hot can tolerate daily, cold only weekly or needs to be re-engaged).

---

**TYPE #3 — INTEREST SEGMENTATION (Behavioral Signals):**

**Behaviors:**

- Clicked a VSL link → Tag "Interest: VSL" → A VSL-focused sequence
- Downloaded templates → Tag "Interest: Templates" → Emails with advanced templates, tools
- Attended a webinar → Tag "Interest: Webinar Topic" → A webinar-specific follow-up

**Each interest:** Personalized emails (VSL clickers receive a VSL follow-up not a generic one, template downloaders receive advanced templates not something unrelated).

---

**TYPE #4 — GOAL SEGMENTATION (Subscriber Objectives):**

**Survey question:** "What's your primary goal? (A) Get your first clients, (B) Scale your existing business, (C) Learn a new skill"

**Tags based on the answer:**

- Goal A → Emails on client acquisition (outreach, pitching, closing)
- Goal B → Emails on scaling (systems, hiring, automation)
- Goal C → Emails on education (fundamentals, practice, mastery)

**Personalization:** Content relevant to the specific goal (not generic for everyone — efficiency, relevance, higher conversion).

---

**TYPE #5 — PRODUCT SEGMENTATION (What Was Purchased):**

**Purchased product A:** Emails for product A's delivery, usage, results, and a related upsell for A.

**Purchased product B:** Different emails specific to B.

**Cross-sell:** Eventually offer B to customers of A (and vice-versa — but with spaced timing, not an immediate bombardment).

---

**The ROI of segmentation (Ladeira's data):**

**Blast emails** (all subscribers get the same email): 15-25% Opens, 2-4% CTR, 1-3% Conversion.

**Segmented emails** (personalized by tags/behaviors): 30-45% Opens, 5-10% CTR, 4-12% Conversion.

**The difference:** 2-4x the metrics (segmentation vs. a blast — the investment in personalization is worth it).
