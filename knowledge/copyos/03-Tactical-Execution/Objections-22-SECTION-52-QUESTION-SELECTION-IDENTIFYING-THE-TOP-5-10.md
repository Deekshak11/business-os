## **SECTION 5.2 — QUESTION SELECTION (IDENTIFYING THE TOP 5-10)**

---

**Don't:** Guess the objections (assumptions are often wrong).

**Do:** Research (ask customers, use surveys, look for patterns in sales calls — a data-driven identification).

---

### **METHODS TO IDENTIFY OBJECTIONS (5 Sources):**

---

**SOURCE #1 — CUSTOMER SURVEYS (A Direct Ask):**

**The survey question:**

"What almost stopped you from buying? (Your hesitations, doubts, concerns — be honest, it's anonymous)"

**Analyze the responses:** Look for common patterns (price was mentioned 67%, time 34%, trust 28%, fit 19%, timing 12% — prioritize by frequency).

---

**SOURCE #2 — SALES CALL RECORDINGS (If Applicable):**

Sales team calls (recorded, with permission): The objections prospects raise verbally (transcribe them, categorize them, and count the frequency).

**The patterns:** The top 5-10 objections will repeat (each call will have similar doubts — the universal concerns are identified).

---

**SOURCE #3 — EMAIL REPLIES (Prospects Ask):**

Sales/support emails: The questions that are asked (the concerns are implicit objections — "Does it work for [this niche]?" is a fit objection, "Are there payment plans?" is a price objection).

**Categorize:** 100 emails → the top 10 questions = the top 10 objections (a frequency analysis).

---

**SOURCE #4 — COMPETITOR RESEARCH (Their FAQs):**

Your competitors' FAQ sections (what do they address?): This indicates the common objections in the industry (if 5 competitors address a "money-back guarantee?" then the trust objection is common).

**Borrow:** The common objections (address them in yours as well, they are industry-standard concerns).

---

**SOURCE #5 — YOUR INTUITION (Experience, Empathy):**

What did YOU hesitate about when buying something similar? (empathy — if you hesitated about "it's expensive," your prospects probably will too).

**The least reliable** (intuition is biased), BUT a **starting point** (to be refined with the data-driven research methods 1-4).

---

**Prioritization (Top 10 for the FAQ):**

```
Objections identified from research (50 total were mentioned):

A frequency count:
1. Price (expensive, can't afford, ROI) — 67% mentioned (the TOP priority to address)
2. Time (busy, long, when will I see results) — 34%
3. Trust (does it work?, are you credible?, what's the guarantee?) — 28%
4. Fit (for my niche?, my situation is different?) — 19%
5. Timing (now vs. later, urgency is absent) — 12%
6. Complexity (is it too complex?, will I understand?) — 9%
7. Support (is help available?, will I be alone?) — 8%
8. Comparison (are there better alternatives?) — 7%
9. Results (are they realistic?, proven?) — 6%
10. Refund (is the guarantee real?, what are the terms?) — 5%

[Select the top 10 — they will cover 89% of the total objections, the remaining 11% are the rare, specific long-tail that aren't scalable to address all of]

The FAQ section: 10 Q&A (the top 10 by frequency), each 80-150w, for a total of 800-1,500w for the section.
```
