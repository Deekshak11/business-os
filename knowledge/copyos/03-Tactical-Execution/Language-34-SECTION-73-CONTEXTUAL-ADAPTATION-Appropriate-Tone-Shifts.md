## **SECTION 7.3 — CONTEXTUAL ADAPTATION (Appropriate Tone Shifts)**

---

**Consistency ≠ rigidity** (the core voice is the same, BUT minor adaptations for context are appropriate).

---

**Contextual shifts that are OK:**

---

**EMAIL vs. SALES LETTER:**

**Email:** Slightly MORE casual (the inbox is personal, an intimate medium, contractions are OK, brevity is valued).

**Sales Letter:** Slightly MORE formal (a standalone page, a professional context, complete sentences are expected).

**The shift:** 10-15% in formality (a minor adjustment, the core voice is maintained).

---

**Example:**

**Baseline voice:** Moderate-casual (as defined in Doc 3).

**Email:** "Hey! Quick update (excited to share): John hit $112k this month (4x his start!). He's seeing results from the 3-Pillar System (the integrated approach finally clicked for him). You next? [Link]"

**Sales Letter:** "Hello. An important update: John reached $112k this month (4x growth since he started). These are the results of the 3-Pillar System (the integrated approach finally worked). You can replicate this. [Details below]"

**The difference:** The email has "Hey!", exclamation points, casual parentheses. The letter has "Hello," periods, a slightly more formal structure. **BUT the core voice is the same** (moderate-casual in both, not a jarring shift from formal↔profane extremes).

---

**AD vs. LONG-FORM:**

**Ad (15-60 sec):** MORE punchy (extreme brevity, every word counts, fragments are OK).

**Long-form (an 8k-word letter):** MORE developed (complete sentences, explanations, varied rhythm).

**The shift:** The level of brevity (not the fundamental tone).

---

**CUSTOMER SUPPORT vs. SALES:**

**Support:** MORE empathetic (problem-solving, patience, not selling).

**Sales:** MORE persuasive (benefits, urgency, action-driving).

**The shift:** The function (helping vs. selling), the core tone is the same (the voice is maintained).

---

**Guideline for adaptation:**

**Minor shifts are OK** (a 10-20% adjustment for context — email slightly more casual, letter slightly more formal, ad more punchy, support more patient).

**Major shifts are NOT OK** (casual email → extremely formal letter = a 50%+ shift, feels like a personality disorder).

**The core voice:** Unchanging (the base formality, base emotion, base language, base vulnerability — these are FIXED in Doc 3, adaptations are minor and around this baseline).
