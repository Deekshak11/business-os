## **SECTION 2.5 — OLFACTORY LANGUAGE (Smelling, Aroma — Rare BUT Powerful)**

---

**Olfactory = the sense MOST linked to memory** (direct connection to the amygdala — smell instantly triggers memories/emotions, bypassing the rational).

**Rare in copy** (obvious olfactory products — perfumes, foods, coffees), BUT when applicable = EXTREMELY powerful (strongest memory/emotion trigger).

---

**Olfactory Words (12):**

Smell, Aroma, Perfume, Fragrance, Freshness, Odor, Essence, Scent, Whiff, Exhale, Olfaction, Aromatic

---

**Usage (when applicable):**

Olfactory products:
- Coffee: "The aroma of fresh coffee (freshly ground beans, a rich fragrance fills the kitchen, a sensory morning ritual)"
- Perfume: "A floral essence (top notes of jasmine, a warm woody base, a scent that lingers for 8 hours on the skin)"
- Food: "The smell of bacon cooking (salty smokiness, the freshness of the morning, a delicious olfactory keto breakfast)"

---

**Metaphorical olfactory (abstract concepts):**

"The smell of success (the sweet scent of victory, not the bitterness of failure from before — an olfactory metaphor, emotion evoked via association)."

**Rare BUT effective** (olfactory language = uncommon in copy, stands out, a unique memory trigger).
