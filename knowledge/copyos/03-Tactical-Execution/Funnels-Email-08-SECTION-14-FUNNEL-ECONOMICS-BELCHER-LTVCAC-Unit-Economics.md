## **SECTION 1.4 — FUNNEL ECONOMICS (BELCHER — LTV:CAC, Unit Economics)**

---

**Perry Belcher** (Traffic & Conversion Summit, decades of funnel economics):

> *"A profitable funnel = simple math:*
>
> *LTV (Customer Lifetime Value) > CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost).*
>
> *Minimum ratio: 3:1 (LTV is 3x CAC — sustainable margins, scalable).*
>
> *Ideal ratio: 5:1+ (LTV is 5x+ CAC — fat margins, aggressive scaling possible).*
>
> *Below 3:1 = unprofitable (you lose money on each customer long-term, cannot scale, a death spiral)."*

---

### **LTV — LIFETIME VALUE (Calculation):**

**Formula:**

```
LTV = (AOV × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan) + Upsells/Ascension

Where:
• AOV (Average Order Value): Average ticket of the first purchase
• Purchase Frequency: How many times they buy (typically per year)
• Customer Lifespan: How long they remain a customer (retention in months)
• Upsells: Additional revenue from cross-sells, upsells, and ascensions
```

---

**Example calculation:**

```
PRODUCT: Copywriting Course R$1,997

AOV: R$1,997 (core offer)

Purchase Frequency: 1.2x/year (some buy only once, some buy a R$497 templates upsell later — average of 1.2 purchases/year)

Customer Lifespan: 2.3 years average (retention tracked, some stay 1 year, some 5 years, median is 2.3)

Upsells average: R$800 (30% of customers buy R$497 templates, 15% buy a R$10k mastermind — weighted average of R$800/customer lifecycle)

LTV CALCULATION:
= (R$1,997 × 1.2 × 2.3) + R$800
= R$5,511 + R$800
= R$6,311 total lifetime value per customer

[LTV = R$6,311 per acquired customer]
```

---

### **CAC — CUSTOMER ACQUISITION COST (Calculation):**

**Formula:**

```
CAC = Total Marketing/Sales Spend ÷ New Customers Acquired (in a given period)

Marketing spend includes:
• Ads (Facebook, Google, native — total budget)
• Tools (software, platforms, analytics — marketing attribution)
• Team (marketing salaries, freelancers, agencies — pro-rated)
• Content creation (videos, graphics, copy — production costs)
```

---

**Example calculation:**

```
PERIOD: 1 Month

Total marketing spend:
• Ads: R$12,000 (Facebook R$8k, Google R$4k)
• Tools: R$800 (ActiveCampaign, ClickFunnels, analytics — monthly)
• Team: R$3,000 (freelancer designer, copywriter hours — pro-rated)
• Content: R$1,200 (videos, graphics — allocated monthly)

Total: R$17,000/month in marketing

Customers acquired: 34 (for the month)

CAC = R$17,000 ÷ 34 = R$500/customer

[CAC = R$500 to acquire each customer]
```

---

### **LTV:CAC RATIO (Funnel Health):**

```
LTV: R$6,311
CAC: R$500

Ratio: R$6,311 ÷ R$500 = 12.6:1

INTERPRETATION (Belcher Benchmarks):

<3:1 = ❌ Unprofitable (losing money long-term, cannot scale)
3:1 to 5:1 = ⚠️ Profitable BUT thin (can scale slowly, moderate margins)
5:1 to 10:1 = ✅ Healthy (fat margins, aggressive scaling possible)
>10:1 = ✅✅ Exceptional (12.6:1 = can scale massively, reinvest profits for growth, compounding)

IMPLICATION: A 12.6:1 ratio means you can spend UP TO R$2,103/customer (LTV R$6,311 ÷ 3 = R$2,103 break-even) — currently spending R$500 = MASSIVE room to increase the ad budget (can scale the budget 4x from R$500→R$2,000/customer and still be profitable at 3:1, allowing for exponential growth).
```

---

**Belcher:**

"The LTV:CAC ratio is the single most important funnel metric (not absolute revenue, not customer count — the RATIO determines scalability).

A 2:1 ratio (LTV R$2k, CAC R$1k): Unprofitable to scale (50% margins before ops expenses, after ops = a loss).

A 10:1 ratio (LTV R$10k, CAC R$1k): Can scale infinitely (can spend up to a CAC of R$3,333 and still be 3:1 profitable — massive room)."

---

### **PAYBACK PERIOD (CAC Recovery Time):**

**Critical metric:** How long it takes to recover CAC (the sooner, the better the cash flow).

---

**Formula:**

```
Payback Period = CAC ÷ Monthly Revenue Per Customer

Example:
CAC: R$500
Monthly revenue/customer: R$200 (R$1,997 product + R$800 upsells ÷ 2.3 years ÷ 12 months = R$274/month average, conservatively R$200)

Payback = R$500 ÷ R$200 = 2.5 months

BENCHMARK (Belcher):
<3 months: ✅✅ Excellent (recover CAC fast, cash flow positive quickly, can scale aggressively)
3-6 months: ✅ Good (acceptable, moderate cash flow)
6-12 months: ⚠️ Slow (tight cash flow, limited scaling)
>12 months: ❌ Problem (cash flow negative for a year+, cannot scale, VC-dependent)

EXAMPLE 2.5 months: Excellent (CAC recovered in 10 weeks, months 3-27.6 = pure profit of LTV R$5,811 after payback).
```

---

**Hormozi** ($100M Leads):

"Payback <3 months = you can scale self-funded (profits from month 3+ are reinvested to acquire more, compounding growth, no need for outside capital).

Payback >12 months = you need VC/funding (bleeding cash for a year before recovering, growth is capital-dependent, vulnerable)."
