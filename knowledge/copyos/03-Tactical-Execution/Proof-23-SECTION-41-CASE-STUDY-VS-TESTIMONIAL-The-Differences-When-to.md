## **SECTION 4.1 — CASE STUDY VS. TESTIMONIAL (The Differences, When to Use Each)**

---

### **A TESTIMONIAL (Tier 1 — Broad, Multiple):**

**Length:** 150-300 words

**Depth:** Moderate (an overview of the transformation, with the 7 elements but each is brief)

**Quantity:** 3-8 (multiple people, for pattern diversity)

**Purpose:** Social proof (others achieved it, you can too — volume matters)

**Best for:** Broad appeal (for a quick scan, multiple voices, and relatability via quantity)

---

### **A CASE STUDY (Tier 3 — Deep, Single):**

**Length:** 800-1,500 words (5-10x more than a testimonial — a deep dive)

**Depth:** Extreme (every detail, step-by-step, week-by-week, with abundant verification)

**Quantity:** 1-3 (only a few — depth > quantity, each is comprehensive)

**Purpose:** Demonstrative proof (HOW the transformation happened exactly — a replicable roadmap)

**Best for:** Complex transformations (business scaling, chronic conditions, technical implementations), high-ticket (>$2k — the depth justifies the investment), B2B (decision committees want details), the skeptical (Stage 5 — the depth overcomes cynicism)

---

**WHEN to use EACH:**

**Use testimonials (3-8):**
- For States 1-3 (moderate-to-high awareness, brief stories are sufficient)
- For a price <$2,000 (doesn't justify the depth of a case study)
- For simple transformations (weight loss, a skill learned — straightforward)
- For the volume of social proof (many voices > a single deep one)

**Use a case study (1-2):**
- For States 4-5 (deep skepticism, they need to see HOW in detail)
- For a price >$2,000 (a high investment, the depth reassures them)
- For complex transformations (business systems, medical protocols — they need to understand the implementation)
- For B2B (committees analyze it, the depth is a signal of professionalism)

**The ideal:** BOTH (3-5 testimonials + 1-2 case studies — breadth + depth combined).
