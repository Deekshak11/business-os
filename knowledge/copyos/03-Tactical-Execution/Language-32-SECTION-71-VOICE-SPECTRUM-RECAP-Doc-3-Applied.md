## **SECTION 7.1 — VOICE SPECTRUM RECAP (Doc 3 Applied)**

---

**Doc 3 Section 7.3** defined voice (a 4-dimension spectrum):

1. **Formality:** Formal ←→ Casual
2. **Emotional Tone:** Rational ←→ Emotional  
3. **Language:** Polished ←→ Profane
4. **Vulnerability:** Polished ←→ Raw

---

**Doc 9 applies this:** Your language choices EXPRESS the voice defined in Doc 3 (consistency throughout the copy).

---

### **VOICE EXAMPLES ANALYSIS (4 Masters — A Spectrum Demonstration):**

---

**VOICE A — GARY HALBERT (Profane, Raw, Casual, Emotional):**

**Spectrum position:**

- Formality: ████████████░░░░░░░░ Extremely Casual
- Emotional: ░░░░░░░░████████████ Highly Emotional
- Language: ░░░░░░████████████ Profane (controlled)
- Vulnerability: ░░░░████████████ Raw (admits everything)

**Language characteristics:**

- **Abundant contractions:** "You're," "I'm," "won't," "can't" (maximum conversational)
- **Occasional profanity:** "Damn," "hell," "screwed" (controlled, not excessive, for moments of emphasis)
- **Slang:** "Kick ass," "nail it," "crush it" (extremely casual)
- **Sentence fragments:** "Nope.", "Wrong.", "Listen." (grammatically incorrect BUT an effective punch)
- **Personal stories:** Vulnerable (prison, failures, flaws — total raw honesty)

**Example of Halbert's voice:**

```
"Listen up.

You're doing it wrong. (And I'm gonna tell you exactly why — no BS, straight talk.)

See, most copywriters think headlines = clever wordplay. Cute puns. Creative awards.

Nope.

WRONG.

Headlines are salesmen (their job is to SELL the reading of the body copy, not to win at Cannes).

A clever headline, but nobody reads the body = FAILURE (pretty useless — you're broke, the editor has an award, who cares?).

Here's what REALLY works (tested with millions of $, not classroom theory)..."
```

**Tone:** Brutally direct (harshly honest), extremely casual (contractions, slang, fragments), controlled profanity ("BS," "broke"), implied vulnerability (his known backstory in prison, credibility via failures), emotional (palpable frustration, a passion for teaching).

**VOICE B — DAVID OGILVY (Polished, Formal, Rational, Polished):**

**Spectrum:**

- Formality: ░░░░████████████░░░░ Highly Formal
- Emotional: ████████████░░░░░░░░ Highly Rational
- Language: ░░░░░░░░████████████ Extremely Polished
- Vulnerability: ████████████░░░░░░░░ Polished (controlled revelations)

**Language:**

- **Formal grammar:** Complete sentences, proper structure, zero fragments
- **British refinement:** "Whilst," "amongst," "one must" (elevated diction)
- **Zero profanity:** Ever (the tone of a gentleman scholar)
- **Research citations:** "Our research shows...", "Tests indicate..." (academic rigor)
- **Understatement:** British (not American hyperbole — "rather effective," not "AMAZING")

**Example of Ogilvy's voice:**

```
"When writing advertisements, one must consider the reader's intelligence.

Our research, conducted over forty years and encompassing thousands of campaigns, demonstrates conclusively that clarity trumps cleverness in every instance.

The headline which states benefits directly outperforms, by a factor of five to one, the headline which attempts wordplay or obfuscation.

This is not opinion. This is fact, measured and verified repeatedly.

I recommend, therefore, that you write simply and directly. State your proposition clearly. Prove it thoroughly. Ask for the order explicitly.

Anything less is a disservice to your client and an insult to your reader's intelligence."
```

**Tone:** Formal (proper grammar, elevated), rational (research-driven, data), polished (zero slang/profanity), polished (controlled, professorial), British (whilst, one must, demonstrates).

**VOICE C — RUSSELL BRUNSON (Enthusiastic, Stories, Casual-Moderate, Emotional):**

**Spectrum:**

- Formality: ░░░░░░░░████░░░░░░░░ Casual-moderate
- Emotional: ░░░░░░████████████ Highly Emotional
- Language: ░░░░░░░░████████░░░░ Polished (clean, PG-rated)
- Vulnerability: ░░░░░░████████░░░░ Moderate-raw (personal stories)

**Language:**

- **Storytelling dominant:** Every point is a story (80% of the copy is narratives)
- **Exclamation points:** Frequent! (visible enthusiasm!)
- **Parenthetical asides:** (lots of these — a thinking-out-loud feel, conversational)
- **"Dude" casual:** Occasional (friendly, not corporate)
- **Vulnerability in stories:** Personal (a broke wrestling coach, failures, doubts — relatable)

**Example of Brunson's voice:**

```
"Okay, so here's what happened (and this is CRAZY)...

I was BROKE. Like, seriously broke. (A wrestling coach, $28k/year, my wife was pregnant, the stress was INSANE.)

I tried everything! (Sold supplements — failed. Tried e-commerce — failed. Coaching — kinda worked but I couldn't scale.)

Then I discovered funnels. (And everything CHANGED — like, literally my entire life.)

Here's the thing (and this is IMPORTANT):

Funnels aren't just 'websites that sell.' (That's what I thought initially — WRONG.)

Funnels are SYSTEMS. (Automated. Scalable. Running 24/7 while you SLEEP.)

That ONE realization? ($100M later — no joke, the actual revenue of ClickFunnels, verified.)

Dude. FUNNELS WORK. (If you're not using them, you're leaving MILLIONS on the table — not exaggerating, literally.)"
```

**Tone:** Enthusiastic (caps, abundant exclamation points), personal stories (the broke coach, failures, discoveries), casual ("dude," "like literally," "no joke"), emotional (palpable excitement), parentheses (thinking aloud, an intimate conversational feel), vulnerable (broke, failed — raw and honest).

**VOICE D — LEANDRO LADEIRA (Didactic, Brazilian, Moderate, Systematic):**

**Spectrum:**

- Formality: ░░░░░░████████░░░░░░ Moderate-formal
- Emotional: ████████░░░░░░░░░░░░ Rational-moderate
- Language: ░░░░░░░░████████████ Polished (professional Brazilian)
- Vulnerability: ████████░░░░░░░░░░░░ Controlled (systematic, not excessively personal)

**Language:**

- **Didactic:** A teacher's tone (explains systematically, step-by-step, with organized frameworks)
- **Brazilian:** Local expressions (occasional controlled use of "né," "cara," not excessive Anglicisms)
- **Visual frameworks:** Boxes, tables, structures (extreme visual organization — a VTSD signature)
- **Data-driven:** Abundant numbers (percentages, timeframes, quantities — precision)
- **Emphasis on warnings:** "NEVER do X" (caps for absolute ethical warnings — fake urgency = brand suicide)

**Example of Ladeira's voice:**

```
"Let's get straight to the point (no beating around the bush).

Urgency MUST be REAL. (This is non-negotiable — an absolute red line.)

Fake urgency (a countdown that resets, 'last units' for 6 months) = BRAND SUICIDE.

Why? (Simple logic):

The customer finds out it's fake (they ALWAYS find out — they check the countdown at 11:59 PM yesterday, check it again today and the countdown is at 11:59 PM again, the reset is obvious).

Trust is PERMANENTLY DESTROYED (they'll never buy from you again, they'll leave public bad reviews, a Google search for '[brand] scam' will pop up).

The short-term ROI (a temporary +60% conversion from the fake urgency) << the long-term death (zero LTV, a ruined reputation).

The math: A $50k gain in 1 month from fake urgency vs. $0 forever after being discovered = the worst possible trade in business.

THE ABSOLUTE RULE: Real always. Fake never.

[Visual structure — boxes, bold for NEVER/ALWAYS, a math breakdown, unambiguous absolutes, didactic and systematic not an emotional rant, controlled Brazilian expressions, CAPS for emphasis on warnings]"
```

**Tone:** Didactic (systematic teaching), Brazilian (straight to the point, occasional "né"), data-driven (the math of $50k vs. $0, an ROI comparison), warnings (absolute CAPS — NEVER, ALWAYS), controlled (not raw and emotional like Halbert, not polished and distant like Ogilvy — a professional, assertive middle ground).
