## **SECTION 1.1 — WHAT ARE POWER WORDS**

---

**Definition:**

**Power Words = words that trigger an emotional/psychological response** (not neutral — they activate System 1, bypass logic, and prompt immediate action/feeling).

---

**Difference from neutral words:**

**Neutral:** "Product available" (factual, zero emotion, slow System 2 processing).

**Power:** "GUARANTEED product available NOW — only 12 units LEFT" (urgency from "now," scarcity from "12 left," certainty from "guaranteed" — multiple System 1 triggers, immediate emotion/action).

---

**Research (BuzzSumo, 2016 — 100 million headlines analyzed):**

Headlines with power words: 8,400 average shares

Headlines without power words: 2,100 average shares

**4x difference** (from power words alone — viral spread is dramatically higher).

---

**Bly** (The Copywriter's Handbook):

> *"Power words are emotional shortcuts (you don't need to explain WHY it's urgent; the word 'NOW' automatically triggers urgency).*
>
> *Use strategically (5-10% of copy should be power words, not 50% — that's overload).*
>
> *Placement: Headlines, subheads, CTAs, P.S., bullets — high-visibility locations."*
