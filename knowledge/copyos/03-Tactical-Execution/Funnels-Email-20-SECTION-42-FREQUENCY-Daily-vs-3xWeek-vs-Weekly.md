## **SECTION 4.2 — FREQUENCY (Daily vs. 3x/Week vs. Weekly)**

---

**The debate:** How many emails to send? (is daily too much? Is weekly too little?)

---

**The data-driven answer (Agora/Empiricus/Ladeira combined):**

---

### **DAILY (7 Emails/Week — Agora/Empiricus Practice):**

**Pros:**
- **Familiarity:** Constant top-of-mind (Doc 1 Section 2.4 — repeated exposure = increased liking, familiarity = trust)
- **Cumulative engagement:** 28-35% opens daily × 365 = 102-128 opens/year per subscriber (high interaction)
- **Revenue:** A pitch every 1/10 days × 365 = 36-37 pitches/year, 12-18% conversion on each = subscribers become buyers multiple times a year (maximized LTV)
- **Momentum:** A daily rhythm (the 9 am email check becomes a subscriber habit, an expectation)

**Cons:**
- **Unsubscribe risk:** Daily = 1-2%/month unsubs (some are overwhelmed — BUT IF the value is 90/10, it's tolerable)
- **Content demand:** 365 emails/year = must produce consistent quality (resource-intensive)
- **Inbox competition:** Daily = some days are ignored (occasional fatigue)

**When to use:**
- Newsletters (financial, health — daily updates with valuable market/health info)
- High-value content (each email is worth reading, not filler)
- Engaged lists (subscribers explicitly opted in for daily, expect the frequency)

**Daily benchmarks:**
- Opens: 25-35% average (sustained for years by Agora — value + 90/10 maintains it)
- Unsubs: 1-2%/month (acceptable — growth of 5-10%/month offsets it, net positive)
- Pitch conversion: 12-18% (high trust from the daily value)

---

### **3X/WEEK (Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday — Ladeira's VTSD Recommendation):**

**Pros:**
- **Balance:** Frequent enough (momentum, they don't forget you), not overwhelming (breathing room, a manageable inbox)
- **Unsubscribe:** Lower at 0.5-1%/month (vs. daily at 1-2%, moderate frequency = higher tolerance)
- **Quality:** 156 emails/year (vs. 365 daily) = each email can be higher quality (time to craft it better, not rushing daily)
- **Sustained engagement:** 30-40% opens (slightly higher than daily because of scarcity — not every day = each one is valued more)

**Cons:**
- **Lower momentum:** 2-3 day gaps (some forget you between emails, familiarity builds slower)
- **Lower revenue:** 15-16 pitches/year (vs. 36 daily) = lower total conversion volume

**When to use:**
- Info products (courses, coaching — 3x/week updates are sufficient)
- Engaged BUT busy audiences (entrepreneurs, professionals — daily is too much, weekly is too little, 3x is the sweet spot)
- Substantial content (each email is a deep 400-600w — quality > quantity)

**3x/week benchmarks:**
- Opens: 30-40% (higher than daily because of scarcity)
- Unsubs: 0.5-1%/month (lower, higher tolerance)
- Pitch conversion: 10-15% (slightly lower than daily, BUT acceptable)

**Ladeira:** "3x/week is my preference for Brazil in 2024 (the optimal balance — sustained engagement, low unsubscribes, quality emails each time, resource-efficient to produce, strong 10-15% conversion)."

---

### **WEEKLY (1 Email/Week — Conservative):**

**Pros:**
- **Unsubscribe:** The lowest at 0.2-0.5%/month (weekly is non-intrusive, almost no one complains)
- **Quality:** 52 emails/year = each one can be a masterpiece (extensive time to craft)
- **Longevity:** Sustainable for decades (low fatigue, low burn)

**Cons:**
- **They forget you:** A 7-day gap = slow familiarity (weak momentum, top-of-mind is lost)
- **Engagement:** 25-35% opens (moderate — scarcity helps, BUT the long gap = some disengage)
- **Revenue:** Only 5-6 pitches/year (low volume, lower LTV)

**When to use:**
- Traditional newsletters (many have a Sunday newsletter — a weekly expectation)
- Audiences with low engagement tolerance (busy executives, weekly is digestible)
- Evergreen content (not time-sensitive daily news)

**Weekly benchmarks:**
- Opens: 25-35%
- Unsubs: 0.2-0.5%/month (the lowest)
- Pitch conversion: 8-12% (lower because familiarity is weaker, trust builds slower)
