## **SECTION 7.1 — TIMING OF PRE-EMPTION (Early vs. Late, Strategic)**

---

**Pre-emption is to address an objection BEFORE the prospect consciously raises it** (a psychological advantage).

---

**Kennedy** (Making Them Believe):

> *"An objection stated aloud is a commitment (a micro one — the prospect said 'it's expensive,' now they are committed to defending the position of 'too expensive,' and the consistency bias makes it harder to reverse).*
>
> *Pre-emption: Address it BEFORE it's verbalized (the prospect reads the answer before consciously formulating the objection — the barrier is removed pre-verbalization, there's no commitment to defend, it's easier to accept the answer).*
>
> *The timing: Throughout the copy (not just in the FAQ at the end — continuous pre-emption, objections are dissolved naturally as they are encountered)."*

---

### **EARLY COPY PRE-EMPTION (In the Headline, Lead, Body):**

**The anticipated objection:** "It's expensive"

**Pre-empt it in the HEADLINE:**

"The R$100k/Month System for Just R$8/Day (Less Than 2 Coffees, a 336x ROI in Year 1 — A No-Brainer Investment)"

**The pre-emption:** The price is mentioned (framed as a trivial R$8/day), the ROI is stated (336x — justifying it upfront), the "expensive" objection is addressed BEFORE even the body of the copy (the headline is the first defense).

---

**Pre-empt it in the LEAD:**

"Does an investment of R$2,997 sound significant? (It's valid — a substantial sum, a natural concern). But it's R$8.20 daily (2 coffees), with a 28x ROI in the first month (a payback of <30 days), and a return of R$1M+ in year 1 (the transparent math checks out). Is it worth it? (Obviously — the hesitation is understandable, but the numbers dissolve the doubt)."

**The pre-emption:** In paragraph 2-3 of the lead (early in the copy, before the offer is formally presented), it acknowledges the concern and immediately breaks it down (the reader hasn't formulated a strong objection yet, encountering the answer early makes it easy to remove the barrier).

---

### **CONTINUOUS PRE-EMPTION (Throughout the Body):**

**In the bullets section:**

"• An implementation of just 20 min/day (not 3h — manageable with your busy schedule, bite-sized modules)"

**It pre-empts:** The time objection (before the FAQ, naturally integrated into the bullets, "just 20 min" subtly addresses the busy concern).

---

**In the proof section:**

"An 89% track record (42 out of 47 succeeded — the 11% of honest failures are shown, for transparency, we don't suspiciously hide failures with a 100% claim)"

**It pre-empts:** The trust objection ("is it too good to be true?" — showing the 11% of failures is honest, credible, and paradoxically builds trust).

---

### **THE FAQ AT THE END (To Reinforce the Pre-Emptions Already Made):**

The FAQ is the second line of defense (the objections have already been pre-empted in the body, the FAQ reinforces them from a different angle, for comprehensive coverage).

**It's not:** The first mention (they've already been addressed throughout — the FAQ is a confirmation, with additional detail).

**It is:** Reinforcement (the ROI was shown in the offer section, the FAQ explains the ROI with a different math angle, strengthening the breakdown through repetition).
