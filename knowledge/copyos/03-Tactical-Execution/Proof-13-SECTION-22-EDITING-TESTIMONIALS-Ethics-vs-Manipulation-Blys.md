## **SECTION 2.2 — EDITING TESTIMONIALS (Ethics vs. Manipulation, Bly's Guidelines)**

---

**Bly** (The Copywriter's Handbook, Testimonials Chapter):

> *"Editing testimonials = is necessary (for clarity, readability).*
>
> *BUT: An ethical line exists (clarity ≠ manipulation).*
>
> *Cross that line = it's fraud (an FTC violation, a destruction of trust)."*

---

### **ETHICAL EDITING (Permitted):**

---

**✅ REMOVE — For Clarity (Without Changing the Meaning):**

**A. Verbal tics:** "Um," "ah," "like," "you know" (filler words — remove them, readability improves, the meaning is unchanged).

Before: "Like, the course, ah, it worked, you know, it was really good."

After: "The course worked, it was really good."

---

**B. Repetitions:** The same point repeated (condense it, you don't lose substance).

Before: "I really liked it. It was very good. I'm very satisfied. Very happy with the result."

After: "I'm very satisfied and happy with the result."

---

**C. Obvious grammar errors:** Typos, subject-verb agreement (correct them for professionalism, but maintain the voice).

Before: "The result were great, I liked it alot."

After: "The results were great, I liked it a lot."

---

**D. Reorganize for clarity:** The order of sentences (for a logical flow, without changing the facts).

Before: "The result was great. Before, I was in a bad spot. I had tried other things."

After: "Before, I was in a bad spot. I had tried other things. The result was great." (chronological, clearer).

---

**✅ ADD — For Context (Transparency, Not Fabrication):**

**Context in brackets:** [To explain unclear references].

Client: "After using that thing, things improved."

Edited: "After using [the 3 Pillars System], things improved."

[Brackets = transparency (you added it, not the client's words, the reader knows).]

---

### **UNETHICAL EDITING (Forbidden — An FTC/Legal Violation):**

---

**❌ CHANGING THE MEANING:**

Client: "The result was GOOD."

Edited: "The result was INCREDIBLE."

**Violation:** You changed the magnitude (good → incredible = an exaggeration, not the client's words).

---

**❌ FABRICATING ELEMENTS:**

Client's testimonial: "I liked the course."

You add: "I liked the course. I made $50,000 in the first month!"

**Violation:** You fabricated a result (the client never said $50k — it's a lie, it's fraud).

---

**❌ OMITTING NEGATIVES (Cherry-Picking):**

Client: "I liked the course. Modules 1-8 were excellent. Modules 9-12 were confusing and didn't help much."

You publish: "I liked the course. Modules 1-8 were excellent."

**Violation:** You omitted the negative (you cherry-picked only the positive — it's a misrepresentation, dishonest).

**The ethical way:** Publish all of it (the positives + the negatives), OR ask for permission to omit it ("Do you mind if I only use the first part? We're redoing modules 9-12 based on your feedback").

---

**❌ COMPOSITE TESTIMONIALS (Combining Multiple People):**

Client A: "My revenue increased by $20k."

Client B: "I got my family time back."

You create: "My revenue increased by $20k and I got my family time back. — A Satisfied Client"

**Violation:** It's a composite (2 people are combined into 1 fake testimonial — it's fraud, neither person said both things, it's a misrepresentation).

---

**The FTC's Guidelines** (USA, similar to CONAR in Brazil):

> *"Testimonials must reflect the TYPICAL experience (or disclose if it's exceptional).*
>
> *You cannot materially change the meaning (editing for clarity is OK, changing the substance = is a violation).*
>
> *You must have proof (written permission, documentation of the results claimed).*
>
> *Violators: Fines of $10k-100k+, lawsuits, forced refunds, brand destruction."*

---

**Bly's ethical line:**

```
PERMITTED (For Clarity):
✓ Remove filler words (um, ah, you know)
✓ Fix obvious grammar (typos, agreement)
✓ Reorganize for logic (a chronological flow)
✓ Add context in brackets (to clarify references)
✓ Condense repetitions (the same point 3x → 1x)

FORBIDDEN (Manipulation):
✗ Change the meaning (good → incredible, liked → loved)
✗ Fabricate elements (add results that weren't mentioned)
✗ Omit negatives (cherry-pick only the positives)
✗ Create a composite (combine multiple people into 1 fake)
✗ Use without permission (a legal requirement)

THE GUIDELINE: "If you're in doubt about whether it's ethical, DON'T edit it (use it raw, or ask the client for permission for a specific edit)."
```
