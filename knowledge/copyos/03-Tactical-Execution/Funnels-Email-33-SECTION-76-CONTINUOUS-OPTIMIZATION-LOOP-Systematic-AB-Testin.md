## **SECTION 7.6 — CONTINUOUS OPTIMIZATION LOOP (Systematic A/B Testing):**

---

**Optimization is not a one-time thing** (set it and forget it — it's continuous testing, iteration, and perpetual improvement).

---

**The loop framework:**

```
1. BASELINE (Measure the current state — 28% opens, 6% CTR, 9% conv)
2. HYPOTHESIS (what to improve — "will curiosity subject lines boost opens?")
3. TEST (A/B — version A is the control, version B is a curiosity subject, 500 each)
4. MEASURE (A opens 28%, B opens 34% — B wins by +6 pts)
5. IMPLEMENT (B becomes the new control, roll it out to everyone)
6. REPEAT (a new hypothesis — "CTA button color green vs. red?", test, measure, implement)

AN INFINITE LOOP: Always be testing (a minimum of 1 test/week, 4-6 tests/month, 50-60 accumulated improvements/year — conversion compounds from 28% in year 1 → 35% in year 2 → 42% in year 3, with continuous optimization).
```

---

**What to test (in order of priority):**

**Priority 1 — Subject Lines (The Biggest Impact):**

Opens are the gatekeeper (an unopened email = irrelevant content).

Test: 10+ variations (curiosity vs. benefit vs. question vs. urgency — a formula from Doc 6 for each).

Impact: +20-80% more opens (from the subject alone — the highest ROI test).

---

**Priority 2 — Send Times:**

Opens vary by 2-3x depending on the hour (9 am vs. 1 pm in Brazil — 38% vs. 18%).

Test: Morning vs. afternoon vs. evening (a week for each, measure).

---

**Priority 3 — CTAs (Calls-to-Action):**

Button text ("Download Now" vs. "Get Instant Access"), color (green vs. red vs. blue), placement (top vs. mid vs. both).

Impact: +15-40% more clicks (from CTA optimization).

---

**Priority 4 — Content Length:**

Short 150w vs. Medium 300w vs. Long 500w (audience preference varies — test it).

---

**Priority 5 — Personalization:**

Generic "Hi" vs. "Hi [Name]" vs. "Hi [Name], [Custom field based on behavior]".

Impact: +8-20% more opens (from personalization).
