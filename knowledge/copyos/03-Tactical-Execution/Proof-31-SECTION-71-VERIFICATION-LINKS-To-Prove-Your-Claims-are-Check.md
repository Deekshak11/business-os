## **SECTION 7.1 — VERIFICATION LINKS (To Prove Your Claims are Checkable):**

---

**Transparency = trust** (radical transparency — don't hide, prove everything, and provide the links).

---

**What to link to:**

✅ **Video testimonials:** YouTube/Vimeo URLs (to watch them on-camera, and verify they're authentic)

✅ **Case studies:** PDF downloads (with analytics screenshots, redacted invoices, and a timeline — to verify the depth)

✅ **Data sources:** A link to the ClickFunnels report, HubSpot benchmarks, and studies cited (the reader can check for themselves — transparency)

✅ **Media mentions:** Links to articles (Globo, HBR, podcasts — to verify the feature is real)

✅ **Credentials:** Your LinkedIn profile (to verify employment, education), and your certifications (to the issuing body for verification)

---

**Example of link integration:**

```
"John's testimonial (a 90sec video): [Watch it here → youtube.com/testimonial-john]

The complete case study (a 12p PDF): [Download it → yoursite.com/case-john.pdf]

The track record data (from 47 companies): Is based on a comparison to the ClickFunnels Benchmark [See the report → clickfunnels.com/benchmark-2023]

The featured article in PEGN: [Read it → pegn.globo.com/article-scaling-2023]

My LinkedIn (to verify my credentials): [My Profile → linkedin.com/in/yourname]"
```

**Links for each claim** — total transparency (the reader can verify EVERYTHING, not just "trust me" — it's checkable).

---

**The impact of verification:**

**Cialdini:** Transparency = a +40-60% increase in trust (vs. claims without links).

**Why:** System 1 (the presence of links = "it must be real, they're offering to let me verify it" even if they don't click on all of them — the offer itself is a signal of honesty).
