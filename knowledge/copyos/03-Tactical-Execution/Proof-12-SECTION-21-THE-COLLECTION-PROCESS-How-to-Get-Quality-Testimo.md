## **SECTION 2.1 — THE COLLECTION PROCESS (How to Get Quality Testimonials)**

---

**A common problem:** "I don't have any testimonials yet" (new product, or never asked).

**The solution:** Systematic collection (a process, timing, and incentives).

---

### **OPTIMAL TIMING (When to Ask):**

---

**A. AT "WIN" MOMENTS (The Emotional Peak — Perfect Timing):**

**Don't:** Ask at a random moment (the client is busy, not thinking about you, low response).

**Do:** Ask IMMEDIATELY after a win (the client has achieved a result, emotion is high, gratitude is at its peak).

---

**Examples of win moments:**

**Product/Service:**
- **Immediately after a result:** The client got their first sale (copywriting), hit a 20 lbs milestone (weight loss), closed a deal (sales training) — a HIGH emotion moment (excitement, gratitude).
- **Triggered automatic email:** The system detects a milestone (the first sale is logged, a weight goal is tracked) → an auto-email says "Congrats! Quick favor..." (perfect timing, automated).

**Info Products:**
- **Module completion:** They finished a key module (often a breakthrough moment — "I finally understand X").
- **A result is achieved:** They applied a technique and it worked (immediate validation — a positive emotion).

---

**Timing research (Bly):**

Ask WITHIN 24-48h of the win: 60-75% response rate.

Ask 1-2 weeks later: 30-40% response (the emotion has cooled).

Ask 1+ month later: 10-20% response (they've forgotten the emotional peak, they're busy).

**Asking immediately = 3-7x higher response** (timing is critical).

---

**B. LIFECYCLE STAGES (The Customer Journey):**

```
Stage 1 (Day 1-7): A quick win moment (the first small result)
→ Ask for: A mini-testimonial (1-2 sentences, easy to give)

Stage 2 (Month 1-3): Significant progress (multiple wins accumulated)  
→ Ask for: A medium testimonial (a paragraph, the transformation is visible)

Stage 3 (Month 6-12): A complete transformation (the full journey)
→ Ask for: A full testimonial (detailed with the 7 elements, potential for a case study)
```

**Multiple asks are OK** (not just once — different stages, different depths of testimonials collected).

---

### **THE QUESTIONING FRAMEWORK (7 Questions to Extract Gold):**

---

**Don't say:** "Can you write me a testimonial?" (it's open-ended, the blank page is intimidating, low completion).

**Do say:** Ask specific questions (it's guided, easy to answer, 4-6x higher completion).

---

**THE 7 QUESTIONS (To Match the 7 Elements of the Perfect Testimonial):**

---

**Q1 — THE BEFORE STATE:**

"What was your situation BEFORE [the product]? (Be specific: numbers for revenue/weight/pain level, your emotional state, how long you were stuck)"

**Extracts:** Element #2 (the quantified before).

---

**Q2 — PREVIOUS ATTEMPTS:**

"What did you try BEFORE [the product]? (Other courses, methods, services — for each: how much you invested, the result, and why it didn't fully work)"

**Extracts:** Element #3 (skepticism, justification for past attempts).

---

**Q3 — THE DECISION WHY:**

"Why did you decide to try [the product] AFTER your previous failures? (What convinced you — the guarantee? Social proof? A recommendation? Desperation?)"

**Extracts:** Element #4 (the catalyst for the decision).

---

**Q4 — THE IMPLEMENTATION:**

"What was the process of using [the product] like? (The timeline in weeks/months, challenges you faced, support you used, completion rate)"

**Extracts:** Element #5 (the journey's details).

---

**Q5 — THE RESULTS AFTER:**

"What results did you achieve? (Be specific: the after numbers, the timeframe, comparing it to the before, the holistic business+life impact)"

**Extracts:** Element #6 (the quantified transformation).

---

**Q6 — THE EMOTIONAL IMPACT:**

"How do you FEEL about the transformation? (Gratitude? Relief? Excitement? — emotional, not just 'satisfied')"

**Extracts:** The base for Element #7 (the emotion for the quote).

---

**Q7 — THE RECOMMENDATION:**

"Would you recommend it to others? Why or why not? (Be honest — if yes, what would you say to someone who's hesitating? If no, why not?)"

**Extracts:** A continuation of Element #7 (the peer-to-peer recommendation, authenticity via honesty even if it's a no).

---

**The testimonial request email (a template using the 7 questions):**

```
Subject: "Quick favor, John? (Congrats on your first $5k sale! 🎉)"

Body:

"Hi John,

I saw you closed a $5,000 project today (CONGRATS! 🎉 — your first high-ticket one, a huge milestone).

A quick favor (will take 5-10min):

Your transformation (from $500/project → to $5,000 in 60 days) = is inspiring.

Others are hesitating (just like you were in Feb 2023) — and your story helps them (proof from a real person, not just my claims).

7 quick questions (you can copy/paste your answers, informal is OK, no need to polish — authentic > perfect):

1. Before the 3 Pillars System: What was your situation? (Revenue, hours, emotional — be specific)
2. What did you try before? (Other courses, the results, why they didn't work)
3. Why did you decide to try it (after the previous ones)? (What convinced you)
4. The implementation: What was it like? (Timeline, challenges, support used)
5. The results: What are your numbers after? (Revenue, clients, hours, holistic)
6. Emotionally: How do you feel? (Beyond the numbers, the life impact)
7. Would you recommend it? (An honest yes/no, what would you say to others hesitating)

[Just paste your answers in a reply to this email — raw is fine, I'll edit for clarity but keep your voice]

I appreciate it! (Your testimonial = helps dozens of others transform just like you did — you're paying it forward).

Best,
[Your Name]

P.S. Thanks in advance (I know you're busy, I massively appreciate the 5-10min). [Optional incentive: Here's a $100 Amazon gift card as a thank you — response is +40% when offered]"
```

**250-word email** — it congratulates (celebrates the win, perfect timing at an emotional high), it asks for a favor (quick 5-10min — manages expectations), it gives context (your story helps others — an altruistic appeal), the 7 questions are (specific, copy/paste is easy, informal is OK — removes the friction of "I have to write well"), it offers to edit (removes the "I'm a bad writer" barrier, with reassurance that their voice will be preserved), it shows gratitude (appreciation upfront), the P.S. has an incentive (a $100 gift card — a small, ethical bribe that boosts response by +40%).

---

**The email's response rate:**

With an incentive ($50-100): 65-80% (high).

Without an incentive: 40-55% (moderate — some give generously, others are busy and ignore it).

**Timing sent:** Within 24h of the win (at the emotional peak) = the rates above.

---

### **ALTERNATIVE METHODS (If Email Has a Low Response):**

---

**B. A PHONE CALL (Personal, Higher Completion):**

Call the client (5-10min), ask the 7 questions verbally (it's conversational).

**Record** (with permission): "Can I record this? (It makes transcription easier and more accurate)."

**Transcribe:** The audio → to text (you or a service like Rev.com for $1/min).

**Edit:** For clarity, but maintain their voice (remove the ums, ahs, repetitions — readable BUT authentic).

**Response rate:** 80-90% (a phone call is personal, it's harder to say no, and it gets completed in real-time).

**The downside:** It's time-intensive (a 10min call + 10min to transcribe/edit = 20min per testimonial vs. 5min to read/edit an email).

---

**C. A VIDEO REQUEST (Highest Impact, Lower Response):**

Ask the client to record a video (on their phone, 60-90 seconds, answering 3-4 key questions).

**Response rate:** 15-30% (low — video is a higher effort, some are camera-shy, quality concerns).

**BUT:** Those who do respond = are GOLD (a video testimonial has +58% more credibility vs. text).

**Incentive boost:** Offer $200-500 (for professional use, it's worth the investment — a video is reusable for years, high ROI).

---

**D. YOU INTERVIEW THEM (Guide the Conversation, Record It):**

A Zoom call (15-20min), you ask the 7 questions, the conversation flows naturally.

**Record:** The video (with permission).

**Edit:** The best clips (60-120 seconds of highlights), or transcribe the full thing (a detailed testimonial).

**Response:** 70-85% (a scheduled call = a commitment, and you guiding them = makes it easier for them).

**Best for:** High-value clients (they're worth your 15-20min investment, it deepens the relationship).

---

### **ETHICAL INCENTIVES (To Boost Response, Not for Manipulation):**

**Acceptable:**
- ✅ Gift cards ($50-200 — a token of gratitude, not a payment specifically for the testimonial)
- ✅ A discount on the future (20% off the next purchase — a loyalty reward)
- ✅ Bonus content (an extra module, a template pack — an added value)
- ✅ A feature/publicity (on your website, a published case study — exposure for their business)
- ✅ Reciprocity (you helped them, you're asking for help back — a fair exchange)

**NOT acceptable:**
- ❌ A large payment ($500+ — you're buying a testimonial, it's not genuine, it's unethical)
- ❌ A positive conditional ("only if it's 5-stars" — manipulation, dishonest)
- ❌ Writing it for them (you write it, they sign off — fake, unethical, and illegal in some jurisdictions)

---

**Abraham** (on ethical persuasion):

> *"A small incentive (a $50-100 gift) = gratitude (you're not buying a testimony — that's the ethical line).*
>
> *A large incentive ($500+) = purchasing (it crosses the ethical line — the testimony is not genuine, it's bought).*
>
> *The guideline: The incentive should be <10% of the product price (a $100 incentive for a $2,000 product = 5%, acceptable; $500 = 25%, questionable)."*
