## **SECTION 1.5 — CATEGORY D: SENSORY WORDS (60 Words)**

---

**(Preview here — Part II develops EACH sense COMPLETELY)**

---

**VISUAL (15):**

See, Imagine, Visualize, Bright, Clear, Dark, Vivid colors, Sharp, Focus, Blurry, Transparent, Opaque, Visually reveal, Show, Demonstrate

---

**AUDITORY (12):**

Hear, Listen, Sound, Noise, Silence, Harmony, Dissonance, Resonate, Echo, Whisper, Scream, Melody

---

**KINESTHETIC (18):**

Feel, Touch, Smooth, Rough, Hot, Cold, Weight, Light, Pressure, Relief, Pain, Comfort, Movement, Static, Flow, Friction, Impact, Embrace

---

**OLFACTORY (8):**

Smell, Aroma, Perfume, Fragrance, Freshness, Odor, Essence, Scent

---

**GUSTATORY (7):**

Savor, Taste, Sweet, Bitter, Salty, Sour, Delicious

---

**Multi-sensory example:**

"IMAGINE: You wake up (VISUAL: soft light through the window, not a harsh alarm), FEEL the comfortable mattress (KINESTHETIC: softness, not back pain), HEAR the peaceful silence (AUDITORY: not stressful traffic), SMELL the fresh coffee (OLFACTORY: aroma), and your first THOUGHT is: 'I only have to work 4 hours today' (EMOTIONAL: freedom, not the dread of a 12-hour day)."

**A 5-sense sentence** (visual light, kinesthetic mattress, auditory silence, olfactory coffee, emotional thought) = total immersion (brain is experiencing vividly, not reading abstractly — peak engagement).
