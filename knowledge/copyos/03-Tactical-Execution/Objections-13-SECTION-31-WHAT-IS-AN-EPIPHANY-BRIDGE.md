## **SECTION 3.1 — WHAT IS AN EPIPHANY BRIDGE**

---

**Russell Brunson** (Expert Secrets, Epiphany Bridge Chapter):

> *"Objections are False Beliefs (limiting beliefs).*
>
> *'It doesn't work' (the belief: the vehicle is broken).*
>
> *'I can't do it' (the belief: an internal incapacity).*
>
> *'Circumstances are preventing me' (the belief: external blocks).*
>
> *You break beliefs: Not through logic (rational arguments = resistance, defenses strengthen — the backfire effect).*
>
> *You break them through a STORY (an Epiphany Bridge — YOU had the same belief, an experience changed your belief, and you succeeded after — for identification, a transformation path is shown not told)."*

---

**An Epiphany Bridge is a personal story** (a 3-act structure):

**Act 1:** Before (YOU had the same False Belief as the reader — for identification).

**Act 2:** The Discovery/Epiphany (an experience/moment that shifted the belief — a turning point).

**Act 3:** After (the new belief = success was achieved — proof that the belief shift works).

---

**The function:** The reader identifies (you had the same belief, it's relatable), sees that a shift is possible (your experience is credible, not theory — you lived it), and wants to replicate it (if YOU shifted and succeeded, the reader can too — the path is shown).
