## **SECTION 4.1 — OPTIMAL SEND TIMES (Brazilian Timezone/Culture)**

---

**Leandro Ladeira** (VTSD — analysis of 2,400+ email campaigns in Brazil, 2018-2024):

> *"US send times ≠ Brazil send times (cultural, timezone, and behavioral differences).*
>
> *US optimal: 6-8 am ET (checking emails on the commute, during the morning routine).*
>
> *Brazil optimal: 9-11 am BRT (Brazilians wake up later, 7-8 am, have coffee from 8-9 am, and check emails from 9-11 am as they start work — a cultural difference of a 2-3h shift from the US).*
>
> *Sending at 6 am in Brazil = 12-18% opens (too early, it gets buried in the inbox by the time they check at 9 am).*
>
> *Sending at 9-11 am = 32-42% opens (perfect timing, at the top of the inbox when they check, fresh attention)."*

---

**Why timing matters so much:**

**Ladeira's A/B Test:**

Identical email (subject, body, offer — EVERYTHING the same).

**Send A:** 09:30 AM BRT → 38% Opens, 8.2% Clicks, 4.7% Conversion

**Send B:** 01:00 PM BRT (lunchtime) → 18% Opens, 3.1% Clicks, 1.8% Conversion

**The difference from timing alone:** 38% vs. 18% opens (2.1x), 4.7% vs. 1.8% conversion (2.6x).

**Same email, different time = a 2.6x difference in revenue** (timing is critical, not trivial).

---

**Empiricus's practice:**

"Daily newsletter (sent at 09:00 AM BRT exactly, automated — consistency).

Opens: 32-38% sustained (for years — optimal timing + value + consistency = maintained engagement).

Experiments: Tested 06:00, 12:00, 18:00, 22:00 (each for 30 days) — ALL performed worse (9 am always won, they permanently reverted)."
