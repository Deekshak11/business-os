## **SECTION 2.1 — WHAT IS SPIN (RACKHAM'S SALES FRAMEWORK ADAPTED)**

---

**Neil Rackham** (SPIN Selling, 1988 — research from 35,000 sales calls, a 12-year study):

> *"Successful salespeople ask 4 types of questions (in a specific sequence):*
>
> *S — Situation (to understand the current state)*
>
> *P — Problem (to identify the specific pain)*
>
> *I — Implication (to amplify the consequences of not solving it)*
>
> *N — Need-Payoff (to create desire for a solution)*
>
> *The sequence matters: S → P → I → N (each one builds on the previous, don't skip, not in a random order)."*

---

**In sales (verbal, conversational):** Questions are asked directly (salesperson → prospect, it's interactive).

**In copy (written, a monologue):** Questions are rhetorical OR statements imply questions (the copywriter anticipates and answers them pre-emptively).

---

**Adaptation for copy:**

**SPIN in sales:** "What's your current revenue?" (a Situation question is asked).

**SPIN in copy:** "If you're stuck at R$30-50k/month..." (a Situation statement — it identifies, the reader self-selects "yes, that's me").

**The same function** (to identify the situation), a different medium (an interactive question vs. an identifying statement).
