## **SECTION 5.1 — FAQ VS. BODY COPY (Placement, Differences)**

---

**An FAQ Section is dedicated** (a question/answer format, it's scannable).

**The Body Copy is a narrative** (objections are addressed in a natural flow, not a Q&A format).

---

**When to use EACH:**

---

**BODY COPY (Pre-Emption Throughout):**

✅ **For the 80%+ of common objections:** Address them naturally in the copy (the price objection → the offer section breaks it down with ROI, the time objection → the bullets show "only 20 min/day," trust → the proof section has 5 tiers — an integrated flow, not a disruptive Q&A in the middle of the copy).

✅ **For the narrative flow:** A story, agitation, a solution (objections are woven in naturally — "you might be thinking 'it's expensive'... BUT consider the ROI..." — it's conversational, not a robotic Q&A).

✅ **For subtlety:** Some objections (can be addressed without stating them explicitly — "concerned about affordability?" isn't mentioned directly, but showing payment plans of R$1,099/month handles affordability without overtly naming the objection).

---

**THE FAQ SECTION (Dedicated at the End OR Before the Offer):**

✅ **For specific, unique questions:** The 20% of objections (that aren't universal — "Does it work for my specific auto-repair niche?" "Can I use it outside of Brazil?" — a specific answer in a dedicated FAQ).

✅ **For a scannable format:** For busy readers (who skim the body and jump to the FAQ to check their specific concerns — the Q&A format is quick to find).

✅ **For reinforcement:** For common objections (already addressed in the body, the FAQ is a second mention from a different angle — repetition reinforces the breakdown).

✅ **As a signal of transparency:** The presence of an FAQ says "we have nothing to hide" (a willingness to openly address ANY question — a trust builder).

---

**Placement of the FAQ:**

**Option A — BEFORE the Offer (Pre-Emptive):**

The order: Benefits → Proof → **FAQ** → Offer → CTA

**Reasoning:** Remove objections BEFORE they see the price (they read the FAQ, their doubts are cleared, THEN the offer is presented with less resistance, leading to higher conversion).

**Bly's preference:** "An FAQ before the offer (to clear up doubts first, so the offer is presented cleanly without any lingering objections)."

---

**Option B — AFTER the Offer (Reactive):**

The order: Benefits → Proof → Offer → **FAQ** → CTA

**Reasoning:** They see the offer first (the price, the stack), objections arise naturally, and the FAQ addresses them (reactive timing, the doubts that have already emerged are addressed).

**Kennedy's preference:** "An FAQ after the offer (it's natural — the price is seen, questions arise, the FAQ answers them in a timely manner)."

---

**Option C — BOTH (Comprehensive):**

A mini-FAQ before (the top 3 universal objections — price, time, trust), a full FAQ after the offer (10-15 comprehensive questions).

**Reasoning:** Pre-empt what's common (before), address what's specific (after) — comprehensive coverage.

---

**Ladeira's VTSD practice:**

"In a VSL: The FAQ section is AFTER the offer (the pitch is from min 35-43, the FAQ is embedded in the slides addressing objections in real-time as the offer raises them — a natural reactive timing).

In a Sales Letter: The FAQ is BEFORE the offer (it's read linearly, doubts are cleared before the pricing is seen = lower resistance to seeing the price).

In an Email: An FAQ is rare (for brevity — 200-400w, not enough space, objections are addressed in the natural flow of the body OR linked to a landing page with a comprehensive FAQ)."
