## **SECTION 5.5 — DAILY BROADCAST (AGORA/EMPIRICUS PRACTICE)**

---

**Agora Financial / Empiricus** (daily emails for decades, billions in newsletter revenue):

> *"A broadcast is ongoing (not a finite sequence — it's perpetually daily, for years).*
>
> *The topics: Varied (personal stories today, market analysis tomorrow, an educational technique after that — diversity prevents monotony).*
>
> *Entertainment value: Critical (daily emails must be INTERESTING each day, not dry and boringly educational — personality, stories, unique insights).*
>
> *Pitches: 1/10 emails (~3x/month — rare, and accepted because of the 90% previous value)."*

---

### **BROADCAST EMAIL TYPES (A Rotation for Variety):**

---

**TYPE A — PERSONAL STORY (40% of Broadcasts):**

**Template:**

```
Subject: "What I learned from [a personal experience] (applicable to YOU)"

Body: A story (200-300w — a struggle, a lesson, an extraction applicable to the subscriber), entertaining + educational, visible personality, relatability.

Example: "I failed a R$15k pitch yesterday (ego hurt, rejection stings — BUT I learned: Timing sometimes matters more than offer quality, the client wasn't ready because the decision committee wasn't aligned, the offer wasn't weak, the timing was wrong, a lesson in patience)."
```

---

**TYPE B — MARKET ANALYSIS/INSIGHTS (30%):**

**Template:**

```
Subject: "A market shift in [topic] (what it means for YOU)"

Body: Analysis (250-350w — an observed trend, data, implications for the subscriber's business/life), actionable (what to adjust, an opportunity to seize).

Example: "AI copywriting tools (ChatGPT, Jasper — they are commoditizing basic copy, R$500 projects are dying). THE OPPORTUNITY: High-value strategy/implementation (R$5-15k — AI can't do it, human expertise is at a premium). Shift your focus (from tactics → strategy, from execution → consulting)."
```

---

**TYPE C — EDUCATIONAL TECHNIQUE (20%):**

**(Similar to the nurture emails — teach 1 concept, actionable, standalone value)**

---

**TYPE D — ENTERTAINMENT/RANT (10%):**

**Extreme personality** (strong opinions, occasional rants, humor, controversial takes — engagement spikes, shareability, "did you SEE his email today?" talk-ability).

**Halbert style:** Occasionally profane, brutally direct, entertaining beyond being educational.

---

**Example 10-day rotation:**

```
Day 1: Personal story
Day 2: Educational technique
Day 3: Market analysis
Day 4: Personal story
Day 5: Education
Day 6: Market insight
Day 7: Entertainment/rant
Day 8: Personal
Day 9: Education
Day 10: PITCH (an offer email — 10% of the sequence)

Repeat the cycle (days 11-20, a pitch in email 20, etc)
```

**Variety:** Prevents monotony (not dry education every day — stories, analysis, entertainment are mixed in, engagement is maintained).

---

**Empiricus's practice in Brazil:**

"Daily emails (at 09:00 AM BRT exactly — robotic consistency).

Opens: 32-38% sustained (for years — value + variety + personality = engagement).

Topics: 60% market/finance analysis (the core of the newsletter), 25% investing education, 10% personal stories from the founder, 5% entertainment/rants.

Pitches: 1-2x/month (for newsletters, special reports, events — rare, and accepted with high 15-25% conversion because of the established trust from the daily value)."
