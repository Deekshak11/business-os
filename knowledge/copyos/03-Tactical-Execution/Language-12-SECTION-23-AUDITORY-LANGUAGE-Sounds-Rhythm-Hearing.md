## **SECTION 2.3 — AUDITORY LANGUAGE (Sounds, Rhythm, Hearing)**

---

**Auditory = second most persuasive sense** (voice tone, sounds, rhythm — strong emotional association).

---

**Auditory Words (25):**

Hear, Listen, Sound, Voice, Whisper, Scream, Silence, Noise, Echo, Resonate, Harmony, Dissonance, Melody, Rhythm, Beat (heartbeat), Click, Ring, Buzz, Alarm, Music, Song, Speech, Conversation, Dialogue, Proclaim

---

**Usage examples:**

1. "Listen: Your client's voice on the phone (excitement audible, tone rising) — 'Yes! The $15k proposal is approved, we start Monday' — the words you've WAITED weeks to hear (a sweet sound, validation, income secured)."

**Auditory:** Voice on the phone (sound medium), tone of excitement (emotional auditory), specific words (dialogue), sweet sound (positive association) — the brain HEARS the scene (doesn't just read it).

---

2. "Silence: The phone doesn't ring (it normally rings 20x/day with demanding clients, constant stress) — the automation is working (the funnel handles it, you are free, auditory peace — the absence of sound = the presence of calm)."

**Auditory:** Silence (deliberate absence of sound), phone normally rings (contrast with noise), 20x/day (frequency), automation's absence of sound (calm) — relief via auditory contrast.

---

3. "Click: The mouse button (a satisfying mechanical sound), order confirmed (a notification beep, a dopamine micro-hit), the email arrives instantly (the 'swoosh' sound of your inbox) — transaction complete, revenue +$2,200 (a cascade of sounds = an auditory success sequence)."

**Auditory:** Click (tactile+auditory), beep (notification sound), swoosh (familiar email sound) — multi-sensory (primarily auditory, enriched by the tactile click).
