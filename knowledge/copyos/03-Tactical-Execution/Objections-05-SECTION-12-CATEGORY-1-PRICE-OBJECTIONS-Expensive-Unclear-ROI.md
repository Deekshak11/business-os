## **SECTION 1.2 — CATEGORY #1: PRICE OBJECTIONS (Expensive, Unclear ROI, Affordability)**

---

**The price objection is the #1 most common** (mentioned by 80% of prospects — "expensive," "can't afford," "not worth it").

---

**Different manifestations:**

- "Too expensive" (perceived value < price)
- "I can't afford it" (budget is insufficient, real or perceived)
- "It's not worth it" (unclear ROI, investment not justified)
- "There are cheaper alternatives" (comparison to competitors)
- "I need to think about it" (often code for "price hesitation")

---

### **OVERCOMING PRICE (8 Specific Techniques):**

---

**TECHNIQUE #1 — ROI CALCULATION (Justify the Investment with Math):**

**Visible formula:**

```
"Investment: R$2,997

Return: R$84,000/month additional (average of 47 tracked companies — a conservative estimate, some were R$50k, some R$150k, the median was R$84k)

ROI: R$84,000 ÷ R$2,997 = a 28x return in the FIRST MONTH alone

Payback: <30 days (investment recovered in the first month, months 2-12 = pure profit of R$1,008,000)

The math: Lose R$1M/year (by not investing R$3k) to 'save' R$3k? (Absurd logic — the investment is a no-brainer)"
```

**How it works:** Transparent math (an overwhelming 28x ROI, fast <30-day payback, not investing = losing R$1M — reframes "expensive R$3k" → "cheap R$3k vs. a R$1M return").

---

**TECHNIQUE #2 — DAILY/HOURLY FRAMING (Minimize the Perception):**

```
"Does R$2,997 sound large? (A perspective shift):

Daily: R$8.20/day (R$2,997 ÷ 365 days)

Hourly (working 8h/day): R$1.02/hour

Comparison: 2 Starbucks coffees/day (R$8-10 — a trivial daily expense, we opt for coffees without thinking; an R$8/day investment in a system that generates R$84k/month? Obviously worth it).

Monthly: R$250/month (with a 12x payment plan if you prefer — less than a Netflix subscription, with an incomparable ROI)."
```

**How it works:** A large number R$2,997 → a small daily R$8.20 (reframes the magnitude, trivializes it, the coffee comparison makes it relatable and absurd not to invest when framed so small).

---

**TECHNIQUE #3 — COMPARISON TO ALTERNATIVES (Yours vs. Worse Options):**

```
"Is R$2,997 'expensive'? (Compared to what?):

ALTERNATIVE A — Hire an Agency:
• Cost: R$10-20k/month (a typical retainer)
• Duration: 6-12 months minimum (contract)
• Total: R$60-240k (in agency fees)
• Learning: Zero (they do it, you don't learn, a permanent dependency)
• R$2,997 vs. R$60k-240k = 20-80x cheaper (ownership vs. rental)

ALTERNATIVE B — 1-on-1 Coaching:
• Rate: R$500-1,500/hour (for expert consultants)
• Hours needed: 40-60h (for implementation guidance equivalent to our program)
• Total: R$20-90k (in coaching fees)
• R$2,997 vs. R$20-90k = 7-30x cheaper

ALTERNATIVE C — Trial-and-Error DIY:
• Time: 18-36 months (a steep learning curve, costly mistakes)
• Cost of mistakes: R$10-50k (wasted ads, wrong hires, failed tests — João spent R$32k on errors before he found us)
• Opportunity cost: R$84k/month × 18 months = R$1.5M in lost revenue (the cost of delay)
• R$2,997 vs. R$1.5M in opportunity = 500x cheaper (a shortcut vs. the long road)

ALTERNATIVE D — Do Nothing:
• Cost: R$0 upfront (seems 'free')
• Reality: R$84k/month × 12 months = R$1,008,000/year in opportunity lost forever (staying stuck costs the MOST — it's invisible BUT real)
• R$2,997 vs. R$1M lost = an obvious investment (paradoxically, inaction is the most expensive option)

R$2,997 is the CHEAPEST option (when you compare it to the real alternatives — an agency, coaching, trial-and-error, and inaction all cost 7-500x MORE)."
```

**How it works:** Comparison provides context (R$2,997 in isolation seems large, but vs. a R$60k agency/R$90k coaching/R$1.5M trial-and-error/R$1M inaction = an obvious BARGAIN, reframing "expensive" → "actually the cheapest").

---

**TECHNIQUE #4 — PAYMENT PLANS (Facilitate Affordability):**

```
"Can't afford R$2,997 upfront? (Understood — tight cash flow is a totally valid concern):

INSTALLMENTS: 3×R$1,099 = R$3,297 total

Monthly: R$1,099/month (less than a part-time employee's payroll at R$1,500, less than a monthly ad budget of R$2-5k — an affordable comparison to regular business expenses).

Daily: R$36/day for 3 months (trivial — a day's lunch expense).

PLUS: Payback is <30 days (total investment of R$3,297, a return of R$84k in the first month — you recover it 25x over in the first month, making the installments irrelevant when the payback is so fast).

Affordability: It's not a barrier (installments facilitate it, the payback is immediate, the ROI covers the investment 28x over — the cash flow concern is dissolved)."
```

**How it works:** Installments (R$2,997 → R$1,099/month is psychologically easier), daily is trivial (R$36/day = lunch), fast payback (the cash flow concern is irrelevant when the return is in the first month).

---

**TECHNIQUE #5 — GUARANTEE TO REMOVE RISK (Applied from Docs 4/8):**

```
"Concerned about the investment? (Valid — R$2,997 is significant, risk aversion is natural):

THE GUARANTEE ELIMINATES IT:

Zero risk for you: Scale 2x minimum OR receive a total of R$9,497 (a R$2,997 refund + you keep the R$6,000 in bonuses + a R$5,000 penalty — you GAIN even in the worst-case scenario).

The math: In the worst case, you GAIN R$6,500 (R$9,497 received - R$2,997 invested).

In the best case: You transform to R$100k+/month (a 336x ROI in year 1).

The R$2,997 investment: Is TOTALLY protected (the downside is an upside of a R$6.5k gain, the upside is R$1M+ in revenue, an asymmetric bet overwhelmingly in your favor).

Is it 'expensive' when the risk is ZERO? (A paradox — you can't lose, you just win different amounts, the price is irrelevant when the guarantee provides absolute protection)."
```

**How it works:** The guarantee (zero risk → actually negative risk = you gain even if you fail, the "expensive" objection is dissolved when you mathematically cannot lose).

---

**TECHNIQUE #6 — TESTIMONIAL (Others Thought It Was Expensive, but It Was Worth It):**

```
"The 'expensive' objection? (You're not alone — others thought the same):

Carlos's testimonial: 'I thought R$2,997 was expensive (as a freelancer with a R$3k/month income — a full month's revenue, a scary investment). BUT: The ROI from the first project was R$5,500 (I applied the pricing calculator techniques — it covered the investment 1.8x with a single project, the rest of my career is profit). Expensive? NOT (the best investment of my career, it has already returned 100x+ in 18 months).'

Maria's testimonial: 'I hesitated because of the price (R$2,997 vs. the R$500-800 courses I had bought before). THE DIFFERENCE: The cheaper courses were shelf-ware (theory I didn't apply, a total of R$2,400 wasted with zero results). This R$2,997 was about implementation (accountability, reviews, guaranteed results — expensive upfront, CHEAPER in the long run because it delivered an outcome vs. the cheap ones that didn't).'

THE PATTERN: They thought it was expensive → They applied it → They recovered it fast → It was massively worth it (in retrospect, the 'expensive' perception inverts to a 'bargain' reality)."
```

**How it works:** Social proof (others had the same objection, tried it anyway, and discovered it was worth it — identification; if THEY overcame their hesitation and succeeded, the prospect can too).

---

**TECHNIQUE #7 — POSTPONE THE OBJECTION (Address the Timing):**

```
"'Not now, maybe later' (a timing objection that often masks price):

The question: When is later? (Be specific — 3 months? 6? 1 year? Honestly, never?)

The reality: 'Later' = never (permanent procrastination, Ariely's present bias — later is an infinite postponement).

PLUS: Delay costs you:

Every month you wait: R$84,000 in lost opportunity (potential revenue not generated, gone forever, time doesn't come back).

3 months of 'thinking': R$252,000 lost (a massive opportunity cost).

6 months: R$504,000 (half a million — thinking costs MORE than a Harvard MBA).

The math: Lose R$250-500k (by delaying 3-6 months) to 'save' R$2,997 today? (Absurd economics — delay is the most expensive option).

AFTER the deadline: The price is R$4,997 (+R$2,000 — you lose the additional saving).

A total delay of 3 months: R$252k in opportunity + a R$2k price increase = a R$254,000 cost for 'thinking' (vs. investing R$3k today = you save R$251k — obvious math)."
```

**How it works:** Postponement (the delay is quantified at a cost of R$250k+ — reframing "later is cheaper" → "later is actually the MOST expensive," creating urgency via a massive opportunity cost).

---

**TECHNIQUE #8 — REFRAME THE VALUE (Price vs. Cost Are Different):**

```
"Price vs. Cost (they are different concepts):

The PRICE: R$2,997 (a one-time payment — what appears on your credit card statement).

The COST: [Price - Value Received] = The real cost

The calculation:
Value received: R$1,008,000 (the ROI in additional revenue in year 1, tracked with an average of 47 companies)
Price paid: R$2,997

THE REAL COST: R$2,997 - R$1,008,000 = -R$1,005,003 (a NEGATIVE cost — you GAIN R$1M+, it doesn't COST you R$3k, the investment returns 336x, the economics are inverted).

The reframe: 'Expensive at R$2,997' (looking at the price alone) → 'You're PAYING me R$1M to use the system' (looking at the net cost — it's absurd NOT to accept when the cost is a massive negative gain)."
```

**How it works:** Reframe (price ≠ cost, the real cost is negative when value >>> price, the "expensive" objection is meaningless when the net cost is a R$1M gain for you).
