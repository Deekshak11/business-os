## **SECTION 4.1 — TECHNIQUE #1: JUSTIFICATION (WARREN — IT'S NOT YOUR FAULT)**

---

**Blair Warren** (One Sentence Persuasion — Doc 1 Section 6.0):

> *"People will do anything for those who: encourage their dreams, justify their failures, allay their fears, confirm their suspicions, and help them throw rocks at their enemies.*
>
> *#2 — JUSTIFY THEIR FAILURES (critical for objections).*
>
> *'I tried it before, it didn't work' (a common objection — skepticism from previous failures).*
>
> *The amateur response: 'This time is different' (a claim that isn't convincing).*
>
> *The master response: 'It's NOT your fault that it failed before (it wasn't you — it was THEM, the products were incomplete, the timing was wrong, the circumstances) — justification absolves them of guilt, their ego is protected, and they're willing to try again)."*

---

**Justification applied to objections:**

---

**The objection:** "I tried 3 copywriting courses, none of them worked (a waste of R$2,400, still zero clients). Why would this one be any different?"

---

**Overcoming it with Justification:**

```
"I understand the skepticism (you were burned for R$2,400, 3 courses, zero results — a valid hesitation).

CRITICAL: It wasn't you (your effort, your ability, your dedication).

It was the COURSES (a flawed approach — analyzing why each one failed):

Course A (Udemy for R$50): Theory videos (you watched 12h, you learned the concepts of headlines/bullets/structures — you gained knowledge). MISSING: Implementation (how do you GET clients? How do you PITCH? How do you PRICE? — the business of copywriting was zero-taught, only the theory of copywriting, a 90% gap).

The result was predictable: Knowledge without clients = R$0 (it's shelf-ware, it's not your fault you didn't apply the knowledge, you weren't taught HOW to apply it in a business context).

Course B (Hotmart for R$800): Similar (20h of theory videos, some templates). MISSING: Accountability (you were alone, you got stuck on module 6, zero help, you abandoned it — a typical 12% completion for courses, you were in the 88% majority that didn't complete it, statistically expected).

The result: Incomplete (you stopped halfway, you didn't finish, zero results — NOT a lack of discipline on your part, a lack of a SUPPORT system; courses don't provide accountability, a solo journey has an inevitably high failure rate).

Course C (a YouTube 'guru' for R$1,500): A hyped promise ('R$50k/month in 90 days guaranteed' — an impossible timeline, a scam). MISSING: Honesty (an absurd promise, the guru never did it themselves, pure marketing, zero substance in the delivery).

The result: You were scammed (you lost R$1,500, you learned nothing new that you couldn't get for free on YouTube, you're angry — NOT your gullibility, it was THEIR FRAUD, you were a victim not at fault).

THE PATTERN IN ALL 3: They were incomplete/lacking (theory not business, solo not accountability, hype not honesty — structural flaws in the courses, not flaws in YOU).

You did the right thing: You applied yourself (you tried 3, you invested, you worked — your effort is validated).

They failed YOU: The courses were delivered incomplete (theory alone, zero support, some were fraud — you didn't fail the courses, the courses failed you).

[JUSTIFICATION: Each failure is analyzed, the fault is externalized (the courses were incomplete, fraud, had gaps), the ego is protected (you tried the right way, they delivered the wrong way), and a willingness to try again is created (the 'I was burned before' barrier is removed, guilt is absolved, they are open to a new attempt).]

HOW this is different:

An implementation focus (80% doing, 20% watching — the inverse of the courses), accountability (calls to get unstuck, you're not alone), a guarantee (results OR we pay YOU R$5k — we only offer it if we can deliver, NOT the empty hype of the courses), honesty (12 months is realistic, not an absurd 90 days — transparency builds the trust the courses lacked).

The pattern: The courses failed because they were incomplete/unsupported/dishonest — we address EACH of those gaps (a complete implementation, support and accountability, radical honesty, and guaranteed results).

Is it worth a try? (A total guarantee protects you, the different approach is validated, the justification removes the 'I was burned before' barrier — it's possible to be open-minded again)."
```

**400 words** — a justification for each failure (a specific analysis of why it failed — a theory gap, an accountability gap, fraud, it was structural not personal), the ego is protected (you tried the right way, they were wrong — absolution), openness is created (the barrier is removed, they are willing to try again because it was justified that it wasn't their fault), a differentiation (we fix each gap the courses had — it's complete, supported, honest, and guaranteed).

---

**Effectiveness:** 9/10 (justification is extremely powerful for the "I tried it before and failed" objection — it absolves, protects the ego, and opens them up to a retry).
