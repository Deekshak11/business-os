## **SECTION 2.3 — FORMAT TYPES (Text, Video, Audio — The Impact of Each)**

---

**Testimonials can be in 3 formats** (each has a different impact, using an optimal mix is best).

---

### **FORMAT A — TEXT (Standard, Universal, The Baseline):**

**Pros:**
- Easy to collect (email answers, quick)
- Easy to publish (copy/paste, simple formatting)
- Scannable (readers skim text fast)
- SEO-friendly (text is indexed, searchable)

**Cons:**
- Lower credibility (vs. video — text is perceived as easier to fake)
- Moderate engagement (vs. video — text is less engaging)

**Impact:** 100% baseline (assumed).

**When to use:** Always (a minimum of text testimonials is the foundation).

---

### **FORMAT B — VIDEO (Highest Impact, More Effort):**

**Pros:**
- **+58% more credibility** (Masterson tested — video vs. text, with the same testimonial content, video wins)
- Authenticity (voice, face, emotion are visible — hard to fake, it's verified as genuine)
- Engagement (85% is watched vs. 40% of text is read — attention is higher)
- Emotional (seeing the person = contagion, mirror neurons, empathy is triggered)
- Built-in verification (a real person on-camera = automatically credible)

**Cons:**
- Harder to collect (the client must record it, equipment/skills vary, quality is inconsistent)
- Production (editing if needed — cuts, captions, uploading)
- File size (hosting, bandwidth — technical issues)

**Impact:** 158% (vs. the text baseline — a +58% boost).

**When to use:** High-ticket (>$2k — the ROI of video production is justified), visual products (before/after transformations), emotional stories (video captures emotion that text cannot).

---

**Optimal video specs:**

- **Length:** 60-120 seconds (for attention spans, key points are covered, not lengthy)
- **Quality:** 1080p is acceptable (you don't need 4K cinema — a modern phone is sufficient, authenticity > polish)
- **Captions:** Always (85% watch with the sound off — accessibility is critical)
- **Lighting:** Natural window light is OK (you don't need a professional studio — an authentic feel)
- **Background:** Clean and simple (not distracting — the focus is on the person)
- **Framing:** A medium close-up (face + shoulders — it feels like eye contact, it's personal)

---

### **FORMAT C — AUDIO (The Middle Ground, Podcast-Style):**

**Pros:**
- Easier than video (no camera-shyness, can be recorded with a phone/mic)
- Voice authenticity (tone and emotion are audible — more than text, less effort than video)
- Accessibility (can be listened to while commuting, exercising — flexible consumption)

**Cons:**
- Less impactful than video (no visual verification of a face)
- Platform (audio players are needed — a SoundCloud or podcast embed)

**Impact:** 125-135% (vs. text — better than text, worse than video, in the middle).

**When to use:** For podcast audiences (who already consume audio), long-form testimonials (10-20min detailed — a natural format for audio).

---

### **THE OPTIMAL MIX OF FORMATS:**

**Don't:** Use all text OR all video (a single format is limiting).

**Do:** Use a mix (for diversity, and the strengths of each format).

---

**Example of a mix in a sales letter:**

```
The proof section (1,500w total):

TESTIMONIALS:
• Text #1-2: (200w each, for a quick scan — John and Maria's detailed 7 elements)
• Video #1: (a 90-second embedded video, Carlos's visual transformation — +58% credibility)
• Text #3: (150w, Laura's case, with a before/after photo included)
• Audio #1: (a 120-second embedded player, Ricardo's detailed podcast-style)

Total: 5 testimonials (3 text, 1 video, 1 audio — a mix of formats, equivalent to 4 text + 1.58 video + 1.3 audio = 6.88 impact units vs. 5 text alone = a +38% boost from format diversity).
```

**Format diversity = engagement** (some prefer to read, some to watch, some to listen — it serves everyone).
