## **SECTION 1.3 — CATEGORY #2: TIME OBJECTIONS (Busy, Long Implementation)**

---

**Manifestations of the time objection:**

- "I don't have time" (a busy schedule, already 60-80h/week)
- "It takes too long to implement" (12 weeks = 3 months, seems like an eternity)
- "When will I see results?" (impatience, they want them instantly)
- "I need to focus on other priorities" (competing demands)

---

### **OVERCOMING TIME (6 Techniques):**

---

**TECHNIQUE #1 — QUANTIFY THE REAL TIME (Minimize the Perception):**

```
"'I don't have time' — I understand (the avatar research validated 60-80h/week working, being busy is real).

The REAL time the system requires:

The setup (12 weeks of implementation):
• Module videos: 20-30 min each (12 modules = 6-8h total, spread over 12 weeks = 30-40 min/week of watching)
• Exercises: 30-45 min for each module (to practice applying it — 12 × 40 min = 8h total, 40 min/week)
• Accountability calls: 90 min every two weeks (6 calls total = 9h, an average of 45 min/week)

TOTAL: 30 min of videos + 40 min of practice + 45 min for calls = 2h/week (for 12 weeks).

The total investment: 24h (12 weeks × 2h/week — less than 1 workday a month, spread over 3 months).

vs. The perception: A '12-week program' sounds massive (BUT the reality of 24h total = 3 workdays compressed, which is manageable when spread out).

PLUS: It SAVES time long-term (the automation implementations, the systems efficiencies — it frees up 20-30h/week after the setup, a net GAIN of 1,000+ hours in year 1)."
```

**How it works:** Quantify (12 weeks sounds long, but 24h total = 3 days spread out = manageable), minimize (anyone has 2h/week, non-negotiable priorities can be adjusted), an inversion (you invest 24h of time, you get back 1,000h/year — a 40:1 temporal ROI).

---

**TECHNIQUE #2 — THE PARADOX (Being Busy Is the Reason to Invest, Not to Avoid):**

```
"'I'm too busy' = that's precisely WHY you need the system (not a reason to avoid it — an urgent reason):

You're busy (60-80h) because of: Inefficiency (everything is manual, no systems, no automation — working IN the business, not ON the business).

The system: Builds efficiency (automations, templates, systems — it frees up 20-30h/week, it reduces being busy from 60h → 35h).

The paradox: 'I'm too busy to implement efficiency' = a guarantee you will stay busy FOREVER (a vicious cycle — you never have time to fix being busy, so you stay permanently busy, burnout is inevitable).

Break the cycle: Invest 24h (12 weeks × 2h, which is manageable), and gain 1,200h/year back (52 weeks × 25h saved — a net of +1,176h in year 1, and every year after).

Being busy is a symptom (the root problem is a lack of systems), the system is the cure (not a band-aid, it fixes the root — being busy dissolves after a complete implementation).

The logic: The busiest people = have the MOST need for systems (efficiency creates time, time creates less busyness — a 24h investment solves the busy problem permanently)."
```

**How it works:** Reversal (the busy objection → being busy is the reason to invest not to avoid, the vicious cycle paradox is shown, the system is the cure for being busy not something that perpetuates it, the logic is an overwhelming need for MORE when busy not less).

---

**TECHNIQUE #3 — QUICK WINS (Immediate Results, Early Momentum):**

```
"The concern: '12 weeks is long, when will I see results?' (Impatience is valid, you want to see progress fast):

QUICK WINS (Intentionally Designed):

Week 1 (Pillar #1 — The Offer):
• Day 3: The offer is rebuilt (a value stack, optimized pricing)
• Day 5: It's tested on 10 people (validated with an 8/10 yes — confidence the offer works)
• Day 7: It's launched (an immediate conversion of 2.1% → 7.8% — a result in the FIRST WEEK, proof that it works)

Week 2:
• Revenue increase: R$8-15k (the offer converts better, visible on the dashboard — a tangible result in 14 days)

Week 4 (Pillar #2 — The Funnel):
• Abandoned cart recovery: 28% (automated emails, R$5-10k/month in revenue recovered — a visible result in month 1)

Week 6:
• LTV increase: R$880 → R$2,400 (the funnel upsells are working — a measurable improvement)

The progression: Results EVERY week (you don't wait 12 weeks to see anything — week 1-2-4-6 milestones, momentum is maintained, impatience is addressed with continuous quick wins).

Month 3 is complete: The full transformation (a typical range of R$28k → R$70-90k — dramatic, BUT the journey had weekly wins, it wasn't a 'black box' of waiting 90 days for a miracle at the end)."
```

**How it works:** Quick wins (a result in week 1, not a 12-week wait — impatience is addressed, early momentum maintains motivation, visible progression makes patience easier).

---

**TECHNIQUE #4 — A TIME-RELATED TESTIMONIAL (Others Were Busy and Managed):**

```
"'I'm too busy' — Others said the same:

João's testimonial: 'I work 65h/week (how could I add 2h/week for a program?). I found it: Mornings from 6-7 am (before work, it was quiet, I got 2h in there — I sacrificed 30 min of sleep, waking up at 5:30 not 6 am, it was doable). For 12 weeks: I implemented it (2h/week consistently, I missed 2 weeks because I was sick, but 10 weeks was enough). The result: I SAVED 27h/week after (automation freed up my time, a net gain of 25h — a 24h investment returned 1,200h/year, it was worth waking up 30 min earlier temporarily).'

Maria's testimonial: 'I'm a single mom (2 kids, I work 50h, I thought I had impossible time). My lunch breaks (30 min daily instead of 60 min, the same productivity, I found 30 min × 5 days = 2.5h/week — enough for the modules). On weekends (the kids are with their dad on Saturdays, 2h free — I attended the calls). I managed (it wasn't easy, BUT it's a priority when you want a result bad enough — you find the time when you're committed).'

The pattern: 'Busy' ≠ impossible (a shift in priority, a small temporary sacrifice of 30 min of sleep/lunch, and the time appears — commitment finds a way)."
```

**How it works:** Social proof from busy people (João with 65h, Maria a single mom with 50h — both busier than average, they managed to find the time; if THEY can, the prospect can, for identification + inspiration).

---

**TECHNIQUE #5 — TEMPORAL OPPORTUNITY COST (Delay = Irreversible Lost Time):**

```
"'I'll do it when I have time' (someday thinking):

The reality: Time NEVER just appears (magically — the calendar doesn't suddenly free up, being busy continues unless there's an intervention).

The cost of delay (temporal):

Today: Busy 60h, revenue R$30k (the status quo).

6 months of 'waiting for time to appear': Still busy 60h (BUT revenue COULD be R$80k if you had started today — R$50k/month × 6 = R$300,000 in opportunity GONE forever, time doesn't come back).

A 1-year delay: R$600,000 lost (a staggering opportunity cost — waiting for the 'right time' costs MORE than the investment by 200x).

The paradox: Waiting to 'have time' = a guarantee you NEVER will (busyness perpetuates, opportunity passes, regret accumulates).

Action today (a priority shift of 2h/week, a trivial sacrifice): Creates future time (efficiency gains of 25h/week = a net of +23h, an investment of 2h returns 25h — time CREATES time when you build systems)."
```

**How it works:** Opportunity cost (a R$600k/year delay vs. a 24h investment = an absurd wait, time never magically appears, action creates time via efficiency — the paradox is resolved).
