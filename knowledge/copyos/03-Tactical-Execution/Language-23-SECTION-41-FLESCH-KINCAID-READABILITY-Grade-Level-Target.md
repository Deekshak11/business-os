## **SECTION 4.1 — FLESCH-KINCAID READABILITY (Grade Level Target)**

---

**Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level** (a readability formula — 1975, the standard):

**Calculates:** Average sentence length + syllables per word → Grade level (1-12+).

**Tools:** Hemingway Editor (free), Microsoft Word (readability statistics), Grammarly (premium).

---

**Interpreting grade levels:**

- **Grade 5-6:** Very simple (short 8w sentences, 1-2 syllable words — mass market tabloids, ads)
- **Grade 7-8:** Simple (conversational — blogs, emails, mass-market copy)
- **Grade 9-10:** Moderate (newspapers, business communications)
- **Grade 11-12:** Complex (academic, technical, sophisticated)
- **Grade 13+:** Very complex (scientific journals, legal documents)

---

### **TARGET FOR COPYWRITING:**

**Mass market (B2C, products <$2k):** Grade 6-8 (simple, accessible to all).

**Why:** Accessibility (80% of the population reads comfortably at a grade 8 level, above that = friction), skimmability (simple = fast to process for busy readers), trust (complex = confusing, simple = transparent).

---

**Sophisticated audiences (B2B, experts, high-ticket >$5k):** Grade 9-11 (moderate complexity is appropriate).

**Why:** Credibility (experts expect sophistication, grade 6 feels condescending, like it's "dumbed down"), precision (complex ideas require nuance, simplicity sometimes oversimplifies).

---

**Example comparison:**

**Grade 6 (Simple):**

"The system works. It has three parts. Each part helps. Together: They are better. Tested 47 times. The result: Good. You can use it."

**Analysis:** Sentences are 3-7w (short), words are 1-2 syllables (simple), the rhythm is choppy (too simple, feels infantilizing).

**Appropriate:** Rarely (maybe for ultra-mass-market ads, tabloids).

---

**Grade 8 (Target for Copywriting):**

"The 3-Pillar system works through integration (offer + funnel + traffic together). It was tested with 47 companies (89% succeeded, with 4.1x average growth). You can replicate this (it's a proven pattern, with a clear roadmap)."

**Analysis:** Sentences are 10-15w (conversational), words are 1-3 syllables (accessible BUT not childish), the rhythm is steady (readable, professional).

**Appropriate:** Most copy (mass B2C, info products, emails).

---

**Grade 11 (Sophisticated):**

"The triadic systematic integration (offer optimization, funnel automation, traffic diversification) demonstrates multiplicative synergistic effects (quantified: isolated efficacy 1×1×1=1 vs. integrated performance 10×3×5=150x), validated across heterogeneous business models (n=47, 23-month longitudinal tracking, a robust methodology)."

**Analysis:** Sentences are 25-35w (dense), words are 3-5 syllables (latinate, technical — triadic, systematic, multiplicative, synergistic, heterogeneous, longitudinal), the rhythm is heavy (academic).

**Appropriate:** Technical B2B, academic audiences, white papers (NOT mass-market copy — it alienates 80%).

---

**Guideline:**

**Aim for:** Grade 8 as a default (conversational, accessible, professional but not childish).

**Adjust:** Grade 6-7 for extremely simple mass market (ads, tabloids), Grade 9-10 for moderate B2B (professionalism), Grade 11+ for experts only (white papers, technical docs).

**Test:** Use the Hemingway Editor (paste your copy, it shows the grade level + highlights complex sentences — edit until you hit your target).
