## **SECTION 3.3 — ALLITERATION & ASSONANCE (Subtle Sound Patterns)**

---

**Alliteration:** Repeating consonant sounds ("Broke to Boss," "Stuck to Scaled").

**Assonance:** Repeating vowel sounds ("Free to Be," "Lean and Mean").

---

**Function:** Memorability (sound patterns are sticky, the brain remembers rhythms).

**No:** Obvious gimmicks ("Peter Piper Picked" nursery rhyme — childish).

**Yes:** Subtle (1-2 instances in the copy, for touchstone phrases, not in every sentence).

---

### **ALLITERATION EXAMPLES (Subtle, Professional):**

1. **"Broke to Boss"** (B-B repetition — a memorable transformation journey).

2. **"Stuck to Scaled"** (S-S — business growth, a rhythm).

3. **"Frustrated to Free"** (F-F — an emotional journey).

4. **"Chaos to Control"** (C-C — restoring order).

5. **"Plateau to Peak"** (P-P — a performance journey).

6. **"Trial to Triumph"** (T-T — a victory arc).

---

**Usage:**

**Touchstone phrases** (Doc 3 Section 5 — a memorable summary of the offer): Alliteration boosts memorability by +20-30%.

"Broke to Boss in 12 Months" = it sticks (B-B sound, rhythm, short — memorable).

vs. "Poor to Chief in 12 Months" = doesn't stick (no sound pattern, clunky).

---

**Occasional headlines:**

"From Frustrated Founder to Free CEO (Framework Inside)"

Alliteration: F-F-F (frustrated, founder, free — triple, strong rhythm, memorable).

---

**Frequency:** 1-2 alliterations per 1,000w of copy (a subtle enhancement, not an overwhelming gimmick).

---

### **ASSONANCE EXAMPLES:**

1. **"Free to Be"** (ee-ee vowel repeat — a freedom journey).

2. **"Pain to Gain"** (ai-ai — transformation).

3. **"Lean and Mean"** (ee-ea — efficiency).

4. **"Bright Flight"** (i-i — an upward trajectory).

---

**Subtlety is critical:**

**Obvious alliteration:** "Peter Piper Picked a Peck of Pickled Peppers" (a gimmick, not professional).

**Subtle:** "A Simple System for Success" (S-S-S — professional, not gimmicky, a natural rhythm).
