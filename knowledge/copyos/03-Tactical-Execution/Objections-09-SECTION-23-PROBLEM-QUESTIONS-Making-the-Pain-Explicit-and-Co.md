## **SECTION 2.3 — PROBLEM QUESTIONS (Making the Pain Explicit and Conscious)**

---

**P — Problem Questions (in Sales):**

"What problems are you experiencing with your current system?" (open-ended, the prospect articulates the pain).

"How often does this problem occur?" (frequency, severity).

---

**Copy adaptation (Statements/Rhetorical Questions):**

```
"Do you feel:

• Your revenue is stuck (in the R$30k range for 12+ months, a frustrating plateau, it's not growing despite your effort)?

• Your hours are excessive (60-80h/week, on the edge of burnout, unsustainable long-term)?

• A strain on your family (absent from dinners, missed kids' events, accumulating guilt)?

• An uncertain future ('where will I be in 2 years if nothing changes?' — anxiety, an unclear trajectory)?"
```

**Each bullet = a Problem question converted into a statement** (it identifies a specific pain, the reader mentally checks "yes" for each = problem consciousness is raised).

---

**The function of P-questions in copy:**

**To make the pain explicit:** Not a vague "do you have problems" (which is generic), but a specific "revenue stuck, excessive hours, family strain, uncertain future" (tangible, multi-dimensional).

**For self-diagnosis:** The reader identifies their own problems (a mental checklist, each "yes" is a problem confirmed in their own awareness, the copywriter isn't telling them — self-discovery = ownership).

**To set up agitation:** The problems are listed (as a foundation), the next Implication step amplifies them (the consequences).
