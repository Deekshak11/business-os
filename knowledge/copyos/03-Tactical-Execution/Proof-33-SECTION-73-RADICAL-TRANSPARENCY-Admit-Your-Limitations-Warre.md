## **SECTION 7.3 — RADICAL TRANSPARENCY (Admit Your Limitations, Warren-Style):**

---

**What to transparently reveal:**

✅ **The failure rate:** "11% didn't achieve 2x (5 of 47 — they were refunded, the reasons were: 3 for incomplete application, and 2 for external circumstances)"

✅ **The method's limitations:** "It works for digital businesses (e-commerce, info products, SaaS, agencies, and consulting). Manufacturing and physical retail = have not been tested, and we don't recommend it (an honesty boundary)."

✅ **That refunds were honored:** "A total of 5 refunds (from Jan 2023 - to Nov 2024), and all were honored (with $2,997 + a $5,000 penalty each = a total of $37,985 was paid out — it's a real guarantee, not empty marketing fulfillment)."

✅ **What you don't know:** "I'm not an expert in scaling paid traffic with $100k+/month budgets (our expertise is with optimal budgets of $5k-20k/month, beyond that = other experts are better)."

---

**Why radical transparency works:**

**The trust paradox:** Admitting negatives = HIGHER credibility (it's counterintuitive BUT validated).

**Signals of honesty:** You're not hiding anything (the 11% failure rate is shown, the honored refunds are disclosed — transparency = there's nothing to hide, you're trustworthy).

**Realistic expectations:** The reader knows it's not 100% (an 11% failure is possible, BUT the 89% who succeed are the majority, and the guarantee protects them — an informed decision is possible).
