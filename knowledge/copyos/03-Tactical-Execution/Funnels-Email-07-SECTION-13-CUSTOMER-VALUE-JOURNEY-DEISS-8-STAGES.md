## **SECTION 1.3 — CUSTOMER VALUE JOURNEY (DEISS 8 STAGES)**

---

**Ryan Deiss** (DigitalMarketer, Customer Value Journey — an 8-stage framework from awareness to advocacy):

> *"The customer journey is not a simple linear path (awareness → purchase, done).*
>
> *It's 8 stages (each requires different content, offers, emotions, and actions — the funnel maps TO the journey, each stage is a specific funnel element).*
>
> *Missing one stage = a leak in the funnel (prospects exit, they don't convert — every stage is critical)."*

---

### **THE 8 STAGES (Deiss's Complete Framework):**

---

**STAGE 1 — AWARENESS (Discovering You Exist):**

**Customer state:** Doesn't know you (cold), scrolling, discovering.

**Stage objective:** Attention (stop the scroll, make them aware).

**Funnel element:** **Ad creative** (Facebook, Google, native — Doc 5 Section 2.C.2 GOATED).

**Content type:** Curiosity (intriguing headlines), educational teaser (a glimpse of a problem solved), entertainment (story hook).

**Emotion:** Curiosity (what is this?), intrigue (is this relevant to me?).

**Desired action:** Click (ad → landing page, initial engagement).

**Metric:** CTR (click-through rate — 1-5% for cold is typical).

---

**STAGE 2 — ENGAGEMENT (Initial Interest, Exploring):**

**State:** Aware of you, exploring (not yet committed).

**Objective:** Capture contact (email opt-in), deliver immediate value.

**Funnel element:** **Lead gen landing page** (Doc 5 Section 2.C.3) + **Lead magnet** (free ebook, webinar, checklist).

**Content:** Educational (solves a small problem for free), credibility (hints of proof), low-barrier CTA (email only).

**Emotion:** Interest (want to know more), hope (a glimpse of a solution).

**Action:** Opt-in (exchange email for free value).

**Metric:** Opt-in rate (20-50% of landing page visitors).

---

**STAGE 3 — SUBSCRIBE (Permission for Ongoing Communication):**

**State:** Email captured, BUT permission is tenuous (easy to unsubscribe if annoyed).

**Objective:** Solidify permission (deliver the promised value, establish a relationship).

**Funnel element:** **Indoctrination sequence** (AWAI — 5-7 welcome emails, Doc 10 Part III Section 3.3).

**Content:** Onboarding (set expectations, deliver the lead magnet, introduce yourself, provide quick wins).

**Emotion:** Gratitude (received free value), curiosity (what's next?).

**Action:** Engage with emails (open, click, consume content — don't unsubscribe).

**Metric:** Open rate for emails 1-3 (40-70% — critical engagement during the indoctrination period).

---

**STAGE 4 — CONVERT (First Purchase, Micro-Commitment):**

**State:** Engaged subscriber, considering a purchase.

**Objective:** First transaction (becomes a paid customer, psychological barrier crossed).

**Funnel element:** **Tripwire offer** (R$47-297 — low-ticket, high-value, irresistible, 10:1+ ROI, Doc 4).

**Content:** Offer presentation (benefits, brief proof, guarantee, urgency), transitional case (from free to paid).

**Emotion:** Excitement (an amazing deal), trust (low risk, high value).

**Action:** First purchase (R$47-297, card entered, buyer status achieved).

**Metric:** Conversion rate (8-25% of subscribers → tripwire buyers).

---

**STAGE 5 — EXCITE (Deliver Exceptionally, Exceed Expectations):**

**State:** New customer (first purchase, evaluating the experience).

**Objective:** Delight (exceed expectations, create a WOW moment, seed advocacy).

**Funnel element:** **Product delivery + surprise** (content quality exceeds what was promised, an unexpected bonus, responsive support).

**Content:** Exceptional product (better than expected), surprise bonus (not mentioned in the offer, an unexpected delight), smooth onboarding (easy setup, immediate quick wins).

**Emotion:** Delight (expectations exceeded), satisfaction (purchase validated as a good one), gratitude (the vendor cares, shows generosity).

**Action:** Consume the product (high engagement, completion, get results), consider the next purchase.

**Metric:** Completion rate (80%+ finish the product — a proxy for engagement), support tickets (low = good UX, high = friction).

---

**STAGE 6 — ASCEND (Upsell, Cross-Sell, Climb the Value Ladder):**

**State:** Satisfied customer (product delivered, trust established).

**Objective:** Increase LTV (sell higher-tier offers, ascend the ladder).

**Funnel element:** **Upsell sequences** (email offers Tier 3 core after Tier 2 tripwire, Tier 4 high-ticket after core).

**Content:** Next offer (a natural progression, builds on the previous one, solves the next problem), stacked benefits, urgency/scarcity.

**Emotion:** Desire (want more, appetite whetted), FOMO (others are ascending, don't want to be left behind).

**Action:** Purchase a higher tier (R$997, R$2,997, R$10k — ascend the ladder).

**Metric:** Ascension rate (30-60% of customers buy a 2nd+ product — LTV multiplier).

---

**STAGE 7 — ADVOCATE (Referrals, Reviews, Testimonials):**

**State:** Raving fan (achieved results, grateful, wants to share).

**Objective:** Amplification (word-of-mouth, generate social proof, R$0 acquisition cost).

**Funnel element:** **Referral program** (incentives to refer friends), **review requests** (collect testimonials, Doc 8), **case study** (showcase the transformation).

**Content:** Ask for advocacy (refer friends for a discount/bonus, write a review for a reward, share on social), make it easy (1-click referral link, simple review form).

**Emotion:** Gratitude (want to reciprocate the value received), pride (showcasing their transformation), belonging (a member of the tribe, the role of an advocate).

**Action:** Refer (friends join), review (write a 5-star review), testimonial (provide a detailed one), social share (post their transformation).

**Metric:** Referral rate (10-30% of customers refer 1+ friends), review rate (5-20% write one), viral coefficient (how many new customers does each customer bring? 0.3-0.8 is typical).

---

**STAGE 8 — PROMOTE (Brand Ambassador, Affiliate, Partner):**

**State:** Super-fan (extreme advocate, potential for a business relationship).

**Objective:** Partnership (affiliate, JV, co-marketing — for mutual scaling benefits).

**Funnel element:** **Affiliate program** (commission structure, provide tools), **partnership opportunities** (co-create products, co-host events).

**Content:** Invite to a partnership (exclusive, selective), attractive terms (20-40% commission, provide marketing tools), training (how to promote effectively).

**Emotion:** Honored (invited to be a partner, not just a customer), entrepreneurial (earn by promoting, a business opportunity).

**Action:** Promote (use affiliate links, create content, introduce you to their audiences).

**Metric:** Affiliate revenue (% of revenue from affiliates — 10-40% for sustainable businesses), partner lifetime value (mega — each partner = 10-100 potential customers).
