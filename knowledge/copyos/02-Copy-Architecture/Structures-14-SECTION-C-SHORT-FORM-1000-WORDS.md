## **SECTION C — SHORT-FORM (<1,000 WORDS)**

---

### **2.C.1 — THE EMAIL (BLY/BRUNSON — 150-400 WORDS)**

---

**The Email = the workhorse** of marketing (Bly: "email still reigns in 2024" — a R$42 ROI for every R$1 invested, DMA data).

**Not:** A replacement for other formats (email complements them — sequences nurture, they don't replace sales pages/VSLs).

**Yes:** A vehicle for multiple purposes (nurturing, selling, content, relationships).

---

**WHEN TO USE EMAIL:**

✅ **State 1** (existing customers — for re-engagement, upsells, and cross-sells)

✅ **Nurture sequences** (leads → customers, Doc 10 — a Soap Opera, the 90/10 Nurture)

✅ **Content delivery** (newsletters, updates, education — relationship building)

✅ **Flash sales** (short-term urgency — 24-48h offers, the deadline is communicated via email)

✅ **Announcements** (launches, webinars, events — a quick broadcast)

---

**WHEN NOT TO USE IT (As a Standalone Sale):**

❌ **State 4-5 cold traffic** (an email alone is insufficient education — you need a VSL/webinar/promo)

❌ **High-ticket** (>R$5k — email sequences YES to nurture, but a single email to close NO)

❌ **Complex products** (requires a demonstration, a deep explanation — a 400w email is insufficient)

---

### **THE EMAIL STRUCTURE (4 Types Based on the Goal):**

---

**TYPE A — THE DIRECT SALE EMAIL (State 1, a Simple Offer, 150-300 Words):**

```
THE STRUCTURE (A Condensed P-A-S):

THE SUBJECT (40 chars, mobile-optimized):
"50% OFF Today — [Product] — Expires at 11:59 PM"
[The Offer + urgency, specific]

THE PREVIEW TEXT (50 chars):
"Save R$1,000. The same platform, for half the price."
[The benefit of the savings, a reinforcement]

THE BODY:

P — THE IMMEDIATE OFFER (30-50 words):
"Hi [Name],

Today only: [Product] is 50% off.

Normally: R$1,997
Today: R$997
You save: R$1,000"

[The offer is in line 1 — no buildup, State 1 is transactional]

A — A VALUE REMINDER (60-80 words):
"The same [main features listed — 3-5 bullets].

The same [core benefit].

Zero changes (just a better price — the value is identical, the cost is half)."

[A brief reminder — they already know the product in State 1, this is just a refresh]

S — URGENCY + THE CTA (60-100 words):
"Expires: Today at 11:59 PM BRT (8 hours left).

After midnight: It goes back to R$1,997 (you'll lose the R$1,000 in savings).

[A GREEN BUTTON: SECURE YOUR 50% OFF →]

No questions. 1 click. 30 seconds.

[Link]"

[Specific urgency, a clear penalty, a visual CTA + a text link, and ease]

P.S. (30-40 words):
"P.S. Seriously — this literally expires at 11:59 PM today. The R$1,000 disappears at midnight.
You use [the product] (you know it works) — lock in half the price now. Click. [Link]"

[The P.S. is always there — 80% of people read it, and it's a critical reinforcement of the urgency]

TOTAL: 180-270 words (ultra-brief, appropriate for State 1).
```

**A direct sale email:** Only for State 1-2 (they're already aware, it's a quick transaction).

**Conversion:** 8-15% (existing customers, a relevant offer, real urgency).

---

**TYPE B — A NURTURE/EDUCATIONAL EMAIL (For Sequences, 250-400 Words):**

```
THE STRUCTURE (A Story + a Lesson + a Soft CTA):

THE SUBJECT (curiosity, not a pitch):
"Why 'Eating Less' Didn't Work for You"
[A mistake angle, identification, curiosity]

THE BODY:

THE STORY (100-150w):
"Maria, 52, tried a low-calorie diet (1,200 cal/day, for 3 months).

She lost: 12 lbs (in the first 6 weeks — it worked!).

After that: A plateau (weeks 7-12, zero loss despite following it strictly).

The frustration: 'Why did it stop working?' (her discipline was maintained, BUT her body stopped responding).

She quit: In week 14 (frustrated, she resumed eating normally).

The result: She regained the 12 lbs + an extra 3 lbs (a total of 15 lbs gained vs. where she started — she was worse off than before she tried).

Sound familiar? (The yo-yo cycle — have you lived through it?)"

[A relatable story — Maria is a mirror of the avatar, a familiar pattern]

THE LESSON (100-150w):
"Why the plateau + regain? (The science explains it)

Metabolic adaptation: Extreme calorie restriction → the body thinks there's a famine → it slows the metabolism by 30-40% (to conserve energy, survival mode — the Minnesota study from 1944, replicated 100+ times).

The result: You burn fewer calories (even with the same activity — your metabolism is slower) = a plateau is inevitable (an intake of 1,200, but now you're burning 1,200 after adapting, zero deficit).

You resume normal eating: Your metabolism is STILL slow (it takes months to recover) = a regain + extra (you're burning 1,500 now vs. 2,000 before — a 500-cal deficit is reversed = a gain).

It wasn't Maria's failure (her discipline). It was the METHOD's failure (the restriction approach is flawed).

[The science education — the mechanism is explained, it justifies the failure, from Warren's principle in Doc 1]"

THE TIE-IN + A SOFT CTA (50-100w):
"The right method: Not restriction (a metabolism crash). It's composition (keto — what you eat, not how much).

In tomorrow's email, I'll reveal the exact method Maria used (she lost 20 lbs and has MAINTAINED it for 18 months — zero rebound, her metabolism was preserved).

Stay tuned.

[An optional link: Learn more now [a landing page] — a soft CTA, not a hard sell]"

[A tie-in to the product, a soft CTA, a promise of value tomorrow — an open loop, they'll return tomorrow]

TOTAL: 250-400 words (a typical nurture email — education + a soft tie-in, not a hard pitch).
```

**A nurture email:** A ratio of 80-90% education/value, and 10-20% a tie-in to the product (Abraham's 90/10 — Doc 1 Section 2.1, Reciprocity).

**Frequency:** Daily or 3-5x/week (Masterson/Agora — consistency, familiarity, Doc 1 Section 2.4).

**The goal:** To build a relationship (not an immediate sale — it's long-term, trust accumulates, and an eventual pitch is accepted naturally).

---

**TYPE C — A SOAP OPERA SEQUENCE EMAIL (Brunson — A 5-7 Email Story Arc):**

**→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 10, Section 4.2** (The complete Soap Opera Sequence).

**A preview of the structure:**

```
Email 1: Set the stage (introduce the problem via a story, a cliff-hanger)
Email 2: High drama (the problem worsens, an attempt to solve it fails, a cliff-hanger)
Email 3: The epiphany (the moment of discovery, "then I realized...", a tease of the solution)
Email 4: The hidden benefits (reveals the benefits of the solution, more value, an optional cliff-hanger)
Email 5-7: Urgency/The Pitch (the hard sell — the offer, the deadline, the close)
```

**Each email:** 200-350 words (a continuation of the story, cliff-hangers drive opens for the next email).

**The sequence:** 5-7 days (builds a narrative, culminates in a pitch).

**Open rates:** 40-60% (storytelling = engagement, cliff-hangers = they must open the next one).

**Conversion:** 3-8% (from the pitch in emails 5-7 — the recipients are invested in the story, and there's reciprocity from the 4-6 emails of value).

---

**TYPE D — A BROADCAST EMAIL (Content/Newsletter, 300-600 Words):**

A weekly newsletter (valuable content, with a soft pitch occasionally in 1 out of 4-5 emails).

The structure: An insight/tip (education), a story (entertainment), and a soft mention of the product (not pushy).

The goal: Engagement (opens, clicks, a relationship), not an immediate sale (long-term nurturing).

### **2.C.2 — THE AD CREATIVE (HORMOZI'S GOATED — 15-90 SECONDS OF VIDEO/IMAGE+TEXT)**

---

**Alex Hormozi** (the GOATED framework — Greatest Of All Time Ads, $100M Leads 2023):

For social ads (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube) — a 15-90 second video OR an image+text.

**The Goal:** Attention (to stop the scroll) → A Click (to a landing page) — NOT a sale in the ad itself (it's step 1 of the funnel).

---

**THE GOATED FRAMEWORK:**

**G — GRAB ATTENTION (The First 3 Seconds — Make or Break):**

**A visual + statement hook** (an extreme pattern interrupt).

Examples:
- "If you make <R$50k/month, stop scrolling (the next 47 seconds = a possible R$10k+/month boost)"
- A visual: You pointing at the camera (direct, confrontational) OR a shocking stat as text on the screen

**3 seconds decide:** 85% of viewers will either scroll past OR stop (Hormozi tested).

A strong hook: 60-75% stop (retention).

A weak hook: 10-20% stop (most scroll).

---

**O — THE OUTCOME PROMISE (Seconds 4-10):**

A big, specific benefit, with a timeframe.

"How to generate R$10-30k/month as a freelance copywriter (starting in 60-90 days, working from home, with no boss, and a high-demand skill)."

---

**A — AUTHORITY/A QUICK PROOF (Seconds 11-20):**

Fast credibility (not lengthy).

"340 students, and 78% got their first sale in 60 days (it's tracked — not anecdotal)."

---

**T — TEASE THE MECHANISM (Seconds 21-40):**

HOW it works (briefly — a tease, not a complete explanation).

"A 3-step system: Learn (with templates), Practice (with revision feedback), and Launch (with client acquisition scripts). It's implementation (not just theory videos alone — it's doing)."

---

**E — AN ENGAGEMENT ASK (Seconds 41-60):**

"Want to know the whole thing? (A free 40-min masterclass — I teach the 3-step process in detail, I show you the templates, and I give you the scripts).

The link is in my bio. Or comment 'COPY' (and I'll DM you the link)."

[The CTA: Low friction — a link in the bio, or a comment (engagement = an algorithm boost)]

---

**D — A DIRECTIVE CTA (Seconds 61-90 If Long, OR Seconds 45-60 If Short):**

"Click the link. It takes 60 seconds to register. The masterclass is free (an R$800 consultation value).

340 people watched it (last week). 78% applied it (and got results).

Are you next?"

[A final push — ease, social proof, a challenge/invitation]

---

**GOATED = 60-90 seconds** (the sweet spot for IG/FB — long enough for a teaser education, short enough for the attention span).

**15-30 seconds:** Ultra-short (for TikTok, Reels — just a hook + an outcome + a CTA).

**90-120 seconds:** Longer (for YouTube, where tolerance is higher, and you can develop it more).

---

### **VARIATIONS BY PLATFORM:**

---

**FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM (60-90 Seconds):**

- **Captions:** 100% of the time (85% of people watch muted — on mobile)
- **The hook:** In the first 3 sec (scrolling is fast, an extreme pattern interrupt is needed)
- **The CTA:** A link in the comments OR the bio (not in the caption — the algorithm penalizes it, and reach is lower)

---

**TIKTOK/REELS (15-45 Seconds):**

- **Ultra-short:** A hook (3 sec) + an outcome (7 sec) + a CTA (5 sec) = 15 sec total
- **A native feel:** Not polished (UGC style — user-generated content, authenticity > production value)
- **Trending:** Sounds, effects (the algorithm favors them — participate in trends)

---

**YOUTUBE PRE-ROLL (15-30 Seconds If Skippable, 90-120 If Non-Skippable):**

**Skippable (after 5 sec):**
- The hook is in the first 5 sec (before the skip button appears — you must capture them)
- The outcome + the CTA are in seconds 6-30 (if they've stayed, they're interested)

**Non-skippable:**
- You can develop it more (90-120 sec), BUT the audience is captive (it can be annoying — balance education/value, don't make it a pure pitch)

### **2.C.3 — THE LEAD GEN LANDING PAGE (BLY — 300-800 WORDS)**

---

**A landing page** (a squeeze page, an opt-in page — it captures an email/info, not a direct sale).

**The Goal:** Lead generation (a conversion = an email is captured, not a purchase).

**The Funnel:** Ad → **Landing Page** (opt-in) → Thank You page (delivery) → Email Sequence → VSL/Sales Page (the pitch).

---

**WHEN TO USE IT:**

✅ **Lead gen campaigns** (to build a list, to nurture later)

✅ **Lead magnets** (a free ebook, webinar, masterclass, or template — a value exchange for an email)

✅ **Cold traffic** (State 3-5 — they're not ready to buy, BUT they're willing to opt-in for free value)

✅ **Multi-step funnels** (not a direct sale — nurturing is required before the pitch)

---

**THE STRUCTURE (300-800 Words):**

```
SECTION 1 — THE HEADLINE (15-30 Words):

The Formula: The benefit of the lead magnet (what they get for free).

Example: "Download for Free: The Complete Guide to Copywriting (127 Pages, 50+ Templates, Used By 8,347 Copywriters) — Zero Cost, Instant Download"

[A clear benefit — a complete guide, with specific pages/templates, social proof of 8,347, an emphasis on it being free, and instant]

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 2 — THE SUBHEAD (20-40 Words):

Expands on the value OR adds urgency/scarcity.

Example: "Learn: The 4 U's Headline Formula, Curiosity Bullets, and Email Structures (The Techniques A-List Copywriters Use, Which Are Normally Taught in R$500+ Courses, but Are YOURS Free Today)"

[It expands — what you'll specifically learn, a value comparison to R$500+ courses, and that it's free today]

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 3 — THE "Inside You'll Discover" BULLETS (150-300 Words):

8-12 bullets (curiosity-driven, teasing the content of the lead magnet).

"Inside you'll discover:

• The 4 U's headline formula (guaranteed to score 30+ points — for a 3-5x conversion rate over weak headlines, tested on 1,000+ headlines at Agora)

• Why 'features' kill sales (a mistake 67% of beginner copywriters make — translating features→benefits saves conversions, with 5 examples)

• A 3-para email template (copy, fill in the blanks, and send — clients respond 40-60% of the time with cold outreach, tested by 340 copywriters)

• [Bullets 4-12 continue — each one teases a specific value, a curiosity gap]"

[The bullets sell the lead magnet (not the product yet — the value of the lead magnet is shown to be compelling)]

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 4 — THE FORM (Visual, Above the Fold):

[EMAIL FIELD: ________________]
(Just the email — minimum friction, not an overwhelming name+phone+company)

[A GREEN BUTTON: DOWNLOAD THE FREE GUIDE NOW]

Below the form: "Instant access. Zero cost. Unsubscribe at any time (with 1-click)."

[Reassurance — instant, free, an easy exit = removes hesitation]

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 5 — SOCIAL PROOF (50-100 Words Below the Form):

"8,347 copywriters have downloaded it (in the last 18 months).

★★★★★ 4.8/5 average rating (340 reviews — 'The best free resource on copywriting,' 'The templates alone are worth R$500,' 'Actionable immediately').

[Logos: Featured in [Publications]] (if applicable)

[Social proof — large numbers, high ratings, brief testimonials, and authority logos]"

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 6 — A PRIVACY/SPAM REASSURANCE (30-50 Words):

"Privacy: We never sell your email (a strict policy). Spam: Zero (only valuable content, 1-2x/week max, and you can always unsubscribe with 1-click)."

[Removes an objection — the fear of spam is common, and reassurance boosts conversion by 10-15%]

TOTAL LANDING PAGE: 300-600 words (lean, focused, with a single goal = to opt-in).
```

---

**Landing page conversion:**

**Cold traffic:** 20-40% (a compelling lead magnet, a simple form — email only).

**Warm traffic:** 40-60% (retargeting, they're already interested).

**Optimization:** A/B test headlines (10+ variations), bullets (the order, the wording), and the CTA button (the color, the text).

---

**Best practices:**

✅ **Above the fold:** The headline + the bullets + the form (no scrolling — everything is visible).

✅ **Minimum friction:** Just the email (not name+phone, each field = a -10-15% conversion rate).

✅ **Clear value:** The benefit of the lead magnet is obvious (127 pages, 50+ templates — it's tangible).

✅ **Social proof:** Numbers (8,347), ratings (4.8/5), testimonials (brief quotes).

✅ **Reassurance:** Privacy, spam, and the ease of unsubscribing (removes fear).

✅ **Mobile-optimized:** 60% of traffic is on mobile (a large form, a thumb-friendly button).
