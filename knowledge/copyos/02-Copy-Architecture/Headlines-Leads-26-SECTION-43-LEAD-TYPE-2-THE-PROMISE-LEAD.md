## **SECTION 4.3 — LEAD TYPE #2: THE PROMISE LEAD**

---

### **WHEN TO USE IT:**

✅ **State 2** (Product-Aware — they are comparing and researching specific products)

✅ **A credible bold promise** (daring BUT believable — the Big Idea criterion from Doc 3)

✅ **When differentiation is needed** (many competitors; the promise makes you stand out)

✅ **When proof is available** (a bold promise requires proof to back it up — not an empty claim)

---

### **THE STRUCTURE (4 Paragraphs, 300-500 Words):**

---

**PARAGRAPH #1 — A BOLD PROMISE (100-150w):**

**The template:**

```
"What if you could [a specific BOLD RESULT] — something that [alternatives didn't deliver / you tried without success]?

That [result] is not just possible.

It's [guaranteed/common/documented] when you [a specific condition / use our method]."
```

---

**An example:**

```
"What if you could get your FIRST SALE as a copywriter in 60 days (not 6 months, not 'eventually' — SIXTY DAYS, guaranteed) — something you tried to achieve with 2-3 other courses but never did (they all promised to 'learn copywriting,' but none guaranteed 'make a sale')?

A first sale in 60 days is not just possible (in theory, with hope).

It's GUARANTEED (literally — a contract: a first sale of a minimum of $500 OR a 100% refund + we pay YOU $500 for your time, for a total of $3,497 that you receive if we fail).

When you use our implementation-focused method (80% hands-on doing, 20% theory watching — the inverse of typical courses with 90% theory and 10% practice)."
```

**150 words** — a bold promise (60 days guaranteed, not vague), a contrast with alternatives (you tried others, they didn't work — it validates your attempts), an extreme guarantee ($3,497 total if it fails — a signal of confidence), a differentiation of the method (80% hands-on vs. 90% theory for a conventional one).

---

**PARAGRAPH #2 — ACKNOWLEDGE THE SKEPTICISM (100-150w):**

**This is critical for State 2:** Comparing = they are skeptical (they've seen competitors' promises, and maybe been burned).

**You must:** Validate their skepticism (don't ignore it, don't dismiss it — acknowledge that it's legitimate).

**The template:**

```
"I know what you're thinking.

'[A typical objection — a skeptical statement].'

You've [already seen/tried] [competitors/alternatives] promising [something similar].

The results: [Mediocre/partial/failed].

You're RIGHT to be skeptical (skepticism is healthy; it's a protection).

This IS different.

And I'm going to PROVE it (not just with claims — with data, a guarantee, and testimonials for verification in the next few minutes)."
```

---

**An example:**

```
"I know.

'Just another copywriting course promising a quick first sale.'

You've already seen 5-10 courses (on Udemy for $50, on Hotmart for $500-2k, on YouTube ads with a 'secret formula' — they all promise to 'master copywriting' and 'earn $10k+/month').

Maybe you bought 1-2 of them.

The result? You learned the concepts (headlines, bullets, structures, theories).

BUT: You still haven't made a real sale (the courses didn't teach you HOW to get clients, HOW to charge, or HOW to close — just copywriting theory, not the business of copywriting).

You're RIGHT to be skeptical (you've been burned before; empty promises are common).

This course IS different (radically — not passive theory videos, but implementation together, hands-on).

And I'm going to prove it — not with more claims (you've seen enough of those), but with DATA (340 students tracked, 89% got their first sale in 60 days, with verifiable invoice screenshots) and a GUARANTEE (a $500 penalty if you don't sell — massive skin in the game, extreme confidence).

In the next few paragraphs: The complete proof."
```

**150 words** — empathy ("I know"), it validates them ("just another course" — an acknowledgment of the saturation), their experience is validated ("you've seen 5-10, you've bought 1-2" — a common pattern), the partial results ("you learned the concepts BUT you didn't sell" — a familiar gap), the skepticism is legitimized ("you're right to be skeptical"), the differentiation ("it's different — it's implementation"), a promise of proof ("data, a guarantee" — specific about what's coming).

---

**PARAGRAPH #3 — TEASE THE PROOF + THE DIFFERENTIATION (100-150w):**

**The template:**

```
"While [others/a typical approach] [do X — the conventional way],

We [do Y — a radically different way].

The core difference: [The mechanism/philosophy in 1-2 sentences].

Why this matters to YOU: [A specific benefit that only the differentiation delivers].

The proof (not my claim — it's objective): [A preview of the data — X people, Y%, a study/tracking]."
```

---

**An example:**

```
"While other copywriting courses TEACH theory (you watch passive videos, take notes, and 'learn' concepts — a gain of knowledge),

We IMPLEMENT it together (you don't watch — you DO; you write real copy in each module, we review EVERY piece with specific written + 10-15min video feedback for each, and you SELL using the copy you created — it's doing, not watching).

The core difference: Learning by DOING (not by watching — neuroscience shows that active practice leads to 8x the retention of passive input; skill development requires real-world repetition, not classroom theory).

Why it matters: For theory courses (the common ones): a 12% completion rate, and 3-8% make a sale (knowledge ≠ action; a massive gap).

For our implementation course: an 87% completion rate (with accountability calls, progress tracked, and if you're stuck, we help in real-time), and 89% make a sale in 60 days (doing = results, watching = shelf-ware).

The objective proof (not just 'trust me'): 340 students were tracked (from Jan 2022 - Nov 2024, 34 months), with verified invoice screenshots (for first sales in the $500-8,000 range, a median of $2,200), and 40+ video testimonials (watch the transformations of real people on-camera)."
```

**150 words** — a contrast (others vs. ours — teach vs. implement), the mechanism (learning by doing, neuroscience), why it matters (completion of 87% vs. 12%, sales of 89% vs. 3-8% — a dramatic difference), a preview of the proof (340 tracked, invoices, videos — layers of verification are mentioned).

---

**PARAGRAPH #4 — A TRANSITION TO THE BODY (40-80w):**

**The template:**

```
"In the next [sections/minutes], I'm going to show you:

• [Element #1 — what you'll learn/get]
• [Element #2]
• [Element #3]

[A reasonable commitment ask].

Here's [the first element]..."
```

---

**An example:**

```
"In the next few sections, I'm going to show you exactly:

• How the implementation system works (a 12-week step-by-step, what happens in each, the structure of the calls, the revision process)
• The proof from 40 students (video testimonials, invoices, a before/after transformation — not cherry-picked, a representative sample)
• The complete offer (an investment of $1,997, with a guarantee of a first sale in 60d OR a total of $2,497 back, and bonuses of templates/scripts/a calculator)

It will take 5-8 minutes to read (it's worth it — for an informed decision, not a guess).

Here's how the implementation works, week by week..."
```

**80 words** — a roadmap (3 bullets of what's coming), a commitment (5-8 min is reasonable), a transition (to start with the first element).

---

**THE TOTAL PROMISE LEAD:** 300-530 words (appropriate for State 2 — moderate education, an emphasis on differentiation, with proof teased).

### **WHEN THE PROMISE LEAD FAILS:**

❌ **A vague promise:** "Results guaranteed" (what results? Guaranteed how? — not specific, not credible).

❌ **An impossible promise:** "A $1M/month in 30 days, guaranteed" (an absurd timeline; the guarantee is not credible).

❌ **Skepticism is ignored:** Not acknowledged (the reader thinks "yeah, right" — and dismisses it, and bounces).

**The fix:** A specific + believable promise, with skepticism validated, and a tease of credible proof.
