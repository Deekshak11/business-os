## **SECTION 4.5 — STEP 5: SEQUENCE THE ORDER (The Psychology of Flow)**

---

**For 15-25 bullets:** The order matters (it's not random — it's for an engagement flow).

---

### **SEQUENCING STRATEGIES (3 Approaches):**

---

**APPROACH A — STRONGEST FIRST (For an Immediate Hook):**

**Bullet #1:** The absolute best one (the biggest benefit, the highest curiosity, the strongest proof).

**The logic:** An immediate hook (the first 3-5 bullets decide whether they will continue to scan or skip the section — so start strong to capture them).

**The rest:** In descending order of power (from strong → to moderate → to good, a gradient).

**The end:** A moderate one (not the strongest — that was saved for the beginning).

---

**An example of the order:**

```
Bullet #1: "• A guarantee of your first sale in 60d OR a total of $2,497 back (89% achieve it, with extreme confidence — the terms are on page 201)"

[The strongest: An extreme guarantee, a success rate, a page — irresistible]

Bullet #2: "• An average 4.1x scale in 9.2 months (tracked for 47 companies, in an honest range of 2.1x-9.7x, with a breakdown of each on page 23)"

[Strong: Social proof of 47, a quantification of 4.1x, an honest range]

Bullet #3: "• The technique on page 47 that generates 80%+ curiosity (validated with eye-tracking, a 7-second application)"

[Strong: Peak curiosity, a specific mechanism]

[Bullets 4-15 continue — a gradient from strong to moderate]

Bullet #15: "• Access to a group of 800+ copywriters (for networking, peer support, and jobs that are posted)"

[Moderate: A nice-to-have, but not critical — a softer end]
```

---

**APPROACH B — WEAKEST FIRST (A Crescendo):**

**The opposite:** Start with a moderate one and build to the strongest.

**Bullet #1:** A moderate benefit (a warm-up).

**Bullets 2-10:** An escalation (each one is stronger than the last, building).

**Bullets 11-15:** THE STRONGEST (a climax — a peak of curiosity/benefit at the end).

**The logic:** A musical crescendo (it builds tension, with a payoff at the end — the last bullets are the most memorable, due to the recency effect).

---

**APPROACH C — CATEGORIZED (Logical Groups):**

**Organized by category** (not by power):

**Bullets 1-5:** Skill benefits

**Bullets 6-10:** Business/financial benefits

**Bullets 11-15:** Lifestyle/time benefits

**Bullets 16-20:** Tools/resources benefits

**Bullets 21-25:** Proof/guarantee benefits

**The logic:** Organization (the brain likes patterns; categories are processable, not a random chaos).

**Within each category:** The strongest comes first (a micro-crescendo for each section).

---

### **CHOOSING AN APPROACH:**

**Approach A (Strongest First):** THE MOST COMMON (it's been tested, and it works — it's the default for Bencivenga/Makepeace).

**Approach B (A Crescendo):** Is rare (and risky — if the initial bullets are weak, the reader might skip the section before getting to the strong ones at the end).

**Approach C (Categorized):** Is acceptable (for the clarity of its organization, BUT with less psychological manipulation — a more rational presentation).

---

**Bencivenga:**

> *"I use Strongest First 90% of the time (it's been tested — an immediate hook retains readers, and the rest of the bullets are read because they are already hooked).*
>
> *A crescendo: Is risky (you lose readers with the moderate early bullets).*
>
> *Categorized: Is OK for long lists of 40+ bullets (where organization is helpful), BUT for 15-25 bullets, strongest first wins (the psychological > the organizational)."*

---

### **A FINAL POLISH (A Rhythm Check):**

**Read the sequenced bullets aloud** (all 15-25 of them, in the chosen order).

**Listen:**

- For the flow? (Are the transitions natural, not jarring jumps?)
- For the rhythm? (Is there a variety of lengths, or is it a monotonous all-the-same?)
- For a crescendo feel? (Is it building to something, or is it flat?)
- For a cliffhanger end? (Is the last bullet a peak of curiosity that makes you want to continue to the body?)

**Adjust:** Move the bullets (swap their order), and edit their lengths (shorten/lengthen them to create rhythm).

**Iterate:** Re-read, adjust, and repeat until the flow is musical (not like a mechanical list).

---

**THE OUTPUT OF STEP 5:** 15-25 FINAL bullets (optimally sequenced, polished, and ready to be published).

**Time:** 30-45 min (for sequencing, a rhythm check, and a final polish).
