## **SECTION 3.1 — THE COMPLETE 28-STEP SALES LETTER CHECKLIST (KENNEDY)**

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE CHECKLIST — THE SALES LETTER (Kennedy's 28-Step)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

PRE-LETTER (The Setup):
☐ 1. The offer is engineered (the entirety of Doc 4 — a 3:1+ ROI, a Level 3+ guarantee)
☐ 2. The Big Idea is defined (Doc 3 — with a 5-6/6 score, and a type chosen for the State/Stage)
☐ 3. The avatar research is done (Doc 2 — their pain, language, and objections are identified)

THE OPENING (Steps 1-7):
☐ 4. The Headline (a 4 U's score of 30+, it's specific, and it stops the scroll)
☐ 5. The Subhead (it expands on the promise OR adds proof, and it doesn't repeat the headline)
☐ 6. The Johnson Box (optional — for direct mail or the top of a landing page, a 40-80w summary)
☐ 7. An appropriate lead type (State-based — an Offer/Promise/Problem-Sol/Story/Secret, and developed with 300-500w)
☐ 8. A smooth transition (from the lead → to the body, a bridge, not a jarring jump)

THE BODY — THE PROBLEM (Steps 8-10):
☐ 9. The problem is identified specifically (not vague, it's multi-dimensional, and relatable)
☐ 10. A deep agitation (SPIN Implication — present, immediate, and short/long-term future, it's holistic, and 600-1,200w)
☐ 11. False solutions are addressed (2-5 attempts the avatar has tried, why each one failed, and a justification from Warren that it's not their fault, 400-600w)

THE BODY — THE SOLUTION (Steps 11-14):
☐ 12. The solution is introduced (the product's name, who created it, who it's for, and a high-level of what it is, 200-300w)
☐ 13. The mechanism is explained (HOW it works, with 3-5 detailed steps, and analogies if it's complex, 600-1,000w)
☐ 14. A differentiation (vs. the alternatives — coaching, courses, DIY, and agencies, with why yours is superior, 300-400w)
☐ 15. The integration is shown (if there are multiple components, how they work together with synergy, not as isolated tactics)

THE BODY — THE BENEFITS (Steps 15-17):
☐ 16. Benefit bullets (15-25 total, organized into 3-5 categories, and scannable)
☐ 17. Elements of curiosity (the bullets tease, they don't tell the whole story — with gaps, page numbers, and specificity)
☐ 18. An emotional appeal (at levels 4-5, with LF8 activated, and an identity transformation mentioned)

THE BODY — THE PROOF (Steps 18-20):
☐ 19. Testimonials (3-5, specific, similar to the avatar, with before/after numbers, emotional quotes, and photos+videos)
☐ 20. Data/The track record (X clients, Y% success, a Z avg result/timeframe, and honest ranges)
☐ 21. A detailed case study (a minimum of 1, verified, with screenshots/docs, and it's comprehensive)
☐ 22. Your credentials (your education, years, clients, media, and expertise — the stack from Doc 8 Section 5)
☐ 23. The guarantee (Level 3-5, with specific terms, in a prominent visual box, from Doc 4)

THE OFFER (Steps 21-24):
☐ 24. A stack presentation (a progressive value reveal, with each item revealed/valued, and the total BEFORE the price)
☐ 25. The price reveal (after the value is complete, with a dramatic contrast, and an optional suspense build-up)
☐ 26. An ROI calculation (the value - the price ÷ the price, with a 3:1+ shown, and the savings quantified)
☐ 27. Payment options (pay-in-full + installments, both are presented, there's a choice)
☐ 28. Framing (daily/monthly if applicable, a trivial amount vs. a massive outcome contrast)

THE FAQ/OBJECTIONS (Steps 25-26):
☐ 29. The top 5-10 objections (identified in Doc 2, with each one being addressed in 80-150w)
☐ 30. A systematic overcoming (with logic, proof, and the guarantee addressing each one, and with techniques from Doc 11)

THE CLOSE (Steps 27-28):
☐ 31. A recap of the transformation (a before → decision → implementation → after, a 100-150w summary of the journey)
☐ 32. A specific CTA (a 3-5 step process, with each action being clear, an emphasis on ease, and a timing of "now")
☐ 33. A visual button (typically green, it's contrasting, and the text is action-oriented like "SECURE MY SPOT", and it's above the fold)
☐ 34. The final urgency (a stack of 3-4 types — a deadline, a price, a bonus, and the number of spots, with the loss from waiting being quantified)

ALWAYS:
☐ 35. A P.S. is included (a reminder of the urgency or the guarantee, 80% of people read it even if they've skimmed, and it's 40-80w)
☐ 36. An optional P.P.S. (with contact info, for handling questions, and for accessibility — it removes friction)

FORMATTING/TECHNICAL:
☐ 37. Frequent subheads (for each 200-300w section, to guide skimmers, and for scannability)
☐ 38. Visual bullets (not in a paragraph form — with bullets, checkmarks, or icons, and a visual hierarchy)
☐ 39. Generous white space (not a wall of text — with paragraphs of 1-3 sentences, and breathing room)
☐ 40. Strategic bold/italics (for emphasis on key phrases, but not overused — 5-10 instances in total)
☐ 41. Images are included (of the product, photos for the testimonials, and data graphs if applicable, for visual breaks in the text)

THE TOTAL SCORE: __/41

37-41 (90-100%): ✅ Excellent (publish with high confidence)
33-36 (80-89%): ✅ Good (publish, minor gaps are acceptable)
29-32 (70-79%): ⚠️ Acceptable (publish BUT refine it)
<29 (<70%): ❌ A problem (there are critical gaps — fix them before publishing, the copy is incomplete)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```
