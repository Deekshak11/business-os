EXAMPLES OF SPECIFIC CELLS (6 Common Combinations):

[State 1 + Stage 1]: "Product 50% OFF Today"
(A direct offer, a virgin market — simple works)

[State 2 + Stage 3]: "The Only Product with the X Mechanism"
(A USP + a mechanism reveal — for differentiation)

[State 3 + Stage 4]: "How to [Benefit] Using a Harvard-Validated Protocol"
(A how-to + an authority mechanism — a credibility boost)

[State 4 + Stage 5]: "Still Suffering from [Problem] After Trying Everything? Here's Why (And a Secret Solution That 89% Don't Know)"
(An empathetic question + a conspiracy + hope — it cuts through saturation with a story approach)

[State 5 + Stage 2]: "Shocking: 89% of [Group] Have a [Hidden Problem] (and Don't Know It Until It's Too Late)"
(A discovery + a shocking, amplified stat — for creating awareness)

[State 3 + Stage 5]: "From [a Struggle] to [Success] — How [Name] Did It (A Step-by-Step You Can Follow)"
(Solution-aware BUT in a saturated market — a story format cuts through)

• Mismatches exist: State 3 + Stage 5 (they know the solutions BUT the market
  is saturated — it requires a story approach even for the solution-aware)

• Each cell = a specific approach (not a generic one — the combination determines
  the optimal headline type)
