## **SECTION 4.1 — STEP 1: EXTRACT THE BENEFITS (From the Product → a List of 50+)**

---

**The objective:** To identify ALL of the product's benefits (not just the obvious ones — be comprehensive).

**The target:** A raw list of 50-100 benefits (quantity first, then filter).

---

### **THE EXTRACTION METHOD:**

---

**A. A FEATURES → BENEFITS TRANSLATION (The Drill from Doc 3):**

**The process:**

For each feature of the product (a module, a tool, a component, a capability):

**Ask:** "So what? What does this MEAN for the customer?" (drill down from levels 1→5).

---

**An example:**

```
The feature: "The course has a 'Headlines with the 4 U's' module"

Level 1 (Feature): The headlines module exists
↓ So what?

Level 2 (Functional): You learn to write better headlines
↓ So what?

Level 3 (Emotional): Your headlines stop the scroll (they are not ignored), and your copy is READ
↓ So what?

Level 4 (Aspirational): A 3-5x higher conversion rate than with weak headlines (a $10k revenue vs. $2k with the same traffic)
↓ So what?

Level 5 (Identity): You become a professional copywriter (with tested headlines, respected by your peers, and with clients who pay a premium for your visible expertise)

THE EXTRACTED BENEFITS (from each level):
• Better headlines (functional)
• Your copy is read, not ignored (emotional — for validation)
• A 3-5x higher conversion rate (aspirational — a tangible $)
• A $10k vs. a $2k revenue (aspirational — a life outcome)
• A professional expert (identity — status, respect)

5 benefits from 1 feature (by drilling down).
```

**Repeat:** For EACH feature of the product (if there are 20 features × 5 levels = 100 potential benefits).

---

**B. CUSTOMER LANGUAGE MINING (Research from Doc 2):**

**Positive reviews** (5 stars):

Extract phrases: "This changed my life," "Finally, a solution that works," "It's worth 10x the price."

**Translate them:** Into bullets ("Life-changing [in a specific way]," "A solution that works where 5 others failed," "A 10x value on your investment").

---

**Interviews** (with 5-10 customers):

Ask: "What do you value MOST about the product?"

The answers: "I saved 10h/week," "I gained confidence," "I got my first client in 30 days."

**Each is a benefit** (with the potential for a bullet).

---

**C. A PROBLEM-SOLVED INVENTORY:**

**A list of the problems** that the product solves (Doc 4 Part III — the obstacles are identified, and each solved obstacle is a benefit).

An obstacle: "They don't know what to eat on keto" → The benefit: "50 meal plans (for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks — zero guesswork, on page 112)."

**20 obstacles:** = a minimum of 20 benefits (each solution is a benefit statement).

---

**D. A COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION:**

**What you HAVE** that your competitors don't (the USP from Doc 3, the unique bonuses from Doc 4).

Each unique element is a benefit (a differentiation).

"The only one with a guarantee of a first sale OR $500" (the benefit: zero risk, with an upside if it fails).

"The only course with hands-on implementation (not just videos)" (the benefit: an 87% completion rate vs. 12%).

---

**THE OUTPUT OF STEP 1:** A raw list of 50-100 benefits (not yet filtered or written into bullets — an exhaustive brainstorm).

**Time:** 60-90 min (for a systematic extraction with each of the methods A-D).
