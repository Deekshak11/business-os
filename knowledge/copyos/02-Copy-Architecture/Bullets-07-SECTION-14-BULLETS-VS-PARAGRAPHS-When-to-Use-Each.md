## **SECTION 1.4 — BULLETS VS. PARAGRAPHS (When to Use Each)**

---

**The differences:**

| Aspect | Bullets | Paragraphs |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Visual** | Distinct (•, indented, spaced) | A continuous flow (a block of text) |
| **Length** | 1-3 lines (20-60w) | 3-8 sentences (60-200w) |
| **Function** | An individual benefit (in a stack) | An idea is developed (to explain) |
| **Read rate** | 70-85% (highly scannable) | 25-45% (lower, often skipped) |
| **Best for** | Listing benefits, features, results | Explaining mechanisms, stories, agitation |
| **Curiosity** | A tease (with intentional gaps) | An explanation (with complete information) |

---

**When to use BULLETS:**

✅ **In the benefits section** (to list what they will gain — 15-25 benefits, one for each bullet)

✅ **For features** (if you need a list — of specifications, components, deliverables)

✅ **For results** (from testimonials — "John: from $28k→$112k," "Maria: lost 32 lbs")

✅ **For quick facts** (stats, numbers, credentials — an ideal scannable format)

✅ **For teasers** (curiosity-driven — fascinations, page references, teased mechanisms)

---

**When to use PARAGRAPHS:**

✅ **To explain a mechanism** (the HOW it works — it requires 100-300w of development, which is insufficient for bullets)

✅ **For stories** (narratives — a protagonist's journey, a before/after, an emotional arc)

✅ **For agitation** (a SPIN Implication — the consequences are developed, and an escalation needs space)

✅ **For detailed proof** (full testimonials — a 150w transformation story won't fit in a bullet)

✅ **For arguments** (logical chains — A leads to B, which leads to C; sequential reasoning)

---

**The ideal balance in a sales letter:**

**Bullets:** 30-40% of the copy (a benefits section of 15-25 bullets × an avg of 30w = 450-750w of a 6-10k total).

**Paragraphs:** 60-70% (the headline, the lead, the problem, the solution, detailed proof, the offer, the FAQ, and the CTA).

**Both:** They complement each other (bullets tease/list, and paragraphs explain/develop).
