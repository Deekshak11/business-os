## **SECTION 2.2 — FORMULA #2: NUMBER HEADLINES**

---

**The base template:**

```
"[NUMBER] Ways/Reasons/Secrets/Steps to [BENEFIT]"
```

---

**Why numbers work (Psychology — Doc 1 Validation):**

**A. They are Concrete** (Heath's SUCCESs from Doc 1):

"7 ways" is tangible (you can count them; they are finite, comprehensible).

vs. "Many ways," which is abstract (how many? Indefinite, potentially overwhelming).

**Concrete is processable** (the brain can handle it; it doesn't overwhelm).

---

**B. They are Specific** (Kahneman's Availability from Doc 1):

Odd numbers (7, 13, 21) imply that a real list exists (you counted the exact number, not an estimated round number).

vs. Round numbers (10, 20, 50), which feel estimated (a round number is perceived as a guess).

**Specific is credible** (7 was counted; 10 was rounded).

---

**C. They promise completeness:**

"5 steps" implies that it's comprehensive (revealing all 5 = complete knowledge).

**The brain:** Wants completeness (the Zeigarnik effect — incomplete tasks are remembered and drive you to complete them).

**A number makes the finish line visible** (5 steps is an achievable amount to understand, not infinite).

---

### **NUMBER SELECTION (Odd vs. Even, Small vs. Large):**

---

**ODD numbers (3, 5, 7, 9, 13, 17, 21) — ARE BETTER:**

**Research (Moz, 2013 — an analysis of 150,000 headlines):**

Headlines with odd numbers: Average CTR of 2.8%

Headlines with even numbers (4, 6, 8, 10): Average CTR of 2.1%

**Odd numbers win by +33%** (a significant difference).

---

**Why are odd numbers better:**

**The perception of specificity:** 7 was counted exactly (credible).

10 is a round estimate (less credible).

**Attention:** Odd is unexpected (the brain notices it — even is expected, boring).

---

**Exceptions when even is OK:**

**12 steps:** Acceptable (12 months is a natural timeframe).

**100 headlines:** A large round number is OK (for swipe files, comprehensiveness).

**In general:** Prefer odd numbers (in the 3-21 range); even numbers are acceptable if there is a natural reason (a timeframe, a round milestone that makes sense in context).

---

**SMALL numbers (3-7) vs. LARGE (15-100):**

**Small (3-7) — Digestible:**

The appeal: They are manageable (you can absorb them all; they are not overwhelming).

"3 secrets" = a 10-min read (achievable).

**Best for:** Short-form content (emails, ads), busy audiences, simple topics.

---

**Large (15-100) — Comprehensive:**

The appeal: They are complete (exhaustive, nothing is left out).

"47 strategies" = hours to read (comprehensive).

**Best for:** Long-form content (guides, courses), audiences that want depth, complex topics.

---

**The tested sweet spot:** **7** (the Goldilocks number — not too few like 3, not too many like 21; it's just right).

A BuzzFeed analysis (of viral headlines): "7 ways" was the most shared (vs. 5, 10, 15 — 7 is optimal).

---

### **VARIATIONS OF NUMBER HEADLINES (6 Templates):**

---

**VARIATION A — Ways:**

```
"[N] Ways to [Benefit]"
```

Examples:
- "7 Ways to Double Your Revenue Without Hiring"
- "5 Ways to Sleep Better Tonight (Start Today)"

**The tone:** Neutral, a list of options (multiple paths, you can choose).

---

**VARIATION B — Reasons:**

```
"[N] Reasons Why [Statement/Outcome]"
```

Examples:
- "13 Reasons Why Your Offer Converts at <3% (Fix Each, and Scale 10x)"
- "5 Reasons You're Stuck at $30k/Month (And How to Escape Each)"

**The tone:** Analytical, diagnostic (a list of reasons = understanding).

---

**VARIATION C — Secrets:**

```
"[N] Secrets of [Desirable Group/Outcome]"
```

Examples:
- "7 Secrets of Copywriters Who Charge $15k+/Project"
- "5 Secrets That $100M+ Companies Use to Scale (You Can, Too)"

**The tone:** Insider, aspirational (secrets appeal to the desire for forbidden knowledge).

---

**VARIATION D — Steps:**

```
"[N] Steps to [Benefit]"
```

Examples:
- "A 3-Step System to Scale a Business 5x (Offer → Funnel → Traffic)"
- "5 Steps to Get Your First Client in 30 Days (A Tested Outreach Protocol)"

**The tone:** A process, systematic (steps = follow an order, achievable).

---

**VARIATION E — Mistakes:**

```
"[N] Mistakes [Group] Makes in [Context] (Costing Them [Consequence])"
```

Examples:
- "7 Mistakes Beginner Copywriters Make (91% Make #3, and It Kills Conversions)"
- "5 Pricing Mistakes That Are Costing You $50k+/Year (Fix Them Tomorrow)"

**The tone:** A warning, corrective (mistakes = avoid them, protective).

---

**VARIATION F — Things/Tactics:**

```
"[N] Things [Group] Knows About [Topic]"
```

Examples:
- "11 Things A-List Copywriters Know (That You Don't — Yet)"
- "7 Traffic Tactics That Agencies Charge $10k/Month For (You Can DIY for Free)"

**The tone:** Educational, empowering (knowing = power).

---

### **WHEN TO USE EACH VARIATION:**

**Ways:** A neutral tone, multiple options (when you don't want to be prescriptive with a single path).

**Reasons:** Analytical audiences (engineers, finance professionals — an appeal to logic).

**Secrets:** An aspirational, insider appeal (for those who want to belong to the elite, to access the forbidden).

**Steps:** Process-oriented (for systematic implementers who want a clear path).

**Mistakes:** A warning, protective (for those who have been burned before and want to avoid repeating it).

**Things/Tactics:** Educational (for learners, knowledge seekers).

### **EXAMPLES OF NUMBER HEADLINES (15 Complete, with Scores):**

---

**Example #1:**

"7 Ways for Copywriters to Get Their First Client in 30 Days (Even with Zero Portfolio) — Outreach Templates, Pitch Scripts, and Proposal Formats All Included"

**4 U's Score:**
- Useful: 9 (first client in 30 days is a clear benefit; templates/scripts are tangible tools)
- Urgent: 6 (the 30-day timeframe is present, but there's no explicit deadline)
- Unique: 7 (the "7 ways" is a specific angle; the included templates provide differentiation)
- Ultra-Specific: 9 (7, 30 days, zero portfolio, 3 deliverables named)
**Total: 31/40** ✅

---

**Example #2:**

"13 Reasons Your Offer is Only Converting at 1-3% (When 8-15% is Possible in the Industry) — Fix Each One for a 10x Conversion Boost, Proven with 47 Companies"

**Score:**
- Useful: 9 (a 10x improvement in conversion is a massive benefit)
- Urgent: 5 (no deadline, BUT "10x" implies you should fix it now)
- Unique: 8 (13 reasons is specific, the 10x claim is bold, 47 companies is proof)
- Ultra-Specific: 10 (13, 1-3%, 8-15%, 10x, 47 — multiple numbers)
**Total: 32/40** ✅

**Example #3:**

"5 Secrets That $100M+ Entrepreneurs Use to Scale (That You Can Apply Even at $30k/Month) — From Interviews with 40 CEOs, with Patterns Identified"

**Score:**
- Useful: 9 (scaling secrets from $100M CEOs, applicable at $30k = aspirational + accessible)

- Unique: 8 (40 CEOs interviewed = depth of research; $100M is specific)
- Ultra-Specific: 9 (5, $100M, $30k, 40 CEOs)
**Total: 30/40** ✅

### **NUMBER HEADLINE PITFALLS:**

---

**PITFALL #1 — A Number Without a Benefit:**

❌ "7 Things About Copywriting"

**The problem:** The number is present, BUT the benefit is absent ("7 things" = what? Why should I care?).

**The fix:** Add a benefit.

✅ "7 Copywriting Techniques That Double Conversions (A-Listers Use Them, You Probably Ignore Them)"

---

**PITFALL #2 — An Impossible Number to Deliver:**

Headline: "147 Ways to Scale a Business"

**The problem:** 147 is overwhelming (nobody will read a list of 147; it's impossible to act on them all).

**The promise:** It's not credible to deliver (the article would be 50k words — unrealistic).

**The fix:** A realistic number.

"7-12 Ways" = digestible, believable to deliver completely.

---

**PITFALL #3 — A Random Number (Not Justified):**

"8.7 Steps to Success" (decimal nonsense — steps are discrete, not fractions).

**The fix:** A natural integer (3, 5, 7 — not decimals, not fractions).
