## **SECTION 2.4 — TYPE #4: THE TEASE MECHANISM BULLET**

---

**The template:**

```
"• How to get [an impressive RESULT] using [a MECHANISM that is teased but not explained] (page [X] reveals it step-by-step)"
```

**A tease = peak curiosity** (it mentions that a mechanism exists, but it does NOT explain it completely).

---

**Examples (15):**

1.  "• How to generate 50+ leads/day by spending only $5/day on Facebook ads (a targeting technique on page 78 — 90% of copywriters ignore it, and it gets a 3x higher conversion rate than average when applied)"

2.  "• The '7-second psoas release' technique that eliminates lower back pain in 83% of cases (the Johns Hopkins protocol on page 47 — a specific position, a breathing pattern, validated by 1,240 patients)"

3.  "• Why 'MCT oil' accelerates ketosis to 3 hours (not the normal 14 days of keto) — the difference in its molecular structure is explained on page 56, with the exact dosage and optimal timing"

4.  "• The '3-paragraph email' that closes high-ticket deals at a 47% rate (vs. 12% for a single long email — a psychological breakdown on page 92, with templates for each para, tested by 340 copywriters)"

5.  "• How a 'value-based pricing calculator' justifies a $15k project (the client happily pays $3k — the ROI math on page 103 shows it, with the exact inputs/outputs and built-in objection handling)"

6-15. [Tease mechanisms — each one mentions that a technique/tool/method EXISTS (with a specific name), a page reference (a tangible proof that it exists), but it doesn't explain HOW it works completely (a gap = you must read the page), and a hint of proof for validation (tested, for patients, by copywriters — for credibility)]

---

**The components:**

- The Benefit: ✅ (the result is clear — 50 leads, it eliminates pain, it accelerates ketosis, it closes at 47%)
- Curiosity: ✅✅✅ (PEAK — the mechanism is teased but not explained, and a specific page reference = you must know)
- Specificity: ✅✅ (the mechanism is specifically named — "a 7-second psoas," "MCT oil," "a 3-paragraph email"; the numbers/pages are abundant)
- Emotional: ✅ (the results = desire; the mechanisms = an intellectual fascination)
- Proof: ✅ (validation is mentioned — Johns Hopkins, tested by 340, for 1,240 patients)

**Score: 5/5** components, with PEAK curiosity.

**Bencivenga:** "The Tease Mechanism is my favorite type (it has the highest curiosity and the maximum selling power)."

---

**When to use it:**

✅ **For fascinations** (curiosity-driven bullets, with page references)

✅ **For mechanism-rich products** (courses, systems — they teach multiple techniques)

✅ **For analytical audiences** (engineers, researchers — curiosity about the mechanism is natural)

✅ **For Stages 3-5** (sophistication — a differentiation of the mechanism, a tease that cuts through)
