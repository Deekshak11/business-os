## **SECTION 4.1 — INTRODUCTION TO LEAD TYPES**

---

**Michael Masterson** (Great Leads, an ENTIRE book dedicated to it — a complete taxonomy of the 6 types):

> *"The lead is the first 100-600 words (after the headline).*
>
> *80% of readers decide whether to continue or leave here (not from the headline alone — the headline hooks, the lead SELLS them on continuing).*
>
> *The wrong lead for the State = a disconnect (the reader thinks: 'this isn't for me,' and bounces).*
>
> *The right lead = engagement (the reader thinks: 'this is exactly what I need,' and continues to the offer).*
>
> *There are 6 types (all successful leads fall into one of these — an exhaustive taxonomy tested for 40 years at Agora)."*

---

### **THE 6 LEAD TYPES (An Overview):**

1.  **THE OFFER LEAD** (State 1 — transactional, direct)
2.  **THE PROMISE LEAD** (State 2 — a bold promise, with skepticism acknowledged)
3.  **THE PROBLEM-SOLUTION LEAD** (State 3 — identify a problem, present a solution)
4.  **THE STORY LEAD** (State 4 — narrative identification, a transformation)
5.  **THE SECRET LEAD** (State 5 — a conspiracy, a revelation, an insider)
6.  **THE INVITATION LEAD** (For webinars, events — join an exclusive group)

---

**The choice is based on:** The State (primarily) + the Stage (secondarily) + the Avatar (tertiarily).

**Don't:** Always use your favorite (personal preference).

**Do:** Match the State (a data-driven selection).

---

**Masterson's data (from an analysis of 10,000+ promos):**

An appropriate lead for the State: 70-85% engagement (most people continue reading the body).

An inappropriate lead: 20-40% engagement (most people bounce after the lead).

**A 2-4x difference** (from the lead type selection alone).
