## **SECTION 1.1 — WHAT IS A BULLET?**

---

**The definition:**

**A bullet is a single benefit statement** (1-3 lines, beginning with • or –, and scannable).

**It is not:** A paragraph (dense text, hard to scan).

**It is:** A line item (visually distinct, catches the eye, and the brain processes it individually).

---

**Its function in copy:**

**The benefits section** (after the headline, the lead, and the problem/solution; BEFORE the proof/offer).

**A list of 15-25 bullets** (what they gain by using the product — a comprehensive stack of benefits).

---

**Gary Bencivenga** (a master of bullets, with billions in revenue from his newsletters):

> *"Bullets are miniature salesmen (each bullet sells one benefit; 20 bullets = 20 salesmen working in parallel).*
>
> *The best bullets tease (they don't tell the whole story — a curiosity gap that they must buy to discover).*
>
> *Fascinations (a term from Makepeace): Bullets so intriguing that the reader CANNOT resist them (they buy just to satisfy their curiosity about what the page 47 technique is)."*

---

**The power of bullets:**

**Ogilvy's research:** Bullets are read 60-80% of the time (vs. 20-40% for body paragraphs).

**Why:** They are visual (they stand out), scannable (they are quick to process), and benefit-focused (WIIFM on each line).

**The implication:** Bullets are high-value real estate in copy (most people read them — so invest the effort to craft them well).

---

**Schwartz** (Breakthrough Advertising):

> *"Bullets amplify desire.*
>
> *Each bullet is an additional layer (a stack of benefits — an accumulating, growing desire).*
>
> *15-25 bullets = an overwhelming desire (so many benefits that the logic is: 'even if only 50% of this is true, it's still worth it')."*
