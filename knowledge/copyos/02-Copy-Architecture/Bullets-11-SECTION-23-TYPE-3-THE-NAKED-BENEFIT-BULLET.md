## **SECTION 2.3 — TYPE #3: THE NAKED BENEFIT BULLET**

---

**The template:**

```
"• [An ultra-specific BENEFIT] — [A micro-detail of proof]"
```

**"Naked" = zero fluff** (every word is functional, for maximum density).

A favorite of Carlton (from his Simple Writing System).

---

**Examples (12):**

1.  "• An average of $15,000/project (for freelance copywriting, tracked for 89 clients in year 1, in a range of $3k-47k)"

2.  "• 32 lbs lost (by Maria, 52, with 120 days of keto, and maintained for 22 months with zero rebound)"

3.  "• A 4.1x revenue growth (for 47 companies, with a median of 9.2 months, in a range of 2.1x-9.7x)"

4.  "• An 78% completion rate (for our course, vs. an industry average of 12% — the accountability calls make the difference)"

5-12. [Naked benefits — each with ultra-specific numbers/names/timeframes, with micro-proof embedded, with zero wasted words, for extreme density]

---

**The scoring:**

- The Benefit: ✅✅ (ultra-clear, with extreme quantification)
- Curiosity: ⚠️ (low-moderate — being naked reveals a lot, so the gap is minimal, BUT the proof details create a "how was that achieved?" curiosity)
- Specificity: ✅✅ (THE HIGHEST — multiple numbers, names, ranges, and timeframes)
- Emotional: ✅ (quantified benefits = a tangible desire)
- Proof: ✅✅ (micro-details = peak credibility)

**Its strength:** Credibility (the specificity is overwhelming), and clarity (zero confusion).

**Its use:** To balance with tease bullets (the naked ones satisfy some curiosity, the tease ones create more).
