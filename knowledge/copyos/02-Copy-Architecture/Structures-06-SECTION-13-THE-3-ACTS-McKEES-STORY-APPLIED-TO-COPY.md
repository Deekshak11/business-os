## **SECTION 1.3 — THE 3 ACTS (McKEE'S STORY APPLIED TO COPY)**

---

**Robert McKee** (Story, 1997 — the screenwriting bible, applicable to copywriting):

Copy = a story (protagonist's journey, obstacles, resolution).

**The 3-Act Structure** (universal to narratives — films, books, copy):

---

### **ACT 1 — SETUP (25% of the Copy):**

**Objective:** Establish the world, the protagonist (the client/avatar), the problem, and the decision to act.

**In copy (components):**

**A. Headline:** Captures attention (introduces the protagonist or the problem).

**B. Lead/Opening:** Establishes the normal world (the current situation), the inciting incident (what changed/triggered action), and the protagonist (the client sees themselves).

**C. Problem identification:** "You have [this problem]" (awareness, recognition).

**D. Decision point:** "You can continue like this (status quo = suffering continues) OR change (a solution is possible)" (a fork in the road).

---

**Example (Weight Loss Copy):**

```
ACT 1 (first 25% of the copy — if 8k words, the first 2k):

Headline: "Still Living with an Extra 40 Lbs Even After 5 Failed Diets?"
[Protagonist identification — you with 40 lbs, who has tried diets]

Lead (first 3 paragraphs):
"You wake up. The mirror. You're 40 lbs overweight (still — despite trying diet A, B, C, D, and E over the last 5 years).
[Normal world — daily reality, repeated failures]

You thought: 'This diet will work' (every time — hope).
[Past attempts, optimism]

The result: You lost 15-20 lbs (initially — it worked!), but 6 months later: you regained it all + an extra 5 lbs (the yo-yo cycle, always).
[Inciting incident — a pattern is recognized, frustration peaks]

Today: You're here (reading this). Why? Something triggered you (you're tired of the cycle, ready for a real change).
[Protagonist's decision point — readiness now]"

[ACT 1 has established: The World (40 lbs, failed diets), the Protagonist (you, frustrated), the Problem (the yo-yo cycle), the Decision (ready to try something different)]
```

**25% of the copy invested:** Setting up the protagonist (identification), establishing the problem (real, relatable), and creating readiness (the decision to act).

---

### **ACT 2 — CONFRONTATION (50% of the Copy — The Bulk):**

**Objective:** Rising obstacles (what prevents the solution), conflicts (internal/external), the "all is lost" moment (rock bottom), and a glimpse of hope.

**In copy:**

**A. Problem agitation:** SPIN Implication (Doc 11 — the consequences of not solving it, escalation).

**B. False solutions:** Conventional attempts (why they failed — the Mistake Big Idea from Doc 3, Warren's justification from Doc 1).

**C. Obstacles identification:** What blocks success (obstacles listed in Doc 4 Part III, creates tension).

**D. "All is lost":** A moment of hopelessness ("nothing works, I'm resigned to it") — an emotional low (sets up the solution = restoration of hope).

**E. Solution tease:** A glimpse ("BUT there is a way that works...") — the beginning of hope.

---

**Example (continued):**

```
ACT 2 (the middle 50% — 4k words if 8k total):

Problem Agitation (1,000w):
"40 lbs isn't just a number (vanity).

It's: Health (pre-diabetes risk, blood pressure 140/90, doctor warned you), Energy (daily 3 PM crash, caffeine-dependent), Relationships (avoiding photos, the beach, intimacy is affected), Self-image (the mirror = disappointment, clothes don't fit, confidence is zero).

[Holistic consequences — SPIN Implication style from Doc 11]

If you don't solve it: In 2 years, it's 60 lbs (the progression continues), a diabetes diagnosis (lifelong medications), worse energy (chronic fatigue), further relationship strain.

[Negative future pacing — Doc 9, creating urgency]

False Solutions (800w):
"You've tried it (validated):

Low-calorie diets (1,200 cal/day — starving, miserable, lost 15 lbs, regained 20).
Why it failed: Your metabolism crashed (starvation mode, adaptive thermogenesis — the Mistake Big Idea from Doc 3).

Exercise programs (gym 5x/week, hours of cardio — exhausting, unsustainable, lost 10 lbs, regained them).
Why: Cardio burns muscle (the cortisol effect), metabolism slows, the weight returns.

[Etc. — 3-5 failed attempts, each explaining WHY it failed, justifying Warren's principle from Doc 1]

Obstacles (600w):
Even IF you knew the right method:

Obstacle #1: You don't know WHAT to eat (specific meals, calculating macros — it's confusing)
Obstacle #2: You don't have TIME to cook (meal prep for 10h/week — impossible with your schedule)
Obstacle #3: You HATE restriction (diets = deprivation, psychologically unsustainable)
[...]

[List 5-8 obstacles — Doc 4 Part III, creates tension]

All Is Lost (400w):
"Maybe: You've thought about giving up (accepting the 40 lbs, 'this is just how it is,' resignation).

'Nothing works (I've tried everything). It's genetics (my mom had weight issues). It's my age (metabolism slows after 40). It's impossible.' (hopelessness — the emotional bottom).

[The protagonist's lowest point — sets up the solution = the hero's emergence]

Solution Glimpse (200w):
"BUT: There is a way that works (validated by 12,000 people, not a theory).

It's not a diet (diets fail — you know that).

It's [the premise]: Eating differently (not less), shifting your body chemistry (ketosis), and burning fat (a natural mechanism).

Here's how..."

[Hope restored — transition to Act 3]
```

**Act 2 = the emotional journey** (deep problem, many obstacles, lost hope, a glimpse of the solution).

**50% of the copy:** The bulk (agitation, obstacles, failures explained — psychological preparation to accept the solution).

---

### **ACT 3 — RESOLUTION (25% of the Copy — Solution, Offer, Action):**

**Objective:** The solution is revealed (the product), the transformation is shown (before → after), the offer is presented, and action is requested.

**In copy:**

**A. Solution reveal:** The product/method is introduced (name, mechanism developed, how it works).

**B. Benefits:** What they gain (transformation detailed, Levels 3-5).

**C. Proof:** Testimonials (others transformed), data (it works for the majority), guarantee (confidence).

**D. Offer:** The stack (core + bonuses), pricing, value ROI.

**E. Urgency:** Why now (deadline, scarcity — Doc 4 Part VI).

**F. CTA:** A command to act (specific, easy, urgent).

---

**Example (conclusion):**

```
ACT 3 (final 25% — 2k words if 8k total):

Solution Reveal (600w):
"The Method: Simplified Keto (not the complicated traditional keto with 20 overwhelming rules).

Just 3 Rules:
1. Protein 1g/lb of bodyweight (satisfying, preserves muscle)
2. Fat 60-70% of calories (energy, satiety, triggers ketosis)
3. Carbs <20g/day (eliminate sugar, grains, maintains ketosis)

That's it. Simple.

The Mechanism: Your body enters ketosis (in 2-4 days), burns fat for fuel (not glucose), your energy is stable (no crashes), and your hunger is low (fat is satisfying).

[Mechanism developed — Doc 3 applied]

Benefits (400w):
• Weight loss (18-24 lbs in 90 days, fat not muscle)
• Energy surge (stable — no 3 PM crash)
• Mental clarity (ketones fuel the brain, sharp focus)
• [5-8 benefits stacked — Doc 4 Value Equation]

Proof (600w):
3 Testimonials (transformation stories, before/after).
Data: 12,000 tested, 78% lost 15+ lbs, 74% maintained it for 24+ months.
Study: [Citation of ketosis research].
Guarantee: Lose 20 lbs in 90 days or your money back + R$500.
[5 tiers — Doc 8 applied]

Offer (600w):
Core R$2,000 + Bonuses R$3,000 = R$5,000 value.
Price: R$997.
ROI: 5:1.
Payment: Pay-in-full or 3×R$397.
[Doc 4 stack presentation]

Urgency (400w):
Expires Friday. Price increases by R$1,000 after. Bonus removed. 30 spots remaining.
[4 types stacked — Doc 4 Part VI]

CTA (200w):
"Click the green button below NOW to secure your spot (it takes 60 seconds, 30 spots left, first-come).

Your transformation begins today (instant access, your first keto meal tonight, ketosis in 2-4 days, results in 7 days).

The you from 1 year in the future will be grateful you decided today.

Act."

[CTA: Command, ease, benefit, urgency, emotional close]
```

**Act 3:** The solution (clear method), the transformation (benefits), validation (proof), the transaction (offer), and action (CTA).

**Final 25%:** The resolution (the journey is complete — problem → solution → offer → action).

---

**McKee applied:**

Copy is NOT just "information" (a dry list of features and benefits).

Copy IS a **STORY** (a protagonist's journey — you = the hero, the problem = the villain, the solution = the weapon, the transformation = the victory).

**Story structure is engaging** (the brain is wired for narratives — Doc 1, stories stick 22x+ more than facts alone).
