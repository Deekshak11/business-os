## **SECTION 7.2 — THE LEAD CHECKLIST (12 Items)**

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE CHECKLIST — LEAD VALIDATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

SELECTION:
☐ 1. The lead type is appropriate for the State (the Decision Tree in Section 4.8)
☐ 2. The length is appropriate (100-200w for State 1, 600-1,200w for State 4)

STRUCTURE:
☐ 3. There is a hook in the first 100w (attention is grabbed, relevance is established)
☐ 4. The paragraphs are structured (each para has a specific function, they are not random)
☐ 5. The transition to the body is smooth (the bridge is clear, not a jarring jump)

CONTENT:
☐ 6. There are identification elements (the avatar sees themselves, it's relatable)
☐ 7. The promise/benefit is clear (what they will gain by continuing to read)
☐ 8. Proof is teased (credibility is mentioned, to be developed in the body)
☐ 9. Curiosity is maintained (gaps remain, not everything is revealed in the lead)

TONE:
☐ 10. It's appropriate for the State (transactional for State 1, empathetic for State 4)
☐ 11. The voice is consistent (Doc 3 — the tone, formality, and language)

VALIDATION:
☐ 12. It has been tested (read aloud, with feedback from 3-5 people, and an engagement check)

SCORE: __/12

11-12: ✅ Excellent
9-10: ✅ Good
7-8: ⚠️ Acceptable
<7: ❌ Rewrite

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```
