## **SECTION 2.3 — FORMULA #3: QUESTION HEADLINES**

---

**The base template:**

```
"[A QUESTION That Identifies a Problem/Desire]?"
```

---

**The psychological power (Auto-Selection + Engagement):**

**A. Automatic self-selection** (Schwartz):

The reader reads the question → and answers it mentally (yes/no).

**If YES:** "This is ME" (relevance is confirmed; they continue reading).

**If NO:** "This doesn't apply" (they leave — which is good; you don't waste their time).

**Auto-filtering** (it lets qualified people in and blocks unqualified ones — efficiency).

---

**B. Active engagement** (not passive):

A statement: "You have problem X" (passive — the reader receives info).

A question: "Do you have problem X?" (active — the reader PARTICIPATES, answers mentally, and is engaged).

**Active engagement:** = higher attention, better memorability (Doc 1 Section 2.2 on Commitment — a mental answer is a micro-commitment, participation).

---

**C. Identification via the negative** (a common approach):

Questions are often negative/problem-focused ("Do you make this mistake?", "Are you stuck?").

**A reader answering "yes":** Identifies with the problem (they are not alone, they feel validated).

**Not:** Accusatory (the tone matters — it should be empathetic, not judgmental).

**Yes:** Relatable ("yes, I do that" — a connection).

---

### **VARIATIONS OF QUESTION HEADLINES (8 Templates):**

---

**VARIATION A — Do You...? (Behavior/Mistake Identification):**

```
"Do You Make These [NUMBER] Mistakes in [TOPIC]?"
```

**A Schwab classic** (1962 — one of the greatest headlines ever, it ran for decades):

"Do You Make These Mistakes in English?"

**Modern adaptations:**

Examples:
- "Do You Make These 7 Mistakes When Writing Headlines? (67% of Copywriters Do — Each Costs 40%+ in Conversions)"
- "Do You Make This $50,000/Year Pricing Mistake? (83% of Freelancers Undercharge; a Calculator Inside Fixes It)"

**The appeal:** Self-diagnostic (it feels like a checklist), negative framing (mistakes to avoid, protective), specificity (numbers, consequences).

---

**VARIATION B — Are You...? (State/Situation Identification):**

```
"Are You [UNDESIRED STATE] About [TOPIC]?"
```

Examples:
- "Are You Frustrated Being Stuck at $30k/Month for 12+ Months? (A 3-Pillar System Breaks the Plateau, Validated by 47 Companies)"
- "Are You Confused About Which Marketing Tactic to Focus On? (A Clarity Framework to Prioritize in 15 Min)"

**The tone:** Empathetic ("frustrated," "confused" validates their feelings), offers a hint of a solution.

---

**VARIATION C — Who Else...? (Implied Social Proof):**

```
"Who Else Wants [BENEFIT]?"
```

**The implication:** Others already have it (you're missing out, social proof).

Examples:
- "Who Else Wants a $10-30k/Month Income from Freelancing? (8,400 Have Already Achieved It, the Method is Inside)"
- "Who Else Wants to Sleep for 8 Hours Straight? (12,000 Insomniacs Have Resolved It, the Protocol is Free)"

**The psychology:** FOMO (others have it, I want it too — the bandwagon effect from Doc 1 Section 2.3 on Social Proof).

---

**VARIATION D — What If...? (Possibility/Hope):**

```
"What If You Could [DESIRABLE OUTCOME] Without [COMMON SACRIFICE]?"
```

Examples:
- "What If You Could Scale to $100k/Month Without Hiring 50 People? (An Automation System Used by 47 Companies Makes It Possible)"
- "What If You Could Retire at 55, Not 65? (A 10-Year Plan, Even on a $50k/Year Salary; the Math is Inside)"

**The tone:** Hopeful, about possibility (it opens the mind; "what if" activates the imagination).

---

**VARIATION E — Why...? (Reason-Seeking):**

```
"Why [COUNTER-INTUITIVE FACT/SITUATION]?"
```

Examples:
- "Why Does 'Eating Less' Make You GAIN Weight? (The Metabolism Paradox of 89% of Diets)"
- "Why Does Working MORE Hours Keep You STUCK? (The Productivity Paradox, Where the Opposite Works)"

**The appeal:** A curiosity mechanism (why the paradox? The brain wants an explanation), an element of a mistake (doing the wrong thing).

---

**VARIATION F — How...? (Method-Seeking):**

```
"How Did [UNLIKELY PERSON] [ACHIEVE A RESULT]?"
```

Examples:
- "How Did a Shy Accountant Make $50k/Month with Copywriting? (With Zero Network and a Template System)"
- "How Did a 67-Year-Old Grandmother Build a $2M Portfolio? (A Simple Dividend Strategy)"

**The appeal:** An implied story (how = a journey; an underdog = if THEY can do it, so can I), a tease of the mechanism.

---

**VARIATION G — Can You...? (A Challenge/Test):**

```
"Can You [PASS THIS TEST/AVOID THIS MISTAKE]?"
```

Examples:
- "Can You Spot the 5 Conversion Killers on This Sales Page? (Most Miss #3 — and It Costs 60% of Sales)"
- "Can You Answer These 3 Questions About Your Offer? (If Not, a Conversion Rate of <5% is Guaranteed)"

**The tone:** A challenge (gamification, test yourself), educational.

---

**VARIATION H — Which...? (A Choice/Comparison):**

```
"Which Is [OPTION A] or [OPTION B]?"
```

Examples:
- "Which Matters More: Traffic Quantity or Offer Quality? (The Answer Surprises 78% of People)"
- "Which Kills Sales: a Weak Headline or a Weak Offer? (Data from 2,400 Campaigns Shows)"

**The appeal:** A comparison (it forces you to think, not an obvious answer — curiosity resolution).

---

### **WHEN TO USE QUESTION HEADLINES:**

✅ **States 3-5** (problem/solution/unaware — questions are a natural part of the discovery phase)

✅ **When identification is needed** (the avatar must see themselves — a question prompts a self-check)

✅ **For skeptical audiences** (Stage 4-5 — questions are less pushy than statements, a softer approach)

✅ **In email subject lines** (questions get higher open rates than statements, tested — "Do you?" > "You should")

✅ **When negative framing is appropriate** (mistakes, problems, struggles — a protective appeal to LF3)

---

**WHEN IT'S LESS IDEAL:**

⚠️ **State 1** (they are already aware — a question is unnecessary; a direct offer is better)

⚠️ **For positive-only audiences** (some prefer positive affirmations; negative questions can alienate them)

### **EXAMPLES OF QUESTION HEADLINES (15 Scored, Diverse):**

---

**Example #1:**

"Have You Been Stuck at $30-50k/Month for 12+ Months, Even Though You're Working 60-80 Hours? (89% Are — You're Missing 3 Pillars; Identify Which in This 8-Min Free Video)"

**Score:**
- Useful: 9 (it identifies the problem of being stuck, hints at a 3-pillar solution, and offers a free video)
- Urgent: 6 (the 12+ month problem timeframe and the quick 8-min video imply some urgency)
- Unique: 8 (the 89% stat, the specific 3 pillars, and the 8 min are all specific)
- Ultra-Specific: 10 ($30-50k, 12+ months, 60-80h, 89%, 3 pillars, 8 min — multiple specifics)
**Total: 33/40** ✅

---

**Example #2:**

"Are You Making This $47,000/Year Pricing Mistake? (Freelancers Who Charge Hourly Instead of Value-Based Are — a Calculator Inside Fixes It in 15 Min)"

**Score:**
- Useful: 10 (a quantified $47k/year mistake, and a calculator that fixes it is a tangible tool)
- Urgent: 5 (losing $47k/year NOW is implied, and a 15-min fix is quick)
- Unique: 7 (hourly vs. value-based is specific; the quantified $47k differentiates it)
- Ultra-Specific: 10 ($47k/year, hourly, value-based, a calculator, 15 min)
**Total: 32/40** ✅

---

**Example #3:**

"Who Else Wants Financial Independence in 10 Years, Not 40? (A High-Income Skill + Aggressive Investing; 8,200 Have Achieved It, with a Free Blueprint)"

**Score:**
- Useful: 9 (financial independence is clear; 10 vs. 40 years is dramatic)
- Urgent: 7 (10 years vs. 40 is a massive amount of time saved, with an implicit urgency to start now)
- Unique: 7 (the combination of a high-income skill + investing, and social proof of 8,200)
- Ultra-Specific: 9 (10 years, 40 years, 8,200)
**Total: 32/40** ✅

### **QUESTION HEADLINE PITFALLS:**

---

**PITFALL #1 — A Question That Can Be Answered with "No" (A Dead-End):**

❌ "Want to Make Money?"

**The problem:** Everyone says "yes" (it doesn't filter), BUT some might say "no" (not interested in money? — it's rare, BUT the question allows for a "no" answer = an immediate exit).

**Better:** A question that assumes "yes" (it doesn't give an easy "no" option).

✅ "How Much Money Do You Want to Make?" (it assumes you want to make some and asks how much — it engages and doesn't allow for an opt-out).

---

**PITFALL #2 — A Question with an Obvious Answer (No Curiosity):**

❌ "Do You Want to Be Healthy?"

**The problem:** The answer is an obvious "yes" (for everyone — no curiosity, no selection, boring).

**The fix:** A question that is NOT obvious.

✅ "Are You Making This Health Mistake That 89% of People Make? (It Seems Healthy, but It's Actually Inflammatory)"

---

**PITFALL #3 — A Vague Question:**

❌ "Are You Ready for Success?"

**The problem:** "Ready for success" is meaningless (success in what? Ready how?).

**The fix:** Be specific.

✅ "Are You Ready to Scale from $30k → $100k in 12 Months? (A 5-Min Self-Assessment to Identify Your Gaps)"
