## **SECTION 1.6 — AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action — The Classic)**

---

**AIDA** (E. St. Elmo Lewis, 1898 — 126 years old, still taught):

The 4 stages of the buyer's journey (awareness → action).

**The ancestor:** Of modern frameworks (The 5 Fundamentals, The 4 Pillars, The 3 Acts — all descend from AIDA).

---

**A — ATTENTION (Capture):**

The headline/hook (Schwab's Fundamental #1).

---

**I — INTEREST (Maintain, Develop):**

The lead/opening (problem identification, relevance established).

**The difference with Attention:** Attention = stop them (5 sec). Interest = keep them reading (5 min+).

---

**D — DESIRE (Create, Intensify):**

Benefits (the transformation), proof (it works), agitation (the pain without a solution).

**Psychologically:** Want → Need (casual desire → an urgent must-have).

---

**A — ACTION (Ask for it, Direct it):**

The CTA (a specific, urgent command).

---

**AIDA = a quick mental framework** (4 letters, easy to remember).

**Is it useful?** Yes (beginner-friendly, a quick check: "do I have A-I-D-A?" — obvious gaps).

**Is it sufficient?** Not on its own (it needs development — The 5 Fundamentals and The 4 Pillars are more detailed).

**Use:** AIDA as a quick checklist (are the 4 elements present?), and other frameworks for development (HOW to execute each element).
