## **SECTION 4.8 — A LEAD SELECTION DECISION TREE**

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
A DECISION TREE — WHICH LEAD TYPE TO USE?
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

INPUT: The Schwartz State (From Doc 2)

State 1 (Most Aware) → THE OFFER LEAD
• They know the product, they want it, they need a deal
• The lead: 100-200w, transactional, with urgency 
• Conversion: 8-15%

State 2 (Product-Aware) → THE PROMISE LEAD
• They are comparing options, and differentiation is needed
• The lead: 300-500w, a bold promise + a tease of proof
• Conversion: 5-12%

State 3 (Solution-Aware) → THE PROBLEM-SOLUTION LEAD
• They are researching solutions, and education is valued
• The lead: 400-700w, the problem + attempts + the solution
• Conversion: 3-8%

State 4 (Problem-Aware) → THE STORY LEAD
• They are suffering, resigned, and burned
• The lead: 600-1,200w, 3 acts, with identification
• Conversion: 2-6%

State 5 (Unaware) → THE SECRET LEAD (or THE STORY LEAD)
• They don't know they have a problem and are in discovery mode
• The lead: 400-700w (Secret), or 800-1,200w (a deep Story)
• Conversion: 1-4%

SPECIAL CASE: A Webinar/Event → THE INVITATION LEAD
• For any State, with an exclusivity angle
• The lead: 200-400w, an exclusive invitation
• Registration: 15-40%

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

SECONDARY FACTORS (To Adjust the Choice):

THE STAGE:
• Stages 1-2: Shorter leads are OK (not saturated, direct works)
• Stages 4-5: Longer leads are needed (saturation, education cuts through)

THE PRICE:
• <$500: Shorter leads (they don't justify a long education)
• $500-5k: Medium leads (a balance)
• >$5k: Longer leads (to justify the investment, with extensive education)

THE AVATAR:
• Analytical: The Problem-Solution Lead (an appeal to logic)
• Emotional: The Story Lead (an appeal to identification)
• Cynical: The Secret Lead (a conspiracy validates their suspicions)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```
