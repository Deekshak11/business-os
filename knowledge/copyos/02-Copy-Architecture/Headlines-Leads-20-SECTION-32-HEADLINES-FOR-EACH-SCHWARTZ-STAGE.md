## **SECTION 3.2 — HEADLINES FOR EACH SCHWARTZ STAGE**

---

**(FOR EACH Stage: 10 headline examples, characteristics, appropriate formulas)**

---

### **STAGE 1 — DIRECT (A Virgin Market, a Pure Claim Works):**

**Headline characteristics:**
- A direct claim (a benefit statement, without elaboration)
- Simple (10-20 words)
- Formulas: Direct Benefit, a basic How-To, Command

**Pre-emption** (Hopkins in Doc 1 — the first one owns the claim).

---

**Examples (10):**

1.  "Make Money Online"
    (The original in the 1990s — Stage 1 then, now a Stage 5 saturated market)

2.  "Lose Weight Fast"
    (Stage 1 for the diet industry in the 1950s-60s)

3.  "Learn Spanish Quickly"
    (Stage 1 for language learning)

4.  "Make Money with Copywriting"
    (Stage 1 for copywriting in Brazil in the ~2010s)

5.  "Scale Your Business"
    (Stage 1 generic)

6-10. [Continue — pure claims, a direct benefit, without mechanism/elaboration, appropriate for Stage 1 virgin markets where a claim alone is a novelty]

**Modern applicability:** Very rare (most markets are already at Stage 3-5).

**If you find a Stage 1:** A direct claim will win (for its simplicity and pre-emption).

---

### **STAGE 2 — EXPANDED (Competition Appears, an Escalation):**

**Characteristics:**
- Amplified claims (bigger, better, faster, more)
- Comparative (implied vs. others)
- Formulas: Direct Benefit + an amplifier, Number (bigger lists), Command (stronger verbs)

---

**Examples (10):**

1.  "Make Money FAST Online"
    (The Stage 2 evolution of "make money" — it adds FAST)

2.  "Lose Weight FASTER Than Ever"
    (a temporal amplification)

3.  "The BEST Way to Learn Spanish"
    (a superlative)

4.  "Scale Your Business 10x FASTER"
    (10x is a dramatic amplification)

5.  "The Ultimate Guide to Copywriting"
    (ultimate is an amplified superlative)

6-10. [Amplifiers — fastest, best, ultimate, 10x, maximum, revolutionary, etc — an escalating arms race]

**The lifecycle:** Stage 2 eventually saturates (everyone says "fastest" — nobody believes it anymore, a transition to Stage 3 mechanisms).

---

### **STAGE 3 — MECHANISM (Claims Are Saturated, the HOW Matters):**

**Characteristics:**
- A mechanism reveal (the HOW it works, not just a promise)
- A specific method (a 3-step, a formula, a protocol — tangible)
- Formulas: How-To + method, Reason-Why, Number + mechanism

---

**Examples (10):**

1.  "Make Money Online Using a 3-Step Funnel System"
    (the mechanism: a 3-step funnel)

2.  "Lose Weight with the Keto Diet"
    (the mechanism: keto — a specific method)

3.  "Scale Your Business Using the OKR Framework"
    (the mechanism: OKRs)

4.  "Copywriting via a Fill-in-the-Blank Template System"
    (the mechanism: templates — a specific tool)

5.  "Spanish Fluency with the 20 Min/Day Immersion Method"
    (the mechanism: a specific immersion method)

6-10. [Various mechanisms — each names the HOW (keto, templates, OKRs, immersion, automation, etc), differentiation via the mechanism even with a common benefit]

**Schwartz:** "The mechanism can be unique even if the benefit is generic" (validated — Stage 3 differentiation via the mechanism is effective).

---

### **STAGE 4 — ELABORATED (Mechanisms Are Saturated, Authority/Conspiracy):**

**Characteristics:**
- Elaborated mechanisms (authority-backed, insider, conspiracy)
- Formulas: Secret, Reason-Why + authority, Story + credentials

---

**Examples (10):**

1.  "The Wall Street Insider's 3-Stock Portfolio System (Beats the S&P 78% of the Time; Brokers Hide It Because of Zero Commissions)"
    (the mechanism: 3-stock + a conspiracy about brokers)

2.  "The Navy SEALs' 4-Hour Recovery Sleep Protocol (Civilians Can Use It, It Was Classified Until 2019)"
    (the authority: Navy SEALs + a declassified angle)

3.  "The Harvard Keto Protocol (Medical-Grade, Used on 1,200 Patients, with an 83% Success Rate — Not the 'Bro-Science' from YouTube)"
    (the authority: Harvard + differentiation from bro-science)

4.  "The Silicon Valley Productivity Secret (The Top 10% Use It, It's Never Been Public — a Former Google PM Reveals It)"
    (an insider: a former Google PM + exclusive knowledge)

5-10. [An elaboration of authority — institutions (Harvard, Navy), insiders (ex-Goldman, ex-Google), conspiracy elements (they hide it because, they suppress it, it was classified), differentiation from conventional mechanisms]

**Stage 4 = cutting through via authority/an insider** (a mechanism alone is saturated; it needs a credibility boost).

---

### **STAGE 5 — IDENTIFICATION (Total Saturation, Experience > Claim):**

**Characteristics:**
- A story/experience (not a claim — identification)
- A personal narrative (a real person's journey)
- Formulas: Story/Testimonial, a deep Question, an extreme Secret

---

**Examples (10):**

1.  "They Laughed When I Said I Was Quitting My $15k Job to Write Online — Now I'm at $80k/Month, and They're Asking Me How (A 7-Year Journey, the System is Inside)"
    (a classic story — from ridicule to vindication)

2.  "From Broke with $2k in Debt to a $2M Net Worth in 12 Years — How I Did It with $200/Month in Dividends (Anyone Can Replicate It)"
    (a dramatic from-to, an underdog, accessible)

3.  "I Wasted $40k on Gurus Before Discovering This (That Failure Taught Me — Now I Have 47 Clients at $100k+)"
    (a confession of vulnerability, learning from failure)

4.  "A 67-Year-Old Grandmother Who Has Never Been Sick: Her 5 Morning Habits (I Copied Them and Haven't Been Sick in 18 Months)"
    (an aspirational figure, a personal replication)

5-10. [Personal stories, dramatic transformations, strong identification — Stage 5 audiences are numb to claims and respond to relatable experiences]

**Stage 5 = only stories work** (claims are ignored, experiences are valued — validated by Schwartz).
