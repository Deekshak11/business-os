## **SECTION 1.5 — CHAIN OF LOGIC / SLIPPERY SLIDE (HALBERT)**

---

**Gary Halbert** (The Boron Letters):

> *"Copy is a chain.*
>
> *Each paragraph is a link.*
>
> *The job of paragraph 1: to get them to read paragraph 2.*
>
> *The job of P2: to get them to read P3.*
>
> *Etc.*
>
> *Break 1 link (it's boring, confusing, or irrelevant) = the chain breaks, the reader leaves.*
>
> *ALL links are strong = the chain holds, the reader reaches the end (the CTA)."*

---

**The Slippery Slide metaphor:**

"Copy is a slide (a playground slide).

The goal: A person sits at the top and SLIDES to the bottom (they don't stop mid-way).

How? The slide is greased (frictionless), sloped (natural momentum), and has no obstacles (it's smooth).

Copy is the same: The first paragraph hooks them, the second pulls them in, the third continues the momentum — they SLIDE to the CTA (they don't stop)."

---

### **SLIPPERY SLIDE TECHNIQUES:**

---

**TECHNIQUE #1 — SHORT SENTENCES (Momentum):**

**Don't:** Use dense paragraphs (5-8 lines of compact text, intimidating, scan-resistant).

**Do:** Use short sentences (1-2 lines).

Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences).

Use abundant white space (visually inviting).

---

**Example:**

❌ Long paragraph (intimidating):

"You've been stuck at R$30k/month for 18 months even though you're working 60 hours/week, you've tried ads (and burned R$10k with no ROI), SEO (6 months with no results), and networking (expensive events, ignored follow-ups), you feel exhausted, frustrated, and alone, questioning why you can't scale like others have, your family asks when things will get better but you don't have an answer, it's not your fault the problem is the system you're using a R$10k system to try to reach R$100k it obviously doesn't work."

[1 sentence, 85 words, a wall of text — the reader bounces]

---

✅ Broken up (momentum):

"You're stuck.

R$30k/month. For 18 months.

You work 60h/week. Revenue doesn't budge.

You tried ads (burned R$10k, zero ROI).

You tried SEO (6 months, zero results).

Networking (expensive events, follow-ups die).

You feel: Exhausted. Frustrated. Alone.

'Why can't I do it?'

Your family asks: 'When will it get better?'

You: No answer.

It's not your fault.

The problem: Your system.

A R$10k system doesn't get you to R$100k. It's bound to fail."

[14 short sentences, the same 85 words, BUT it's scannable — the momentum carries you]

---

**Each short sentence:** is easy to digest (the brain processes it and continues).

**Accumulated:** Momentum builds (you're sliding, not stopping).

---

**TECHNIQUE #2 — CURIOSITY GAPS (Open Loops):**

**Don't:** Reveal everything (complete information, the loop is closed, satisfaction = stop reading).

**Do:** Tease (it's incomplete, a curiosity gap opens, they MUST continue reading to close the loop).

---

**Example:**

❌ Complete (loop closed):

"Our method uses ketosis to burn fat. Ketosis is a metabolic state where the body burns fat as its primary fuel. It works by eliminating carbs from your diet."

[Explained completely — curiosity is satisfied, they can stop reading]

---

✅ Incomplete (loop open):

"Our method uses a mechanism your body NATURALLY has (but the majority never activates it — why? Carbs block it).

The mechanism is called ketosis (it burns fat 24/7, even while you sleep).

How do you activate it? With 3 simple rules (which I'll reveal in a moment — but first, here's why most diets BLOCK this natural mechanism...)."

[Opened a loop — "the mechanism is ketosis" BUT "how to activate it?" hasn't been answered yet, "3 rules" were mentioned but not revealed, "why do they block it?" is a question that was opened — they MUST continue reading]

---

**Open loops strategy:**

Open a loop in paragraph 5 (a question is raised).

Close the loop in paragraph 15 (the answer is given — 10 paragraphs later).

**In between:** Other loops are opened/closed (multiple threads, weaving).

**The reader:** Is engaged (their brain is tracking the loops, wants closure — they continue reading).

---

**TECHNIQUE #3 — SEAMLESS TRANSITIONS (Connectors):**

**Connectors between paragraphs** (glue, not abrupt jumps).

**Words/phrases:**

"And there's more..." (continuation, accumulation)

"But wait..." (a pivot, a surprise is coming)

"Here's why..." (an explanation follows)

"Not only that..." (adds a layer)

"First... then... finally..." (sequence, progression)

"Now..." (a temporal transition)

"You might be thinking..." (an objection is anticipated and addressed)

---

**Example of transitions:**

```
Para 1: "You've tried diets (5+, all of them failed)."

Para 2: "Why did they fail? [transition: a question connects and promises an answer]

Your metabolism crashed (extreme restriction = starvation mode)."

Para 3: "And there's more [transition: accumulation]. It's not just your metabolism.

It's psychological: Restriction = deprivation (unsustainable, willpower is finite, you inevitably break)."

Para 4: "But wait [transition: a pivot is coming]. It's not you (your fault).

It's the METHOD (it's flawed — the restriction approach is obsolete)."

Para 5: "Here's what REALLY works [transition: a solution tease]..."
```

**The flow:** Each paragraph connects to the next (logically, emotionally, narratively).

**Not:** A random order (paragraph 1 is topic A, paragraph 2 is topic Z, unrelated — it's jarring, it breaks the chain).

**Yes:** A logical progression (A → B → C → D — each one builds on the previous one).

---

**TECHNIQUE #4 — ONE-LINE PARAGRAPHS (Halbert's Signature):**

**Occasionally:** A 1-line paragraph (for extreme emphasis, to stop the scroll).

---

**Example:**

```
"You're doing it wrong.

[1-line para — a punch, stops the scroll]

It's not your fault (no one told you).

But the outcome? Predictable (failure).

Here's why..."
```

**A 1-line paragraph:** creates emphasis (it stands out visually, the brain notices it).

**Overuse:** Loses its impact (if everything is 1-line, there's no emphasis, it's annoying).

**Strategic use:** 5-10x in long copy (8k words), at key moments (revelations, pivots, commands).
