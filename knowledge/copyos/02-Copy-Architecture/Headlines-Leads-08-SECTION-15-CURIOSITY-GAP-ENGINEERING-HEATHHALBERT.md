## **SECTION 1.5 — CURIOSITY GAP ENGINEERING (HEATH/HALBERT)**

---

**Chip Heath** (Made to Stick, Doc 1 Section 5.0 — the Unexpected element of SUCCESs):

Curiosity is a knowledge gap (between what you know and what you want to know).

**The brain hates gaps** (incompleteness = discomfort, an urge to resolve it — you continue to read/click to close the gap).

---

**Halbert** (Boron Letters, a master of curiosity):

> *"Curiosity is a psychological itch (the brain must scratch it).*
>
> *An open loop creates the itch (a question without an answer, a revelation without the reveal).*
>
> *The only way to scratch it: Keep reading (until the loop closes).*
>
> *Curiosity headlines have the highest engagement (tested — people CANNOT resist a gap)."*

---

### **TYPES OF CURIOSITY GAPS:**

---

**TYPE A — BLIND CURIOSITY (The What? Gap):**

**Structure:** Mentions "something" (without specifying what it is).

**Example:**

"The 7-Letter Word That Doubles Sales Letter Conversions (A-List Copywriters Always Use It, You Probably Ignore It)"

**The gap:** What's the word? (7 letters is a teaser, not revealed — you must read to find out).

**Curiosity:** High (specific enough to be intriguing — 7 letters is tangible, not a vague "magic word," BUT it's not revealed = a gap).

---

**TYPE B — MECHANISTIC CURIOSITY (The How? Gap):**

**Structure:** Promises a result, but doesn't explain HOW (a mechanism gap).

**Example:**

"How 12,000 People Lost Weight by Eating Fat, Bacon, and Butter (The Paradox That Works — The Science Explains It, the Method is Inside)"

**The gap:** How does eating fat make you lose weight? (a counter-intuitive paradox — the mechanism isn't explained in the headline; you must read).

**Curiosity:** The mechanism (the brain wants to understand HOW the paradox works — the mention of science = it will be explained; the gap = you must continue).

---

**TYPE C — SPECIFIC CURIOSITY (Which One Specifically? Gap):**

**Structure:** Mentions a list/number, but doesn't reveal the complete list.

**Example:**

"3 Fatal Mistakes Beginner Copywriters Make (89% Make #2, and It Destroys Conversions by 60%+)"

**The gap:** What are the 3 mistakes? (the numbers are mentioned, but not revealed — you must read).

**Specific curiosity:** Mistake #2 is specially mentioned (89% make it, it destroys 60% — the stakes are high; you must know which one to avoid).

---

**TYPE D — FORBIDDEN CURIOSITY (The Secret? Gap):**

**Structure:** Something hidden/forbidden (a conspiracy, inside info).

**Example:**

"What Wall Street Doesn't Want You to Know About Retirement (A Former Goldman VP Reveals It After 22 Years)"

**The gap:** What are they hiding? (forbidden = valued, an insider revealing it = access; the gap = you must read).

**Curiosity:** A conspiracy (Doc 3 Big Idea type) + authority (a former VP) = credibility + intrigue.

---

### **BALANCING Curiosity + Clarity:**

**Too much curiosity:** It's vague (it doesn't say anything).

Example: "The Secret That Changed Everything" (vague — a secret about what? Changed what? Zero specifics).

The reader thinks: "Vague clickbait, I don't trust it" (ignores it).

---

**Too much clarity:** No gap (everything is revealed).

Example: "Use Ketosis to Burn Fat via a Diet of 70% Fat, 25% Protein, and 5% Carbs" (the mechanism is completely explained in the headline — no gap, no curiosity).

The reader thinks: "OK, I get it" (premature satisfaction, may not continue — they already know the mechanism).

---

**THE SWEET SPOT (The Balance):**

**Example:**

"Why Fat Doesn't Make You Fat (and Carbs DO) — The Insulin Mechanism That 12,000 People Tested, and 78% Lost Weight by Eating Bacon"

**Clarity:** The benefit (lose weight), a hint of the mechanism (insulin), social proof (12,000, 78%).

**Curiosity:** HOW does insulin work exactly? (mentioned, but not explained), why are carbs the villain? (stated, but not developed), what was the method they tested? (the results are shown, but not the process).

**The balance:** Enough clarity (to understand the gist and be interested), enough curiosity (gaps remain, you must continue).

---

**Halbert's formula:**

```
Curiosity Headline = [A specific BENEFIT] + [A MECHANISM that is teased but not explained] +
[PROOF that is mentioned but not detailed] + [Intentional GAPS that you must read to close]
```

**Each component:** Some info (not totally vague) + gaps (not complete).

**The result:** Engaged (the itch of curiosity) + qualified (the benefit is clear about who it's for).
