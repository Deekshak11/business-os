## **SECTION 1.7 — HEADLINE TESTING METHODOLOGY (A SYSTEMATIC HOPKINS/OGILVY APPROACH)**

---

**Claude Hopkins** (Scientific Advertising, 1923 — a pioneer in testing):

> *"Test everything. Assume nothing.*
>
> *The cost to test: $50-500 (a small ad run, split traffic).*
>
> *The cost of NOT testing: $5,000-50,000 (waste on the wrong headline scaled).*
>
> *The ROI of testing: 10:1 to 100:1 (it prevents disasters and identifies winners)."*

---

**Ogilvy** (validation 40 years later, in the 1960s):

> *"Never write fewer than 16 headlines.*
>
> *Test the top 4-5.*
>
> *The winner is typically NOT your favorite (surprises are constant — the market ≠ your taste).*
>
> *Data decides (not opinion, not a committee vote, not the founder's preference)."*

---

### **THE COMPLETE TESTING PROCESS (7 Systematic Steps):**

---

**STEP 1 — GENERATE QUANTITY (A Minimum of 20-30 Headlines):**

**The method:**

**A. Use the 10 formulas** (Part II of this doc):

For each formula (How-To, Number, Question, etc.) → generate 2-3 headlines = 20-30 in total.

**Time:** 60-90 minutes (brainstorming, without the filter of self-criticism — quantity first).

---

**B. Variations of the Big Idea** (Doc 3):

The elements of the Big Idea (paradox, mechanism, proof, conspiracy) → each angle = a headline variation.

**Time:** 30-45 minutes (extract angles, express each).

---

**C. Swipe adaptation** (Part VI of this doc):

Scan the 300+ swipes (a similar type, a similar State) → adapt 5-10 (don't copy directly — adapt the structure, change the specifics).

**Time:** 20-30 minutes (scan, adapt).

---

**Total for Step 1:** 110-165 minutes (2-3 hours) → 20-30 rough headlines.

**Output:** A list of 20-30 (diverse, not yet filtered).

---

**STEP 2 — SCORE WITH THE 4 U's (Eliminate Those with <25 Points):**

**The process:**

For EACH headline (20-30):

1.  Score Useful (0-10)
2.  Score Urgent (0-10)
3.  Score Unique (0-10)
4.  Score Ultra-Specific (0-10)
5.  Sum the total (0-40)

**The spreadsheet:**

| Headline | U | U | U | U-S | Total |
|----------|---|---|---|-----|-------|
| #1 | 8 | 5 | 7 | 9 | 29 ✅ |
| #2 | 6 | 3 | 5 | 6 | 20 ❌ |
| [Continue for 20-30 rows] |

**Sort:** Descending by total.

**The cut line:** Eliminate those with <25 points (they are weak and not worth refining).

**Remaining:** Typically 10-15 headlines (in the 25-40 point range).

**Time for Step 2:** 30-60 minutes (rigorously scoring 20-30 headlines).

---

**STEP 3 — REFINE THE TOP 10 (Boost Scores from 25-29 to 30+):**

**For headlines scored 25-29** (borderline — they have potential, BUT are below the threshold):

**Identify the lowest U** (which one scored 0-6? — the weakest link).

**A specific fix:**

---

**An example of refinement:**

**Original headline:** "A System to Scale a Digital Business 3x" (Score: U=7, U=3, U=6, U=7 = 23 ❌)

**Lowest U:** Urgent (3 points — zero timing).

**Fix Urgent:** Add a deadline/window.

"A System to Scale a Business 3x in 90 Days Before the Market Saturates (The Window is Closing, Early-Mover Advantage)"

**Re-score Urgent:** 3 → 8 (+5 points via the "90 days" deadline + the "before it saturates" window).

**New total:** 7+8+6+7 = 28 points (improved, BUT still <30).

**Second lowest:** Unique (6 points — "a system to scale" is familiar).

**Fix Unique:** Add a specific mechanism.

"The 3-Pillar Integrated System to Scale 3x in 90 Days (Offer+Funnel+Traffic Synergy, Not Isolated Tactics) Before the Market Saturates"

**Re-score Unique:** 6 → 8 (+2 points via "the 3-pillar integrated system" + the "not isolated" differentiation).

**New total:** 7+8+8+7 = **30 points** ✅ (now at the threshold, usable).

---

**Iterate:** Refine each 25-29 headline until it's 30+ (or discard it if it doesn't improve after 2-3 iterations).

**Time for Step 3:** 45-90 minutes (refining 5-10 borderline headlines).

---

**Output of Step 3:** 8-12 headlines scored 30+ points (strong candidates).

---

**STEP 4 — PEER REVIEW (Voting, Not Just You):**

**The setup:**

Show the top 8-12 headlines (30+ points) to:
- 3-5 copywriters (peers, with experience)
- 5-10 people from the avatar (the target audience)

**Total:** 8-15 people (a mix of expertise + the target).

---

**The question:**

"Which headline makes you want to read/know more THE MOST?" (a forced choice to rank the top 3).

**The voting:**

The tally:
- Headline #1: 8 first-place votes (popular)
- Headline #4: 5 votes (second)
- Headline #7: 2 votes (third)
- Others: 0-1 votes each

**The top 3-5 by voting:** The final shortlist (validated by audience preference, not just you).

**Common surprises:** Your favorite gets 0 votes (humbling — the market's taste ≠ yours).

**Time for Step 4:** 1-3 days (to recruit people, collect votes, and tally).

---

**STEP 5 — A/B TEST (If Budget/Time Allows — The Gold Standard):**

**The setup:**

The top 3-5 headlines (post-voting) → tested with real traffic.

**Methods:**

**A. Facebook/Google Ads (Cheap, Fast):**

- Budget: $50-100 per headline ($150-500 total for 3-5 headlines)
- Audience: The target avatar (Doc 2 — age, interests, geo)
- Creative: Headline + a simple image (to test the headline specifically, not a complex creative)
- Objective: Traffic (measure CTR — click-through rate)
- Duration: 48-72h (a minimum of 500-1,000 impressions each for statistical significance)

**Measure:** CTR%

Example results:
- Headline A: 1.2% CTR
- Headline B: 3.8% CTR ⭐ (the winner — 3.2x better)
- Headline C: 2.1% CTR

**The winner:** Headline B (data-driven, not based on opinion).

---

**B. Email Subject Lines (Free, with an Existing List):**

- Segment the list (1,000-5,000 subscribers)
- Split: 20% for each headline (200-1,000 each if the list is 1k)
- Send: Simultaneously (same day/time, control the variables)
- Measure: Open rate%

Example:
- Subject A: 18% open
- Subject B: 31% open ⭐
- Subject C: 22% open

**The winner:** Subject B (1.7x better than A).

---

**C. Landing Page Headline (Traffic Split):**

- The same page, with a different headline (an A/B test tool: Google Optimize, Optimizely)
- Traffic: A 50/50 split (a minimum of 500-1,000 visitors for each variation)
- Measure: Scroll depth (what % scrolls past the headline) OR conversion rate

---

**Time for Step 5:** 2-5 days (to set up the test, run it, collect data, and analyze).

**Cost:** $0-500 (email is free, ads are $150-500).

---

**STEP 6 — ANALYZE THE RESULTS (Data Interpretation):**

**A clear winner** (CTR/Open is 2x+ higher than the others):

**Action:** Commit (use the winner as the control, scale with confidence).

---

**A close race** (the difference is <20% — e.g., 3.2% vs. 3.8% CTR):

**Action:** Test more deeply (a larger sample of 2,000-5,000 each, measure conversions and not just CTR — sometimes a high CTR leads to a low CVR = the headline attracts the wrong people).

---

**All performed poorly** (CTR <1% for all, open <15% for all):

**Action:** NONE of them resonated (a weak batch).

**Go back to Step 1:** Generate a NEW batch (20-30 different ones); something is missing from the current batch (the wrong angle? The wrong State? The wrong benefit?).

**Humility:** The market rejected it (don't force it — listen, and iterate).

---

**STEP 7 — CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT (The Winner Becomes the Control, Test Challengers):**

**After the winner is identified:**

The winner = **THE CONTROL** (the current champion, the benchmark).

**Periodically** (quarterly, or when you have a creative spark):

Generate a **CHALLENGER** (a new headline, a different angle).

**Test:** The Control vs. the Challenger (A/B).

**If the Challenger wins:** It becomes the new control (a continuous improvement cycle).

**If the Control wins:** It remains (BUT it has been tested and validated as still being the best).

---

**Hopkins:**

> *"Advertising is continuous testing (not a one-and-done thing).*
>
> *Today's winner might be tomorrow's loser (markets evolve, saturation increases).*
>
> *Always test (with humility — you don't know everything; the market teaches you)."*
