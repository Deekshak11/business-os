## **SECTION 1.4 — P-A-S FORMULA (AWAI — Problem, Agitate, Solve)**

---

**AWAI** (American Writers & Artists Institute — the copywriting training standard):

A simplification of the 3 Acts into 3 steps (easier to remember, quick to apply).

---

**THE FORMULA:**

---

**P — PROBLEM (Identify):**

**What:** The client has a problem (state it explicitly and specifically).

**Example:** "You've been 40 lbs overweight for 5+ years (you've tried diets, but always regained the weight)."

**In copy:** The opening (the first 2-3 paragraphs after the headline).

**Objective:** Recognition ("Yes, that's ME" — identification, Doc 1 Section 2.4, Liking).

---

**A — AGITATE (Amplify):**

**What:** Make the pain worse (the consequences of not solving it, emotional escalation).

**Technique:** SPIN Implication (Doc 11 — "if you don't solve this, in 6 months/2 years/5 years...").

**Example:** "40 lbs today → 60 lbs in 2 years (the progression continues), a diabetes diagnosis (lifelong medications), strained relationships, destroyed self-image."

**In copy:** The middle section (after problem ID, before the solution).

**Objective:** Urgency (you cannot ignore it, you must act — intensified pain = increased motivation).

---

**Halbert (the master of agitation):**

> *"Rub salt in the wound (agitate the pain).*
>
> *Don't: Gently mention the problem.*
>
> *Do: Make them FEEL the pain viscerally (vivid, sensory, emotional — Doc 9, Sensory Language).*
>
> *Pain intense enough = the solution is irresistible (relief is highly valued)."*

---

**Balance:** Agitate BUT don't depress (Doc 4 Section 6.2.7 — pain without hope = avoidance).

**Agitation + Hope** (a solution exists) = motivation for action.

**Agitation alone** (doom, no solution) = paralysis.

---

**S — SOLVE (Present the Solution):**

**What:** The product is the hero (solves the agitated problem).

**Example:** "Simplified Keto (just 3 rules) eliminates 40 lbs in 90 days, without the yo-yo effect (the ketosis mechanism prevents rebound, 78% of tracked users maintained it for 24+ months)."

**In copy:** The solution section (after agitation — timing: pain peaks → solution = relief).

**Objective:** Position the product as THE answer (not just an option — THE solution to the agitated problem).

---

**P-A-S is the 3 Acts simplified:**

```
P (Problem) = Act 1 (Setup — identify the protagonist, the problem)
A (Agitate) = Act 2 (Confrontation — obstacles, amplified pain)
S (Solve) = Act 3 (Resolution — the solution, the transformation)
```

**The same structure** (3 Acts is comprehensive, P-A-S is simplified and memorable).

**Use:** P-A-S when you need a quick framework (easy to remember, fast to apply).

Use the 3 Acts when you need a detailed structure (screenplay-level rigor, long-form).

---

**Application of P-A-S:**

**Short email** (200w):

```
P (50w): "Stuck at R$30k/month for 18 months (working 60h, not growing)."

A (70w): "In 2 years: Still at R$30k (inflation = a real loss), 60h burnout (health deteriorates), family suffers (your absence), you'll think 'I wasted years.'"

S (80w): "Our 3-Pillar System scales you 3-5x (47 companies, 9 months avg, without hiring a team, without massive ad spend). Here it is: [link]"
```

**Long sales letter** (8k words):

P (500w), A (2,000w), S (5,500w — solution is developed extensively).

**Proportions are flexible** (short = equal thirds, long = S is dominant).
