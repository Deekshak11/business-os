# **SECTION 0 — PREREQUISITES & HOW TO USE**

---

### **Critical Prerequisites:**

**→ REQUIREMENT: Docs 2-3**

✅ **Doc 2** (Diagnosis):
- State/Stage diagnosed (determines which headline type to choose — Part III matrix)
- Avatar's language (headlines mirror their words — Doc 2 Part VIII research)
- Specific pain/desire (headlines communicate these — not generic ones)

✅ **Doc 3** (Positioning):
- Big Idea created (score 5-6/6 — headlines EXPRESS the Big Idea, they don't create it)
- Mechanism identified (some headlines reveal the mechanism — Reason-Why, How-To)
- Voice defined (headline tone = consistent voice — formal vs. casual)

**Optional:**
- Doc 4 (Offer) — Guarantee headlines (if the guarantee is a differentiator)
- Doc 5 (Structures) — Context where the headline is used (sales letter, VSL, email)

---

### **Time Investment:**

**Full Read-Through (first time):** 4-6 hours
- Part I (Fundamentals): 45-60 min
- Part II (10 Formulas): 90-120 min
- Part III (State/Stage): 45-60 min
- Part IV (6 Leads): 120-180 min
- Parts V-VI (Openings, Swipes): 60-90 min

**Practical Application (writing headlines for a project):** 30 min-2 hr
- Decision tree (5 min): State/Stage → Headline type
- Generate headlines (30-60 min): 10-20 variations using formulas
- Score with 4 U's (20-30 min): Score each headline, eliminate those <25 points
- Test top 3-5 (optional, 1-3 days for A/B testing ads)
- Choose the winner (based on data or voting)

**Total for the first time:** 5-8 hours (reading + application).

**Subsequent use:** 30 min-1 hr (consulting formulas, swipes, generating headlines directly).

---

### **Expected Output:**

**Minimum:** 10 headline variations (each scored 25-35 points).

**Ideal:** 20-30 headlines (top 5-10 scored 30+ points, tested, winner chosen).

**Complete Lead:** 300-1,000 words (type based on State, developed using templates in Part IV).

---

### **How to Use This Document:**

---

**FIRST TIME (Learning the System):**

**Linear sequence:**

1.  **Part I** (Fundamentals) — Understand why headlines are critical, the 4 U's scoring system, and universal principles.
2.  **Part II** (10 Formulas) — Learn classic formulas (tested for decades) and when to use each.
3.  **Part III** (State/Stage) — Use the decision matrix (choose a type based on the Doc 2 diagnosis).
4.  **Part IV** (6 Leads) — Learn lead types, when to use each, and their structures.
5.  **Part V** (Openings) — Collier's 27 types (diversity of approaches).
6.  **Part VI** (Swipes) — Scan 300+ examples (absorb patterns).
7.  **Part VII** (Checklists) — The validation process.

**Practice:** Write 10 headline variations, score them, choose a lead type, and write an opening.

---

**PRACTICAL USE (Real Project):**

**Quick workflow:**

1.  **Quickly consult Doc 2:** Your project's State/Stage (already diagnosed — just refresh).

2.  **Part III Decision Matrix** (5 min): State/Stage → Output: recommended headline type.

3.  **Part II Specific Formula** (10 min): Read the recommended formula (structure, examples).

4.  **Generate headlines** (30 min): Use the formula + the Big Idea from Doc 3 to create 10-15 variations.

5.  **Score with 4 U's** (15 min): Score each headline, eliminate those <25, and shortlist 5-10 (30+ points).

6.  **Part VI Swipes** (15 min): Scan swipes of a similar type (for inspiration, patterns).

7.  **Choose the top 3-5** (5 min): Based on intuition + scores; test if budget allows.

8.  **Part IV Lead Type** (10 min): Decision tree → Lead type; read the structure.

9.  **Write the lead** (30-60 min): Follow the lead type template, 300-1,000 words.

10. **Validate with checklists** (10 min): Headline checklist 7.1, Lead checklist 7.2.

**Total:** 2.5-3.5 hours (headlines + complete lead, validated).

---

**QUICK CONSULTATION (Emergency):**

**"I need a headline NOW (in 30 minutes):"**

1.  Part III matrix (2 min): State/Stage → type
2.  Part II formula (3 min): Read the template
3.  Generate 5 headlines (15 min): Quickly, using the formula
4.  Score the top 2-3 (5 min): Quick 4 U's
5.  Choose one (2 min): The highest score
6.  Done (30 min total)

**Not ideal** (you didn't test 20 variations, no A/B test), BUT it's a **functional emergency solution** (deadline is today).
