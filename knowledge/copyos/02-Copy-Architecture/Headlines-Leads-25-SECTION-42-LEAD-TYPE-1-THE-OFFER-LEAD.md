## **SECTION 4.2 — LEAD TYPE #1: THE OFFER LEAD**

---

### **WHEN TO USE IT:**

✅ **State 1** (Most Aware — they know the product, they want it, they just need a deal)

✅ **Existing customers** (an email list, for re-engagement, upsells)

✅ **Hot traffic** (retargeting, 3+ visits, add-to-carts — they are almost ready)

✅ **Launch announcements** (for subscribers who have been waiting, with total awareness)

✅ **Flash sales** (with a short urgency of 24-48h, for a quick transaction)

---

### **THE STRUCTURE (3 Paragraphs, 100-200 Words Total):**

---

**PARAGRAPH #1 — STATE THE OFFER DIRECTLY (30-50w):**

No buildup, no story — an IMMEDIATE offer.

**The template:**

```
"[A timing qualifier: Today/This week/For the launch], [Product name]: [a specific deal].

[Pricing: A comparison of normal vs. today].

[A quantified savings]."
```

---

**An example:**

```
"Today only, the ClickFunnels Annual Plan: a 50% discount.

Normally: $297/month ($3,564/year).

Today: $1,782/year.

You save: $1,782 (half the price for the same complete platform)."
```

**50 words** — the offer is stated in the first 4 lines, it's specific (pricing, savings), with no fluff.

---

**PARAGRAPH #2 — A BRIEF VALUE REMINDER (40-70w):**

**Don't:** Explain the whole product (in State 1, they already know — it's tedious).

**Do:** Remind them of the main features (a refresh, not a lesson).

**The template:**

```
"The same [list of 3-5 main features].

The same [core benefit].

Zero changes (just a better price — identical value, half the investment)."
```

---

**An example:**

```
"The same unlimited funnels, 200+ templates, 40+ integrations (Stripe, PayPal, email tools), A/B testing, and complete analytics.

The same platform you know (trusted and used by 100k+ businesses).

Zero features removed (just a 50% price — total value, half the cost)."
```

**60 words** — a feature reminder (in bullets, scannable), reassurance (nothing is removed, everything is the same).

---

**PARAGRAPH #3 — URGENCY + A CTA (30-50w):**

**The template:**

```
"Expires: [a specific deadline with date/time/timezone].

After that: [The penalty — the price goes back, the deal is gone].

[A CTA button/link]."
```

---

**An example:**

```
"Expires: Friday, November 29, 2024, at 11:59 PM ET.

After midnight: The price goes back to $297/month (you lose the $1,782 savings).

[A GREEN BUTTON: SECURE 50% OFF NOW →]"
```

**40 words** — a specific deadline (the full date), a clear penalty (a loss of $1,782), a visual CTA (a green button, a specific action).

---

**THE TOTAL OFFER LEAD:** 100-160 words (ultra-concise, appropriate for State 1 — transactional, direct, zero fluff).

---

### **EXAMPLES OF COMPLETE OFFER LEADS (5 Annotated):**

---

**Example #1 — An Email to an Existing Customer (SaaS):**

```
Subject: "50% OFF the Annual — Expires at Midnight"

Body:

"Hi [Name],

A quick offer (it expires tonight at midnight):

The ClickFunnels Annual: 50% off.

Normally $297/month ($3,564/year), today $1,782/year.

Save $1,782.

The same platform (unlimited funnels, templates, integrations, everything).

It expires tonight at 11:59 PM ET.

After that: It's back to $297/month.

[A BUTTON: Lock in 50% OFF]

Thanks,
Russell"

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TOTAL: 85 words

ANNOTATIONS:

[Subject]: The offer + urgency (50% off, expires — transactional for State 1)

[Line 1]: Personal (hi Name), a qualifier (quick — it respects their time)

[Lines 2-4]: An immediate offer (no intro, straight to the point for State 1 = they want the deal now)

[Lines 5-6]: Specific pricing (normal vs. today, with anchoring from Doc 1), the savings ($1,782 — a large, tangible number)

[Line 7]: A value reminder (the same platform — reassurance, not a downgrade)

[Lines 8-9]: A specific deadline (tonight at 11:59 PM ET — timezone clarity), a penalty (the price goes back — a loss)

[Button]: A visual CTA (green, "lock in" implies scarcity, a repeat of "50% off" for clarity)

[Sign-off]: Casual (Thanks, Russell — friendly, a brand personality)

PERFECT FOR STATE 1:
✓ A direct offer (in the first lines)
✓ It assumes knowledge (it doesn't explain what ClickFunnels is)
✓ Short (85w — it respects the time of a busy State 1 person who has already decided)
✓ Transactional (deal-focused, not for building a relationship)
✓ Urgency (tonight — specific, real)

EXPECTED CONVERSION: 8-15% (for existing State 1 customers, with a compelling offer and real urgency)
```

---

**[Examples #2-5 of the Offer Lead — for the scenarios of: a product launch for subscribers, retargeting for an abandoned cart, an upsell post-purchase, and reactivating inactive customers — each 80-150w, annotated with State 1 elements and conversion benchmarks]**

---

### **WHEN THE OFFER LEAD FAILS:**

❌ **For cold traffic in States 4-5:** A direct offer to someone who doesn't know the product = confusion ("a discount on what? Who are you?").

❌ **A weak offer:** A deal that isn't compelling (10% off a commodity product = "meh"); an offer lead exposes its weakness.

❌ **A complex product:** It requires an explanation (enterprise SaaS, consulting — an offer alone is insufficient; it needs education).

**The solution:** Use a different lead type (a Problem-Solution for State 3, a Story for State 4, a Secret for State 5).
