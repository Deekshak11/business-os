## **SECTION 2.2 — TYPE #2: THE REVERSE HOOK BULLET**

---

**The template:**

```
"• Why [a NEGATIVE/the OPPOSITE of what's expected] is actually [POSITIVE/CORRECT] (page [X] explains the paradox)"
```

**An inversion** (counter-intuitive, a Twist Big Idea from Doc 3 applied to bullets).

---

**Examples (12):**

1.  "• Why working MORE hours keeps you stuck at $30k (the productivity paradox — fewer strategic hours, MORE revenue; a breakdown on page 67)"

2.  "• Why fat DOESN'T make you fat (and carbs DO) — the insulin mechanism on page 34 reverses 50 years of wrong nutritional dogma"

3.  "• Why 'cheap' pricing COSTS you sales (the psychology of value — $497 converts BETTER than $97, tested on 2,400 offers, with data on page 89)"

4.  "• Why diversification LIMITS wealth (concentration builds it — a Buffett quote on page 23, and the math shows that 12-stock returns are > 50-stock returns over 30 years)"

5-12. [Paradoxes — work less to earn more, eat fat to lose fat, charge more to sell more, focus narrowly to scale bigger, etc — each an inversion of conventional wisdom, with a page reference for curiosity]

---

**The components:**

- The Benefit: ✅ (resolving the paradox = a valuable understanding)
- Curiosity: ✅✅ (HIGH — "why the opposite?" = the brain must know, and the page isn't detailed)
- Specificity: ✅ (page numbers, and specific named mechanisms)
- Emotional: ✅ (an inversion = a surprise, and permission to do the opposite)
- Proof: ✅ (a page reference = proof exists and is documented)

**Component score:** 5/5 ✅

**Curiosity:** Is at its highest (a paradox + a page gap = an irresistible combination).

---

**When to use it:**

✅ **For Stages 3-5** (sophistication — an inversion cuts through the saturated conventional)

✅ **For counter-intuitive Big Ideas** (the Mistake and Twist types from Doc 3 — the bullets amplify them)

✅ **For analytical audiences** (who love resolved paradoxes and are curious about the mechanism)
