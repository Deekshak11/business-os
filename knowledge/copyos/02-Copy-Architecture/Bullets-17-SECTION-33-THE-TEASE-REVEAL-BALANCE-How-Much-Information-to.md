## **SECTION 3.3 — THE TEASE-REVEAL BALANCE (How Much Information to Give)**

---

**The spectrum:**

```
0% Reveal ←──────────────→ 100% Reveal
(Totally blind)   THE SWEET SPOT    (Complete)
                   ↓
              60-70% Tease
              30-40% Reveal
```

---

**A 100% Reveal (Everything is Explained):**

"• Use the keto diet to lose weight (eat 70% fat, 25% protein, and 5% carbs; your body enters ketosis and burns fat for fuel; the mechanisms of reduced insulin and prevented fat storage have been studied in 12,000 participants, 78% of whom lost 15+ lbs in 90 days and 74% of whom maintained it for 24 months)"

**The problem:** Zero curiosity (everything is revealed — complete satisfaction, no need to buy to discover).

**Its use:** In the body copy (where paragraphs explain completely), NOT in bullets (bullets tease).

---

**A 0% Reveal (Nothing is Said):**

"• The incredible secret that transforms everything"

**The problem:** Totally vague (a secret about what? It transforms what? — not compelling, empty clickbait).

**Its use:** NEVER (vagueness = distrust, not genuine curiosity).

---

**THE SWEET SPOT (60-70% Tease, 30-40% Reveal):**

"• Why fat DOESN'T make you fat (the insulin mechanism — carbs are the real villain; the exact keto protocol is on page 34, with the macros, the timing of meals, and it has been tested by 12k people, with 78% losing 15+ lbs)"

**The reveal (30-40%):**
- A hint of the mechanism: "insulin" (it's mentioned, but not fully explained)
- The villain: "carbs" (they are identified, but it's not detailed why)
- The result: "78% lost 15+ lbs" (the outcome is shown)

**The tease (60-70%):**
- HOW insulin works: It's not explained (a gap)
- The exact keto protocol: It's mentioned as being on page 34, but not given in the bullet (a gap)
- The exact macros: They are referenced, but not stated (a gap)
- The timing of meals: It's mentioned, but not detailed (a gap)

**The balance:** Enough info (for qualified interest — keto, insulin, the results), but not enough to be complete (the gaps drive them to read page 34 = to buy).

---

**Makepeace's rule:**

> *"Reveal enough to qualify (so the reader knows if it's relevant and if the benefit appeals to them).*
>
> *Conceal enough to intrigue (gaps remain; they must buy to close them).*
>
> *A 60/40 tease/reveal is the tested optimal (from decades of newsletters and billions in revenue — it's the sweet spot)."*
