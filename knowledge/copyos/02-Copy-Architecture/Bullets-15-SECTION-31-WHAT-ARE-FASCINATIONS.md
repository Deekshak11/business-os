## **SECTION 3.1 — WHAT ARE FASCINATIONS?**

---

**Gary Bencivenga coined** the term "fascinations" (vs. the generic "bullets").

**Makepeace mastered them** (in his financial newsletters and Wall Street promos — with billions in revenue attributed to fascination bullets alone).

---

**The definition:**

**A fascination is a bullet SO intriguing that the reader CANNOT resist** buying (the unsatisfied curiosity is painful; the purchase is the only relief).

**The difference from a normal bullet:**

**A bullet:** A benefit statement, with some curiosity (mild-moderate).

**A fascination:** Has EXTREME curiosity (it's obsessive — the brain loops on "what is it? I MUST know," and it can't focus until it's resolved).

---

**Makepeace:**

> *"A fascination is when a reader is still thinking about your bullet at 3 AM (they can't sleep, the curiosity is itching).*
>
> *That's no exaggeration — clients have emailed me to say: 'I bought your newsletter at 2 AM because I couldn't sleep from not knowing what the technique on page 67 was' (literally).*
>
> *That's the power of a fascination (irresistible curiosity, where the purchase is a relief)."*

---

**The characteristics of fascinations:**

✅ **A blind reference** (it mentions something but doesn't say what — "the 7-letter word," "the technique on page 47")

✅ **A page number** (it's specific — for implicit credibility and because the content exists)

✅ **A hint of a benefit** (the outcome is teased — "it doubles conversions," "it eliminates pain" — it's desirable)

✅ **An obscured mechanism** (the HOW it works is hidden, for a maximum gap)

✅ **Embedded proof** (validation is mentioned — it has been tested, validated, and used — for credibility)
