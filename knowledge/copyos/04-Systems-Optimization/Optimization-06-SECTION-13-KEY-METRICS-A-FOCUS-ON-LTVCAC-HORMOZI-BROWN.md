## **SECTION 1.3 — KEY METRICS (A FOCUS ON LTV:CAC — HORMOZI + BROWN)**

---

**Alex Hormozi** ($100M Offers, $100M Leads):

> *"A business has 1 metric that matters MOST: the LTV:CAC ratio.*
>
> *Everything else (revenue, customer count, traffic) = vanity metrics (they are impressive, BUT they don't reveal the real health).*
>
> *An LTV:CAC of <3:1 = a dying business (bleeding cash, can't scale, eventual death).*
>
> *An LTV:CAC of 10:1+ = a business that's printing money (infinite scaling, fat margins, compounding)."*

---

**Todd Brown** (E5):

> *"Metrics are for diagnostics (not for celebration).*
>
> *Does a R$100k/month revenue sound great? (A premature celebration).*
>
> *BUT: If the CAC is R$800, and the LTV is R$1,200, the ratio is 1.5:1 = that's a PROBLEM (you're losing R$400+ on each customer long-term after ops/overhead costs).*
>
> *vs. A R$20k/month revenue (which sounds small), BUT the CAC is R$50, and the LTV is R$2,000, for a ratio of 40:1 = that's EXCELLENT (aggressive scaling is possible, with healthy margins).*
>
> *Focus on the ratio (not the absolute revenue — the ratio is about scalability, sustainability, and real health)."*

### **METRIC #1 — LTV (CUSTOMER LIFETIME VALUE):**

---

**The definition:**

**LTV = The total revenue generated by a customer** (over the entire relationship, not a single purchase — the complete lifecycle).

---

**The formula (Abraham's — with 3 Levers):**

```
LTV = (Average Transaction Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan)

Where:
• Average Transaction Value: The average purchase ticket (the initial + upsells + cross-sells on average)
• Purchase Frequency: How many times they buy per year
• Customer Lifespan: How many years/months they remain an active customer (retention)
```

---

**An example calculation:**

```
THE PRODUCT: A R$1,997 Copywriting Course

The Average Transaction Value:
• The initial purchase: R$1,997 (the core course)
• The upsells (30% of customers buy R$497 templates within 90 days): R$1,997 + (0.30 × R$497) = R$2,146
• The cross-sells (15% buy a R$10,000 advanced mastermind within 12 months): +a R$1,500 weighted average
• The total AOV: R$3,646 (the initial + upsells + cross-sells over the lifecycle)

The Purchase Frequency:
• Repeat purchases: 1.2x/year (some only buy the core once, some buy an upsell in year 2, for an average of 1.2 purchases annually)

The Customer Lifespan:
• The tracked retention: 2.3 years on average (some only for 1 year, some are engaged for 5+ years, the median is 2.3)
• The churn rate: 43% in year 1 (they don't return), 57% are retained in year 2+, 30% are retained in year 3+ — the average lifespan is calculated at 2.3 years

THE LTV CALCULATION:
= R$3,646 × 1.2 × 2.3
= R$10,064 total lifetime value per customer

[LTV = R$10,064 — each acquired customer generates THIS much revenue over their complete lifecycle]
```

---

**How to INCREASE LTV (Abraham's 3 Levers — Each is Optimizable):**

---

**LEVER #1 — Increase the Transaction Value (AOV ↑):**

**The tactics:**

**A. Upsells/Cross-sells:**
- Order bumps (+20-40% of customers, +R$47-297 to the AOV)
- One-click upsells (+10-25%, +R$297-997)
- Backend offers (months later, +R$1,000-10,000)

**B. A price increase:**
- Raise prices (test R$1,997 vs. R$2,497 — sometimes higher converts BETTER in affluent markets)
- Premium tiers (offer R$1,997 + a R$4,997 premium — some will choose the premium)

**C. Bundles:**
- Package products (the Core + Templates = a R$2,497 bundle vs. R$1,997 + R$797 separately = a perceived saving, the AOV increases)

**The impact:** AOV from R$3,646 → R$5,200 (by optimizing upsells + pricing + bundles) = a +43% LTV (from lever 1 alone).

---

**LEVER #2 — Increase the Purchase Frequency (Purchases/Year ↑):**

**The tactics:**

**A. Continuity/Membership:**
- Offer a monthly subscription (R$97-297/month ongoing after the core) — for recurring revenue, an infinite frequency

**B. Consumables:**
- Products that run out (supplements that are reordered monthly, template updates quarterly — natural repeat purchases)

**C. New product launches:**
- Different products (not just upsells of the same one) — customers are interested in variety (launch 2-4/year, engaged customers will buy multiple)

**D. Win-back campaigns:**
- Reactivation emails (Doc 10 Section 3.4 — exclusive offers for dormant customers, 12-20% reactivate)

**The impact:** Frequency from 1.2x → 2.1x/year (by adding a R$197/month membership with an average of 8 months of retention) = a +75% LTV (from lever 2).

---

**LEVER #3 — Increase the Customer Lifespan (Retention ↑):**

**The tactics:**

**A. Deliver value continuously:**
- A strong onboarding (the indoctrination from Doc 10 — 70-85% of engagement is maintained)
- Ongoing content (90/10 educational emails, constant value)
- Product updates (new features, improvements — customers stay engaged for years)

**B. A community:**
- An exclusive group (800+ members — for networking, support, and belonging — Cialdini's Unity, a +40% retention with communities vs. solo)

**C. Customer success:**
- Check-ins (proactive — "How's it going? Need help?" — to prevent churn before it happens)
- Results tracking (celebrate their wins — for dopamine, a positive association with the brand)

**D. Loyalty programs:**
- Discounts for long-term customers (10% off in year 2, 15% off in year 3 — an incentive to stay)

**The impact:** Lifespan from 2.3 years → 3.8 years (with retention tactics, a community, and success support) = a +65% LTV (from lever 3).

---

**CUMULATIVE LTV OPTIMIZATION (All 3 Levers Combined):**

```
The baseline LTV: R$10,064

Lever #1 (AOV ↑43%): R$3,646 → R$5,200
Lever #2 (Frequency ↑75%): 1.2x → 2.1x
Lever #3 (Lifespan ↑65%): 2.3 → 3.8 years

The new LTV = R$5,200 × 2.1 × 3.8 = R$41,496

The improvement: From R$10,064 → R$41,496 = +312% (a 4.1x increase in LTV — the 3 levers optimized simultaneously are compounding, not additive; it's exponential).
```

**Abraham:**

"Small improvements on each lever (10-20% each) = a massive increase in the combined LTV (it's multiplicative — 1.1 × 1.2 × 1.15 = 1.52x, NOT the additive 0.45x).

The focus: Optimize ALL 3 levers (not just 1 — for the synergy)."

### **METRIC #2 — CAC (CUSTOMER ACQUISITION COST):**

---

**The definition:**

**CAC = The total cost to acquire a customer** (the marketing spend ÷ the customers acquired in a given period).

---

**The formula:**

```
CAC = Total Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers Acquired

The Marketing Spend includes:
• The ad spend (Facebook, Google, Native, YouTube — the total budget)
• The creative production (videos, images, copywriting — pro-rated monthly)
• The tools/software (the email platform, the funnel builder, the analytics — the monthly fees)
• The team/freelancers (marketing salaries, a VA, designers — pro-rated)
• The affiliates/partners (the commissions paid — if applicable)
```

---

**A detailed example calculation:**

```
THE PERIOD: November 2024 (1 month)

The Marketing Spend breakdown:
• Facebook Ads: R$8,000 (the campaign budget)
• Google Ads: R$4,000
• Native Ads (Taboola): R$2,000
• The email platform (ActiveCampaign): R$800/month
• The funnel builder (ClickFunnels): R$600/month
• The analytics (Hotjar, Google Analytics Pro): R$200/month
• A freelance copywriter: R$3,000 (pro-rated — a sales page rewrite, 10 emails)
• A designer: R$1,500 (for ad creatives, a landing page)
• VA support: R$1,200/month (for email responses, admin)
• Affiliates: R$2,700 (a 10% commission on 27 sales via affiliates for R$1,997 each = R$5,391 in affiliate revenue, 10% = R$539... wait, 27 × R$1,997 × 10% = R$5,391.90 in commissions paid)

TOTAL Marketing Spend: R$8k + R$4k + R$2k + R$800 + R$600 + R$200 + R$3k + R$1.5k + R$1.2k + R$5,391.90 = R$26,691.90

New Customers Acquired: 115 (in November 2024 — with tracked conversions from all sources)

CAC = R$26,691.90 ÷ 115 = R$232.10/customer

[CAC = R$232 per acquired customer — the average marketing cost to acquire each one]
```

---

**How to REDUCE CAC (Improve Efficiency):**

---

**TACTIC #1 — Improve Ad Targeting (Reduce Waste):**

**The action:** Segment your audiences (test different demographics, interests, and behaviors — eliminate the low-performers and double down on the winners).

**The impact:** CAC from R$232 → R$180 (the targeting is refined, waste is reduced by 22%).

---

**TACTIC #2 — Improve the Creative (Increase the CTR):**

**The action:** Test 10-20 ad creatives (the GOATED framework from Doc 5 Section 2.C.2), the winner has a CTR of 3.2% vs. the 1.8% baseline (1.78x better).

**The impact:** The same budget, 1.78x more clicks, the CAC is ÷ 1.78 = from R$180 → R$101 (from creative optimization alone).

---

**TACTIC #3 — Improve the Conversion Funnel (Increase the CVR):**

**The action:** Optimize the landing page (a headline test, a lead test, an offer test — from the Doc 12 Section 1.1 hierarchy), the CVR goes from 25% → 40% (+15 points).

**The impact:** The same traffic, 1.6x more conversions, the CAC is ÷ 1.6 = from R$101 → R$63 (from funnel optimization).

---

**TACTIC #4 — Organic/Free Channels (Zero Marginal Cost):**

**The action:** SEO (content marketing, rankings for long-tail keywords), referrals (an incentive for customers to refer — 10-30% will refer 1+ friends, a R$0 CAC for referrals), partnerships (JVs, co-marketing — a traffic share, a split cost).

**The impact:** 30% of customers come from organic/referrals (a R$0 CAC) blended with the 70% from paid (a R$63 CAC) = a blended CAC of R$44 (30% × R$0 + 70% × R$63).

---

**CUMULATIVE CAC REDUCTION:**

```
The baseline CAC: R$232

Tactic #1 (Targeting): R$232 → R$180 (-22%)
Tactic #2 (The Creative): R$180 → R$101 (-44%)
Tactic #3 (The Funnel CVR): R$101 → R$63 (-38%)
Tactic #4 (30% Organic): R$63 → R$44 (-30%)

The final CAC: R$44 (vs. the R$232 baseline = an -81% reduction, 5.3x more efficient)
```

**Hormozi:**

"CAC optimization is margin expansion (every R$100 you reduce the CAC by is an additional R$100 in profit per customer, and it scales beautifully — 1,000 customers is an additional R$100k in annual profit)."

### **METRIC #3 — THE LTV:CAC RATIO (THE NORTH STAR METRIC):**

---

**The formula:**

```
LTV:CAC RATIO = LTV ÷ CAC
```

---

**The benchmarks (Hormozi + Brown + SaaS Capital):**

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE LTV:CAC RATIO — HEALTHY BUSINESS BENCHMARKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

<1:1 = ❌❌ A DEATH SPIRAL
• You lose money on every customer (LTV < CAC — it's unsustainable)
• An example: An LTV of R$800, a CAC of R$1,200 = a -R$400 loss per customer
• The action: STOP the ads immediately (you're bleeding cash), fix the offer OR pivot
• Survival: <6 months (a short runway, you're VC-dependent OR you'll close)

1:1 to 2:1 = ❌ UNPROFITABLE (Pre-Product-Market Fit)
• A rough breakeven (LTV = CAC, zero profit after ops/overhead)
• An example: An LTV of R$2,000, a CAC of R$1,500 = a R$500 gross profit (BUT R$600 in ops = a -R$100 net loss)
• The action: Fix the offer (increase the LTV with upsells) OR reduce the CAC (with targeting, the creative)
• You can't scale (the more you scale, the more you lose — you're trapped)

2:1 to 3:1 = ⚠️ MARGINAL (Survival Mode)
• A slight profit (covering ops, but barely)
• An example: An LTV of R$3,000, a CAC of R$1,200 = a R$1,800 gross (R$1,000 in ops = a R$800 net profit, which is thin)
• The action: Optimize (to reach a minimum of 3:1 before scaling)
• You can exist, you can't thrive

3:1 = ✅ MINIMUM VIABLE (A Healthy Breakeven)
• The industry standard minimum (SaaS Capital, Bessemer benchmarks)
• An example: An LTV of R$6,000, a CAC of R$2,000 = a R$4,000 gross (R$2,500 in ops = a R$1,500 net profit, which is acceptable)
• The action: Maintain (it's sustainable), optimize toward 5:1 (for higher scaling confidence)
• You can scale slowly (cautious growth, moderate margins)

3:1 to 5:1 = ✅ PROFITABLE (Growth Mode)
• Healthy margins (ops are covered, the profit is significant)
• An example: An LTV of R$10,000, a CAC of R$2,000 = 5:1 (a R$8,000 gross, R$4,000 in ops = a R$4,000 net profit, a 40% margin)
• The action: Scale (increase the ad budget, expand the channels, confidence is warranted)
• You can scale moderately-aggressively (it's capital efficient, self-funded growth is possible)

5:1 to 10:1 = ✅✅ EXCELLENT (Scale Aggressively)
• Fat margins (a 50-60% profit)
• An example: An LTV of R$20,000, a CAC of R$2,500 = 8:1 (a R$17,500 gross, R$7,000 in ops = a R$10,500 net, a 52% margin)
• The action: Scale aggressively (outbid competitors in auctions, geographic expansion, omnichannel)
• Compounding growth (reinvest profits → more customers → more profits → a loop)

>10:1 = ✅✅✅ EXCEPTIONAL (Print Money, Dominate)
• Massive margins (a 60-80% profit)
• An example: An LTV of R$41,496, a CAC of R$44 = 943:1 (the example from the previous Section 1.3 — an outlier, BUT possible with extreme optimization)
• The action: Dominate the market (an unlimited budget ceiling, you can profitably acquire ALL available customers, a monopoly of the economics)
• Rare (the top 1% of businesses — a combination of an exceptional product + marketing genius + market timing)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

THE STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS OF THE RATIO:

<3:1: FIX the offer/product (don't scale — fix the foundation first)
3-5:1: SCALE cautiously (test channels, a slow, data-driven geographic expansion)
5-10:1: SCALE aggressively (multiply the channels, increase the budgets 2-5x, growth is the primary goal)
>10:1: DOMINATE (an unlimited growth ceiling, acquire competitors, vertical integration, category ownership is possible)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

---

**Perry Marshall** (80/20 Sales & Marketing):

"The LTV:CAC ratio is the single number that tells you the health of a business (it's comprehensive — it incorporates the offer quality, the marketing efficiency, the retention, everything).

A low ratio (<3:1): There's a problem somewhere (a weak offer, a high CAC, poor retention, OR all three — a diagnosis is needed).

A high ratio (>5:1): Multiple things are right (a strong offer, efficient marketing, happy customers who stay — celebrate, scale, and protect your advantages)."

---

### **A Strategic Example of the Ratio (For Decision-Making):**

```
THE SCENARIO: An LTV of R$10,064, a CAC of R$232, a ratio of 43:1 (exceptional)

THE DECISION: How much can we SPEND on CAC (the maximum) before it becomes unprofitable?

THE CALCULATION:
• An LTV of R$10,064 ÷ 3 (the minimum ratio) = a R$3,354 maximum CAC for breakeven
• Currently spending: A R$232 CAC
• The headroom: R$3,354 - R$232 = R$3,122 in additional CAC is possible

THE IMPLICATION:
• You can increase the ad budget by 14x (from the current R$232 → a maximum of R$3,354) and still be 3:1 profitable
• The opportunity: Outbid EVERY competitor (if they are limited to a R$500-1,000 CAC, you can afford R$3,354 — you can win ALL the auctions and dominate the traffic)

THE STRATEGY:
• Phase 1: Increase the budget 2x (from R$232 → a R$464 CAC — to test the headroom, a 21:1 ratio is still excellent)
• Phase 2: If successful, increase it 5x (from R$232 → a R$1,160 CAC — a healthy 8.7:1 ratio)
• Phase 3: An aggressive 10x (from R$232 → a R$2,320 CAC — an acceptable 4.3:1 ratio)
• Monitor: At each phase, to ensure that conversions/retention are stable (that scaling isn't degrading the quality)

The result: 10x more customers (from 115/month → a potential 1,150/month), revenue from R$344k → R$3.44M/month (a 10x scale, while maintaining a 4:1+ ratio and being massively profitable).
```

**The ratio is a strategic ceiling** (it tells you how much you can spend, how much you can scale, and where the limits are).

### **METRIC #4 — THE PAYBACK PERIOD (CAC Recovery Time):**

---

**The definition:**

**The Payback Period = How long it takes to recover the CAC** (the months until the customer's revenue = the CAC invested).

**The formula:**

```
The Payback Period (in months) = CAC ÷ The Monthly Revenue Per Customer

Where:
• CAC: The acquisition cost (already calculated — R$232 in the example)
• The Monthly Revenue/Customer: The LTV ÷ The Lifespan (in months) = R$10,064 ÷ (2.3 years × 12) = R$10,064 ÷ 27.6 = R$365/month on average
```

---

**The calculation:**

```
The CAC: R$232
The Monthly Revenue/Customer: R$365

The Payback Period = R$232 ÷ R$365 = 0.64 months (19 days)

[The CAC is recovered in 19 days — excellent, cash flow becomes positive fast]
```

---

**Payback benchmarks (SaaS Capital, Bessemer):**

```
<3 months = ✅✅ EXCELLENT (cash flow is positive quickly, you can scale self-funded by reinvesting the profits from month 3+)

3-6 months = ✅ GOOD (acceptable, a moderate cash flow, cautious scaling is possible)

6-12 months = ⚠️ SLOW (a tight cash flow for a year, scaling requires external capital OR slow organic growth)

>12 months = ❌ A PROBLEM (a negative cash flow for a year+, VC-dependent OR you can't scale, a vulnerable short runway)
```

**The 19-day payback example:**

✅✅ Excellent (just a few weeks, months 1-27 are pure profit of R$10,064 - R$232 = R$9,832 per customer after the payback, an immediate cash flow positive for scaling, you can reinvest the profits to acquire more in a compounding loop).

---

**The Strategic Implication of the Payback:**

**A fast payback (<3m):** You can scale self-funded (you can reinvest the profits from month 3+ to acquire customers in month 4+, for compounding growth, with zero VC needed).

**A slow payback (>12m):** It requires capital (you'll bleed cash for a year before becoming profitable, growth is capital-dependent, and you'll be vulnerable if the funding dries up).

---

**Hormozi:**

"A payback of <6 months = you can bootstrap your scaling (reinvest the profits, for compounding growth, and maintain 100% of your equity).

A payback of >12 months = you'll need investors (you'll dilute your equity by 20-40%, you'll potentially lose control, and you'll be vulnerable).

Optimize the offer/funnel FIRST (to reduce the payback before you scale — those are the priorities)."
