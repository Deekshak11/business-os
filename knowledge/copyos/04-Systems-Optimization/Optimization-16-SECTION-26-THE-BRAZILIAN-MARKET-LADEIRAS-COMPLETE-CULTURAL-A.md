## **SECTION 2.6 — THE BRAZILIAN MARKET (LADEIRA'S COMPLETE CULTURAL ADAPTATIONS)**

---

**Leandro Ladeira** (VTSD — A Brazil Market Specialist from 2015-2024):

> *"The US frameworks (from Brunson, Hormozi, and Kennedy) are universal in their PRINCIPLES (they apply globally — human psychology is identical).*
>
> *BUT: The EXECUTION in Brazil is not the same as in the US (the culture, the language, the economy, the legal aspects, the timing, the payments — these are critical differences that, if ignored, will lead to the failure of campaigns in Brazil that are using a literal translation of US copy).*
>
> *Translation is not Adaptation (a word-for-word translation is a disaster, an adaptation to the cultural context is success).*
>
> *Examples of failures: US companies from 2018-2020 that entered Brazil (Shopify, ClickFunnels, Kajabi) with a literal translation of their copy — their conversion was 40-60% lower in Brazil vs. the US with the same offers, they were frustrated, and some gave up on the 'difficult' market — it wasn't a difficult market, it was a culturally inadequate COPY; after adapting with a consultation from Ladeira, their conversion normalized to 90-95% of the US benchmarks)."*

### **ADAPTATION #1 — THE TONE & THE LANGUAGE (A Personal Narrative vs. a Direct Transactional):**

---

**A cultural difference in communication:**

**The US (Direct, Transactional, Bullet-Points):**

The copy's style: Factual, bullet-heavy, the benefits are listed efficiently, the emotion is moderate, and the storytelling is secondary (the facts are primary).

An example of the US style:
```
"Get these results:
• Lose 20 lbs
• Increase your energy
• Sleep better
• Feel confident

The price: $497
The guarantee: 60-day
[The CTA: Buy Now]"
```

**Brazil (Narrative, Relational, Storytelling):**

The copy's style: Abundant stories, an emotional connection is a priority, the facts are secondary (they are woven into a narrative), an extensive relationship-building, a rushed transactional approach will meet with cultural resistance.

An adapted example for Brazil:
```
"Imagine: You wake up (with high energy, not fatigue). In the mirror: 20 lbs less (the jeans from your 20s fit again, you feel pride). Your family notices: 'You're different — more willing, more present.' You FEEL confidence (not the shyness of hiding your body).

Maria, 52, from Curitiba: She lived this transformation (a 90-day protocol, and she has maintained it for 22 months — it's real and sustainable, not a yo-yo).

'I thought it was impossible at 52 (I had tried 5 diets and each one had failed) — BUT the protocol worked. Today I feel like I'm 30. My family is happy, and I'm happy.' — Maria.

The complete system: R$1,497 (in 12 interest-free installments of R$144 — it's accessible).

The guarantee: 90 days (a generous amount of time to try it completely, with zero risk).

[The CTA: I Want Maria's Transformation]"
```

**The differences:**
- The US uses bulleted facts (lose 20, energy, sleep — they are listed efficiently)
- Brazil uses a narrative (imagine waking up, in the mirror, your family notices — an immersive and sensorial story from Doc 9, a deep emotional connection)
- The US states the price directly ($497 is stated)
- Brazil states the price + installments (R$1,497 in 12 interest-free installments of R$144 — the cultural accessibility of the payment)
- The US has a transactional CTA ("Buy Now")
- Brazil has a relational CTA ("I Want Maria's Transformation" — an aspiration, an identification with Maria's story, NOT a cold "buy" verb)

---

**THE CASUAL BRAZILIAN LANGUAGE (A Critical Moderation):**

**Acceptable (Moderate, Controlled):**

- "Cara" (occasionally, 1-2x per 1,000w — it's friendly, but not excessive)
- "Mano" (rarely, and specific to younger demographics of 25-35, to be avoided for 40+ audiences)
- "Olha" (common, and acceptable for all ages — "Olha, here is the truth...")
- "Né?" (a confirmation tag — "It makes sense, né?" — it's conversational, BUT not in every sentence)
- "Tá" instead of "está" (casually acceptable in emails, but NOT in formal sales letters)

**To be avoided (Excessive, Unprofessional):**

- "Pô," "Velho," "Brother," "Firmeza" (heavy slang — it alienates the 40+ demographics, and is unprofessional in B2B)
- Profanity ("porra," "caralho" — even a moderate Halbert in the US might use "damn" occasionally, but Brazil has a higher cultural sensitivity, to be avoided unless the brand is VERY casual and for a young 18-30 demographic AND the product is lifestyle-related, not for business/finance/health professionals)
- Excessive Anglicisms ("insight," "feedback," "follow-up" — they are acceptable in tech/business contexts, BUT "Envie um follow-up" = "Envie um acompanhamento" is better for the purity of the Portuguese that is preferred by some older/more traditional audiences)

**Ladeira's balance:**

"A personal and relational tone (a Brazilian cultural expectation — the cold corporate US style is rejected in Brazil as 'distant and not human'), BUT it must maintain its professionalism (not an excessive casualness of 'mano' in every line — that's ridiculous and has zero credibility; a balance of 70% professionally accessible and 30% casual warmth is the sweet spot that has been tested in 2,400 campaigns)."

### **ADAPTATION #2 — LOCALIZED SOCIAL PROOF (Brazilian, with Cities and the R$ Currency):**

---

**US Copy (Generic and International):**

"10,000 customers worldwide"
"$10k/month on average"
"New York, California, Texas"

**The perception in Brazil:** It's distant (it's not me, it's not my country, the numbers in $ are confusing and require a mental conversion, and the American cities have zero identification — it works in the US, it doesn't in Brazil).

---

**Adapted for Brazil (Specific and Localized):**

"8,347 Brazilians (in 18 months)"
"R$30k/month on average (some are R$50k+, with an honest range of R$18-80k)"
"São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, Curitiba, Belo Horizonte, Porto Alegre"

**The perception:** Identification (Brazilians are me, I know the cities OR I'm near them, I understand R$ immediately without calculating a conversion, and an honest range shows a credible variance).

---

**The localization of testimonials:**

❌ "John, 43, an entrepreneur from New York" (foreign, distant)

✅ "João Silva, 43, an entrepreneur from São Paulo, SP" (a Brazilian name, a specific city in Brazil, a total identification)

❌ "$10k/month in revenue" (confusing — is $10k = R$50k? R$60k? it depends on the exchange rate, a mental math friction)

✅ "R$30,000/month in revenue" (the local currency, an immediate comprehension, no mental conversion needed)

---

**Ladeira:**

"The localization of testimonials has a +25-40% in credibility in Brazil (the same testimonial from João in NYC vs. João in SP — the Brazilian one is 1.4x more believable due to the identification with the nationality, the familiarity of the city, and the immediate understanding of the R$ currency vs. the $ which requires a slight but cumulatively significant cognitive load over 20 testimonials)."

### **ADAPTATION #3 — COMPLIANCE IN BRAZIL (The CDC, the LGPD, and Procon — Specific Legal Aspects):**

---

**The CDC (The Consumer Defense Code — Law 8.078/1990):**

```
THE CONSUMER'S RIGHTS IN BRAZIL (Stronger than the US FTC):

Art. 49 — THE RIGHT OF REPENTANCE (A 7-Day Cooling-Off Period):
• For online purchases (at a distance, not in-person): The consumer HAS the right to cancel within 7 days AFTER receipt (for a physical product) OR access (for a digital one), with NO justification needed, and a 100% refund is mandatory.
• The copy's implication: You CANNOT block this right (some US products have "all digital sales are final" — which is ILLEGAL in Brazil, the CDC overrides the company's terms).
• The guarantee must INCLUDE a minimum of 7 days (you can offer MORE — 30d, 60d, 90d — but you can't offer LESS, 7d is the legal floor).

Art. 6 — CLEAR INFORMATION (Transparency is Mandatory):
• The total price: Must be shown (the product's price + any taxes + the shipping if it's physical — all upfront, no surprises at the checkout).
• The installments: If you offer them, you must show the total of the installments (12x R$100 = a R$1,200 total, which must be made clear, you can't hide the total and only show the low installment).
• The essential characteristics: What the product is, what it does, and its limitations — a complete transparency, any omissions are misleading advertising and are subject to a fine.

Art. 39 — ABUSIVE PRACTICES (Are Prohibited):
• Tie-in sales: "I'll only sell you A if you also buy B" (is illegal — they must be offered separately)
• A refusal to sell without justification: You can't have a fake perpetual "sold out" (you must either have a real stock OR not claim scarcity)
• Abusive prices: Dramatic increases without justification (from R$100 → R$500 overnight is an abuse, a gradual increase from R$100 → R$120 is acceptable)

THE PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS:
• Fines: From R$1,000-10,000,000 (the severity depends, and they are astronomical for repeat offenders)
• A civil public action: A class-action from consumers (for collective damages)
• A Procon investigation: Damage to the brand's reputation (a public search for "Company X Procon" is a permanent bad PR)
```

---

**The LGPD (The General Law for the Protection of Data — Brazil's GDPR-Equivalent, from 2020):**

```
THE EMAIL OPT-IN REQUIREMENTS:

A MANDATORY Double Opt-In (A Best Practice, Quasi-Legal):
• Step 1: The subscriber enters their email in the form
• Step 2: A confirmation email is sent ("Confirm your subscription by clicking this link")
• Step 3: The click confirms it (only AFTER the click is the subscriber legal, and the consent is validated)

A single opt-in (which is common in the US): Is risky in Brazil (a strict interpretation of the LGPD by some is that the consent must be "unequivocal" — a double opt-in is the safest way to prove consent).

A LINK to a Privacy Policy:
• On every landing page: There must be a link to a privacy policy (at a minimum, in the footer, and it must be visible and clickable)
• The policy's content: How the data will be used (only for email marketing, it will NEVER be sold to a third-party — for transparency)

An EASY Unsubscribe:
• In every email: An unsubscribe link (with 1-click, NOT a friction-filled "email us a request" — an immediate automatic unsubscribe is mandatory)
• It must be honored immediately (you can't delay it for 5-10 days for "processing" — an instant removal from the list)

THE LGPD'S PENALTIES:
• Fines: 2% of your revenue (with a max of R$50 million per infraction — which is massive for large businesses)
• A suspension: Your database will be blocked (you can't use illegally captured emails — the business's campaigns will be stopped)

LADEIRA: "LGPD compliance is non-negotiable (a double opt-in, a visible privacy policy, a 1-click unsubscribe — set it up correctly once and you'll never have to worry; a violation puts the company at an existential risk of fines + brand damage)."
```

---

**PROCON (The Consumer Defense Agency — An Active Enforcement):**

**Procon's powers:**

- To investigate complaints (consumers report, and Procon investigates — they can subpoena documents, conduct interviews, and do audits)
- To apply fines (for CDC violations — from R$1k-10M, depending)
- To publish (the Procon website lists the fined companies — a public shaming, which Google indexes, a permanent brand damage of "Company X was fined by Procon in 2023" showing up in searches)

**Common Procon violations:**

- A difficult cancellation (a consumer wants to cancel, and the company delays/refuses — Procon will fine them and force an immediate cancellation)
- An improper charge (a charge after a cancellation, OR an unauthorized charge — a mandatory refund + damages)
- Misleading advertising (it promises X but delivers a different Y — a bait-and-switch, Procon will investigate and fine)
- Fake urgencies (a countdown that resets, a "last units" that lasts for 6 perpetual months — consumers will report it, Procon will validate that it's fake, and they will fine + force it to cease)

**Public cases (As Deterrent Examples):**

- Hotmart in 2021: Multiple courses had fake urgencies (a 24h countdown that reset daily — consumers reported it, the São Paulo Procon fined them R$380,000, and forced them to remove all the fake countdowns from ALL their products, a public case that's indexed on Google as "Hotmart Procon fake countdown 2021")
- Eduzz in 2022: They made it difficult to cancel (consumers wanted to cancel their subscriptions, and "technical" site errors were preventing them — Procon investigated, discovered an intentional dark pattern, fined them R$520,000, and forced them to facilitate a 1-click cancellation)

**The lesson:** Urgencies MUST be real (a cohort must close on the stated date of enforcement, prices must genuinely increase after the deadline, and spots must have a real physical sell-out limit), and cancellations MUST be easy (1-click, immediate, with zero friction — compliance protects the brand's long-term survival > the short-term revenue from fake urgencies that are a trap).

### **ADAPTATION #4 — REAL URGENCIES (An Emphasis from Ladeira — For Brand Survival):**

---

**Ladeira (An Absolute Principle of VTSD):**

> *"A fake urgency in Brazil is BRAND SUICIDE (that's not an exaggeration — it's a literal brand death).*
>
> *Why is Brazil more sensitive than the US:*
>
> *• Procon enforces it (the US FTC has rare enforcement, but Brazil's Procon is active with consumer reports, and investigations are common).*
>
> *• A culture of distrust (Brazilians have historically been scammed by the government/companies — a default distrust, a detected fake urgency confirms their suspicion of a 'scam,' and the brand is irreversibly destroyed).*
>
> *• The internet's memory (Google Reviews, the Reclame Aqui site with 40M visitors/month — the complaints are permanently indexed, a search for 'Company X fake countdown' will show up for years, the damage is lasting).*
>
> *The ABSOLUTE rule: Always be real. Never be fake. Zero exceptions.*
>
> *Does a cohort close? The stated date MUST be when it closes (not fake).*
>
> *Does the price go up? It MUST increase on the stated date (not just for marketing).*
>
> *Are the spots limited? They MUST sell-out at a physical limit (not a perpetual fake '5 left').*
>
> *Compliance is survival (it's not an optional legal checkbox, it's an existential protection of the brand)."*

---

**Examples of the enforcement of REAL urgencies (How to Implement Them Correctly):**

```
TYPE #1 URGENCY — A DEADLINE (For a Cohort-Based Program):

A REAL Implementation:
"Enrollment closes on Friday, December 6, 2024, at 11:59 PM BRT.

Why does it close? The cohort starts on Monday, December 9 (it's cohort-based learning, late entries would disrupt the pedagogy of the group that has already started, we physically cannot accept anyone after Friday — the deadline has a REAL logistical reason).

After Friday: The next cohort is in January 2025 (a 6-week wait — it's genuinely the next one, NOT a fake 'it reopens in 1 day')."

THE ENFORCEMENT:
• On Friday at 11:59 PM: The countdown ends, the sales page goes OFFLINE (it's removed, it gives a 404 error, OR it's replaced with a 'Sold Out' page).
• On Monday, Dec 9: The cohort starts (onboarding emails are sent to the students, the calls begin, PROOF that it has started — not a fake delay).
• On Saturday-Sunday post-deadline: ZERO sales are accepted (even emails with "please, an exception" are denied for consistency, the deadline is absolutely honored — this builds trust for future campaigns that "his deadlines are REAL, I can trust them").

The Public VERIFICATION:
• Student's Google Reviews: "The cohort was confirmed to close on Friday, and it started on-time on Monday, the deadline was real" (social proof of the deadline's enforcement).
• The email to subscribers: "The cohort closed on Friday (100/100 spots were sold out), the next one is in January — the wait list is [link]" (a transparent enforcement, not a fake reopening tomorrow).

[A REAL deadline builds trust (consumers will test it — they'll wait until Friday at 11:59 PM, they'll check on Saturday and confirm the site is offline, and in future campaigns they will trust the stated deadline, so the urgency will work because it has been historically validated as REAL)]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

TYPE #4 URGENCY — A PRICE INCREASE (A Real Execution):

A REAL Implementation:
"Today's price: R$1,997 (for the launch).

After Sunday at 11:59 PM: The price will be R$2,997 (a +R$1,000 increase — the launch period is ending, the regular pricing is beginning)."

THE ENFORCEMENT:
• On Monday at 00:00: The site's price is updated to R$2,997 (the checkout will show the new price, NOT R$1,997 anymore).
• No exceptions: Emails on Monday with "can I have the R$1,997 price?" are denied ("the deadline passed on Sunday, the current price is R$2,997, the next discount will be at [a future event]").
• Future validation: The site's history (on Archive.org, with dated screenshots) will show the price WAS R$1,997 in Nov, and R$2,997 from Dec+ (a verifiable transparency, not a fake claim of "normally R$5k" when it was never actually sold for that).

THE VERIFICATION:
• The customers on Monday: Will pay R$2,997 (NOT R$1,997 — the increase is real and enforced).
• Google Reviews: "The price was confirmed to increase on Monday, the Sunday deadline was real, I lost R$1,000 by hesitating" (social proof of the enforcement, the regret validates that the urgency was real NOT empty marketing).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

TYPE #2 URGENCY — SCARCITY (Real Spots with a Physical Limit):

A REAL Implementation:
"A maximum of 100 students in the cohort (a quality limit — bi-weekly calls with 20-40 people are manageable, beyond 100 makes individual attention impossible, the pedagogy would suffer).

The spots: 73 are filled (updated in real-time as sales happen), with 27 REMAINING."

THE ENFORCEMENT:
• Real-time updates: As sales happen, the "27 remaining" → "26 remaining" → ... → "0 remaining, SOLD OUT" (it's a live tracking, not a fake static number).
• A physical limit: When 100 are sold, sales STOP (the page goes offline, OR it becomes a "sold out" page, with zero exceptions — even emails begging "please, the 101st?" are denied, the limit is honored).
• The next cohort: Is genuinely the next event (6-12 weeks later — NOT a fake "sold out today, reopens tomorrow" scam).

THE VERIFICATION:
• The student count: Is exactly 100 (on the bi-weekly calls, the attendance roster will have 100 names, NOT 150 from a "sold out at 100 BUT we accepted 50 more anyway" — the integrity of the stated limit).

[A REAL scarcity builds trust (consumers will test it by buying one of the "last 3 spots" on a Friday, and on Monday they will check and confirm it's sold out; in future campaigns, the scarcity will be believed because it has been historically enforced)]

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

A FAKE URGENCY (Examples to AVOID — Brand Suicide):

❌ A RESETTING COUNTDOWN:
"The offer expires at 11:59 PM" (a ticking countdown timer).
The consumer: Waits until 00:00 (to test it).
At 00:01: The countdown RESETS to 23:59 again (the fake is exposed).
The consumer: "It's a SCAM. A fake urgency. I will never buy from you." (the trust is permanently destroyed, a 1-star Google Review of "a fake countdown from a dishonest company," the brand damage is irreversible).

❌ A PERPETUAL "LAST UNITS":
"Only 5 units left" (from 6 months ago).
The consumer checks today: It's still "5 left" (impossible — did they NOT sell any in 6 months? OR did they restock daily with a fake scarcity? Both are dishonest).
The consumer: Reports it to Procon (who will investigate, and fine if it's confirmed to be fake).

❌ A NON-ENFORCED DEADLINE:
"The deadline is on Sunday" (as stated).
On Monday: Sales CONTINUE at the same price (the deadline was ignored).
The consumer: "The deadline was fake, I won't trust their future deadlines, I will ignore all of their urgencies" (the boy-who-cried-wolf, the power of urgency is permanently lost).

LADEIRA'S ABSOLUTE:
"A fake urgency in Brazil will ALWAYS be detected (consumers will test it, they will report it to Procon, they will publically shame you on Google Reviews) = the brand will die (that's NOT hyperbole — it's a literal death, with zero trust, a collapse in conversions, and an impossible recovery from a reputation destroyed in the digital age).

ONLY real urgencies (cohorts that genuinely close, prices that genuinely increase, spots that are genuinely limited for physical reasons) = will lead to long-term survival, and a short-term sacrifice (a smaller moderate urgency vs. a fake extreme one) = a long-term gain (of trust, of reputation, and of a sustainable business for decades vs. a quick cash grab for 6 months and then the death of the brand)."
```

### **ADAPTATION #5 — PAYMENT METHODS (Card + Boleto + Pix — Accessibility is Critical):**

---

**The payment landscape in Brazil (Different from the 95% Card usage in the US):**

```
THE PAYMENT PREFERENCES IN BRAZIL (From Ladeira's Data of 2,400 Campaigns):

THE CREDIT CARD: 60% prefer it (it's dominant, BUT NOT exclusively)
• Installments are CRITICAL: 12x interest-free is a cultural expectation (a R$1,200 total, in 12x R$100/month installments — it's psychologically easier to pay R$100/month vs. R$1,200 upfront, even if the total is identical with zero interest)
• Without installments: A -40% in conversion (an offer of R$1,200 with only a full payment option means the 60% who would buy with installments can't/won't pay in full, they are lost customers)
• The impact of installments: A R$1,200 full-payment converts at 5%, but a 12x R$100 installment converts at 8.3% (a +66% in conversion with installments vs. full payment — a massive difference from the payment flexibility alone)

THE BOLETO BANCÁRIO: 25% prefer it (especially the older 45-65 demographic, the lower-income, and the 15% of the population who are unbanked)
• What it is: A printed ticket (with a barcode), that can be paid at any bank/lottery/pharmacy, with a 1-3 day processing time (it's slower than an instant card payment, BUT it's been a trusted cultural payment method for 30 years, and some prefer a tangible piece of paper)
• Without a boleto: A -25% in potential customers (who can't/won't use a card — due to credit issues, privacy concerns, or a cultural preference in the older demographics)
• Adding a boleto: A +25% in conversions from the accessibility (offering the option is inclusive, it's a conversion boost)

PIX: 15% prefer it (it's been growing rapidly since 2020+ — it's instant, free, the young 18-35 demographic has adopted it en masse, and the older demographic is slowly adopting it)
• What it is: An instant transfer (from bank-to-bank, with a QR code scan, the payment completes in 10 seconds, it's free with zero fees, it was launched by the Central Bank in 2020, and has been adopted by 70% of the population in 2024)
• Without Pix: A -15% from the young demographics (who prefer an instant and free option, a card has merchant fees OR the temptation of debt with installments which they want to avoid, and Pix is a responsible instant payment)
• Adding Pix: A +15% in conversions from the accessibility for the young

THE TOTAL IMPACT OF OFFERING ALL 3:
• Card only: A 100% baseline conversion
• Card + Boleto: 125% (a +25% from the accessibility)
• Card + Boleto + Pix: 165% (a +65% for ALL 3 vs. card only)

THE COST OF ADDING THE METHODS:
• Boleto: The gateway fees are 2-4% of the transaction (with Mercado Pago, PagSeguro — an acceptable cost of R$24-48 per R$1,200 sale = a 96-98% margin that is still fat for a digital product)
• Pix: The gateway fees are 0.5-1.5% (the cheapest — it's instant, with an extremely low cost of R$6-18 per R$1,200 sale)

THE ROI: A +65% in conversions (from offering all 3) vs. an average blended cost of +1-3% in fees = a 20:1+ ROI (it's obvious to always offer all 3 in Brazil).
```

**Ladeira:**

"The payment methods in Brazil are NON-NEGOTIABLE (a trio of card installments + boleto + Pix is essential, offering only a card is literally leaving 40% of your customers on the table who can't/won't pay with a card only, accessibility is conversion, and the 1-3% cost in fees is trivial vs. the massive +65% gain in conversions — always offer all 3)."

### **ADAPTATION #6 — THE TIMING OF EMAILS (The 9-11 am BRT Sweet Spot — From Ladeira's Data):**

---

**(Already developed in Doc 10 Section 4.1 — a recap applied here in an optimization context)**

---

```
THE SEND TIME IN BRAZIL vs. THE US (Cultural Differences):

THE US OPTIMAL TIME: 6-8 am ET (they wake up at 5-6 am, their commute is from 6-8 am, they check their emails on the train/in the car, and they are in the office from 8-9 am)
THE OPENS: 28-35% (on the East Coast, and in the Midwest — the majority of the population)

THE BRAZIL OPTIMAL TIME: 9-11 am BRT (they wake up later than in the US, from 6:30-7:30 am, they have a leisurely coffee from 8-9 am, they start work at 9-10 am, and they check their emails mid-morning from 9-11 am)
THE OPENS: 35-42% (from Ladeira's tracking of 10,000+ emails — the peak window)

THE DIFFERENCE: A 2-3h shift (Brazil is later than the US — the cultural rhythm is different, the wake-up times, the commute patterns, and the work start times are all shifted).

TO BE AVOIDED IN BRAZIL:
• 6-8 am BRT: 14-22% opens (it's too early, it will be buried in their inbox by the time they check later at 9 am)
• 12-2 pm: 16-20% opens (lunchtime — the lowest window, they are eating not checking their emails)
• 6-8 pm: 14-18% opens (dinnertime with the family — avoid interrupting)

THE WEEKEND:
• Saturday: 20-24% opens (lower than a weekday's 35-42%, BUT some do check, it's acceptable to send educational content NOT a pitch)
• Sunday: 12-18% opens (the lowest — family, rest, church, to be avoided unless it's critical)

THE RECOMMENDATION: The default send time is 09:30 AM BRT (in the middle of the 9-11 am peak window, which is timezone-safe for the majority of subscribers in São Paulo/Rio/Minas which are UTC-3).
```

**The optimization:**

A US copy sent at 6 am ET in Brazil will have a 14% open rate (the timing is culturally wrong).

The same copy sent at 9:30 am BRT will have a 38% open rate (a +24 point, 2.7x improvement from the TIMING alone, with zero changes to the copy).

**The ROI of the timing:** An investment of R$0 (just a change in the send time setting of the platform), with a return of +171% in opens = an infinite ROI (optimizing the timing is free, and the impact is massive).
