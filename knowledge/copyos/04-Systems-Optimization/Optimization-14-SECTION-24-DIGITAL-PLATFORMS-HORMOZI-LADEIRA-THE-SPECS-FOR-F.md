## **SECTION 2.4 — DIGITAL PLATFORMS (HORMOZI + LADEIRA — THE SPECS FOR FB, YOUTUBE, AND TIKTOK)**

### **FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM ADS (The GOATED Framework — From Hormozi's $100M Leads):**

**The Creative's Structure (15-90 Seconds):**

```
GOATED = Hook + Retain + Reward + CTA (a 4-part framework from Doc 5 Section 2.C.2)

THE HOOK (The First 1-3 Seconds — CRITICAL):

The objective: To STOP the scroll (a pattern interrupt, System 1 captures their attention immediately before they scroll past).

The hook techniques:
• A direct call-out: "Hey [avatar]!" (face-to-camera, with eye contact) — "Entrepreneurs over 40, listen up!"
• A shocking statement: "89% of online businesses FAIL in their first year" (a shocking stat, a fear trigger)
• A visual pattern interrupt: Something unexpected (a person dropping their phone, money burning, an unexpected action)
• A provocative question: "Are you losing R$50k/year WITHOUT KNOWING IT?" (a self-check, for curiosity)

THE TIMING: The hook MUST capture their attention in 1-3 sec (the scroll velocity of a Facebook feed is an average of 0.5-2 sec per post, the window is tiny, the hook must be instant OR it's lost forever).

THE METRICS: The thumb-stop rate (the % of scrollers who stop — a strong hook is 15-30%, a weak hook is 2-8%, a 3-15x difference from the hook's quality alone).

THE CREATIVE'S SPECS:
• The format: A 1:1 square (for the feed) OR a 4:5 or 9:16 vertical (for stories/reels — for mobile optimization)
• The captions: ALWAYS ON (85% are viewed muted — a large, readable text overlay, captions embedded in the video, accessibility is critical)
• Face-to-camera: A +23% in engagement (from Hormozi's data — the human face gets System 1's attention automatically vs. b-roll footage which is ignored)
• The branding: ZERO in the first 3 sec (a logo is a skip trigger — the brain thinks "ah, an ad" → it scrolls, delay the branding until sec 8-10 after the hook has worked)

EXAMPLES OF HOOKS (3 Types):

Hook #1 (A Direct Call-Out):
[THE VISUAL: A person's face-to-camera, a close-up, a serious expression]
[THE AUDIO: "Digital entrepreneurs making R$30-50k/month, stop!"]
[THE TEXT OVERLAY: "YOU — Yes, YOU 👈"]
[The duration: 2 seconds — it's direct, with eye contact, the text emphasizes it, the scroll stops]

Hook #2 (A Shocking Stat):
[THE VISUAL: Large numbers animating on the screen]
[THE TEXT OVERLAY: "89% of R$30-50k Online Businesses NEVER Surpass R$100k/Month"]
[THE AUDIO: A voice-over saying "Why?"]
[The duration: 3 seconds — a shocking stat of 89% stuck, "why?" opens a curiosity gap]

Hook #3 (A Provocative Question):
[THE VISUAL: A person with a confused expression, their hands gesturing "what?"]
[THE TEXT OVERLAY: "Are You Losing R$2,000/Day WITHOUT KNOWING IT?"]
[THE AUDIO: The person speaks "Seriously — R$2k is gone daily, invisibly"]
[The duration: 3 seconds — a loss framing of a massive R$2k/day, "without knowing it" means a revelation is coming, for intrigue]

[Each hook: A pattern interrupt (a combination of visual, audio, and text), a precise 1-3 sec timing, with the ONLY objective being to stop the scroll — not to sell yet, just to CAPTURE]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

RETAIN (Seconds 3-15 — To Keep Them Watching):

The objective: The viewer has stopped (the hook worked), now you have to KEEP them watching (so they don't scroll after 3 sec — sustain their interest for an additional 12 sec, to bridge the hook to the reward).

The retain techniques:
• A promise of value: "In the next 30 seconds, I'm going to reveal why you're stuck and how to fix it" (a tease of a revelation, that it's worth watching)
• A story setup: "3 months ago, João was exactly where you are..." (a narrative hook, for identification)
• Social proof: "12,000 people have discovered this, and they've transformed..." (a bandwagon, for FOMO)
• A brief agitation: "Every day you don't know this, you're losing R$2k (a week is R$14k, a month is R$60k gone)" — to amplify the pain, for the urgency to keep watching to resolve it

THE TIMING: Seconds 3-15 (a 12-second retention — a critical window, 60% of viewers decide to continue OR exit here, the retain techniques are the difference between a 60% and a 20% completion).

THE SPECS:
• The text overlay continues (the captions are rolling, and are readable without audio)
• B-roll footage (cut from the talking head, show related things — dashboard screenshots, lifestyle clips, the product in use — a visual variety maintains attention)
• The music: A subtle background (at 10-20% volume, not distracting, to set the mood — an uptempo for urgency, a downtempo for calm)

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

REWARD (Seconds 15-25 — To Deliver Value and Build Credibility):

The objective: The viewer is watching at 15 sec (they've been retained), now you REWARD them (deliver the value you promised in the hook, earn credibility, and begin reciprocity — you've given something for free before you ask).

The reward techniques:
• A quick actionable tip: "Here's a technique you can apply today: [a 10-sec teaching of 1 framework]" (standalone value, the viewer leaves satisfied EVEN if they don't click the link)
• An insight reveal: "The reason 89% are stuck? The 3 missing pillars (the offer+the funnel+the traffic are isolated, they need to be INTEGRATED)" (an actionable diagnosis, a self-check is possible)
• A framework preview: "The 3-component system works together like this: [a high-level explanation in 10 sec]" (genuine education, not just a tease)

THE VALUE: A R$200-500 equivalent (15 min of free consulting advice — reciprocity is established, the viewer thinks "they gave me free value, they're generous and trustworthy").

THE TIMING: Seconds 15-25 (a 10-second reward — sufficient to deliver value, but insufficient to be complete (a gap is maintained to drive the CTA click to learn MORE and get the complete thing), a critical balance).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

THE CTA (Seconds 25-30 — To Ask for a Low-Friction Click):

The objective: Value has been delivered (credibility + reciprocity have been established), now you ASK for an action (a click on a link to learn more and get the complete thing, OR to opt-in, OR to register — it's low-barrier, NOT a high-friction "buy").

The CTA Formula:
"Do this [specific ACTION] to get this [complete BENEFIT] ([with this emphasized EASE] — with this [optional URGENCY])"

EXAMPLES OF A CTA:

CTA #1 (For a Lead Magnet):
[THE VISUAL: A button animates on the screen "DOWNLOAD THE FREE GUIDE"]
[THE AUDIO: "Click the link in the bio to download the complete 127-page guide — with 50+ templates, the 4 U's system, all for FREE. Link in bio. Download now."]
[THE TEXT OVERLAY: "👆 LINK IN BIO 👆 A FREE 127p Guide"]
[The duration: 5 seconds — the action is clear "click the link in the bio," the benefit is "a 127p guide with 50+ templates," the ease is "it's free and instant," and the soft urgency is "now"]

CTA #2 (For a Webinar Registration):
[THE VISUAL: A screenshot of the webinar's landing page]
[THE AUDIO: "Register for the free webinar on Tuesday at 8 pm — the 3 secrets to scaling to R$100k will be revealed live. The seats are limited to 100, and 34 are left. The link is in the comments."]
[THE TEXT: "A FREE Webinar on Tuesday at 8 pm — The Link is in the Comments 👇"]
[The duration: 5 seconds — the action is "register," the benefit is "the 3 secrets to R$100k," the scarcity is "34 are left," the urgency is "Tuesday," the ease is "it's free," and the location is "the link in the comments"]

THE PLACEMENT OF THE CTA:
• In the bio (for Instagram — you can't hyperlink in the caption, the bio link is the only one)
• In the first comment (for Facebook — it's clickable, the algorithm doesn't suppress it as hard as caption links)
• A swipe-up (for Stories — a direct link, the lowest friction)
• The Learn More button (for a native Facebook ad — it's automatic, the FB pixel tracking is integrated)

THE FRICTION: Should be minimal (a maximum of 1-2 clicks — from the link in the bio → to the landing page opt-in email → done, NOT a link → to a page → to a 10-field form → to a confirmation email click → to finally getting access = friction that kills 80% of conversions).

```

**A COMPLETE FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM AD EXAMPLE (A 30-Second Script):**

```
[THE DURATION: 30 seconds total — the GOATED structure]

[00-03 sec — THE HOOK]:
THE VISUAL: An entrepreneur face-to-camera, an office in the background, a serious expression
THE AUDIO: "Entrepreneurs at R$30-50k/month — STOP."
THE TEXT: "YOU (Yes, You 👈) Are You Stuck at R$30-50k?"
[To stop the scroll — a direct call-out, with eye contact, the text emphasizes it, for identification]

[03-08 sec — TO RETAIN]:
THE VISUAL: A cut to a dashboard screenshot of a R$28k revenue (stuck), a flatline graph for 18 months
THE AUDIO: "Have you been stuck for 18 months even though you're working 60-80h? You've tried ads, hiring, automation — and each one has failed?"
THE TEXT: "Stuck for 18m? 😤 Tried Everything? 😓"
[A deep identification — the situation is described exactly for the avatar, the pain is validated, a story is set up]

[08-18 sec — TO RETAIN (continued) + THE REWARD (begins)]:
THE VISUAL: A cut back to the face, explaining with hand gestures
THE AUDIO: "The reason you're stuck? The 3 missing pillars (a weak offer that converts at 2%, an absent funnel with a R$800 LTV, inconsistent single-source traffic). You need INTEGRATION (a 10×3×5=150x synergy, not an isolated partial 1×1×1=1). A system that 47 companies have used to scale an average of 4x in 9 months."
THE TEXT: "An Integration of the 3 Pillars = 150x 📈"
[The problem is diagnosed — the 3 pillars, the integration thesis, the social proof of 47 companies, and a 4x result — a free educational value is delivered, credibility is built]

[18-23 sec — THE REWARD (complete)]:
THE VISUAL: A quick flash of a testimonial (João's face, with a text overlay of "R$28k→R$112k in 9m")
THE AUDIO: "João, from an e-commerce in SP: Went from a stuck R$28k → R$112k in nine months with integration (the complete video is in the link)."
THE TEXT: "João: 4x Growth in 9 Months ✅"
[A specific testimonial with social proof, the result is in numbers, the timeframe is realistic, the video is available for verification]

[23-30 sec — THE CTA]:
THE VISUAL: A button animation of "WATCH THE FREE VIDEO" + an arrow pointing
THE AUDIO: "Do you want the complete 3 pillars for FREE? A 12-min video with a self-check diagnosis to see which pillar YOU are missing — the link is in the comments, watch it now, identify your gaps, it's actionable today."
THE TEXT: "👇 LINK IN THE COMMENTS 👇 A FREE 12-min Self-Check Video"
[A clear CTA — "the link in the comments," the benefit is "a 12-min self-check diagnosis," the ease is "it's free," it's actionable "today," with a soft urgency of "now"]

[THE END: Exactly 30 seconds — the GOATED is complete, the 3-sec hook has retained 60-75%, a genuine value was delivered in the reward, a soft CTA to click a link not to buy, minimal friction, and it's compliant with Facebook's approval for being educational not purely sales]
```

**THE EXPECTED METRICS (For a 30-sec GOATED Facebook Ad):**

```
The campaign: A R$5,000 budget (for a test)

Impressions: 250,000 (an average R$20 CPM in Brazil)
Thumb-stops (watched for 3+ sec): 50,000 (20% — a strong hook stopped the scroll)
Completions (watched for 30 sec): 22,500 (45% of thumb-stops → completed — the retain worked)
Clicks (the link in the comments): 2,250 (10% of completers → clicked — the CTA was effective)
Landing page visits: 2,025 (90% of clicks → landed — the link worked)
Opt-ins (on the video's landing page): 607 (30% of those who landed → opted-in with their email — the offer was compelling)

THE COST ANALYSIS:
• The CPM: R$20 (for the impressions)
• The cost per 3-sec view: R$0.10 (R$5,000 ÷ 50,000)
• The cost per completion: R$0.22 (÷ 22,500)
• The CPC (for a click): R$2.22 (÷ 2,250 — which is cheap)
• The cost per opt-in: R$8.24 (÷ 607 — an excellent lead gen)

THE BENCHMARK: A lead gen on Facebook in Brazil is R$15-40 per opt-in (from Ladeira), and R$8.24 is 2-5x better (with a GOATED creative + a compelling offer + an optimized funnel).

THE NEXT STEP: To nurture the 607 opt-ins (with the email sequence from Doc 10 — to convert them → to R$1,997 customers, with a target of 8-15% = 48-91 customers, for a R$96k-182k revenue, and an ROI of a R$5k ad spend → a R$96-182k revenue = 19-36x on the first campaign).
```

### **YOUTUBE ADS (Pre-Roll Strategies):**

---

**YouTube is different from Facebook** (the viewing context is different — YouTube is for intentional video watching, Facebook is for casual scrolling of the feed).

---

**FORMAT A — 6-SECOND NON-SKIPPABLE (Bumper Ads):**

**The structure:**

```
[00-02 sec]: The brand + a visual hook (the logo + a benefit statement)
[02-04 sec]: A tease of the offer ("A free copywriting training...")
[04-06 sec]: The CTA ("The link is in the description")

TOTAL: 6 sec (a forced view, they can't skip it — a captive audience, BUT there's a risk of irritation if it's too salesy, it must be value-forward not pushy)
```

**Best for:** Brand awareness (for reminder campaigns), for retargeting (they already know you, 6 sec is enough for a reminder), for simple offers (a quick pitch for a free lead magnet).

**The metrics:** A 100% view rate (it's a non-skip), and a 0.5-2% CTR (low — 6 sec is insufficient to persuade, it only plants a seed).

---

**FORMAT B — 30-SECOND SKIPPABLE (TrueView):**

**The structure:**

```
[00-05 sec — THE HOOK, before the Skip Button]:
The skip button appears at sec 5 (a YouTube spec — you must hook them BEFORE the skip is available OR 90% will skip instantly when the button appears).

CRITICAL: The first 5 sec are for maximum capture (after sec 5, the viewer CAN skip, and 70-85% will skip if the hook is weak).

The hook techniques:
• A massive stated benefit: "Want R$10-30k/month in copywriting? Watch this..."
• An intriguing question: "Do you know the secret of a headline that doubles sales? Here it is..."
• A visual pattern interrupt: Something unexpected (an extreme close-up of a person's face, giant text on the screen, a sudden action)

[05-15 sec — TO RETAIN (The Viewer Didn't Skip — Engage Them)]:
A tease of the value: "The next 25 seconds are worth R$500 in consulting — yours for free, watch..."
A micro-story: "Maria, 52, with zero experience → R$12k/month in 90 days. How? [an explanation in 10 sec]..."

[15-25 sec — THE REWARD (To Deliver a Quick Value)]:
Teach something: "The 4 U's headline formula: Useful, Urgent, Unique, and Ultra-Specific — a score of 30+ converts 3x better than weak headlines. [An example in 5 sec]"
Standalone value: The viewer leaves educated EVEN if they don't click (for reciprocity, a positive brand impression)

[25-30 sec — THE CTA]:
"The full training is free: [the URL is spoken clearly] OR click the link below the video. It's a complete 47 min, with templates included, at zero cost."
A soft CTA: "Learn more," not "buy," with an emphasis on it being free, and on the ease (a simple click on the link).

[THE END: 30 sec — the viewers who watched the full ad (15-40% is typical for a skippable) are highly engaged, the CTA click rate is 8-20% for those watchers, a warm qualified traffic]
```

**The metrics for a 30-sec skippable:**

The view rate (impressions → 30-sec completes): 15-40% (it's dependent on the hook — a strong one is 30-40%, a weak one is 15-25%).

The CTR (viewers → clicks): 5-15% (for engaged viewers, with a relevant and low-friction CTA).

**Best for:** Lead generation (a free webinar, a guide, a training), for retargeting (warm audiences will tolerate 30 sec), for educational products (a sample of the value is demonstrated in the 30 sec).

---

**FORMAT C — LONG-FORM (2-3 Min Discovery Ads):**

**(Rare, BUT powerful for specific niches — health, finance, complex B2B)**

**The structure:** A mini-VSL (a 5-sec hook, a 20-sec problem, a 30-sec solution tease, a 20-sec proof, a 10-sec CTA — a total of 90-180 sec).

**The metrics:** A 5-15% view rate (low — it's lengthy, BUT the viewers are EXTREMELY qualified, with a 15-30% CTR from those viewers, and an 8-20% conversion from the clickers — quality > quantity).

**Best for:** High-ticket (viewers who invest 2-3 min are signaling a serious interest), complex products (that require an extended explanation).

---

### **TIKTOK (9-15 Seconds — For a UGC Lo-Fi Authenticity):**

---

**TikTok is different from ALL other platforms** (the culture is predominantly younger, 18-35, authenticity > polish, trends dominate, and the algorithm favors native content NOT obvious ads).

---

**The Creative Specs for TikTok:**

```
THE LENGTH: 9-15 seconds (ultra-short — the average attention span on TikTok is 8 sec, a long-form 30 sec+ will be skipped)

THE FORMAT: A 9:16 vertical (for a full-screen mobile, a horizontal one will be letterboxed and ignored)

THE STYLE: UGC (User-Generated Content — a lo-fi, phone-recorded aesthetic, NOT a polished corporate production)

Why UGC: The TikTok algorithm PUNISHES polished ads (users skip them, the engagement is low, the algorithm buries them), and it REWARDS native-looking content (it appears to be organic in the feed, users engage with it, the algorithm promotes it).

Authenticity > production value (the opposite of YouTube/Facebook — the TikTok culture values what's REAL > what's professional).

THE HOOK: 1 second (not 3 — the TikTok scroll is faster, the hook must be INSTANT or it's lost)

The TikTok hook techniques:
• A LARGE text overlay: "If You're Stuck at R$30k/Month, Watch This 👀" (it appears in frame 1, it's instantly readable, for curiosity)
• An extreme close-up of a face: An immediate eye contact (System 1's attention to a human face)
• A sudden action: A jump cut, a fast movement, a visual surprise (to physically stop the scroll)
• A trending sound: Use a trending audio (the algorithm favors it, users will recognize it = a boost in engagement)

CAPTIONS: Are essential (99% of TikTok is muted by default — a text overlay on every frame, a large 48-72pt font, a high contrast of white text with a black outline, for 100% mobile readability)

TREND-JACKING: Is critical (TikTok is a trends platform — for sounds, formats, and viral memes, you can hijack trends and apply them to your niche):

An example of a trend: "Tell me [X] without telling me [X]" (a viral format)

Your adaptation: "Tell me you're stuck at R$30k without telling me you're stuck at R$30k" → [Show relatable scenarios: working at midnight, drinking coffee 6x/day, a flatline revenue dashboard, being absent from your family] → [The text: "The 3 Pillars System will fix it — the link is in the bio"]

A trend's format + your message = a native feel (the algorithm will promote it, users will engage with it because the format is familiar, and your message will deliver YOUR conversion).

EXAMPLES FOR TIKTOK (2 Scripts of 9-12 sec):

Script #1 (A Problem-Awareness Hook):
[00-01 sec]: A LARGE text overlay appears "Stuck at R$30k? 👀"
[01-04 sec]: A close-up of a face, a person speaks "Are you working 60h, with the same revenue, and you're frustrated AF?"
[04-08 sec]: A cut to a dashboard screenshot (a R$28k revenue with an 18-month flatline graph), with a text overlay of "18 Months STUCK 😤"
[08-12 sec]: The face is back, "You're missing the 3 pillars. The link in the bio is a FREE 12-min diagnosis. Identify which one YOU are missing. Go." The text is: "👆 LINK IN BIO 👆 A FREE 12-min diagnosis"
[THE END: 12 sec total — a 1-sec text hook, a 3-sec retention for identification, the problem is shown for 4 sec visually, a clear 4-sec action CTA, a lo-fi UGC feel of a phone recording, a frustrated and relatable authentic tone, a native TikTok aesthetic]

Script #2 (A Trend-Jack):
[A trending audio: The viral "Oh No" song from TikTok]
[00-02 sec]: The text "POV: You're checking your revenue dashboard..." (POV is a TikTok trend format)
[02-05 sec]: A screen-recording of a phone (the dashboard app is opening, loading, and the number reveals R$29,347)
[05-08 sec]: A reaction from a face (a disappointed sigh), with a text overlay of "The Same as Last Month 😔 Stuck"
[08-12 sec]: The text: "An Integration of the 3 Pillars Will Fix It (A FREE Diagnosis is in the Link in the Bio)" + an arrow pointing up
[THE END: 12 sec — a trending audio is hijacked, the POV format is native to TikTok, a relatable stuck revenue, a solution tease with a link in the bio, a 100% phone-recorded UGC aesthetic, the algorithm favors it as native not an obvious ad]

THE METRICS FOR TIKTOK:
• The completion rate (for 9-15 sec): 60-80% (it's short enough, and the native feel retains them)
• The engagement rate (for likes, comments, and shares): 3-8% (high — native content gets organic engagement, ads are typically 0.5-2%)
• The CTR (for clicks on the link in the bio): 1-4% (viewers → clicks — a low absolute, BUT the volume compensates on TikTok's massive scale)

BEST FOR: Younger demographics (18-35), B2C impulse products, brand awareness (the potential for virality), NOT for B2B OR high-ticket (the TikTok culture is for casual entertainment, for serious B2B, LinkedIn is better).
```
