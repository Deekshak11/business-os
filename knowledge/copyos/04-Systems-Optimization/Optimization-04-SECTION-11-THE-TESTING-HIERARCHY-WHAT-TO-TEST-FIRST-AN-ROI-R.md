## **SECTION 1.1 — THE TESTING HIERARCHY (WHAT TO TEST FIRST — AN ROI RANKING)**

---

**A consensus principle of ALL the masters** (Hopkins, Ogilvy, Halbert, Brown, Agora):

> *"Test the big levers first. Polish the small details later.*
>
> *The ROI of testing the offer is 10-100x that of testing the copy.*
>
> *The ROI of testing the headline is 5-20x that of testing the bullets.*
>
> *Testing priority = ROI priority (to maximize the impact per hour invested)."*

---

### **THE TESTING HIERARCHY (8 TIERS — In Order of Priority):**

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE TESTING PRIORITY HIERARCHY (An ROI Ranking)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

TIER 1 — MEGA-IMPACT (50:1 to 500:1 ROI)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

PRIORITY #1 — THE OFFER (The Biggest Lever — Halbert's "50% of Success"):

What to test:
• Price points (R$497 vs. R$997 vs. R$1,997)
• Bonuses (which to include, how many, the order of the value stack)
• The guarantee (30d vs. 90d vs. results-based vs. a penalty)
• Payment terms (full vs. 3x installments vs. 12x)
• The stack presentation (the order of the reveal, the itemized value)

The impact variance: 300-500% (a weak offer = 1% conversion, a strong offer = 5-15% — a 5-15x difference from the OFFER ALONE).

The testing cost: $0-500 (a 10-person survey for Joyner's test, or an A/B test with a small sample of 100 per variation).

The testing ROI: 100:1 to 500:1 (a $500 test prevents a $50k-250k waste from scaling the wrong offer).

Halbert validated: "The Offer is always > the Copy. A mediocre offer + brilliant copy = failure. A brilliant offer + mediocre copy = success."

Time invested: 4-8h (to engineer offer variations from Doc 4, test, and analyze).

Frequency: Quarterly (for big offer changes — not weekly, as it's disruptive).

---

PRIORITY #2 — THE BIG IDEA / POSITIONING (The Angle — Schwartz/Masterson):

What to test:
• The Big Idea type (Discovery vs. Conspiracy vs. Mistake vs. Twist vs. Shortcut — Doc 3 Section 1.2)
• The mechanism's positioning (which mechanism to emphasize — if the product has multiple)
• The identification of the enemy (Us vs. Them — which enemy resonates more with the avatar)

The impact variance: 200-400% (a compelling Big Idea = 8% conversion, a meh Big Idea = 2% — a 4x difference).

The testing cost: $500-2,000 (small campaigns with different Big Ideas, to measure engagement + conversion).

The ROI: 50:1 to 200:1 (a $2k test prevents a $100k-400k loss from scaling the wrong angle).

Schwartz: "Different positioning = a different product perception (even for the same identical physical product, the angle changes everything)."

Time: 8-15h (to create 3-5 Big Ideas with the Doc 3 process, write copy variations for each, and test them small).

Frequency: Annually OR for major launches (the Big Idea is foundational, it doesn't change frequently).

---

PRIORITY #3 — THE HEADLINE (Ogilvy's "80 Cents of Your Dollar"):

What to test:
• Headline variations (10-20 generated with the Doc 6 formulas, test the top 3-5)
• The formula types (How-To vs. Question vs. Secret vs. Number — which one resonates)
• The level of specificity (vague vs. ultra-specific numbers)
• The balance of the 4 U's (emphasize Urgent vs. Unique vs. Useful — what the avatar prioritizes)

The impact variance: 200-500% (a strong headline = a 5% ad CTR, a weak headline = a 1% CTR — a 5x difference, validated by Ogilvy's research).

The testing cost: $200-1,000 (A/B testing headlines with a small ad budget, or a landing page split with 500-1,000 visitors for each).

The ROI: 20:1 to 100:1 (a $1k test prevents $20k-100k in traffic wasted on a weak headline).

Caples: "I tested 100 headlines — the top performer got 19.5x the response of the worst performer (for the same product, with the same body copy, ONLY the headline was different — a staggering variance)."

Time: 2-4h (to generate 10-20 with Doc 6, score them with the 4 U's, select the top 5, and set up the test).

Frequency: Weekly-monthly (headlines are high-leverage, test variations continuously).

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

TIER 2 — HIGH-IMPACT (10:1 to 50:1 ROI)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

PRIORITY #4 — THE LEAD TYPE (Masterson's 6 Types — Doc 6 Section 4):

What to test:
• The lead types (Offer vs. Promise vs. Problem-Solution vs. Story vs. Secret)
• Variations of the opening hooks (the first 100 words)
• The State-matching (a lead type that is appropriate for the Schwartz State — from Doc 2)

The impact: 100-300% (a compelling lead = 60% read the body, a boring lead = 20% read — a 3x difference in engagement).

The testing: A small sample (100-200 reads for each variation, to measure scroll depth and time-on-page).

The ROI: 10:1 to 50:1

Time: 3-5h (to write 2-3 different lead types with the Doc 6 templates, and test them).

Frequency: Quarterly (the lead is a foundational opening, it doesn't change constantly).

---

PRIORITY #5 — THE BULLETS (Bencivenga/Makepeace — Doc 7):

What to test:
• The mix of bullet types (the ratio of Specific Mechanism vs. Conspiracy vs. Warning)
• The quantity (15 vs. 20 vs. 25 bullets)
• The order (the strongest first vs. a crescendo)
• The intensity of the fascination (moderate vs. extreme curiosity)

The impact: 50-150% (compelling bullets = 12% conversion, boring bullets = 5% — 2.4x).

The testing: A/B testing the bullet sections (the same offer, different bullets).

The ROI: 15:1 to 40:1

Time: 2-3h (to rewrite bullet section variations with the Doc 7 types, and test them).

Frequency: Monthly (bullets are high-value, iterate them regularly).

---

PRIORITY #6 — THE CTA (Call-to-Action — A Command + Ease):

What to test:
• The button text ("Buy Now" vs. "Get Instant Access" vs. "Start Your Transformation")
• The button color (green vs. orange vs. red — it's platform-dependent)
• The placement (after the benefits, after the proof, both — multiple CTAs)
• The emphasis on ease ("it takes 60 seconds" vs. "3 clicks" vs. "instant")

The impact: 30-80% (a strong CTA = 10% click rate, a weak CTA = 6% — 1.67x).

The testing: A simple A/B (button variations, track the clicks).

The ROI: 10:1 to 30:1

Time: 30 min - 1h (to create CTA variations, and test them).

Frequency: Monthly.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

TIER 3 — MEDIUM-IMPACT (5:1 to 15:1 ROI)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

PRIORITY #7 — PROOF ELEMENTS (Doc 8 — Emphasis on the Tier):

Test: Video vs. text testimonials (+58% from Masterson), the placement of the proof (after the benefits vs. after the mechanism), the quantity (3 vs. 5 vs. 8 testimonials).

The impact: 20-60%

The ROI: 8:1 to 20:1

---

PRIORITY #8 — COPY LENGTH (Long vs. Short):

Test: 2,000w vs. 6,000w vs. 10,000w (appropriate for the State/Stage — from Doc 2).

The impact: 15-50% (the wrong length = a bounce, the right length = engagement).

The ROI: 5:1 to 15:1

---

PRIORITY #9 — EMAIL SEQUENCES (Doc 10 — The Order, Timing, and Content):

Test: A Soap Opera vs. a 90/10 Nurture (different engagement), the send time (9 am vs. 3 pm, per Ladeira), the frequency (daily vs. 3x/week).

The impact: 20-70% (the wrong sequence type for the State = low opens, the right one = high).

The ROI: 8:1 to 25:1

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

TIER 4 — LOW-IMPACT (2:1 to 8:1 ROI)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

PRIORITY #10 — THE DESIGN/LAYOUT (Visual Elements):

Test: The color scheme, fonts, images, spacing.

The impact: 10-25% (a professional design vs. an amateur one = credibility, BUT it's not the core of the persuasion).

The ROI: 3:1 to 8:1

Ogilvy: "Design matters (10-25%), BUT the copy matters MORE (70%). Test the copy BEFORE the design."

---

PRIORITY #11 — BUTTON DETAILS (The Color, Size, and Micro-Text):

Test: A green vs. an orange button (it's platform-specific — green wins on FB, orange often wins on landing pages), the size (large vs. medium), the shape (rounded vs. square).

The impact: 5-15% (button optimization is the final 5-15% you can squeeze out, after everything else has been optimized).

The ROI: 2:1 to 5:1

Hormozi: "Testing the button color is the last priority (after the offer, headline, copy, and CTA text have been optimized — it's polishing the details)."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

A SUMMARY OF THE TESTING HIERARCHY:

The ORDER of the tests: Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3 → Tier 4 (in descending order of ROI)

NEVER: Test Tier 4 (the button color) BEFORE Tier 1 (the offer) — that's backward, a low-leverage waste of time.

ALWAYS: The biggest levers first (the offer, the Big Idea, the headline — each with a 100-500% impact), the details last (the design, the button — a 5-25% impact).

Compounding: Tier 1 optimized (+300%) × Tier 2 (+100%) × Tier 3 (+40%) = a cumulative 12x improvement is possible (vs. the baseline, over years of systematic testing).

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```
