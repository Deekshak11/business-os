## **SECTION 6.1 — CLASSIC BRAND TAGLINES (30 Analyzed)**

---

**TAGLINE #1:**

```
"Just Do It" — Nike (1988-Present)

ANALYSIS:

LENGTH: 3 words (ultra-short — memorable, easy to repeat)

MEANING:
• Surface: A command to take action (do it — motivational)
• Deep: Anti-overthinking (just = eliminate hesitation, do = action, it = whatever the goal is — universally applicable to running, the gym, life decisions)

EMOTIONAL APPEAL:
• LF6 (superiority — athletes do, they don't talk; action = winners)
• LF8 (social approval — be part of the Nike tribe, the aspirational athletes)

MEMORABILITY:
• Simple (3 words, 1 syllable each — the brain processes it instantly)
• Rhythmic (Just-Do-It = 3 beats, it's musical)
• Actionable (a command verb — the imperative motivates)
• Universal (applies to anything — not just shoes, it's a life philosophy)

LONGEVITY: 36 years (unchanged from 1988-2024 — timeless, not dated)

WHY IT WORKS:
• Extreme simplicity (Nike products are complex — tech shoes, clothing, BUT the tagline is SIMPLE and balances it — making it accessible)
• Aspirational identity (be the person who "just does it" — not a hesitator, a doer)
• Brand consistency (every ad, athlete, and campaign reinforces it — Cialdini's repetition, familiarity = sympathy)

COPYWRITING LESSONS:
• Short can be POWERFUL (no need for a lengthy explanation, 3 words are sufficient IF they are the right words).
• Command verbs (action-oriented) = higher motivation than passive statements.
• Universal application (vague can work IF it resonates emotionally — "it" = anything, flexibility).
```
