## **SECTION 1.4 — CVR BENCHMARKS (PER FUNNEL STEP — AGORA/LADEIRA DATA)**

---

**Why benchmarks are critical:**

**Without benchmarks:** You don't know if a 5% CVR is good/bad (there's no context, you can't judge the performance).

**With benchmarks:** 5% vs. an industry 12% = a PROBLEM (you're underperforming by 2.4x, action is needed), OR 5% vs. an industry 2% = EXCELLENT (you're outperforming by 2.5x, you can scale with confidence).

**Benchmarks provide context** (Ariely from Doc 1 — decisions are relative, not absolute).

---

### **THE MASTER BENCHMARKS TABLE (50+ KPIs — By Channel, Niche, and Step):**

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
CVR BENCHMARKS — INDUSTRY STANDARDS (Validated by Multiple Sources)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

PAID ADS (Cold Traffic → Click)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM ADS:
• Impressions → Clicks (CTR):
  - Cold audiences: 1-3% (a baseline — moderate interest)
  - Warm (retargeting site visitors): 3-8% (a boost from familiarity)
  - Hot (retargeting purchasers): 5-12% (a validated buying intent)
  Source: Facebook Ads Benchmarks 2024 (10M+ ads analyzed)

• Cost-Per-Click (CPC):
  - Brazil: R$1.50-8.00 (it's niche-dependent — finance is R$5-8, e-comm is R$1.50-3)
  - USA: $0.50-3.00
  Source: Wordstream 2024, Ladeira's VTSD Brazil data

GOOGLE SEARCH ADS:
• CTR (Based on search intent):
  - Branded keywords: 8-15% (high intent — they are specifically searching for you)
  - Non-branded commercial: 3-8% (a generic "buy X" — moderate intent)
  - Informational: 1-4% (the research phase — a low commercial intent)
  Source: Google Ads Benchmarks 2024

• CPC:
  - Brazil: R$2-15 (finance/B2B is R$8-15, e-comm is R$2-5)
  Source: SEMrush Brazil 2024

YOUTUBE ADS:
• View Rate (Impressions → 30sec+ Views):
  - Skippable (TrueView): 15-40% (a compelling hook retains them)
  - Non-skippable (6-15sec): 100% (a forced view, BUT with a risk of irritation)
  Source: YouTube Ads Benchmarks

• CPC: R$0.50-3.00 in Brazil (cheaper than Facebook — still less competition)

NATIVE ADS (Taboola, Outbrain, MGID):
• CTR:
  - A compelling thumbnail + headline: 0.3-0.8% (low in absolute terms, BUT the volume compensates)
  - An advertorial bridge: 0.5-1.2% (the editorial format boosts it)
  Source: Taboola Benchmarks, Ladeira's VTSD (2,400 campaigns in Brazil)

• CPC: R$0.30-2.00 (the cheapest paid channel — an arbitrage opportunity)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

LANDING PAGES (Click → Email Opt-In)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

LEAD MAGNET PAGES (A Free Opt-In):
• Visitors → Email Opt-Ins:
  - Cold traffic (directly from an ad): 15-30% (with a compelling offer and a simple form)
  - Warm traffic (retargeting, blog readers): 30-50% (pre-existing trust)
  - Hot traffic (webinar registrants): 50-70% (high intent)
  Benchmark source: Unbounce 2024 (100k+ landing pages), Ladeira

• The impact of form fields:
  - Email only: 40% opt-in (minimal friction)
  - Email + Name: 32% (a -20% for each additional field)
  - Email + Name + Phone: 25% (a -38% vs. email only)
  Source: HubSpot Form Benchmarks

TRIPWIRE PAGES (Low-Ticket R$27-297):
• Visitors → Purchase:
  - Cold (directly from an ad): 2-8% (the impulse range)
  - Warm (lead magnet subscribers): 5-15% (trust has been established)
  - Hot (webinar attendees): 8-25% (education + urgency)
  Source: The ClickFunnels Benchmark Report 2023

SALES PAGES (A Core Offer of R$500-5,000):
• Visitors → Purchase:
  - Cold: 0.5-2% (insufficient education, zero trust)
  - Warm (nurtured with emails for 7-14 days): 2-8% (a relationship has been built)
  - Hot (complete webinar/VSL viewers): 3-15% (value has been delivered, there's urgency)
  Source: Agora Financial (decades of newsletters), Empiricus in Brazil

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

EMAIL MARKETING (Sequences & Broadcasts)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

OPEN RATES (Sends → Opens):
• The #1 welcome email (indoctrination): 60-80% (peak interest)
• Welcome emails #2-7: 40-65% (a declining gradient)
• A nurture sequence (educational): 30-45% (consistent value)
• A daily broadcast (Agora style): 25-35% (sustained for years)
• Promotional emails (a pitch): 20-35% (some resistance)
• Re-engagement (a win-back): 10-25% (a cold list)
Source: Mailchimp Industry Average 2024, Agora's internal data, Ladeira's VTSD

CLICK-THROUGH RATES (Opens → Clicks):
• Educational (value links): 8-15% (driven by curiosity)
• Promotional (an offer CTA): 3-8% (buying intent)
• A broadcast (varied content): 5-12% (engagement)
Source: Campaign Monitor 2024

CONVERSION RATES (Email Recipients → Purchase):
• A single promotional email: 1-3% (the baseline)
• A Soap Opera sequence (5-7 emails): 8-18% (story engagement, trust)
• A 90/10 Nurture (a pitch email after 9 value emails): 12-25% (extreme reciprocity)
• A PLF launch sequence (15-20 emails): 20-40% (the highest — anticipation is built, real scarcity)
Source: Brunson's ClickFunnels data, Agora's decades of practice

UNSUBSCRIBE RATES (Per Email):
• Welcome/Educational: 0.1-0.3% (high value, low unsubs)
• Promotional: 0.3-0.8% (some resistance to the pitch)
• A daily frequency (if it's 90/10 value): an aggregate of 0.5-1.5%/month (acceptable)
• Acceptable: <1% per email, an aggregate of <2%/month
• A problem: >2% per email (insufficient value, excessive frequency)
Source: Mailchimp benchmarks

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

WEBINARS (Registration → Sale)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

REGISTRATION → ATTENDANCE:
• Live webinars: A 40-60% show-up rate (registered → attended)
• Automated/Replay: 25-45% (lower — no live urgency)
• With reminder emails (3-5): A +15-25% in attendance (indoctrination is critical)
Source: WebinarJam data, Brunson's Perfect Webinar stats

ATTENDEES → PURCHASE:
• A live webinar (a 60-90min Perfect Webinar): 10-25% (education + pitch + Q&A + urgency)
• Replay viewers (a 48h window): 3-10% (lower — no live energy, BUT the volume compensates)
• An automated evergreen: 8-20% (a well-done simulation of a live event, with a disclosed fake countdown)
Source: Brunson's ClickFunnels, Agora's webinar practice

OVERALL (Registered → Purchase):
• Live: 40% attend × 15% buy = 6% overall (registered → customer)
• Replay: 35% watch × 6% buy = 2.1% overall
• Combined: 6% + 2.1% = 8.1% of total registered → customers (live + replay)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

A VSL (Video Sales Letter — Ladeira's VTSD Data for Brazil)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

VIEWING METRICS:
• Ad → VSL page click-through: 30-50% (a warm advertorial bridge, per Ladeira)
• VSL start → 50% watched: 60-75% (a strong hook retains them)
• VSL 50% → 100% completion: 45-65% (value is delivered, they stick to the end)
• The overall completion rate: 45-65% (visitors → complete watch)

CONVERSION (VSL Viewers → Purchase):
• Cold traffic (directly from an ad to the VSL, no bridge): 0.5-2% (low — not warmed up enough)
• Warm traffic (advertorial → VSL): 3-8% (Ladeira's VTSD method — the bridge is critical)
• Hot traffic (retargeting viewers who watched 75%+): 5-12% (a validated high intent)
Source: Ladeira's VTSD with 2,400+ campaigns in Brazil from 2018-2024

THE IMPACT OF THE CTA TIMING (Ladeira Specific):
• A CTA that's visible from min 0-34 of the VSL: 2.1% conversion (premature — value hasn't been delivered)
• A CTA that appears at min 35-38: 4.7% conversion (optimal — 35 min of value, with a 60-70% completion rate still)
• A late CTA at min 40+: A completion rate of only 40% (many exit before 40 min and miss the CTA)
The sweet spot: The CTA appears at min 35-38 (tested in 2,400 VSLs, a consistent pattern)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

THE CHECKOUT / ORDER FLOW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

THE ORDER BUMP (A Checkbox Offer):
• The checkbox is presented → It's accepted: 20-40% (Ladeira's Brazil data, Brunson's US data)
• High-ticket orders (R$1k+): 35-50% (a higher willingness, a relative R$47 bump is trivial)
• Low-ticket (R$50-300): 20-30% (a relative R$27 bump is 10-50% of the order, more hesitation)

A ONE-CLICK UPSELL (Immediately Post-Purchase):
• The upsell offer → It's accepted: 10-25% (Brunson's ClickFunnels data)
• The relevance is critical (it must be related to the core purchase): 20-30%
• If it's unrelated/random: 5-12% (lower — confusion)

A DOWNSELL (A Declined Upsell → A Cheaper Offer):
• The upsell is declined → The downsell is accepted: 15-30%
• The psychology: Rejection-then-retreat (Cialdini from Doc 1 Section 2.1.3B — a smaller ask after a larger rejection, a +23% in compliance)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

HIGH-TICKET (Application → Call → Close)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

THE APPLICATION IS SUBMITTED → A CALL IS BOOKED:
• For qualified applicants: 60-80% (an extensive pre-qualifying form, a commitment is demonstrated by filling it out)
• For unqualified/tire-kickers: 20-40% (the filter is working — you only want the serious ones)
Source: Kennedy's high-ticket practice, Bly's B2B

THE CALL IS BOOKED → THE CALL IS ATTENDED:
• The show-up rate: 70-85% (reminder emails/SMS are critical, as are calendar invites)
• The no-shows: 15-30% (acceptable — an offer to reschedule, some are never serious)

THE CALL IS ATTENDED → THE SALE IS CLOSED:
• For high-ticket ($5k-50k): 20-40% (trust has been built pre-call, the qualification is working, the human closer is skilled)
• For ultra-high ($50k+): 10-25% (decision committees, multiple touches are needed, a longer cycle)
Source: Kennedy's Marketing to the Affluent, Bly's B2B consulting

THE OVERALL FUNNEL (From Ad → a High-Ticket Customer):
• Ad → Application: 5-15%
• Application → Call: 70%
• Call → Close: 30%
• Overall: 1-3% (ad viewers → high-ticket customer, a low absolute BUT an LTV of R$10k-50k compensates)
```

**How to use the table (50+ benchmarks) — a comparison of YOUR metrics vs. the industry:**

```
AN EXAMPLE COMPARISON:

Your Current Funnel:
• Ad CTR: 2.1% (the benchmark is 1-3%, STATUS: ✅ Good — in the middle range)
• Landing Page Opt-in: 18% (the benchmark is 20-40%, STATUS: ⚠️ Below — a potential bottleneck)
• Opt-in → Tripwire: 12% (the benchmark is 5-15%, STATUS: ✅ Excellent — above)
• Email Opens: 32% (the benchmark is 25-40%, STATUS: ✅ Good — on the high end)
• Email → Sale: 6% (the benchmark is 8-25%, STATUS: ⚠️ Below — a weak sequence)

THE IDENTIFIED BOTTLENECKS:
#1 Priority: The landing page opt-in (18% vs. 20-40%) — FIX this first
#2 Priority: The email → sale (6% vs. 8-25%) — FIX this second, after #1 is resolved

THE ACTION:
• For the landing page: Test the headlines (10 variations from Doc 6), test the lead type (the decision tree from Doc 6, appropriate for the State), test the form (email only vs. the friction of email+name)
• For the email: Test the sequence type (a Soap Opera vs. a Nurture — from Doc 10), test the send time (Ladeira's 9-11 am vs. the current time), test the pitch ratio (the current 20% pitch, test Abraham's 10% 90/10 IF the audience values education more)
```

**Benchmarks are a diagnostic tool** (they identify exactly WHERE the funnel is weak, for a targeted fix, not a guess).

---

**Ladeira's emphasis on Brazil:**

"US benchmarks are not always the same as Brazil's (the culture, the economy, and the platform adoption are different).

Examples of the differences:

• Email opens: The US average is 21% (Mailchimp), in Brazil it's 28-32% (Ladeira — Brazilians check their emails more frequently on mobile, it's a cultural habit).

• VSL completion: In the US it's 35-50%, in Brazil it's 45-65% (Brazilians tolerate longer videos, the YouTube culture is ingrained, their attention span is paradoxically HIGHER for videos vs. text).

• Payment methods: In the US it's 95% credit card, in Brazil it's 60% card + 25% boleto + 15% Pix (offering ALL 3 in Brazil = a +65% in conversion vs. card only).

Use BRAZIL-SPECIFIC benchmarks when they are available (from Ladeira, Empiricus, as they are culturally and economically accurate), and US benchmarks only when BR data is absent (as a directional guide, adjusted down by -10-20% for a conservative estimate of the cultural differences)."
