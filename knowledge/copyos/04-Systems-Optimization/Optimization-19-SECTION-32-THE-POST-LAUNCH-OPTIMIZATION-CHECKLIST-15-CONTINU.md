## **SECTION 3.2 — THE POST-LAUNCH OPTIMIZATION CHECKLIST (15 CONTINUOUS ITEMS)**

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE CHECKLIST — POST-LAUNCH (A Continuous Improvement Loop)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

WEEKLY (A Minimum of 1 Test):
☐ 1. Is there a test scheduled for this week? (the headline, the CTA, the bullets, the lead — something is being tested, it's NOT stagnant)

☐ 2. Is it a single-variable test? (the discipline — only 1 element is being changed, not a multiple of confounded ones)

☐ 3. Is the sample size adequate? (a minimum of 100-200 conversions per variation — for statistical validity)

☐ 4. Has the statistical significance been checked? (a confidence of ≥95%, and a p≤0.05 — a calculator has been used, it's NOT just an eyeball of "it looks better")

☐ 5. Has the winner been implemented? (if it's significant, it has been rolled out to 100% of the traffic — NOT a paralysis of "I'm still thinking," the data decides)

MONTHLY (A Metrics Review):
☐ 6. Has the LTV:CAC ratio been calculated? (with the current month's data, to be compared to last month, and to see if the trend is improving/stable/declining)

☐ 7. Has the bottleneck been identified? (the CVR for each step has been measured, the lowest has been identified, and a 5-whys diagnosis has been initiated)

☐ 8. Has the top priority fix been chosen? (from the Testing Hierarchy in Section 1.1 — Tier 1 first, NOT a premature Tier 4 for the button color)

☐ 9. Have the learnings been documented? (in a spreadsheet with the test results, the winners, the losers, and the hypotheses of why — an institutional knowledge is being built, it's NOT being lost after each forgotten test)

QUARTERLY (Major Reviews):
☐ 10. Is there an aggregate improvement? (a quarter vs. a quarter comparison — is the conversion up? Is the revenue up? Is the LTV:CAC better? — a positive trend validates that the optimization is working)

☐ 11. Does the offer need a refresh? (are the bonuses stale? Is the guarantee still competitive? Are the urgency types still effective? — the offer must evolve with the quarterly market changes)

☐ 12. Is the Big Idea still resonant? (is the angle working? OR is the market saturated, have competitors copied it, and do you need a NEW Big Idea from Doc 3 — a pivot annually if needed)

☐ 13. Is the specialization appropriate? (are B2B tactics being used for a B2C audience? A high-ticket approach for a low-ticket product? — an audit for an alignment with the context)

☐ 14. Is the compliance current? (have the platform's guidelines been updated? Have there been any changes to the CDC/LGPD? — a legal quarterly review to avoid any surprise bans/fines)

CONTINUOUS (The Mindset):
☐ 15. Is humility being maintained? (the market teaches, you listen — your favorites will lose regularly, there will be constant surprises, a healthy death of the ego, and always being data-driven NOT opinion-driven)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

POST-LAUNCH is NEVER "DONE" (optimization is perpetual, testing is continuous, and the improvements compound — a year 1 baseline → a 2-3x conversion by year 3 via dozens of accumulated small-win tests, with a systematic and disciplined patience being rewarded).

Hopkins: "Advertising isn't creative (it's not an art that's done once). Advertising is SCIENTIFIC (you must test continuously, always improve, the market evolves and you evolve with it, being stagnant is death in competitive markets)."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```
