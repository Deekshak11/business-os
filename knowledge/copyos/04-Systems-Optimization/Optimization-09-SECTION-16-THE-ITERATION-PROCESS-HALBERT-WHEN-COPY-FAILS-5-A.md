## **SECTION 1.6 — THE ITERATION PROCESS (HALBERT — WHEN COPY FAILS, 5 ATTEMPTS)**

---

**Gary Halbert** (The Boron Letters, Letter #23 — "Failure Is Temporary"):

> *"Did your copy bomb? (A <1% conversion, a disappointing revenue).*
>
> *Don't panic. Don't quit.*
>
> *ITERATE.*
>
> *I've had promos that failed 3-4x before I got it right on the 5th iteration (a data-driven persistence, not an ego-driven stubbornness).*
>
> *The market teaches you (every failure is a lesson — was the headline wrong? The lead type? The offer? The Big Idea? The market?).*
>
> *Commit to a MINIMUM of 5 iterations before you declare 'it doesn't work' (90% of copywriters quit at iteration 1-2 — the persistent 10% win, not because they are more talented, but because they PERSIST, learn, and iterate)."*

---

### **THE ITERATION SEQUENCE (When Copy Fails — The Order of the 5 Attempts):**

---

**ITERATION #1 — TEST 10-20 NEW HEADLINES:**

**The rationale:** 80% of copy problems are the headline (Ogilvy's "80 cents of your dollar" — if the headline fails, the body is never read, and it's irrelevant how good it is).

---

**The process:**

1.  **Generate:** 10-20 headlines (with the formulas from Doc 6 — How-To, Question, Secret, Number, Story, 2-3 variations of each formula).

2.  **Score:** Each headline with the 4 U's (eliminate those with <25 points, shortlist the 10-15 that scored 25-35).

3.  **A peer review:** Show the top 10-15 to 5-10 people from your avatar (and have them vote for their top 3-5 favorites).

4.  **Test:** A/B test the top 3-5 (with a small amount of traffic, 100-300 visitors for each, and measure the ad's CTR OR the landing page's scroll depth).

5.  **Implement:** The winner (the one with the highest CTR/engagement) becomes the new control.

---

**The expected outcome:**

**If the headline WAS the problem:**

The conversion will improve dramatically (a +50-200% is typical for a swap from a weak headline to a strong one).

**Declare:** The problem is solved (the headline fix was sufficient, the copy works now).

---

**If the headline was NOT the problem:**

The conversion will improve modestly (<+20%, OR it will stay the same, OR it will decline).

**The conclusion:** The headline was not the root issue (the problem is deeper — move on to Iteration #2).

---

**The investment for Iteration #1:** 3-6h (to generate 20, score them, review them, and set up the test).

**The success rate:** 60-70% (most copy problems are solved with a headline fix — as validated by Ogilvy/Halbert for decades).

---

**ITERATION #2 — TEST 2-3 DIFFERENT LEAD TYPES:**

**The rationale:** If the headline is OK (it's been tested, it scored 30+, but the conversion is still low), the problem is the lead (the first 300-500 words after the headline — the tone is wrong, the type is inappropriate for the State).

---

**The process:**

1.  **The current lead type:** Identify it (from Doc 6 — is it an Offer? A Promise? A Problem-Solution? A Story? A Secret? An Invitation?).

2.  **A check of the State:** Is the avatar's State known? (from Doc 2 — if it's State 3, the current lead is a Promise, BUT the benchmark is that a Promise is appropriate for State 2, the mismatch is the problem).

3.  **Create variations:** Write 2-3 different lead types (with the templates from Doc 6 — if the current one is a Promise, test a Story + a Problem-Solution as alternatives).

4.  **Test:** A/B test the leads (the same headline, but a different 300-500w lead, and measure the scroll depth + the time-on-page + the conversion).

5.  **Implement:** The winning lead type.

---

**The expected outcome:**

**If the lead WAS the problem:**

The engagement will improve (a +30-100% in scroll depth, a +20-80% in conversion — a lead that's appropriate for the State will resonate, an inappropriate one will cause them to bounce).

---

**If the lead was NOT the problem:**

A minimal improvement of <+15%.

**The conclusion:** The lead is OK (the problem is deeper — is it the offer? The Big Idea? — move on to Iteration #3).

---

**The investment:** 4-8h (to write 2-3 leads of 300-500w each = 900-1,500w total, and to test them).

**The success rate:** 20-30% (lead fixes work IF a State mismatch was the issue, otherwise they don't).

---

**ITERATION #3 — REDESIGN THE OFFER (The Bonuses, the Guarantee, the Urgency):**

**The rationale:** The headline has been tested (it's OK), the lead has been tested (it's OK), but the conversion is still low → the problem is the OFFER (the value is insufficient, the risk is perceived as high, and urgency is absent).

---

**The process:**

1.  **An audit of the current offer:** Score it with the Value Equation (from Doc 4 Part I — the Dream Outcome, the Perceived Likelihood, the Time Delay, and the Effort, each from 1-10).

2.  **Identify the weakest component:** Which component scored the lowest? (e.g., the Perceived Likelihood is a 4/10, with a basic generic 30-day guarantee — it's weak).

3.  **A targeted redesign:**
    -   Add bonuses (that resolve obstacles — from Doc 4 Part III, 3-5 bonuses that address the avatar's top obstacles)
    -   Upgrade the guarantee (from a Level 2 → a results-based Level 4, from Doc 4 Part V)
    -   Add urgencies (a real deadline, scarcity, a price increase — from Doc 4 Part VI, a stack of 3-4 types)

4.  **Retest the offer:** Joyner's 10-person test (from Doc 4 Section 2.3 — if 8-10/10 say yes, the offer is now irresistible).

5.  **Implement:** The new offer (the copy is unchanged except for a rewritten offer section, the stack presentation, the upgraded guarantee, and the added urgency).

---

**The expected outcome:**

**If the offer WAS the problem:**

The conversion will jump (a +100-300% is typical — a weak R$997 offer converts at 2%, the same strong R$997 offer converts at 8% = a 4x difference from the offer engineering alone).

---

**The investment:** 6-10h (a complete offer redesign from Doc 4, to create the bonuses, upgrade the guarantee, engineer the urgency, and test it on 10 people).

**The success rate:** 50-60% (offer problems are common — many copywriters focus on the copy and neglect the offer, fixing the offer is a breakthrough).

---

**ITERATION #4 — PIVOT THE BIG IDEA (A Completely New Angle):**

**The rationale:** The headline is OK, the lead is OK, the offer is OK (each has been tested and improved, but the conversion is still <3%) → the problem is the POSITIONING (the Big Idea's angle doesn't resonate with the avatar, there's a Stage mismatch, the mechanism is wrong).

---

**The process:**

1.  **The current Big Idea:** Identify it (from Doc 3 — what type is it? A Discovery? A Conspiracy? A Mistake? A Twist? A Shortcut?).

2.  **A recheck of the Stage:** Was the avatar's Stage diagnosed correctly? (from Doc 2 — if it's Stage 3, a Shortcut Big Idea is appropriate, BUT if it's actually Stage 5, you need a Story/Identification — there's a mismatch).

3.  **Create new Big Ideas:** 3-5 different types (from Doc 3 Section 1.2 — try a Conspiracy IF the current one was a Discovery, try a Mistake IF it was a Shortcut, a radical diversification of the angles).

4.  **Write copy variations:** Each Big Idea will have a different headline + lead + an emphasis on the bullets (300-500w variations, not full 10k rewrites — to test the angles, not the full copy yet).

5.  **Test:** Small campaigns (R$500-1,000 for each Big Idea, to measure engagement + conversion).

6.  **The winner:** The Big Idea with the highest conversion is the new positioning (THEN rewrite the full copy based on the winning angle).

---

**The expected outcome:**

**If the Big Idea WAS the problem:**

The conversion will be transformed (a +200-500% is possible — the market ignores the wrong angle, the market engages insanely with the right angle, Schwartz's Breakthrough validates that the Big Idea is a massive lever).

---

**The investment:** 12-20h (to create 3-5 Big Ideas, write the copy variations, set up the test campaigns, analyze them, and do a full rewrite for the winner).

**The success rate:** 15-25% (Big Idea problems are rarer — most issues are the headline/offer, BUT when it IS the Big Idea, the fix is transformative).

---

**ITERATION #5 — THE WRONG MARKET (Pivot the Avatar OR the Product):**

**The rationale:** Iterations 1-4 ALL failed (20 headlines were tested, 3 lead types, the offer was redesigned with bonuses/a guarantee/urgency, and the Big Idea was pivoted to 3-5 angles — NOTHING worked, the conversion is still <1%).

**The conclusion:** The problem is NOT the copy/offer/angle (you've tested everything).

**The problem IS:** THE MARKET (Axiom 2 from Doc 1 — the pre-existing desire is ABSENT, it's the wrong market, a product/avatar mismatch).

---

**The actions:**

**Option A — PIVOT THE AVATAR:**

**The current avatar:** Targeting young entrepreneurs, 25-40.

**The test:** Target older entrepreneurs, 40-60 (with a different pain/desire, a higher purchasing power, and a different urgency).

**The rationale:** Maybe the product works, BUT the avatar is wrong (a shift in age, demographics, or psychographics = a different market segment, the desire might exist there and not here).

---

**Option B — PIVOT THE PRODUCT:**

**The current product:** A copywriting course (an info product).

**The test:** A done-for-you copywriting service (a different model — you don't teach them, you DO it for them).

**The rationale:** Maybe the market wants the result (not to learn the skill), so DFY > DIY for this avatar.

---

**Option C — PIVOT THE MARKET COMPLETELY:**

**Abandon:** The current market (digital entrepreneurs for copywriting).

**Enter:** A different market (e.g., copywriting for real estate agents — a different niche, a different pain, potentially less saturated).

**The rationale:** Maybe the market is at Stage 5 saturation and impossible to penetrate (choose a different, less competitive Stage 2-3 market).

---

**Halbert:**

"Iteration 5 is the ultimate humility (to admit: it's not the copy, it's not the offer, it's not the angle — it's the wrong MARKET I've chosen, Axiom 2 has been violated, there's insufficient pre-existing desire).

A hard pivot (it's painful for the ego, you've invested months/years, BUT the sunk cost fallacy is death, cut your losses and move to a viable market).

I pivoted 4x in my career (I failed in the jewelry, vitamins, and real estate markets — I succeeded with financial newsletters — the market CHOICE matters MORE than copywriting skill, a starving crowd is always > a copy genius)."

---

**The investment for Iteration #5:** Massive (a pivot is a restart — new avatar research from Doc 2, new positioning from Doc 3, a new offer from Doc 4, new copy from Docs 5-11 — it's essentially a rebuild).

**The success rate:** N/A (a pivot is an admission of the current failure, success will depend on the new market chosen — the research from Doc 2 is critical to avoid repeating the mistake).
