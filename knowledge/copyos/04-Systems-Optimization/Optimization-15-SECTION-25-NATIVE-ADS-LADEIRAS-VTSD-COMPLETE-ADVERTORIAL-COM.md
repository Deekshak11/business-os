## **SECTION 2.5 — NATIVE ADS (LADEIRA'S VTSD — COMPLETE ADVERTORIAL COMPLIANCE)**

---

**Leandro Ladeira** (VTSD — A Native Ads Specialist in Brazil from 2018-2024):

> *"Native ads (Taboola, Outbrain, MGID) are an arbitrage opportunity (a R$0.30-2.00 CPC, the cheapest paid channel vs. Facebook at R$1.50-8.00, and Google at R$2-15).*
>
> *BUT: Compliance is CRITICAL (the platforms will quickly ban you for violations — the guidelines are strict, the enforcement is harsh, an account ban means lost traffic + a wasted budget).*
>
> *An advertorial is a MANDATORY bridge (a native ad → directly to a VSL = an 80% bounce rate, but an ad → an advertorial → a VSL = a 45-65% completion rate, the bridge warms up the cold traffic with 3-5 min of editorial content).*
>
> *Native ads in Brazil from 2020-2024: A 340% growth (the adoption is increasing, the competition is still moderate vs. a saturated Facebook, there's a 2-4 year window of opportunity before saturation — act now for an advantage)."*

### **THE NATIVE AD'S CREATIVE (The Thumbnail + The Headline — An Optimization):**

---

**THE THUMBNAIL (An Image/Video Thumbnail — Emotional OR Authoritative):**

**The types of thumbnails to test:**

**Type A — An Emotional Face (For a High CTR):**
- A person expressing a STRONG emotion (shock 😲, fear 😨, curiosity 🤔, joy 😃 — slightly exaggerated, so the emotion is readable in a small 300×200px thumbnail)
- Eye contact with the camera (for a face-to-face engagement)
- A text overlay (optional — "89% STUCK" for a shocking number, OR "REVEALED" for urgency)

**An example:** A doctor's face with a shocked expression, with a text overlay of "DOCTORS ARE SHOCKED" (emotion + authority combined).

**The CTR:** 0.8-1.5% (emotional faces work for click-bait, Ladeira has tested 2,400 campaigns, and faces get a 2-3x higher CTR than objects/landscapes).

---

**Type B — An Authority Figure (For Credibility):**

- A person who is a recognizable authority (a doctor in a coat, a businessman in a suit, an expert in an office/lab setting)
- A serious expression (not a casual smile — for an authoritative gravitas)
- Props of authority (a stethoscope, books in the background, visible certificates)

**An example:** A doctor in their 60s, in a white coat, with a stethoscope, in a hospital background, with a serious and thoughtful expression.

**The CTR:** 0.6-1.2% (lower than the emotional one, BUT it's qualified traffic — the clickers trust the authority, they have a serious interest, and a higher downstream conversion compensates for the lower CTR).

---

**Type C — A Shocking Visual Stat:**

- LARGE numbers on the screen (89%, R$50k, 12,000 — a specific stat)
- Contrasting colors (red numbers on a yellow background — for visibility)
- An icon/symbol (⚠️ a warning, 📈 for growth, 💰 for money — a visual shorthand)

**An example:** A black screen, with large red text of "89% STUCK" (for visibility), and a white subtitle of "Why?" (for curiosity).

**The CTR:** 0.5-1.0% (stats work for analytical audiences, in B2B and finance, with less emotion but a specific targeting).

---

**THE TESTING:** 10-20 thumbnail variations (different emotions, varied authority figures, multiple stats — test ALL, the winner is often a surprise, a smiling Maria might have a 1.4x higher CTR than a shocked Maria in campaign X, and the opposite in campaign Y — it's avatar-dependent, so always test).

---

**THE HEADLINE (An Editorial Curiosity, NOT an Obvious Sale):**

**The native headline formula:**

```
"[AN AUTHORITY FIGURE] [A SURPRISING ACTION] [AN INTRIGUING RESULT] ([A CREDIBILITY DETAIL])"
```

---

**Examples of native headlines (in an editorial style):**

1. "A 67-Year-Old Retired Doctor Who Never Gets Sick: Reveals 5 Simple Morning Habits (Confirmed by a Harvard Study with 12,000 Participants)"

**The elements:**
- The authority: A doctor (credible for health)
- The age: 67 (older = wisdom, good health at 67 is impressive)
- Never sick: An intriguing result (how is that possible?)
- 5 morning habits: An actionable tease (5 is countable, morning is specific, simple is for ease)
- A Harvard study with 12k participants: Credibility (the institution, the large sample)

**The tone:** Editorial (it reads like a UOL/Globo health article, NOT "Buy This Supplement Now!")

**The CTR:** 0.9-1.4% (from Ladeira's data — high curiosity, credible authority, an implied actionability).

---

2. "A 47-Year-Old Brazilian Discovers a Method to Make R$30k/Month Working From Home (Companies Are Trying to Hide It — It's Revealed Here)"

**The elements:**
- Brazilian: A localization (an identification of the nationality)
- 47 years old: For relatability (middle-aged, not a young 25 or an old 70)
- R$30k/month: A desirable outcome (an aspirational BUT believable income)
- From home: Freedom (an appeal to location independence)
- Companies are hiding it: A conspiracy (curiosity about why they would hide it, an Us vs. Them)
- Revealed here: Exclusivity (access to unique information)

**The tone:** Discovery/Conspiracy (an investigative editorial, not an obvious advertorial).

**The CTR:** 1.0-1.6% (conspiracy angles work in Brazil — a cultural distrust of institutions, "they're hiding it" resonates, as validated by Ladeira).

---

3. "A Shocking Study: 89% of Digital Entrepreneurs NEVER Surpass R$100k/Month (The Research Identifies 3 Common Fatal Errors)"

**The elements:**
- A study: The authority of science (for credibility)
- Shocking: An emotion (a shocking stat is coming)
- 89%: A specific stat (the majority are stuck — an identification of "Am I part of the 89%?")
- NEVER: A finality (doom without the info — an implicit urgency)
- R$100k/month: An aspirational milestone (a common goal for entrepreneurs)
- 3 fatal errors: Actionable and countable (3 is specific, errors are an avoidable protective, and fatal implies severe consequences)

**The tone:** A research editorial (study-based, a scientific veneer, credible).

**The CTR:** 0.7-1.3% (a shocking stat for identification, an appeal to the protective LF3 to avoid errors).

**THE PLATFORM'S COMPLIANCE RULES (Critical — To Avoid a Ban):**

---

**THE TABOOLA GUIDELINES (The Strictest — Premium Publishers Demand Quality):**

```
PROHIBITED (An Instant Rejection OR a Ban):
❌ Excessive clickbait ("You WON'T BELIEVE THIS!!!" — all caps, abuse of exclamation marks)
❌ Before/After images (for weight loss, for financial gains — some publishers ban them, others allow them IF they have disclaimers like "results are not typical")
❌ Extreme sensationalism ("A SHOCKING DISCOVERY THAT CHANGES EVERYTHING" — hyperbole)
❌ Nudity/Sexually suggestive content (obvious — premium publishers are family-friendly)
❌ The appearance of fake news (a headline that mimics real news BUT is a pure ad — it's deceptive, publishers will reject it)
❌ Negative targeting ("Are you FAT? UGLY?" — body-shaming, excessive negativity)

REQUIRED (For Compliance):
✅ A disclaimer: "Advertorial Content" OR "Advertisement" (at the top of the advertorial page, in a visible 10-12pt font minimum, for transparency, a Brazilian FTC-equivalent)
✅ An accurate representation (if the headline promises X, the advertorial must deliver X — no bait-and-switch)
✅ A substantiation of claims (medical/financial claims require cited studies, you can't claim it "cures diabetes" without a backing of peer-reviewed research)
✅ A link to a privacy policy (for LGPD compliance in Brazil — landing pages must have a visible privacy link)

THE APPROVAL PROCESS:
• Submit the creative (the thumbnail + the headline + the advertorial's URL)
• The review: 24-48h (a manual human review by Taboola — it's strict)
• A rejection: Is common on the first submit (a 50-60% initial rejection rate — you must iterate, resubmit, and learn the guidelines deeply)
• An appeal: Is possible (if you believe the rejection was an error, you can provide substantiation, and it's sometimes overturned)

LADEIRA: "Read the 47-page Taboola guidelines PDF (it's tedious BUT essential — ignorance will get you banned, knowledge will get your campaigns approved, a 2-3h investment in reading will save you R$5-20k in wasted banned campaigns)."
```

---

**THE OUTBRAIN GUIDELINES (Moderate — Balanced):**

Similar to Taboola BUT slightly more lenient (before/afters are sometimes OK, moderate sensationalism is acceptable).

**The review:** 12-24h (faster than Taboola).

**The rejection rate:** 30-40% (lower — the guidelines are less strict).

---

**THE MGID GUIDELINES (Lenient — A Testing Ground):**

**The most permissive** (before/afters are OK, a higher tolerance for sensationalism, the quality of the international traffic is variable BUT it's cheap).

**The review:** 6-12h (the fastest).

**The rejection:** 15-25% (rare — they approve most, the quality control is lower).

**Use it for:** Testing (to validate that an offer converts before investing in the more expensive premium Taboola).

**THE ADVERTORIAL'S COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST:**

```
☐ Is there a disclaimer? ("Advertorial Content" at the top of the page, in a visible 10pt minimum, with a link to a privacy policy)

☐ Is there a dateline/byline? (to simulate a real article — "São Paulo, Nov 19, 2024, 14:37 | By: The Editorial Staff" OR a fictitious author "Maria Silva, a Health Reporter")

☐ Is the tone editorial? (third-person "Dr. Chen states...," not a first-person sales pitch of "I discovered...")

☐ Is the headline accurate? (it promises X, the advertorial delivers a discussion of X — not a "click for something SHOCKING" bait-and-switch → to a boring and unrelated advertorial)

☐ Are the claims substantiated? (a medical claim of "it reduces blood pressure by 15 points" must cite a Harvard/Johns Hopkins study, a financial claim of "a 12% annual return" must cite a backtest data source, you can't make a claim without backing)

☐ Is the before/after compliant? (if you use one, a disclaimer of "results are not typical" + "individual results may vary" is required by the FTC-equivalent, OR avoid it entirely to be safest)

☐ Is the sensationalism moderate? (curiosity is YES, excessive hyperbole is NO — "Discover a method" is OK, "A MIRACLE CURE FOR EVERYTHING" will be rejected)

☐ Is the transition natural? (from the advertorial → to the CTA link for the VSL/sales page, it should flow editorially "to learn more, the specialist has prepared a video explaining..." — NOT a jarring "BUY NOW 50% OFF!!!")

☐ Is the visual quality good? (professional stock images OR real photos, NOT low-res blurry screenshots of memes — publishers reject low quality)

☐ Is the length adequate? (the target is 800-1,200w — too short at 300w is too thin and has an insufficient editorial feel, too long at 2,000w+ is boring and will be abandoned, the sweet spot is 800-1,200 for maintained engagement)

IF 10/10 are ✅: Approval is likely (90%+ of compliant campaigns pass the review)
IF <8/10: A risk of rejection (resubmit after fixing the compliance gaps)
```

**OPTIMIZING THE ADVERTORIAL (To Improve the CTR from the Advertorial → to the VSL/Sales Page):**

---

**TACTIC #1 — Headline Testing (20+ Variations Are Typical for Native):**

**The process:**

Native ad campaigns are creative warfare (there are 100s of competitors, your ad is competing for attention in a widget with a 0.5-sec scroll decision, the headline MUST be instantly intriguing OR it will be ignored).

**Generate:** 20-30 headlines (with the formulas from Doc 6 — Question, Secret, Discovery, Number, Story — with 4-6 variations of each).

**Test:** With a small budget of R$50-100 for each headline (a minimum of 100-200 clicks for statistics), and measure the CTR (clicks ÷ impressions).

**The winner:** Is often a surprise (your favorite might be in the #8 position, while a random #17 wins with a 1.7x higher CTR — the market decides, be humble).

**Iterate:** The winner becomes the control, you generate 20 NEW challengers, and you test again (it's continuous — headline warfare is never-ending in native ads, combating saturation requires constant fresh angles).

**Ladeira:** "I test 40-60 headlines per campaign (does that seem excessive? — native is EXTREMELY competitive, a 0.1% difference in CTR is a R$10k/month difference in revenue at scale, a 3h investment in testing returns R$120k/year in headline optimization, a 40:1+ ROI for testing native headlines)."

---

**TACTIC #2 — A Range of Emotional Thumbnails (Test 10-15):**

**The emotions to test:**
- Shock 😲: A "WHAT?!" expression
- Fear 😨: A worried/anxious face
- Curiosity 🤔: A thoughtful pondering
- Authority 😐: A serious expert
- Joy 😃: A happy transformation
- Anger 😠: A relatable frustrated expression (a stressed entrepreneur's face)

**Test each emotion** (with the same headline, but a different thumbnail emotion — to isolate the variable).

**The winner will vary by niche:**
- For health: A mix of Fear + Authority (a worried doctor = credibility + urgency)
- For finance: Shock + Curiosity (a surprising stat, they want to know why)
- For business: Frustration + Authority (a relatable stressed entrepreneur, a serious expert)

**Ladeira's data:** Emotion > a neutral face with a 2.1x higher CTR on average (a generic neutral smile is ignored, a STRONG emotion stops the scroll).

---

**TACTIC #3 — The Advertorial's Opening Hook (The First 100 Words Are Critical):**

**A/B test the openings:**

**Opening A (A Person-Specific Story):**
"Maria Santos, 52 years old, an accountant in Curitiba, had suffered from chronic lower back pain for 8 years. She had tried 5 treatments (doctors, physical therapy, acupuncture — a total of R$22k spent). Nothing was permanent. In January 2023: She discovered a 7-second technique from Johns Hopkins. Today: She is 95% pain-free, and has maintained it for 22 months. Here's what she did..."

**Opening B (Leading with a Shocking Stat):**
"A shocking study from Johns Hopkins: 83% of chronic lower back pain can be eliminated with a simple 7-second technique (tested on 1,240 patients, published in the peer-reviewed JAMA in 2023). Most chronic sufferers have NEVER heard of it (doctors don't teach it — it's a gap in the standard medical school curriculum). Here is the technique..."

**The test:** Which opening retains them for longer (measure the scroll depth), and has a higher click rate on the CTA.

**The winner:** Will vary (some avatars will prefer the identifying story of Opening A, while others will prefer the authoritative stat of Opening B — the test will decide).

---

**TACTIC #4 — The CTA's Placement in the Advertorial (Single vs. Multiple):**

**The test:**

**Version A:** The CTA is only at the END of the advertorial (after 1,000w of education — a single CTA of "Watch the complete video").

**Version B:** Multiple CTAs (after a 400w teaser + at the complete end of 1,000w — 2 CTAs in total, an early exit option for the engaged + a late exit for the educated).

**Ladeira's test:** Multiple CTAs = a +18-30% in total clicks (some readers decide early after the 400w teaser is enough, and they are captured by CTA #1; others need the full 1,000w to be convinced, and they are captured by CTA #2 — don't force them all to read the complete thing, accommodate their different readiness).

**The best practice:** 2 CTAs (midway at 40-50% of the article + at the 100% end) — a balance that captures the eager + the cautious.

• Visits to the advertorial: 3,125 (100% of the native ad's clicks)
• 50%+ scroll rate: 1,875 (60% — they are engaged and reading, the hook is working)
• 100% scroll rate: 1,250 (40% — they completed the advertorial, they are educated and warm)
• CTA clicks: 625 (50% of the completers → clicked the CTA — the transition was effective, the CTR from the advertorial → to the VSL is 20% of the total visitors, and 50% of the completers)

THE ACTION: 625 clickers → to the VSL page

STEP 3 — THE VSL (45 Min — From Doc 5 Section A.2)

THE COMPLETION RATE (for watching 50%+): 406 (65% — the advertorial warmed them up, their interest was validated by their click, Ladeira's benchmark is a 45-65% completion for warm traffic vs. 25-40% for direct cold traffic)

THE CONVERSION (of completers → to a purchase):
• 50%+ viewers: 406
• Purchasers: 28
• The CVR: 6.9% (of completers → to customers)

THE VSL'S METRICS:
• The CTA appears: At min 35 (Ladeira's optimal timing — value has been delivered for 35 min, the completion is still at 60-70%, the conversion is maximized)

THE CAMPAIGN'S TOTAL METRICS

THE LTV:CAC RATIO: R$10,064 ÷ R$329 = 30.6:1 ✅✅✅ (exceptional — MASSIVE scaling is possible, you can increase the CAC up to R$3,354 and still be 3:1 profitable, with a headroom of an additional R$3,025 to spend/customer = a 10x potential to scale the budget from R$15k → R$150k while maintaining profitability)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A SCALING PROJECTION (Based on the Test's Success)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

IF you scale the budget 10x (from R$15,000 → R$150,000/month):

The assumptions (conservative — the metrics will maintain 80% of the test's performance at scale):
• The CPC: From R$1.60 → R$2.40 (a 50% increase due to competition from a higher bidding at scale, and an increase in volume)
• The CTR: From 0.5% → 0.4% (a slight creative fatigue, a -20% drop)
• The advertorial → to the VSL: From 20% → 16% (a conservative -20%)
• The VSL's conversion: From 6.9% → 5.5% (a slight -20% decline in quality at scale)

The projected scale metrics:
• The budget: R$150,000/month
• The clicks: R$150k ÷ R$2.40 = 62,500
• The advertorial → to the VSL: 62,500 × 16% = 10,000 VSL viewers
• The VSL → to customers: 10,000 × 5.5% = 550 customers/month

The revenue: 550 × R$1,997 = R$1,098,350/month

The scaled CAC: R$150,000 ÷ 550 = R$273/customer (vs. the R$329 of the test — it's actually BETTER due to an economy of scale and efficiencies from the learning curve)

The LTV:CAC: R$10,064 ÷ R$273 = 36.9:1 (it maintains its exceptional status, the scaling is validated)

The profit: R$1,098,350 in revenue - R$150,000 in ads - R$50,000 in ops (the team, the tools, and the overhead have scaled) = R$898,350/month in profit (a 90% margin for a digital product)

ANNUALLY: R$898k × 12 = R$10.78M in profit/year (from a single optimized and scaled native channel — a massive potential validated by a R$15k test)
