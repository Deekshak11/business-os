## **SECTION 2.3 — HIGH-TICKET ($1,000-50,000 — KENNEDY'S TRUST-BASED MARKETING)**

---

**Dan Kennedy** (Trust-Based Marketing, Marketing to the Affluent):

> *"Selling high-ticket is not the same as low-ticket (the psychology is fundamentally different).*
>
> *Low-ticket ($10-500): It's an impulse (a quick decision, a low risk, emotion > logic).*
>
> *High-ticket ($1k-50k): It's a deliberation (a slow decision, a high risk, trust > everything).*
>
> *High-ticket without TRUST = zero sales (it doesn't matter how good the copy, the offer, or the product is — an absence of trust is an impenetrable barrier).*
>
> *Building trust is MANDATORY (it's not optional — you have to move the prospect from 0% trust → to a 70%+ trust threshold for a purchase, which is a journey of weeks-months, with 15-30 touchpoints being typical)."*

---

### **THE TRUST EQUATION REVISITED (From Kennedy — Doc 8 Section 2 Applied to High-Ticket):**

```
TRUST = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Interest

THE HIGH-TICKET REQUIREMENTS (Each Component is Amplified):

CREDIBILITY (That You KNOW — Tier 4 Authority from Doc 8):
• A stack of 7+ elements of credentials (formal education + years of quantified experience + specific results you've achieved + notable client associations + third-party media + awards/recognition + a transparent honesty about your limitations)
• Demonstrated content (a blog, videos, a podcast — your expertise is visible for free, teaching is a signal of authority)
• Industry certifications (if it's regulated — a CFP in finance, coaching certifications, vertical-specific credentials)

For B2C, it's acceptable: To have 3-4 elements of credentials (which is enough for low-ticket)
HIGH-TICKET requires: 6-7 elements (it must be comprehensive, with zero doubt, and an overwhelming authority)

RELIABILITY (That You DO what you promise — Consistency):
• Email consistency (a weekly minimum of 52 sends/year — you never miss one, reliability is demonstrated)
• Promises are kept (if you say "a response in <24h" = you actually respond in <24h, always; if you say "the cohort starts on Monday" = it starts on Monday, it's not delayed; consistency builds trust slowly)
• Under-promise and over-deliver (deliver MORE than you promised — a positive surprise, trust compounds)
• Transparency (admit when you make a mistake, fix it publicly, honesty is long-term reliability)

For LOW-TICKET: Moderate reliability is OK (some delays are forgiven)
For HIGH-TICKET: Reliability MUST be impeccable (a single broken promise destroys trust, a high-ticket prospect is unforgiving, they have multiple competitor options, they will switch instantly)

INTIMACY (That You CARE — A Personal Connection):
• Vulnerability (share your struggles, from the Character Flaws in Doc 3 — it humanizes you, makes you relatable)
• Personal details (your family, hobbies, a life beyond business — intimacy builds)
• A personal response (emails are signed by you, not "the team," some high-potential prospects are replied to personally)
• Calls (voice/video contact — face-to-face intimacy is > distant email)

For LOW-TICKET: A low intimacy is OK (being transactional is acceptable)
For HIGH-TICKET: Intimacy is CRITICAL (a deep relationship is needed — a $10k-50k decision requires ultimate trust, intimacy creates trust, being distant creates resistance)

SELF-INTEREST (To be Minimized — A Consultative Approach, NOT Salesy):
• Give 10x before you ask 1x (from Abraham — extensive free consulting, a detailed diagnosis, honest recommendations even if it's "don't buy from me," a low visible self-interest)
• Transparent pricing (no hidden fees, no surprises — total honesty builds trust)
• Admit your limitations ("I'm NOT an expert in [X], I'll refer you to a specialist in [Y]" — a low self-interest, a high priority on the client's interests is visible)

For HIGH-TICKET: Self-interest must be INVISIBLE (you must appear 100% focused on THEM, and zero-focused on selling — a purely consultative approach, the positioning of a trusted advisor; a vendor is instantly rejected)
```

**The High-Ticket Trust Journey (From 0% → 70%+ — A Timeline of Weeks-Months):**

```
PHASE 1 — AWARENESS (0-20% Trust, Weeks 1-2):

Touchpoint 1: An ad/content (a blog, a podcast, a video — they discover you exist)
→ The action: An opt-in for a lead magnet (free value, the email is captured)

The trust level: 5-10% (just awareness, a glimpse of your credibility)

PHASE 2 — EDUCATION (20-40% Trust, Weeks 3-6):

Touchpoints 2-10: An email nurture sequence (an educational 90/10 from Abraham — it teaches frameworks, case studies, and insights — a R$2-5k equivalent in FREE value is delivered over 4 weeks)
→ The action: Engagement (30-45% opens, 5-12% clicks, some replies — a relationship is being built)

The trust level: 30-40% (credibility is established through teaching, reliability from the consistency of the emails, a moderate intimacy from the stories you've shared)

PHASE 3 — CONSIDERATION (40-60% Trust, Weeks 7-10):

Touchpoints 11-15: Deeper content (an invitation to a webinar, a detailed case study to download, a FREE 30-min 1-on-1 strategy call is offered — a personal diagnosis of their situation, honest recommendations even if it's "it's maybe not a fit for you now, the timing is wrong" — a low self-interest is demonstrated)
→ The action: They attend the webinar (2-5% of the nurture list), OR they book a strategy call (5-15% of the highly engaged)

The trust level: 50-60% (intimacy is increased with personal interactions, self-interest is perceived as low with a consultative approach, credibility is reinforced with the webinar's expertise)

PHASE 4 — THE DECISION (60-70%+ Trust, Weeks 11-12):

Touchpoint 16: An application (to qualify them — an extensive 10-15 questions, a commitment is demonstrated by filling it out, psychological barriers are crossed)
→ Touchpoint 17: A call is scheduled (for a qualified applicant, 60-80% of applications → booked calls)
→ Touchpoint 18: A pre-call email (for preparation — "bring [X], think about [Y], the call's agenda is [Z]" — professionalism, respect for their time)
→ Touchpoint 19: THE CALL (30-60 min — a deep diagnosis, a custom strategic roadmap, a mutual decision of "is this a fit for both of us?" honestly)
→ Touchpoint 20: A post-call follow-up (an email summary of the decisions, the next steps if it's a yes, and a gracious response if it's a no — professionalism regardless)

The trust level: 70-80% (the threshold is crossed — a purchase decision will be made IF the call went well, it's a mutual fit, and the solution is clear)

THE CLOSE RATE: 20-40% of calls → high-ticket customers (the trust is at 70%+ going into the call, the closing is natural, not pressured, and a consultative approach is maintained throughout)

THE TOTAL TIMELINE: 12 weeks is typical (from awareness → to a decision — it's a slow, deliberate process, trust is built systematically, you can't rush high-ticket).

THE TOTAL TOUCHPOINTS: 20+ (emails, content, a webinar, calls, follow-ups — a multi-touch approach is essential, a single-touch high-ticket has an impossible <1% conversion).
```

**The High-Ticket Copy Structure (Different from Low-Ticket):**

---

**THE HEADLINE (Authority + ROI, Not Hype):**

❌ **The low-ticket hype:** "Get RICH QUICK — An INCREDIBLE and AMAZING System!"

✅ **The high-ticket authority:** "Private Mentorship: Scale to $1M in Revenue in 18 Months (The Avg Client Result is $847k in Year 1, with a 12:1 ROI — Limited to 12 Clients/Year, an Application is Required)"

**The elements of a high-ticket headline:**
- Implied authority ("Private Mentorship," "Limited to 12 clients" — for exclusivity)
- A specific and realistic outcome ($1M in 18 months — it's bold BUT has a believable timeline, not an absurd "30 days")
- Social proof (an avg client result of $847k — data from a track record)
- A stated ROI (12:1 — the language of a CFO for business buyers)
- Qualification ("An Application is Required" — to filter for the serious, not a desperate open-to-all)

---

**THE LEAD (From the Problem → to a Cost Quantification → to a Diagnostic Offer):**

```
A HIGH-TICKET LEAD EXAMPLE (For a $25,000 Business Scaling Mentorship):

"You are the CEO/founder of a business with $500k-3M in revenue (a specific range — for self-selection).

BUT: Your growth has stagnated over the last 12-24 months (a common plateau in this revenue range — for identification).

You work 60-80h/week (in an operational role, not a strategic one — IN the business, not ON the business).

You're hitting an invisible revenue ceiling (you've tried to scale — hiring didn't work and your margins collapsed, you increased your marketing spend but your revenue was flat with a negative ROI, and you have no systems, just operational chaos).

THE COST of this plateau (quantified for your business):

Your potential revenue (the industry is growing at 15-25%/year, you are stuck at 0-5%):
• Your current revenue: $2M
• Your potential (at the 20% industry pace): $2.4M (a +$400k/year it should be)
• The gap: $400,000/year LEFT ON THE TABLE (the opportunity cost of the plateau — your competitors are capturing the growth, you're missing it)

If the plateau continues for 3 years (if you don't fix it):
• The opportunity cost: $400k × 3 years = $1.2 MILLION in lost potential (an invisible bleeding — the revenue you COULD have had BUT didn't, gone forever)

The personal cost (beyond the numbers):
• Burnout: 60-80h is unsustainable (your health is deteriorating, your relationships are strained, the joy of work is lost — a plateau is psychologically destructive)
• A psychological ceiling: "Maybe $2M is my limit" (doubt creeps in, confidence erodes, your identity as an entrepreneur is questioned — it's painful)

A FREE DIAGNOSIS (A 30-Min Strategy Call):

Before I pitch you (anything), I'll diagnose your situation SPECIFICALLY:
• Where exactly are you stuck? (in operations? marketing? sales? the team? the systems? — to pinpoint the bottleneck in YOUR business)
• Why are you hitting a ceiling? (the 2-3 root causes are identified — not the symptoms, the FUNDAMENTAL blockers)
• The potential roadmap: IF you get unstuck, where could you be in 18 months? (a realistic projection for YOUR context, not generic promises)

The call is consultative (a deep diagnosis, honest recommendations — even IF "it's not a fit for us to work together now, here's what you can fix on your own in the next 6 months" — a low self-interest is demonstrated, you'll be helped regardless of a purchase).

A ZERO-pitch call (it's a diagnosis only — trust is built, not a sales pressure).

After the call: You decide (is it a fit? Do you want help? OR do you want to DIY the free recommendations I gave you? — it's autonomous, with total respect).

The investment for the call: $0 (a free 30-min strategy call — typically a $500-1,000 consulting value, yours complimentary, with zero obligation).

```

---

**THE BODY (An Ultra-Detailed Case Study, with an Audit-Level Documented ROI):**

```
A CASE STUDY — TECHSOLUTIONS INC (A High-Ticket B2B SaaS Client):

[An 800-1,500 word case study — similar to the structure in Doc 8 Section 1.4 BUT with:]

THE HIGH-TICKET DIFFERENCES:

• MORE transparent financials (the before/after revenue, the profit margins, the EBITDA growth — a CFO-level of detail, not just a vague "revenue increased")
• A week-by-week timeline (the 18-month journey — a breakdown of each quarter, the milestones, and the setbacks are included honestly, it's not a sanitized perfect story)
• The investment is disclosed (a $25,000 mentorship fee — for total transparency, the ROI is calculated against it: a $25k investment → an additional $2.1M in revenue over 18 months = a documented 84:1 ROI)
• EXTENSIVE verification (audited excerpts of financial statements, a detailed 5-min CEO video testimonial, a mutual connection on LinkedIn to verify the real company, a third-party press mention in a TechCrunch article featuring their growth)

[A high-ticket case study has an AUDIT-LEVEL of detail (for a CFO/board that is reviewing it, with extreme skepticism, the proof must withstand professional scrutiny, a casual testimonial is not enough)]
```

---

**THE OFFER (The Investment is Framed, Not a "Price"):**

**The language is different for high-ticket:**

❌ **For low-ticket:** "The price: R$1,997"

✅ **For high-ticket:** "The investment: $25,000 (for a 12-month partnership, with a minimum 10:1 ROI target — the average client achieves 15:1, and YOUR custom projection will be discussed on the call)"

**The framing:**
- "An investment," not a "price" (it's a business term, it implies an ROI, it's professional)
- "A partnership," not a "purchase" (a 12-month long-term relationship, not a single transaction)
- The ROI target is stated (a 10:1 minimum — the CFO's language, the expectations are clear)
- A custom projection (specific to YOU — for personalization, not a generic one for everyone)

---

**THE GUARANTEE (Stronger, OR Performance-Based):**

**The high-ticket guarantee options:**

**Option A — An extended timeframe:**
"A 12-month guarantee (not a short 30-60 days) — if at ANY time in the first year you decide it's not a fit, you'll get a 100% refund + you can keep all the templates/recordings/resources that have been delivered (an $8,000 value)."

**Option B — Results-based + a compensation for their time:**
"The guarantee: Achieve a minimum of $500k in additional revenue in 18 months OR you'll get a $25,000 refund + a $25,000 compensation for YOUR time invested (a total of $50,000 that you will receive if we don't deliver — a NEGATIVE risk for you, only upside)."

**Kennedy for the affluent:** "A compensation for their time is critical for the affluent (their money is < their time's value — compensating their time is the ULTIMATE signal of confidence in your product + a respect for the client's time's worth)."

---

**THE CTA (Multi-Step, with Qualification, Not a Direct Buy):**

❌ **For low-ticket:** A "Buy Now" button (a direct purchase)

✅ **For high-ticket:** "Schedule a Complimentary Strategy Call (30 Min — To Diagnose YOUR Situation Specifically, with Zero Obligation, the Investment Decision is Made AFTER a Mutual Fit is Determined on the Call)"

**A high-ticket CTA:**
- Is soft ("Schedule a call" NOT "Buy")
- Emphasizes that it's free ("Complimentary" — zero risk of time/money)
- States the value ("To Diagnose YOUR situation" — personalized, consultative)
- Has zero obligation (it removes the pressure — their autonomous decision is respected)
- Implies a mutual fit (it's a TWO-WAY decision — you are also qualifying THEM, which implies exclusivity)
