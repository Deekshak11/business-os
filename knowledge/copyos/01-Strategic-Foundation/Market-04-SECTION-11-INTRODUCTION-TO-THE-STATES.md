## **SECTION 1.1 — INTRODUCTION TO THE STATES**

---

### **THE DISCOVERY (Schwartz, 1966):**

Eugene M. Schwartz, *Breakthrough Advertising* (1966), Chapter 2, pages 12-18:

> *"Your market is not a single, uniform mass.*
>
> *It is a series of layers — of audiences at different levels of awareness of your product and what it does.*
>
> *Each level — each degree of awareness — requires a radically different approach in your advertising."*

---

**Context of the discovery:**

Schwartz (20 years of writing copy, 1946-1966) observed a disturbing pattern:

The **same copy** for the same product:
- ✅ Worked PERFECTLY with audience A (high conversion)
- ❌ FAILED COMPLETELY with audience B (zero conversion)

**Same elements:**
- Same product (identical)
- Same offer (price, terms, guarantee)
- Same copy (headline, bullets, proof)
- Same quality of execution

**The only difference:** THE AUDIENCE.

---

**Schwartz investigated:** "What makes audiences A and B different?"

**The breakthrough discovery:**

It wasn't demographics (age, income, location — they were similar).

It wasn't psychographics (values, beliefs — they were similar).

**It was their AWARENESS LEVEL** (where they were in their knowledge journey about the solution).

---

**Audience A:** Already knew the product, knew what it did, and wanted to buy it (they just needed a deal).

**Short, transactional copy** = perfect (direct offer, urgency, done).

---

**Audience B:** Didn't know the product, didn't know solutions existed, and barely recognized their problem.

**The same short copy** = total confusion ("A discount on what? What is this? Why do I need it?").

**The copy needed:** Long-form education (to build awareness, explain the problem, reveal the solution, and THEN offer the product).

---

**The Insight:**

> *"The copy that works depends less on WHAT you're selling than on WHERE your prospect is in their awareness journey."*

**The market = layers of awareness** (not a uniform mass).

**Each layer = a radically different copy approach.**

---

### **THE 5 STATES (Layers of Awareness):**

Schwartz identified **5 distinct levels** (from most aware to least):

---

**STATE 1 — MOST AWARE:**

The customer knows YOUR PRODUCT specifically.

They know what it does, how it works, and that they want to buy it.

They just need: A deal (the best offer, a push to act now).

**Copy:** Short (300-800w), transactional.

---

**STATE 2 — PRODUCT-AWARE:**

The customer knows that PRODUCTS of this type exist (category awareness).

They know 2-3 alternatives/competitors.

They haven't decided WHICH one to buy.

They need: Differentiation (why yours > others).

**Copy:** Medium (1k-3kw), differentiation-focused.

---

**STATE 3 — SOLUTION-AWARE:**

The customer knows that SOLUTIONS for their problem exist (in a general sense).

They don't know YOUR product yet.

They need: To identify your product as THE solution.

**Copy:** Medium-long (3k-6kw), educational + positioning.

---

**STATE 4 — PROBLEM-AWARE:**

The customer knows they HAVE A PROBLEM (they feel the pain).

They DON'T know that solutions exist (or they think it's impossible to solve).

They need: To create hope (a solution is possible) + reveal your solution.

**Copy:** Long (6k-10kw), deep agitation + education.

---

**STATE 5 — UNAWARE:**

The customer DOESN'T KNOW they have a problem.

Or they know but don't consider it serious.

They need: To create awareness of the problem + the solution.

**Copy:** Very long (8k-12kw), story/discovery-driven.

---

### **Why States Matter (Critical Importance):**

**Fatal Error #1 — Mismatch (Copy for the Wrong State):**

Writing State 1 copy (short, direct offer) for a State 5 audience (who don't know they have a problem):

**Result:** Total confusion.

Prospect: "Discount on what? What is this? Why do I need it? Who are you?"

Bounce rate: 90%+

Conversion: ~0%

---

Writing State 5 copy (long, story-driven, educational) for a State 1 audience (who already want to buy):

**Result:** Impatience.

Prospect: "I ALREADY KNOW what it is. I ALREADY WANT to buy it. Why are you making me read a 10,000-word story? Get to the point!"

Bounce: 70%+

Conversion: -60% vs. appropriate copy

---

**Schwartz tested this** (1950s-1960s, hundreds of campaigns):

**Product X, Appropriate Copy for the State:**

State 1 audience + State 1 copy = 12% CVR ✅

State 5 audience + State 5 copy = 4% CVR ✅ (lower but appropriate — cold traffic)

---

**Product X, INAPPROPRIATE Copy:**

State 1 audience + State 5 copy = 3% CVR ❌ (-75% vs. appropriate)

State 5 audience + State 1 copy = 0.2% CVR ❌ (-95% vs. appropriate)

---

**The difference: 300-500% in conversion** (diagnosing correctly vs. getting it wrong).

---

Schwartz:

> *"Write copy for the wrong level of awareness and you might as well not write at all.*
>
> *More money is wasted on great copy addressed to the wrong awareness level than on poor copy addressed to the right level."*

---

**Implication:**

**Diagnosing the State = THE FIRST STEP** (before the headline, before the structure, before ANY word is written).

Wrong State = all subsequent work is wasted.

---

### **Preview of the 5 States (Visual Table):**

| State | What the Customer Knows | Copy Length | Headline Type | Lead Type | When to Use |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| **1-Most Aware** | Your product, wants it | 300-800w | Direct Offer | Offer Lead | Existing customers, hot traffic |
| **2-Product-Aware** | Category, options | 1k-3kw | USP/Differentiation | Promise Lead | Comparison shopping, multiple players |
| **3-Solution-Aware** | Solutions exist | 3k-6kw | News/How-to | Problem-Solution | Researching solutions, generic Google search |
| **4-Problem-Aware** | Has a problem | 6k-10kw | Question/Problem | Story Lead | Suffering, forums, chronic pain |
| **5-Unaware** | Nothing (problem unrecognized) | 8k-12kw | Story/Curiosity | Secret/Story | Cold, native ads, discovery |

---

**A clear pattern:**

The LESS aware → The MORE copy is needed (education, awareness creation, trust building).

The MORE aware → The LESS copy is needed (they already know, they just need a trigger).

---

### **How to Diagnose the State (Quick Process):**

**Questions for the avatar (interview or inferred via research):**

---

**Question #1:** "Do you know [YOUR PRODUCT by name]?"

**YES** → State 1 or 2 (they know you)

**NO** → State 3, 4, or 5 (they don't know you yet)

---

**Question #2** (if NO to #1): "Do you know that products/solutions for [the problem] exist?"

**YES** → State 3 (solution-aware)

**NO** → State 4 or 5 (they don't know about solutions)

---

**Question #3** (if NO to #2): "Do you know you have [the problem]? Do you consider it serious?"

**YES, it's serious** → State 4 (problem-aware, suffering)

**YES, but not serious** → State 5 (aware but normalized)

**NO** → State 5 (completely unaware)

---

**Decision Tree:**

```
Do they know your product?
    ├─ YES → Do they want to buy?
    │         ├─ YES → STATE 1 (Most Aware)
    │         └─ NO → STATE 2 (Product-Aware, comparing)
    │
    └─ NO → Do they know solutions exist?
              ├─ YES → STATE 3 (Solution-Aware)
              └─ NO → Do they know they have a problem?
                        ├─ YES, it's serious → STATE 4 (Problem-Aware)
                        └─ NO or not serious → STATE 5 (Unaware)
```

---

**Additional validation** (confirming signs):

**State 1:** They ask about price/availability (not "what is it?"), mention the product by name.

**State 2:** They compare products ("X vs. Y?"), read multiple reviews.

**State 3:** They Google "[how to solve problem]" (generic solutions), consume educational content.

**State 4:** They're on forums ("has anyone solved this?"), use emotional language ("suffering," "desperate").

**State 5:** They aren't actively searching, they discover via content/ads (serendipity).

---

**Diagnostic time:** 30-60 minutes (interviewing 3-5 people OR analyzing traffic sources/behavior).

---

### **Implications for Copy (A Teaser — Details in 1.2-1.6):**

**The State determines:**

1. **Length** (300w for State 1 vs. 12,000w for State 5)
2. **Headline type** (Offer vs. Story)
3. **Lead type** (6 Masterson types — Doc 6, choice based on State)
4. **Structure** (transactional vs. educational vs. story-driven)
5. **Proof level** (minimal for State 1 vs. massive for State 5)
6. **Education needed** (zero for State 1 vs. extensive for State 5)
7. **Problem agitation** (minimal for State 1-2 vs. deep for State 4-5)

**Diagnosing it wrong = all elements are wrong = failure.**
