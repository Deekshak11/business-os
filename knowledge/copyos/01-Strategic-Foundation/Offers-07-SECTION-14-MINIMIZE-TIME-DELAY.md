## **SECTION 1.4 — MINIMIZE TIME DELAY**

---

### **TACTICS:**

**TACTIC #1 — QUICK WINS STRUCTURE:**

Timeline breakdown:

"Days 1-7: [A small but real quick win]
Days 8-30: [Significant progress]
Days 31-90: [Complete transformation]"

**Example:**

```
Days 1-7: First client outreach (ready-made cold email templates, you send 10-20, typically get 2-4 responses), confidence boost ("people are responding!")

Days 8-30: First client closed (60-70% probability in this timeframe, tracked), first project for R$500-2,000, proof of concept ("I CAN sell copywriting")

Days 31-90: Consistent clients (2-3 projects completed, pipeline of 4-6 prospects, rhythm established), total revenue of R$5-15k generated, skill solidified
```

**Quick win on day 7:** Maintains motivation (they saw SOMETHING, not waiting 90 days).

**Visible progression:** = hope is sustained (each milestone achieved = belief the next milestone is possible).

---

**TACTIC #2 — IMMEDIATE ACCESS (Instant Gratification):**

Digital products: "Access in 60 seconds after purchase (instant email, password works immediately, streaming available now)."

**Not:** "We'll send the link in 24-48h" (delay = excitement cools).

**Instant:** = endorphin hit maintained (purchase → immediate access → excitement → engagement starts → momentum).

---

**TACTIC #3 — TIMELINE FRAMING (Optimistic Language):**

Kahneman Doc 1 Section 3.6 (Framing): Same duration, different frame = different perception.

```
Pessimistic: "A long 90-day wait"
↓
Neutral: "90 days"
↓
Optimistic: "In just 90 days" or "A quick 3 months" or "12 short weeks"
```

**Words:** "Just," "quick," "short" vs. "long," "wait" (psychological framing).

**Same duration** (90 days), perceived as shorter (optimistic framing).

---

**TACTIC #4 — COMPARISON TO A SLOWER ALTERNATIVE:**

"90 days (vs. 3 years of traditional classes — 12x faster)."

Comparison: Makes 90 days seem FAST (vs. standing alone, 90 days might seem long).

Contrast: = perceived time compression (Doc 1 Section 3.4 Anchoring — 3 years is the anchor, 90 days = a temporal bargain).
