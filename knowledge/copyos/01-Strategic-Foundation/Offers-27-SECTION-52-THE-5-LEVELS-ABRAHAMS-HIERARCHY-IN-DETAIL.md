## **SECTION 5.2 — THE 5 LEVELS (ABRAHAM'S HIERARCHY) IN DETAIL**

---

### **5.2.1 — LEVEL 1: BASIC MONEY-BACK (The Bare Minimum)**

---

**Standard formula:**

"Satisfaction guaranteed or your money back"

---

**Component analysis:**

**"Satisfaction"** — A vague term (subjective, what is "satisfied"? Each person defines it differently).

**"Guaranteed"** — A promise (BUT with no timeframe = when does it expire? Forever? 30 days? Unclear).

**"Money back"** — A refund (BUT how much? 100%? Partial? It doesn't specify).

---

**Problems (why it's weak):**

❌ **Extreme vagueness:** Zero specificity ("satisfaction" isn't defined, the timeframe isn't stated, the amount isn't clear).

Prospect: "Satisfaction how? How long do I have to test it? How much do I get back?" (confusion, doubt).

❌ **Generic:** Literally EVERYONE offers a "satisfaction guarantee" (a commodity, it doesn't differentiate).

Search on Amazon: 90%+ of products have a "satisfaction guaranteed" (meaningless saturation).

❌ **Low credibility:** Vague terms = suspicion ("it's probably difficult to claim, the fine print hides restrictions").

❌ **High-friction claim:** Vague = you can dispute it ("you weren't truly unsatisfied" — a subjective fight).

The customer fears: "They're going to make it difficult to get a refund" (high skepticism).

---

**When to use it (barely acceptable):**

⚠️ **Minimum legal requirement:** Some jurisdictions require it (consumer protection laws).

⚠️ **Better than nothing:** If you absolutely can't offer anything better (extreme risk aversion, new and untested product).

**BUT:** You can virtually always offer Level 2+ (being specific costs $0 extra, it's just about clarity).

---

**Conversion impact:**

Baseline (assumed to be 100% for comparison with other levels).

**vs. No guarantee:** +20-30% (something is better than nothing).

**vs. Levels 2-5:** -15% to -70% (weak in comparison).

---

**Refund rate:** 8-12% (vagueness = easier to abuse, lower initial commitment).

---

**Recommendation:** **AVOID Level 1** (use a minimum of Level 2 — it costs nothing to upgrade, and clarity = credibility).

---

### **5.2.2 — LEVEL 2: SPECIFIC MONEY-BACK (Acceptable Standard)**

---

**Improved formula:**

"[Timeframe] days, no questions asked, 100% refund"

**Example:** "30 days, no questions asked, 100% refund"

---

**Improvements vs. Level 1:**

✅ **Specific timeframe:** "30 days" (clarity — the customer knows exactly how long they have to test and can plan accordingly).

✅ **"No questions asked":** Removes friction (they don't need to justify/explain — it's an automatic, easy claim).

✅ **"100%":** Explicit (not partial, not "up to 80%" — it's total).

---

**Why it's better:**

**Clarity = credibility** (specific = thought-through, not a vague, forgettable promise).

**"No questions asked":** = psychological safety ("they won't make it difficult, they won't question me, no confrontation" — ease of claim).

**30 days:** = sufficient testing time (1 month is reasonable to try the product and see if it works).

---

**Common variations:**

- **30 days:** Standard for e-commerce, low-ticket info products (<$500)
- **60 days:** Mid-ticket info products ($500-2,000), SaaS software
- **90 days:** High-ticket info products ($2k+), consulting, transformations that require time

**Choose the timeframe:** Based on how long it REALLY takes to test/see results (an honest assessment).

A product with fast results (7-14 days): 30 days is sufficient.

A product with slow results (a 60-90 day transformation): 60-90 days are necessary (30 days = insufficient testing time, frustration).

---

**Conversion impact:**

**+15-25% vs. Level 1** (specificity = increased trust).

**Industry standard:** Most use Level 2 (it's acceptable, expected).

**Differentiation:** Minimal (everyone has 30-60 days) — not a competitive advantage.

---

**Refund rate:** 6-10% (slightly better than Level 1, clarity helps BUT it's still an accessible claim).

---

**When to use:** The acceptable minimum for most products (don't skip the guarantee, use a minimum of Level 2).

**Better:** Levels 3-5 (for differentiation and a bigger conversion boost).

---

### **5.2.3 — LEVEL 3: BETTER-THAN-MONEY-BACK (Ideal for Info Products)**

---

**Superior formula:**

"[60-90] days: 100% refund + KEEP all the bonuses (R$[X] value)"

**Complete example:**

"60-Day Better-Than-Money-Back Guarantee:

If within 60 days you decide this product isn't for you (for any reason — no questions asked):

You get a 100% refund (a full R$997 refund).

And: You KEEP ALL 5 bonuses:
• Templates Vault (R$500 value)
• Swipe File of 200+ (R$300 value)
• VIP Group Access (R$800 value — lifetime, you keep it even if you refund the core)
• [Bonuses 4-5...]

Total bonuses kept: R$1,600+

The result: Even if you refund the core (you get R$997 back), you KEEP R$1,600 in bonuses (a net gain of R$600+ for you).

You literally CANNOT LOSE (worst case = the bonuses are free, best case = the core works + you get the bonuses)."

---

**Psychological power (multi-mechanism):**

**A. Zero downside** (Doc 1 Section 3.5 — Loss Aversion):

Worst case: Refund the core BUT keep R$1,600 in bonuses = a GAIN of R$600 (not a loss).

Best case: The core works + bonuses = total value of R$3,597.

**Downside eliminated:** = loss aversion is completely neutralized (you can't lose, you can only gain).

Overwhelming logic: "It's literally risk-free, there's only an upside."

---

**B. Amplified reciprocity** (Doc 1 Section 2.1):

You give them R$1,600 in bonuses to KEEP (a massive favor — Regan's Coke for R$0.10 → +82%, here R$1,600 → a massive effect).

The obligation to reciprocate: Intense (you gave SO much, they must reciprocate — by buying + trying it seriously).

---

**C. Endowment effect** (Doc 1 Section 3.7):

The customer "owns" the bonuses (they're kept after purchase and used during the trial).

Endowment: They value the bonuses 2-3x more (ownership increases value).

Refunding the core: = giving back what they feel they're endowed with (painful) — less likely to refund.

**Kept bonuses:** = partial value is retained (psychology: "I got something worthwhile, it wasn't a total waste, maybe I'll keep trying the core").

---

**D. Guilt removal:**

A typical refund: The customer feels guilt ("I'm taking it back, I wasted their time/resources").

Better-than: NO guilt (you WANT them to keep the bonuses — "it's a gift, not a loan" — a guilt-free refund).

**Guilt-free:** = easier to claim (psychologically), but paradoxically LESS likely to be claimed (the guilt of commitment doesn't exist BUT commitment remains via the owned bonuses).

---

**Conversion impact:**

**+40-60% vs. Level 1** (zero downside = a massive objection removed).

**+25-35% vs. Level 2** (better-than adds significant value).

---

**Refund rate:**

**4-6%** (LOWER than Level 2 — the paradox).

**Why lower:**
- The customer kept the bonuses (R$1,600 value) — less likely to refund everything when partial value has already been obtained
- The endowment effect (the bonuses are owned, harder to give back even the core)
- Commitment via reciprocity (you gave so much, they're committed to trying harder)

**Paradox validated:** A better guarantee (more generous) = fewer refunds (filtering + commitment + endowment).

---

**When to use (highly recommended):**

✅ **Info products** (digital — bonuses cost $0 to deliver, high perceived value)

✅ **Courses/training** (a modules/bonuses structure is natural)

✅ **SaaS** (keep access to certain features even if they cancel the main one)

✅ **Any product with stackable bonuses** (better-than requires bonuses to exist — if there are no bonuses, you can't use it)

---

**ROI for you:**

Conversions: +40-60% (more sales).

Refunds: -20-40% (fewer refunds vs. Level 2).

**Net revenue:** +50-90% (a double win — more conversions, fewer refunds).

**Cost:** $0 (digital bonuses, zero marginal cost).

**Pure upside:** Level 3 vs. Level 2.

---

**Abraham tested** (direct mail in the 1990s-2000s):

Better-than (keep the bonuses) vs. Standard (money-back only):

**Conversions:** +52% (better-than won).

**Refunds:** 4.2% vs. 7.1% (better-than was LOWER — paradox confirmed).

**Net revenue:** +89% (conversions ↑↑ + refunds ↓ = a massive win).

**Conclusion:** "Better-than is FREE money (it costs nothing to implement with digital bonuses, conversions spike, and refunds drop — a no-brainer upgrade from Level 2)."

---

### **5.2.4 — LEVEL 4: RESULTS GUARANTEE (CONDITIONAL — Hormozi's Favorite, Ideal for Most)**

---

**Optimal formula:**

"[90-120] days: If you [APPLY the complete method/system — a specific condition] and do NOT get [a specific, measurable MINIMUM RESULT], you get a 100% refund + [keep the bonuses if stacking Levels 3 + 4]"

---

**Detailed example:**

```
90-DAY RESULTS-BASED GUARANTEE:

THE CONDITION (you must):
1. Complete modules 1-12 (watch all videos — progress is tracked on the platform, 100% completion is required)
2. Do the exercises in each module (submit the assignments — a minimum of 10 out of 12 must be submitted and validated)
3. Participate in the group calls (attend a minimum of 3 out of 6 bi-weekly calls — an engagement requirement)
4. Do prospect outreach (a minimum of 20 outreach attempts — emails sent, DMs, calls are tracked — activity is validated)

And you do NOT get:
• Your first copywriting sale (a minimum R$500 project — with invoice/screenshot proof)
• Within 90 days (from the purchase date)

THEN you receive:
• A 100% refund (R$2,997 back, full)
• You KEEP all the bonuses (R$1,200 value — templates, swipes, lifetime group access)
• Zero questions asked (we honor the conditional terms, an automatic refund process)

No bureaucracy. No justifications. We honor the contract.
```

---

**Critical elements:**

**A. CONDITIONAL (Not unconditional):**

**Requires:** The customer to apply the method (completion, participation, activity — effort/commitment).

**Filters:** Tire-kickers self-select OUT (they read the conditions and think, "I have to work? Too much effort, I'll pass").

**Only the committed:** Buy (they know they need to work, they accept it, and they're willing).

**Result:** The customer base = high-quality (committed, not casual browsers).

---

**B. SPECIFIC RESULT (Measurable, Objective):**

**No:** "Satisfaction," "improvement," "results" (subjective, vague).

**Yes:** "First sale of R$500+" (a clear yes/no — they either achieved it or they didn't, an invoice proves it).

**Measurable:** = no dispute (they either sold or they didn't, it's objective).

**Minimum:** Realistic (a first sale of R$500 is achievable, not R$50k — it sets a reasonable bar).

---

**C. SUFFICIENT TIMEFRAME:**

**90-120 days:** Sufficient to learn + apply + see results (not a rushed 30 days, not indefinite).

A balance: Long enough for a fair test (the customer has real time to try), short enough to be contained (not open-ended for years).

---

**D. KEEP BONUSES (Stacking Levels 3+4):**

The better-than element is added (they keep R$1,200 in bonuses even if they refund the core).

**Best of both:** Conditional (filtering) + Better-than (zero downside).

---

**Why Level 4 > Level 3 (conditional vs. unconditional):**

---

**Level 3 (unconditional better-than):**

Anyone can refund (for any reason, without conditions).

**Result:** Some tire-kickers buy (low barrier) → don't use it seriously (low commitment) → refund (an easy out).

Refund rate: 4-6%.

---

**Level 4 (conditional results-based):**

They must apply it completely (the condition) to qualify for a refund.

**Result:**
- Tire-kickers DON'T buy (they self-filter — "too much work required, not for me")
- The committed buy (they accept the terms, they're willing to work)
- The committed → apply it → get results (the majority — 85-92% get results when they apply it fully)
- Results → they're happy → they DON'T refund (success = satisfaction)

Refund rate: **2-4%** (HALF of Level 3).

---

**BUT the conversion:**

Level 3: +40-60% vs. Level 1.

Level 4: +50-70% vs. Level 1.

**Level 4 is BETTER** in both dimensions (higher conversions AND lower refunds).

---

**Why conversions are HIGHER even with a condition (counter-intuitive):**

**Conditional = an EXTREME credibility signal.**

"They're guaranteeing a SPECIFIC result (the first sale) conditional on you applying it.

The logic: They would only offer this if it REALLY works (absolute confidence — otherwise they'd lose massively by refunding everyone).

Therefore: The product MUST work (an inference — the strength of the guarantee = a signal of the product's strength)."

**Perceived Likelihood:** ↑↑↑ (Value Equation — a conditional results guarantee = the highest tier of credibility).

---

**Hormozi tested** (Gym Launch, sold licensing to 1,000+ gyms, 2017-2020):

**Guarantee A (Unconditional 30-day):**

Conversions: 18.2% (baseline).

Refunds: 6.8%.

Net: 17.0% (18.2% - 6.8% refunded).

---

**Guarantee B (Conditional results-based 90-day):**

"If you run our system for 90 days (attend the calls, implement the playbook, track the metrics) and don't add a minimum of 30 net members to your gym, you get a 100% refund."

Conversions: 24.7% (**+36% vs. A**).

Refunds: 2.1% (**-69% refund rate vs. A**).

Net: 24.2% (24.7% - 2.1% refunded).

---

**Net conversions:** 24.2% vs. 17.0% = **+42% NET** (after refunds).

**Conditional > Unconditional** on both metrics (conversions UP, refunds DOWN).

---

**Hormozi:**

> *"A results guarantee (conditional) is BEST for most products.*
>
> *It filters out tire-kickers (they don't buy — good, you don't want them).*
>
> *It attracts the committed (they buy — good, they apply it, get results, and become happy customers).*
>
> *It signals confidence (you only offer it if the product works — credibility).*
>
> *Lower refunds (committed customers = results = satisfaction).*
>
> *Higher conversions (the credibility signal overcomes the conditional 'work required' barrier).*
>
> *It's a win-win-win (for you, the customer, and the health of the business)."*

---

**When to use (IDEAL for most):**

✅ **Info products** ($500-5,000 — results are measurable, the effort required is reasonable)

✅ **Coaching/training** (transformation requires participation — a condition is natural)

✅ **Software/SaaS** (usage-based — "if you use it for X hours and don't see Y result, you get a refund")

✅ **Services** (deliverables are measurable)

✅ **Any product:** Where the results are objectively MEASURABLE (a clear yes/no, not subjective).

---

**When NOT to use:**

❌ **Results are NOT measurable:** Subjective outcomes (happiness, satisfaction — you can't measure them objectively).

Better: Level 3 (an unconditional better-than).

❌ **The effort required is EXTREME:** If applying the method requires 40h/week of effort (an unreasonable barrier).

Better: Lower the effort (make the method easier) OR use Level 3.

---

### **5.2.5 — LEVEL 5: PERFORMANCE/PENALTY GUARANTEE (The Ultimate — High-Ticket)**

---

**VERSION A — Pay-for-Performance (Results-Based Payment):**

---

**Formula:**

"Pay $0 upfront. We implement the [solution]. After a [period], you pay based on the RESULTS achieved. Zero results = zero payment."

---

**Example (ROI-based Consulting):**

```
PERFORMANCE GUARANTEE STRUCTURE:

UPFRONT: $0 (you pay nothing initially — zero risk)

IMPLEMENTATION: 12 weeks (our team implements the complete system — you participate, BUT we do the heavy lifting)

AFTER 90 DAYS (implementation + 30 days for results):

We measure: The increase in revenue OR cost savings (documented, verified in your books/reports)

PAYMENT TIERS (based on results):

Tier 1 — No results (<$25k impact):
You pay: $0 (we failed, you owe nothing)

Tier 2 — Moderate results ($25k-75k impact):
You pay: $10,000 (40% of the low-end results, we share the gain)

Tier 3 — Good results ($75k-150k impact):
You pay: $25,000 (33% of the mid-tier results)

Tier 4 — Excellent results ($150k+ impact):
You pay: $40,000 (capped — a max of 27% even if the impact is $500k+, your upside is unlimited)

THE GUARANTEE: If there's zero impact (Tier 1), there's zero payment. We worked for free (our risk is total).
```

---

**Psychological (from the client's perspective):**

**Risk:** ZERO (they don't pay upfront, they only pay IF there are results — they cannot lose).

**Upside:** Unlimited (Tier 4 caps your fee at $40k even if they gain $500k — 92.5% of the upside is theirs).

**Confidence signal:** ULTIMATE ("they'd only offer pay-for-performance if they're CERTAIN it delivers — otherwise they'd be working for free and losing massively").

**Perceived likelihood:** 100% ("they're SO confident, they're guaranteeing their own payment on the results — it must work").

---

**Conversion:**

**80-95%** (almost everyone — zero risk, overwhelming logic).

**The "it's expensive" objection:** Eliminated (they don't pay until they see results — the expense is justified by the results themselves).

**The "does it work?" objection:** Eliminated (performance-based = you only pay if it works).

---

**YOUR risk:**

**TOTAL** (you do the work, you might not get paid anything if there are zero results).

**Mitigation:** Rigorous qualification (only accept clients with a high probability of success — screen, interview, validate the fit).

**Requirement:** EXTREME confidence in your product/service (only offer this if you KNOW you can deliver in 90%+ of cases — otherwise you'll be working for free repeatedly).

---

**VERSION B — Penalty Guarantee (Time Compensation):**

---

**Formula:**

"If we don't deliver [the result] in [the timeframe], you get a 100% refund + we PAY you a R$[penalty] for your wasted time."

---

**Example (High-Ticket Consulting):**

```
PENALTY GUARANTEE:

If after 120 days (4 months of implementation) you don't identify a MINIMUM of:

• R$200,000 in cost savings (documented operational efficiencies), OR
• R$150,000 in revenue opportunities (new channels, pricing optimization, identified and validated)

A total minimum impact of: R$350,000

THEN you receive:

1. A 100% refund: R$50,000 (the total fee back)

2. A PENALTY PAYMENT: An additional R$50,000 (as compensation for your team's invested time — C-level time is valuable, 40-60h over 4 months is worth R$50k+)

TOTAL: You receive R$100,000 (double the fee) if we fail.

Our risk: A R$100,000 loss + 4 months of work (if we fail).

Your risk: ZERO (an upside of R$350k+ impact OR a R$100k payment — you cannot lose)."
```

---

**A penalty = MAXIMUM skin in the game** (you don't just return the fee — you PAY double).

**The signal:** "We're so confident that we're risking a R$100k loss if we fail."

**Perceived likelihood:** 100% (no one would offer this unless they were absolutely CERTAIN).

---

**For the AFFLUENT (Kennedy Doc 2 Section 7):**

**Time > Money** (executives, high-earners).

**Compensate for their TIME** (not just their money) = respects their most valuable resource.

The R$50,000 penalty = values their time (40-60h of C-level time is worth R$50k+ — you're acknowledging that).

**The affluent respond:** Compensating for their time is the ultimate respect (money is easy to replace, time is not).

---

**Conversion (for the affluent):**

**85-95%** (virtually everyone — negative risk, time is valued, massive upside).

**The price objection:** Irrelevant (they don't pay until they see results, and there's a penalty if you fail — the price becomes a non-issue).

---

**When to use Level 5:**

✅ **High-ticket** (>R$10,000 — the margins support the risk, a penalty is affordable if you deliver well)

✅ **EXTREME confidence** (you know you can deliver 95%+ — a proven track record, a validated methodology)

✅ **Affluent clients** (time compensation is critical — a Kennedy audience)

✅ **Competitive differentiation** (no one else offers it — an instant standout)

✅ **Measurable results** (ROI, revenue, savings — objective, verifiable)

---

**When NOT to:**

❌ **Low confidence:** If your success rate is <80%, you'll lose money (working for free + frequent penalties).

❌ **Low-ticket:** (<R$2,000 — the margins don't support the risk of a penalty).

❌ **Subjective results:** You can't measure them objectively (satisfaction, mood — not quantifiable).

---

**Refund/penalty rate for Level 5:**

**<2% typically** (almost zero).

**Why:** Only the ultra-confident offer it (you've rigorously screened clients, you know you can deliver), only the ultra-committed bought it (they've self-selected with the highest intent), you deliver extremely well (your confidence was justified), the results are achieved (90-95%+), and they're happy (they don't claim it even though they could).
