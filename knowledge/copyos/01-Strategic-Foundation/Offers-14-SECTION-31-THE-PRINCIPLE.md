## **SECTION 3.1 — THE PRINCIPLE**

---

**Hormozi** ($100M Offers):

> *"An irresistible offer = Core product + Bonuses that solve ALL the problems preventing the customer from achieving the result.*
>
> *Not: The core product alone (the customer faces 10 obstacles to reach the result, you solve 1, 9 still stand in their way — frustration, friction, they don't buy, or they buy and don't use it).*
>
> *Yes: Core + Bonuses that address EACH obstacle (the path to the result is completely cleared, with no remaining barriers — success feels inevitable)."*

---

**Analogy:**

The customer wants to go from Point A (problem) → Point B (result).

**The Path:** There are 10 obstacles in the way (each one prevents progress).

---

**Weak offer:**

The core product removes obstacle #1.

**BUT:** Obstacles #2-10 still exist (the customer is stuck even after buying).

Frustration: "I bought it, it doesn't work" (not that the product is flawed — unresolved obstacles are blocking them).

Refund or bad review.

---

**Strong offer:**

The core removes obstacle #1.

Bonus #1 removes obstacle #2.

Bonus #2 removes #3.

[...]

Bonus #9 removes #10.

**The path is completely cleared** (no obstacles remaining).

Customer: Success is inevitable (if they follow the path, the result will come — nothing is blocking them).

Satisfaction: High (it worked — because the obstacles were removed, not just because a product was sold).

---

**Implication:**

**Each unresolved obstacle = a potential objection = friction = conversion ↓.**

**Solving ALL of them = zero objections = zero friction = conversion ↑↑.**

---

**Hormozi:**

> *"Your job: Identify EVERY obstacle (even small ones) between the customer and the result.*
>
> *Then: Create a solution for EACH one (core or bonus).*
>
> *The offer stack = a stack of obstacle solutions.*
>
> *A complete stack (all obstacles solved) = irresistible (logic: nothing blocks success, so it's worth any price)."*
