## **SECTION 3.2 — IDENTIFY OBSTACLES (Exhaustive Brainstorm)**

---

### **THE PROCESS:**

**Central question:** "What PREVENTS the customer from achieving [desired result]?"

**Exhaustive brainstorm:** List ALL obstacles (15-30 is typical).

**Don't filter** yet (quantity, comprehensive — even small obstacles).

---

### **OBSTACLE CATEGORIES:**

**A. KNOWLEDGE (They don't know):**

They don't know HOW to do [a specific task].

Example for weight loss:
- They don't know what to eat (specific meals)
- They don't know how much to eat (portions, macros)
- They don't know when to eat (timing, frequency)
- They don't know what exercises to do (if including exercise)

---

**B. SKILL (They can't execute):**

They know what to do BUT don't have the skill to execute it well.

Example for copywriting:
- They know they need to "write a headline" BUT don't know how to write a GOOD headline (skill gap)
- They know they "need bullets" BUT don't know how to create curiosity bullets (technique is missing)

---

**C. ACCESS (They don't have the resources/tools):**

They need tools/resources they don't have.

Example for business:
- They don't have the software (CRM, analytics, automation tools — $200-500/month)
- They don't have templates (legal contracts, SOPs)
- They don't have a network (contacts, mentors, peers)

---

**D. TIME (They don't have enough time):**

The process requires time they don't have.

Example:
- Meal prep 10-15h/week (they don't have it — they already work 60h)
- Gym 6h/week (they don't have it)
- Research 20h (they don't have it)

---

**E. MONEY (They don't have the capital to invest):**

The solution requires an upfront investment they don't have.

Example for scaling a business:
- Ads require a $5-10k test budget (they don't have it)
- Hiring requires a $5k/month payroll (they don't have it)
- Tools/software cost $500-1k/month (they don't have it)

---

**F. PSYCHOLOGICAL (Fear, doubt, mindset):**

Mental blocks (False Beliefs — Doc 2 Part V).

Example:
- Fear of failing publicly (ego protection)
- Doubt in their own capability ("I'm not capable")
- Imposter syndrome (not the "type of person" who succeeds)
- Fear of success (identity change, losing friends, etc.)

---

**G. SOCIAL (Other people are obstacles):**

Family isn't supportive, peers doubt them, the environment isn't conducive.

Example:
- Spouse is skeptical (doesn't support financially/emotionally)
- Friends mock ("you're going to fail")
- Environment (home office is impossible, kids interrupt)

---

**H. INFORMATION OVERLOAD (Confusion, contradictions):**

Too much conflicting info (they don't know which path to follow).

Example:
- 100 diet approaches (keto, paleo, vegan, IF — which one?)
- Gurus contradicting each other (A says X, B says the opposite Y)
- Analysis paralysis (research forever, never start)

---

### **BRAINSTORM WORKSHEET:**

```
OBJECTIVE: [Customer achieves X result]

WHAT'S PREVENTING IT? (List EVERYTHING, 15-30+):

KNOWLEDGE obstacles:
1. [They don't know how to do Y]
2. [They don't know which Z to choose]
[...]

SKILL obstacles:
1. [They can't execute W well]
[...]

ACCESS obstacles:
1. [They don't have tool/resource V]
[...]

TIME obstacles:
1. [They don't have the necessary T hours]
[...]

MONEY obstacles:
1. [They don't have $S to invest]
[...]

PSYCHOLOGICAL obstacles:
1. [Fear of R]
2. [Doubt about Q]
[...]

SOCIAL obstacles:
1. [Person P is not supportive]
[...]

INFO OVERLOAD:
1. [Confusion about O]
[...]

TOTAL OBSTACLES: [Count — aim for 15-30]
```

**Exhaustive:** = comprehensive (don't miss any obstacles — missing 1 = customer gets stuck).

---

**Brainstorm validation:**

Research (Doc 2): Reviews (why they failed), interviews (what stopped them), forums (discussions about barriers).

**Common obstacles will emerge** (patterns — if 70%+ mention the same barrier = priority to address).
