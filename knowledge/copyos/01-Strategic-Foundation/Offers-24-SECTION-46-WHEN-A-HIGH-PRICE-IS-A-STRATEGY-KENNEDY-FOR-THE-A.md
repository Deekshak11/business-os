## **SECTION 4.6 — WHEN A HIGH PRICE IS A STRATEGY (KENNEDY FOR THE AFFLUENT)**

---

### **COUNTER-INTUITIVE (Price UP = Sales UP):**

**Experiment (cited by Cialdini — Turquoise Jewelry):**

Store: Sales of turquoise jewelry were low.

Owner leaves a note: "Everything in this display case **× ½**" (intention: cut the price by 50%, a clearance sale).

**Employee read it WRONG:** "× 2" (DOUBLED the price — a mistake).

**Result:** Sales **DOUBLED** (they didn't drop — the opposite of what was expected).

---

**The Mechanism (Heuristic — Doc 1 Section 3.4):**

**"Expensive = Good"** (a System 1 shortcut).

A high price = a signal of quality (especially for experiential/subjective products — jewelry, wine, art, coaching, courses where quality isn't obvious pre-purchase).

**Doubling the price:** Signaled "it must be better quality" (even though the quality was unchanged) → demand increased.

---

### **WHEN A HIGH PRICE WORKS:**

✅ **Experiential/subjective products** (quality isn't objectively measurable — wine taste, coaching value, course transformation)

✅ **Affluent markets** (Doc 2 Section 7 — income >R$200k/year, price insensitive, value time > money)

✅ **Premium positioning** (exclusivity, status — LF6/LF8, high price = a barrier to entry = a signal of exclusivity)

✅ **Low price = quality doubt** (in some categories, cheap is assumed to be bad — "too cheap, must be low quality")

---

### **WHEN IT DOESN'T WORK:**

❌ **Commodities** (easy comparison shopping — features are identical, price is the only differentiator, low wins)

❌ **Price-sensitive markets** (low income, high price elasticity — demand collapses as price rises)

❌ **Objective quality** (measurable specs — RAM, storage, MPG — you can't charge 10x for the same specs, buyers will compare)

---

**Kennedy (Marketing to the Affluent — Doc 2 Section 7):**

> *"For the affluent, a HIGH price = a quality signal.*
>
> *A discount = a red flag ('Why is it cheap? Is something wrong?').*
>
> *Premium pricing = a confidence signal ('It's expensive because it's WORTH it').*
>
> *Counter-intuitive: I raised my price by 30% (R$10k → R$13k), and conversions went up by +18% (not -18%).*
>
> *The affluent = a different psychology (price ascending ≠ price sensitive descending)."*

---

**Application:**

**Test:** If your conversions are LOW and you have premium positioning (high quality, affluent avatar):

Consider: **RAISING the price by 20-50%** (counter-intuitive).

Monitor: Conversions might INCREASE (the quality signal effect > price resistance).

**Data decides** (not assumptions — test it).
