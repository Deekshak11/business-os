## **SECTION 4.4 — COST-PER-DAY FRAMING**

---

### **THE PRINCIPLE (Temporal Framing):**

**Same price, different time unit = MASSIVELY different perception.**

```
R$1,095/year = seems LARGE (a number over a thousand, a scary lump sum)

R$3/day = seems TRIVIAL (less than a coffee, anyone can afford it)
```

**R$1,095 ÷ 365 days = R$3/day** (same price, different framing).

---

### **WHEN TO USE:**

✅ **Subscriptions/memberships** (annual, monthly — ongoing)

✅ **Info products** (lifetime access, ongoing value)

✅ **Services** (annual contracts)

---

### **COPY LANGUAGE:**

```
"Investment: Just R$2.74/day (less than a Starbucks coffee) for a skill that generates R$10-30k/month."
```

**R$2.74/day** (R$1,000/year ÷ 365) = trivial.

**vs. R$10-30k/month** (the outcome) = absurd ROI (R$3/day → R$10k+/month = a 100-300x perceived return).

**Contrast:** Trivial daily cost vs. massive monthly gain = irresistible logic.

---

**Variations:**

**Monthly:** R$1,200/year = "R$100/month" (more digestible than a R$1,200 lump sum)

**Weekly:** R$500/year = "R$9.50/week" (granular, small)

**Hourly** (rarely): R$1,000 value ÷ 40h of content = "R$25/hour of learning" (comparison to a university at R$200+/hour — a bargain)

---

**Choose the unit:** That makes the price seem the SMALLEST (usually daily, sometimes monthly).
