## **SECTION 2.2 — THE 6-CRITERIA CHECKLIST (RIGOROUS SCORING)**

---

### **THE SCORING METHODOLOGY (Calibrated by Empiricus):**

For EACH Big Idea (the 5 top candidates from Section 2.1):

Score it rigorously (being harsh > being generous — false positives = committing to a weak idea, a waste).

---

**CRITERION #1 — BOLD:**

**The question:** "Is the promise a SIGNIFICANT transformation (life-changing, visible, dramatic) or a small, incremental improvement (10-20%, subtle)?"

---

**Scoring guideline:**

**1 point (Bold):**
- A LARGE, quantified transformation (a 3x+ change, or a significant absolute change)
- It's visible/noticeable (others would comment, not just something you'd perceive)
- It's life-changing (it alters daily life, not just a metric improvement)

**Examples of 1 point:**
- "20 lbs lost" (a visible body change)
- "R$30k → R$100k/month" (a 3.3x income increase, life-altering)
- "Fluent in a language" (unlocking the ability to communicate)
- "Eliminates 8 years of chronic pain" (a quality of life transformation)
- "A 4-hour workday" (dramatic time freedom)

---

**0 points (Not bold):**
- "Lose a few pounds" (vague, probably small)
- "Improve by 10-15%" (incremental, not transformative)
- "Optimize a process" (an efficiency gain, not a life-change)
- "Learn concepts" (knowledge, not an outcome)

---

**Calibration examples (edge cases):**

"Lose 10 lbs": **0.5** (borderline — visible if the person is small, not visible if they're large, typically round down to 0)

"Lose 15 lbs": **1** (clearly visible on most people)

"Revenue +50%": **1** (significant — R$30k → R$45k = an extra R$15k/month, life-changing)

"Revenue +15%": **0** (incremental — R$30k → R$34.5k = R$4.5k, nice but not transformative)

---

**CRITERION #2 — NEW:**

**The question:** "Has this angle already been used by 10+ competitors (is it saturated) or is it genuinely fresh (new to the market)?"

---

**Validation process:**

**Google research (20-30min):**

Search: `"[Big Idea core concept]" + "[your niche]"` or `"[a unique keyword from the Big Idea]"`

Example: Big Idea "Dividend Aristocrats" → Google `"dividend aristocrats investing strategy"`.

The results: How many competitors/articles appear?

---

**Scoring:**

**1 point (New):**
- 0-5 results (genuinely fresh, you're an early adopter of the angle)
- The concept exists BUT the presentation is new (a unique reframing of the perspective)
- A specific sub-niche (the angle exists broadly BUT not in YOUR sub-niche — "Keto for women 40+" = specific even if keto is generally saturated)

**0 points (Not new):**
- 20+ competitors saying something similar (saturated)
- An industry cliché (everyone uses it, a commodity angle)
- You're the 50th person to say the same thing (late, not differentiated)

---

**Edge cases:**

Big Idea: "The Keto diet helps you lose weight"

Google: 10 million results (ultra-saturated).

**Score: 0** (not new — everyone says it).

---

**BUT:**

Big Idea: "Carb-Cycling Keto for Women 40+ (Hormonal Optimization for Perimenopause — Balancing Estrogen/Progesterone Via Specific Carb Cycling)"

Google: 200 results (niche-specific).

**Score: 1** (new enough — a specific sub-niche presentation).

---

**Common mistake:** "I haven't seen my competitors using it, so it's new" (your limited research ≠ the market's reality).

**Solution:** Research OUTSIDE your bubble (Google in incognito, a different geo, broader keywords — be comprehensive).

---

**CRITERION #3 — BELIEVABLE:**

**The question:** "Does the claim violate known science/logic OR is it plausible (at the edge of what's possible BUT not beyond)?"

---

**Red flags (0 points — not believable):**

❌ An impossible timeframe ("50 lbs in 7 days" = 7lbs/day, biologically unsafe)

❌ Impossible results ("R$1M/month in 30 days from scratch" — an absurd timeline)

❌ Miracle claims ("It cures any disease," "It never fails," "100% guaranteed always")

❌ It contradicts fundamental science ("Calories don't matter," "Exercise doesn't burn anything")

---

**Green lights (1 point — believable):**

✅ Supported by science/studies (the mechanism is logical, research validates it)

✅ A realistic timeframe (ambitious BUT achievable — "20 lbs in 90 days" = 0.22 lbs/day, at the edge of what's healthily possible)

✅ Variance in the results is acknowledged ("73% achieved it, not 100%" — honesty, not everyone succeeds)

✅ The mechanism is explained (the HOW is clarified — the logic is transparent, it's not magic)

✅ Testimonials exist (real people have achieved it — proof the concept works, it's not just a theory)

---

**Calibration:**

**Bold + Believable = the sweet spot** (the maximum promise WITHOUT crossing into being a scam).

A test: "If a prospect Googles the claim and finds contradicting science/data, does the claim collapse?"

If YES (it contradicts): **0** (not believable).

If it holds up to scrutiny: **1** (believable).

---

**Examples of calibration:**

"Fluent in Spanish in 90 days": **1** (at the edge of possible — there are documented cases of intensive immersion, it's ambitious but achievable)

"Fluent in 10 languages in 30 days": **0** (there are limits to brain processing, it's absurd)

"Your first copywriting sale in 60 days": **1** (achievable with effort, cases exist)

"R$50k/month in copywriting in 30 days from scratch": **0** (an impossible timeline — skill development + client acquisition takes time)

---

**CRITERION #4 — EXCLUSIVE:**

**The question:** "Do only WE offer/have/present this, or does everyone say the same thing?"

---

**Validation (labor-intensive, but worth it):**

**Research your competitors** (the top 10-20 in your niche):

Visit their sites, read their copy, headlines, and offers.

**A checklist:**
- Does any of them use the same Big Idea/angle? (Yes = not exclusive)
- Does any of them have the same mechanism? (Yes = not exclusive)
- Does any of them make the same claim? (Yes = not exclusive)

---

**Scoring:**

**1 point (Exclusive):**
- Zero competitors say anything similar (genuinely unique, validated by research)
- You have an element that no one else has (a proprietary ingredient, a unique guarantee, a patent-pending process)
- Your presentation angle is your own (even if the mechanism is similar, the frame/presentation is differentiated enough)

**0 points (Not exclusive):**
- 3+ competitors make a similar claim (generic, a commodity)
- It's an industry standard (everyone has it, it's expected)

---

**Examples:**

Big Idea: "A guarantee of your first sale in 30 days or a refund + a R$500 penalty"

Research: Check the top 15 copywriting courses in Brazil — none of them offer a guarantee with a PENALTY (some have a 30-60 day money-back guarantee, but none have a +R$500 penalty component).

**Score: 1** (exclusive — only you offer the penalty component, verified).

---

Big Idea: "A proven system that generates results"

Research: Literally EVERYONE says "a proven system" (it's commodity language).

**Score: 0** (zero exclusivity, generic).

---

**Maintaining exclusivity:**

Once you've launched: Monitor your competitors (they might copy you in 3-6 months).

If they copy you (exclusivity is lost): Pivot your Big Idea or add a new exclusive element (iterate, stay ahead).

---

**CRITERION #5 — EMOTIONAL:**

**The question:** "Does it activate 1-2 of the LF8 (Doc 1 Section 7.0 — the biological impulses) OR is it just rational/analytical?"

---

**The LF8 checklist:**

☐ LF1 — Survival (life/death, safety)  
☐ LF2 — Enjoyment of Food (the pleasure of eating)  
☐ LF3 — Freedom from Fear/Pain (avoiding suffering)  
☐ LF4 — Sexual Companionship (attraction)  
☐ LF5 — Comfortable Living Conditions (money, comfort, ease)  
☐ LF6 — To Be Superior (status, winning, autonomy)  
☐ LF7 — Care of Loved Ones (protecting family)  
☐ LF8 — Social Approval (admiration, belonging)

---

**Scoring:**

**1 point (Emotional):**
- 1-2 LF8 are CLEARLY activated (it's obvious which ones)
- Emotional language is present (feelings, desires, fears — not just facts)
- The avatar FEELS something (not just "thinks it makes sense")

**0 points (Not emotional):**
- Zero LF8 are identified (purely rational — optimization, metrics, efficiency without an emotional dimension)
- A B2B-only tone (features, ROI, data — no human element)

---

**Examples:**

"Earn R$20k/month working from home, with time for your family, and no boss": **1** (LF5 money+comfort, LF6 autonomy, LF7 family time — multi-LF8)

"Optimize your database query performance by 23%": **0** (rational only, zero LF8 — B2B technical, not emotional)

---

**B2B calibration:**

B2B CAN score a 1 if an emotional element is added:

"Optimize your database by 23% → Save $180k/year → Job security (no cost-cutting layoffs) + A bonus/promotion (the hero who saved $180k) + Go home at 6 pm, not 9 pm (family time)"

→ Rational (23%, $180k) + Emotional (LF1 job security, LF6 promotion, LF7 family) = **1** (an emotional element is present).

**Purely rational B2B:** Scores a 0, BUT that's acceptable (some B2B decisions are genuinely rational — for CFOs, engineers — the emotion is low, the data is high).

**Consumer (B2C):** Emotional is CRITICAL (a score of 0 = will likely fail — consumers buy on emotion and justify with logic).

---

**CRITERION #6 — URGENT:**

**The question:** "Does the Big Idea imply acting NOW (timing, a window, a consequence) or 'whenever you want' (no urgency)?"

---

**Scoring:**

**1 point (Urgent):**
- A timing dimension ("before the market gets saturated," "the window is closing," "while there's still time")
- A consequence of delay ("every day without it = $X lost," "the damage is accumulating")
- A temporary opportunity ("the loophole is closing," "the advantage is diminishing")
- The urgency is inherent or easy to layer in (via language or the offer)

**0 points (Not urgent):**
- "Learn at your own pace" (explicitly not urgent)
- "Always available" (permanent = the opposite of urgency)
- No time hooks (the benefit is static, not time-sensitive)

---

**Examples:**

"Before the cryptocurrency market goes mainstream (an early adopter's advantage)": **1** (a window is closing, a first-mover urgency)

"Learn copywriting (an always useful skill)": **0** (skill development isn't inherently time-sensitive — you can add urgency via the offer, but the Big Idea itself doesn't have it)

---

**Note:** Urgency can be ADDED via the offer (a deadline, from Doc 4 Section 7) even if the Big Idea scores a 0 here.

**BUT:** A Big Idea WITH built-in urgency is STRONGER (message urgency + offer urgency = double reinforcement, it's consistent).

---

**Common mistake:** Confusing "important" with "urgent" (importance ≠ urgency — it can be important BUT not urgent if there's no deadline/window/consequence dimension).

Example: "Health is important" (it's important, everyone agrees) BUT "Is health urgent?" (it depends — for acute pain, YES, for prevention, NO, unless you frame it as "the damage is accumulating").

---

### **THE SCORING WORKFLOW (Practical):**

**Setup (10min):**

A Google Sheet or Excel file:

**The columns:** Big Idea | Bold | New | Believable | Exclusive | Emotional | Urgent | TOTAL | Notes

**The rows:** 1 per Big Idea (20-40 rows if you've scored all of them after the Phase 2 elimination).

---

**Scoring (30-60min):**

For each Big Idea:

1. Read the Big Idea (to refresh)
2. Score it on Bold: 0 or 1 (be harsh — if in doubt, a 0)
3. Score it on New: 0 or 1 (do research if needed — a quick Google check)
4. Score it on Believable: 0 or 1 (a science/logic check)
5. Score it on Exclusive: 0 or 1 (a competitor check — you've already researched this in Phase 2, refer to it)
6. Score it on Emotional: 0 or 1 (is an LF8 present? Identify which one)
7. Score it on Urgent: 0 or 1 (does a timing dimension exist?)
8. SUM the total (0-6)
9. Notes: A quick note (why you scored it that way, what's missing if it's <6)

---

**Calibration (critical):**

**Be harsh > be generous** (rigor prevents false positives).

**If in doubt:** Score it a 0 (be conservative — it forces refinement, ensures only the strong ones proceed).

**Team scoring:** Average the scores (3 people score independently, then you average them — it reduces individual bias).

**Solo scoring:** Score it twice (today, then tomorrow — a consistency check, sometimes your perspective shifts overnight).

---

**Section 2.2 output:** A complete spreadsheet (all scores), sorted in descending order, with the top 5 identified (in the 4-6/6 range).
