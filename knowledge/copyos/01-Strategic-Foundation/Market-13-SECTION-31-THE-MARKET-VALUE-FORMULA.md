## **SECTION 3.1 — THE MARKET VALUE FORMULA**

---

**Schwartz** (*Breakthrough Advertising*, Ch. 1, pages 19-24):

> *"Not all markets are created equal.*
>
> *Market value depends on TWO factors: SIZE (how many people) and INTENSITY (how badly they want the solution).*
>
> *The formula: MARKET VALUE = Mass × Intensity*
>
> *A small market with intense desire > a large market with mild interest."*

---

**(Already detailed in Doc 1, Section 1.2 — here it's a specific APPLICATION with a worksheet)**

---

### **THE FORMULA:**

```
MARKET VALUE = Mass (absolute size) × Intensity (strength of desire, 1-10)
```

---

**MASS (Market Size):**

How many people have the desire/problem?

**Estimation methods:**

a) **Google Keyword Planner:** Searches/month for the main keywords
b) **Facebook Audience Insights:** People matching the criteria (interests, demographics)
c) **Industry reports:** Market size estimates (IBISWorld, Statista, etc.)
d) **Groups/Forums:** Active members (multiply by 10-50 for the total addressable market)

**Ranges:**
- 100-1,000: Very low Mass
- 1,000-10,000: Low Mass
- 10,000-100,000: Medium Mass
- 100,000-1,000,000: High Mass
- 1,000,000+: Very high Mass

**Be conservative:** Use the LOWER estimate (if the methods give different ranges, choose the lower bound — it's better to under-promise).

---

**INTENSITY (Strength of Desire — a 1-10 Scale):**

How strong is the pain/desire? The urgency? The willingness to pay?

**Validation methods:**

---

**Method A — How Much They've Spent Trying to Solve It:**

| $ Spent on Attempts | Intensity Score |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| $0-100 | 2-4 (casual interest) |
| $100-500 | 5-6 (moderate desire) |
| $500-2,000 | 7-8 (strong pain) |
| $2,000-10,000 | 9 (extreme pain) |
| $10,000+ | 10 (desperation) |

**The logic:** Money spent = revealed preference (actions > words).

If they've spent $5k+ trying = Intensity 9+ (validated by behavior, not just "they say" they want it).

---

**Method B — They Lose Sleep Over It:**

| Frequency of Insomnia | Intensity |
|--------------------|-----------|
| Never | ≤5 |
| Occasionally (1-2x/month) | 6-7 |
| Frequently (1-2x/week) | 8 |
| Every night (or most) | 9-10 |

**The logic:** Sleep loss = a biological stress indicator (the body prioritizes sleep — if a problem prevents sleep, it's serious).

---

**Method C — It Affects Their Health/Relationships:**

| Holistic Impact | Intensity |
|-----------------|-----------|
| None (an isolated issue) | ≤5 |
| Mild (an inconvenience) | 6 |
| Moderate (affects daily function) | 7-8 |
| Severe (destroys quality of life) | 9 |
| Critical (relationships ending, job loss, health crisis) | 10 |

**The logic:** A holistic impact = total life disruption (it's not just an "inconvenience" — it's life-altering).

---

**Method D — They're Willing to Pay for a Definitive Solution:**

| Willingness to Pay | Intensity |
|-------------------|-----------|
| <$100 | ≤5 |
| $100-500 | 6-7 |
| $500-2,000 | 8 |
| $2,000-10,000 | 9 |
| $10,000+ or "any price" | 10 |

**The logic:** WTP (willingness to pay) = the perceived value of the solution (high WTP = high pain).

**A direct question** (in interviews): "If there was a DEFINITIVE solution (100% guaranteed to solve it permanently), how much would you pay?"

An honest answer = an Intensity indicator.

---

**THE FINAL SCORE (Triangulate):**

Combine the 4 methods (take the average):

They've spent: $3k (Intensity 9)
Sleep: Frequently (8)
Impact: Severe (9)
WTP: $5k (9)

**Average:** (9+8+9+9) ÷ 4 = **8.75** → Round to **9**

**Final Intensity: 9/10** (extreme pain, validated by multiple methods).

---

### **THE 4 QUADRANTS (Mass × Intensity Combinations):**

---

**QUADRANT A — HIGH Mass × HIGH Intensity:**

**Example:** "Making money" (billions of people × a strong, universal desire, LF5)

**Market Value:** Extremely high (10,000,000 × 8 = 80,000,000)

✅ **Advantages:** Astronomical revenue potential (a huge market + high WTP)

❌ **Disadvantages:** FIERCE competition (everyone wants this market — Stage 4-5 saturation, extremely high CAC, hard to differentiate)

**Viability:** Difficult (unless you have a revolutionary Big Idea — Doc 3, or a sustainable advantage that can be defended for decades — Apple, Google scale).

**The strategy if you pursue it:** Niche down within it (e.g., "Making money" → "Making money as a dentist" — smaller Mass but the specificity = differentiation).

---

**QUADRANT B — HIGH Mass × LOW Intensity:**

**Example:** "Being more organized" (billions would be interested × a vague desire of 2-4, a nice-to-have)

**Market Value:** Medium-low (10,000,000 × 3 = 30,000,000 — a large but misleading number)

❌ **The problem:** The desire is insufficient to motivate ACTION/PAYMENT.

People say "yes, I want to be organized" (in a survey).

BUT: They don't ACT (they don't buy, they don't implement — Intensity <5 = no urgency).

**The result:** Extremely low CVR (0.1-0.5% typical — interest ≠ action).

**Viability:** POOR (avoid it — a lot of marketing effort, little return).

**Schwartz:** "A large market with mild interest = a waste of time. They want it, but they don't want it ENOUGH."

---

**QUADRANT C — LOW Mass × HIGH Intensity:** ⭐ **THE SWEET SPOT**

**Example:** "Curing severe cystic acne in competitive teenagers (athletes, performers) who are applying to college (photos are needed, the deadline is close, self-image is critical)"

**The breakdown:**
- Mass: ~500,000 (US+Brazil+Europe, teenagers with SEVERE cystic acne — a subset of general acne)
- Intensity: 9-10 (extreme social pain, a real college application deadline, self-image at a developmental age, willing to pay $2-5k for a definitive solution)

**Market Value:** 500,000 × 9 = **4,500,000** (high despite the "low" mass)

---

✅ **Advantages (Why It's the Sweet Spot):**

**Less competition:** A specific niche (most ignore markets <1M — "it's too small").

**Extremely high CVR:** A pain of 9-10 = a conversion of 5-15% (vs. 0.5-2% for mass markets).

**Premium pricing:** High intensity = a WTP of $2-5k (vs. $50-200 for a mass market).

**High customer LTV:** The problem is solved = deep gratitude (loyalty, referrals, repeat purchases of related products).

**Organic word-of-mouth:** Niches = tight communities (teen acne support groups, parents' forums — WOM spreads fast).

**Precise targeting:** They congregate in specific places (dermatologist offices, acne forums, skincare subreddits — easy to target).

---

❌ **The disadvantage:** There's a ceiling (500k is the max addressable — you can't scale to 10M).

**BUT:** 500k × 10% CVR × $3k avg = $150M in potential revenue (enough for most businesses).

---

**Schwartz (his wisdom):**

> *"I would rather have 10,000 people in AGONY (Intensity 9-10) than 10 million people who are mildly interested (Intensity 2-3).*
>
> *Why?*
>
> *10k in agony: 10% CVR, $1k WTP = $1M in revenue, loyal customers, referrals, premium positioning.*
>
> *10M mild interest: 0.1% CVR, $10 WTP = $1M in revenue, a price war, commoditization, high churn.*
>
> *The SAME revenue. But the first = a sustainable business. The second = hell (competing on price, constantly fighting churn)."*

---

**QUADRANT D — LOW Mass × LOW Intensity:**

**Example:** "A collection of Bulgarian stamps issued between 1952-1957"

**Market Value:** Awful (1,000 people globally × an Intensity of 2 for a casual hobby = 2,000)

❌ **The problems:** A tiny market + a weak desire = impossible to scale, impossible to have premium pricing, impossible to sustain a business.

**Viability:** NEVER (avoid it completely — not worth the effort even as a "side project").

---

**Prioritization (Schwartz's Golden Rule):**

> **"Prioritize INTENSITY over MASS."**

**Why:**

High Intensity → High WTP, High CVR, High loyalty, High referrals, Premium positioning is possible.

High Mass alone (without Intensity) → Low WTP, Low CVR, Commoditization, Price wars.

**Better:** Quadrant C (Low Mass, High Intensity) > Quadrant B (High Mass, Low Intensity).

---

**The exception:** Quadrant A (High/High) if you can get it (it's ideal, but rare/difficult).
