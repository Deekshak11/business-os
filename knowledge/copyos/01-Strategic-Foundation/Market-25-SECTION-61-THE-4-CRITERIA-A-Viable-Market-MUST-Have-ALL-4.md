## **SECTION 6.1 — THE 4 CRITERIA (A Viable Market MUST Have ALL 4)**

---

**Todd Brown** (The E5 Method):

> *"A viable market must have ALL FOUR criteria.*
>
> *Not three. Not 'mostly'.*
>
> *ALL FOUR.*
>
> *If it's missing one = reconsider the market."*

---

**THE 4 (The first 4 of E5 — Market focused):**

---

**CRITERION #1 — PAIN (Urgent Pain, Not Casual):**

**Definition:** A problem they want to solve NOW (not "someday," not "it would be nice").

**Validation:**

✅ Urgent pain:
- They lose sleep (biological stress)
- It affects their daily function (work, family, health)
- They are actively seeking a solution (on Google, forums, spending $)
- The timeline: They want to solve it in days-weeks (not years)

❌ Casual pain:
- "It would be nice to have" (a nice-to-have)
- It doesn't affect them significantly (a minor inconvenience)
- They aren't looking (they're waiting for it to "fall into their lap")
- The timeline: "Someday" (no urgency)

**The validation method:**

Ask: "If you could solve [the problem] today (for free), would you?"

✅ "YES, RIGHT NOW!" → It's urgent (Pain criterion is met)

⚠️ "Yes, but it's not a priority" → It's casual (Pain is insufficient)

**The threshold:** An Intensity of 7+ (on the Schwartz scale) = the Pain criterion is met.

---

**CRITERION #2 — PURCHASING POWER:**

**Definition:** The avatar is ABLE to pay (they have the income, resources, access to $).

**Validation:**

✅ Adequate purchasing power:
- Sufficient income (3-5x+ the product price — e.g., a $1k product, the avatar's income is $3-5k+/month)
- They already spend in the category (proven spending behavior — they've spent $500+ trying)
- They have access to payment methods (a credit card, PayPal, bank transfer, financing)
- They have decision-making authority (they can buy without approval — they're not children/dependents)

❌ Insufficient power:
- Low income (<2x the product price)
- They've never spent in the category (no behavioral validation)
- No access to $ (unbanked, no card)
- They need approval (teens, employees without budget authority in B2B)

**The validation method:**

Research demographics: The avatar's average income (census data, surveys, LinkedIn data).

**The threshold:** An income of ≥3x the product price (a comfortable purchase, not extreme financial strain).

---

**CRITERION #3 — EASY TO TARGET (They're Reachable, They Congregate):**

**Definition:** The avatar congregates in specific places WHERE you can reach them via media/advertising.

**Validation:**

✅ Easy to target:
- Specific groups (Facebook group IDs, subreddit names)
- Dedicated forums (specific URLs, active communities)
- Regular events (annual conferences, monthly meetups)
- Publications (niche magazines, blogs, podcasts — with advertising opportunities)
- Specific keywords (Google/FB ads targeting — with sufficient volume)
- Clear demographics/interests (FB targeting is possible — age+interest+behavior)

❌ Hard/Impossible to target:
- They're dispersed (they don't congregate — isolated individuals without a community)
- They're private (not online, not accessible via ads/content)
- They're broad/generic (targeting "everyone" = extremely high CAC, a waste)
- Low-volume keywords (<100 searches/month — insufficient scale)

**The validation method:**

List 10 specific places where the avatar is (online/offline).

**The threshold:** 5+ identified places (sufficient targeting options) = the Easy criterion is met.

---

**CRITERION #4 — GROWING (The Market is Growing, Not Shrinking):**

**Definition:** The market is expanding (not in decline) — it's future-proof.

**Validation:**

✅ Growing:
- Google Trends: Searches are rising (a 5-year trend ↑)
- Industry reports: A positive CAGR (>5%/year growth)
- New entrants: Startups/new products are emerging (capital is flowing in)
- Media coverage: It's increasing (articles, podcasts about the category ↑)
- VC funding: Investments are growing (Crunchbase data)

❌ Declining:
- Google Trends: ↓ (searches are falling)
- The industry: Negative growth (shrinking)
- Exits: Players are leaving the market (closing, pivoting away)
- Media: It's decreasing (the category is discussed less)

**The validation method:**

Google Trends (over 5 years): The general trend line (↑ growing, → flat is OK, ↓ declining should be avoided).

**The threshold:** Flat or ↑ = the Growing criterion is met (declining = a red flag).

---

### **THE E5 CHECKLIST:**

```
☐ P: Urgent PAIN (Intensity 7+, they want to solve it NOW)
☐ P: PURCHASING POWER (income ≥3x the price, they've spent $500+ in the category)
☐ E: EASY TO TARGET (they congregate in 5+ places, they're reachable)
☐ G: A GROWING market (trends are ↑ or →, not ↓)

SCORE: __/4

4/4 ✅ → A VIABLE MARKET (proceed)
3/4 ⚠️ → Questionable (identify what's missing, can it be mitigated?)
<3/4 ❌ → NOT VIABLE (choose a different market)
```

**Brown's rule:** ALL 4 are necessary (non-negotiable).

Missing just 1 = eventual failure (e.g., Pain+Power+Growing BUT Hard to Target = impossible CAC, it won't scale).
