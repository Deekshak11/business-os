## **SECTION 1.3 — MAXIMIZE PERCEIVED LIKELIHOOD**

---

### **OBJECTIVE:**

Make the customer BELIEVE the outcome is achievable (not just theoretically possible, but PROBABLE for THEM specifically).

Low likelihood (30% — "maybe it works") = low value.

High likelihood (85% — "almost certain it works") = high value.

---

### **TACTICS:**

---

**TACTIC #1 — PROOF STACK (5 Tiers — Doc 8):**

**→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 8 completed** (Detailed Proof Hierarchy)

Stack ALL 5 tiers (not just 1-2):

**Tier 1:** Testimonials (4-5, specific, avatar-similar, transformation stories)

**Tier 2:** Data/Track Record (X clients, Y% success, Z avg time/result)

**Tier 3:** Demonstrations (before/after, detailed case studies, video proof)

**Tier 4:** Credentials (your expertise, years, clients, media, authority)

**Tier 5:** Guarantee (results-based, conditional, penalty — the ultimate proof of confidence)

**Masterson:** Stacking 5 tiers = +140% conversion (vs. 1 tier alone — cumulative effect of proof).

---

**TACTIC #2 — CONDITIONAL GUARANTEE (Results-Based, Not Just Money-Back):**

**No:** "Satisfaction guaranteed" (vague, low impact).

**Yes:** "If you [CONDITION: apply the complete method, watch the modules, do the exercises] and don't get [MINIMUM RESULT: your first sale in 60 days, lose a minimum of 15 lbs], you get a 100% refund + R$500 for your time."

**Conditional:** = filters out non-serious people (only the committed apply and succeed) + signals extreme confidence (you GUARANTEE a specific result, not vague satisfaction).

**Penalty** (+R$500) = skin in the game (you pay IF you fail — the ultimate confidence signal).

Perceived likelihood: ↑↑ (if YOU risk a penalty, the product MUST work — overwhelming logic).

**The Paradox** (validated by Abraham): A stronger guarantee = FEWER refunds (not more).

Why: It filters (only serious people buy) + it's a confidence signal (they believe it will work and commit harder).

**(Developed in Part V of this doc — 5 levels of guarantees, paradox explained scientifically)**

---

**TACTIC #3 — MASSIVE SOCIAL PROOF:**

"10,487 customers" (large number — they can't all be wrong, high likelihood).

"78% achieved the result" (the majority — if 78% did, I probably can too).

"Tracked for 24 months" (long-term — not a flash in the pan, sustainable).

**Each element:** Boosts likelihood (social validation — Cialdini Doc 1 Section 2.3).

---

**TACTIC #4 — UNDERDOG TESTIMONIALS:**

People who were in a WORSE situation than the prospect (and succeeded).

"Maria, 67 years old, zero tech experience, made her first sale in 52 days" (if SHE — older, no experience — did it, the prospect — younger/more capable — definitely can).

**Representativeness** (Kahneman Doc 1 Section 3.3): "She represents a possibility for me" (identification).

Likelihood: ↑ (if the underdog succeeded, I'm comparatively advantaged).

---

**TACTIC #5 — SIMPLICITY EMPHASIS:**

"A step-by-step system (follow the recipe, the results come)."

"Fill-in-the-blank templates (not an intimidating blank page)."

"24/7 support (never stuck alone)."

**Simplicity:** = perception of being achievable (not complex/intimidating).

Likelihood: ↑ (simple = I CAN do this, complex = maybe I can't).
