## **SECTION 3.2 — THE AVAILABILITY HEURISTIC**

---

### **THE PRINCIPLE:**

> "We judge the frequency or probability of events by the ease with which examples come to mind."

---

**An S1 shortcut:** "If I can remember it easily = it must be common/probable."

It works most of the time (common things ARE easier to remember).

**But:** It makes systematic errors when **vividness ≠ frequency** (rare but dramatic events are super-available, they seem more common than they are).

---

### **THE EXPERIMENTS:**

**Words with K:**

"Are there more words that BEGIN with K or that have K in the THIRD position?"

The intuitive answer: "Begin with K" (easy to remember: Kitchen, King, Know).

**The reality:** K in the 3rd position is **2x+ more common** (Ask, Like, Make, Take, etc.).

**Why the error:** We search for words by their first letter (a search strategy). Searching by the 3rd letter is difficult (a cognitive load). Therefore: Words that begin with K are easier to remember = they seem more common (the heuristic fails).

---

**Famous Couples:**

A list with 20 famous men (Brad Pitt, etc.) + 20 non-famous women.

"Were there more men or women on the list?"

The answer: "More men" (they are more memorable).

**The reality:** 50/50 (equal).

**The mechanism:** The famous men are more available (easier to remember) = they seem to be the majority.

---

**Divorce (Emotionality Increases Availability):**

Participants read **vivid stories** of tragic divorces (betrayal, financial ruin, custody battles — emotionally intense).

Then they estimate: "What is the probability of YOUR divorce in the next 5 years?"

**The result:** The estimate goes up by **+40%** (vs. a control group without the stories).

**Nothing changed in their marriages** (the same relationships, the same problems).

**But:** The vivid stories made "divorce" **super-available** mentally → It seems more probable (the heuristic is amplified by emotion).

---

**Causes of Death:**

"What kills more people annually: shark attacks or airplane crashes?"

The common answer: "Sharks" (or that they're tied).

**The reality:** Airplane crashes kill **30x+ more** (statistically).

**Why the error:**

Shark attacks are sensational (the media covers them intensely, "Shark Week," the movie Jaws) → They are **super-available** (it's easy to remember cases).

Airplane crashes are individually less vivid (an aggregated statistic, not specific memorable stories).

**Availability (sharks) ≠ Real frequency (airplanes).**

S1 uses availability (and is wrong). S2 could correct it (if it consulted the data), but it doesn't activate (it's lazy).

---

• Applications:
  1. A vivid story > stats (Rokia +108%)
  2. Sensory language (5 senses, Vitale)
  3. Specific case studies > aggregated data
