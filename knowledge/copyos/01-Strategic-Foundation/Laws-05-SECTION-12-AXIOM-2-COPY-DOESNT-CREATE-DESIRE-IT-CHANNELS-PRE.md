## **SECTION 1.2 — AXIOM 2: COPY DOESN'T CREATE DESIRE — IT CHANNELS PRE-EXISTING DESIRE**

---

### **THE DISCOVERY (Schwartz, 1966):**

**Eugene M. Schwartz**, after 20 years of writing copy for health products (Rodale Press), investments (financial newsletters), and self-help (correspondence courses), distilled his greatest discovery in *Breakthrough Advertising* (1966).

**Page 1 (the book's opening):**

> *"Your market is a mob.*
>
> *A great, blind, wild mob.*
>
> *Roaring with desire.*
>
> *Your job is not to create that desire.*
>
> *Your job is to direct it — to channel it — to focus it on your particular product."*

---

And then, page 18 (the central revelation):

> *"This is the copywriter's task:*
>
> *Not to CREATE this mass desire —*
>
> *But to CHANNEL and DIRECT it."*

---

**A conceptual revolution (destroying a common illusion):**

Until Schwartz (pre-1966), most copywriters thought:

"My job is to CONVINCE people to want X."

"I will CREATE desire with brilliant copy, a perfect argument, and irresistible persuasion."

"People don't want it yet, but I will make them want it."

---

**Schwartz destroyed this fantasy:**

"**You CANNOT create desire.**

Desire either already exists (in the market, in the hearts and minds of millions).

**OR** desire does not exist (and never will, no matter your copy).

---

**Your real job:**

1. **Identify WHERE** intense desire ALREADY exists (which market/segment)
2. **Direct** that desire toward YOUR product (vs. a competitor or vs. inertia/procrastination)
3. **Amplify** the existing desire (make it conscious if it's latent, make it urgent if it's vague)

---

**You CANNOT:**

❌ Make a sedentary person (who HATES exercise) desire to work out  
❌ Make a person happy in their corporate job (loves security/benefits) desire risky entrepreneurship  
❌ Make a person uninterested in money (their values are different — art, spirituality) desire material wealth  
❌ Make a person without a specific pain desire your specific solution

---

**Desire is a PRE-CONDITION** (it exists before you, independent of you).

**Copy works WHERE desire exists.**

**Copy fails WHERE desire does not exist** (no matter how brilliant the copy is).

---

Schwartz:

> *"Copy is not some magic formula that creates desire where none exists.*
>
> *Copy is simply a tool to channel existing desire toward your product.*
>
> *If desire doesn't exist, copy is impotent."*

---

### **THE DEFINITIVE ANALOGY (Schwartz):**

**A copywriter is NOT:**

❌ A **magician** (pulling a rabbit out of an empty hat — creating something from nothing)

❌ A **divine creator** (generating desire ex nihilo, from a vacuum)

---

**A copywriter IS:**

✅ A **hydraulic engineer** (or water engineer)

---

**The engineer's job:**

**STEP 1:** Find a RIVER with a strong flow (running water, high volume, constant speed).

**STEP 2:** Build a CHANNEL (a dam, an aqueduct, a pipeline).

**STEP 3:** Direct the river's water to a MILL (the water wheel turns, grains are ground, flour is produced).

---

**The engineer does NOT create the river** (impossible — it requires rain, springs, gravity, geography).

**The river already exists** (natural forces).

**The engineer only CHANNELS it** (directs the existing flow for a productive use).

---

**If an engineer tries to "create a river" where there's no water:**

They build a channel in the desert. The mill is ready. **Zero water.**

**Total failure** (no matter how well-built the channel is).

---

**Copywriting is IDENTICAL:**

**The river = DESIRE** (it already exists in the market, or it doesn't).

**The channel = COPY** (you build it to direct the desire).

**The mill = THE PRODUCT** (it receives the channeled desire, converts it into sales).

---

**Copy channels pre-existing desire to the product.**

**Copy does not create desire from scratch** (impossible — desire is the "river," you don't control the rain/springs).

---

Schwartz:

> *"The power, the force, the overwhelming urge to own that makes advertising work, comes from the MARKET ITSELF — not from the copy.*
>
> *Copy cannot create desire for a product.*
>
> *It can only take the hopes, dreams, fears and desires that already exist in the hearts of millions of people, and focus those already-existing desires onto a particular product.*
>
> *This is the copywriter's task."*

---

### **WHY THIS IS TRUE (3 Irrefutable Reasons):**

---

#### **REASON #1 — Desires are Biological/Cultural (Not Created by Advertising)**

**Life-Force 8** (Whitman — Section 7.0 of this document):

8 biological impulses hardwired into the human brain (via evolution — 200,000+ years of Homo sapiens):

1. **Survival** (self-preservation)
2. **Enjoyment of Food & Beverages**
3. **Freedom from Fear, Pain, and Danger**
4. **Sexual Companionship** (reproduction, attraction, romance)
5. **Comfortable Living Conditions** (physical comfort, money, convenience)
6. **To Be Superior** (to compete, to win, status, hierarchy)
7. **Care and Protection of Loved Ones** (protecting family, providing for children)
8. **Social Approval** (to be accepted, admired, respected by the group)

---

**These 8 have existed for:**

200,000+ years (the evolution of Homo sapiens)

**Some (food, reproduction, survival) have existed for:**

Millions of years (all animal life has these drives).

---

**Advertising has existed for:**

120 years (1900s-2024).

---

**Advertising did NOT CREATE:**

❌ The desire for food (LF2 — hunger has existed for millions of years, a fundamental biological drive)

❌ The desire for sex/companionship (LF4 — reproduction; the species wouldn't exist without it)

❌ The desire for safety/survival (LF1, LF3 — the fear of death/pain is ancestral)

❌ The desire for status/superiority (LF6, LF8 — primates have social hierarchies, dominance)

❌ The desire for comfort/money (LF5 — resources = survival, comfort-seeking is universal)

❌ The desire to protect one's family (LF7 — the parental instinct, millions of years old)

---

**Advertising only:**

✅ **CONNECTS products to pre-existing desires**

---

**Examples:**

**Perfume:**
- Does NOT create: The desire for sexual attraction (LF4 — has existed for millions of years)
- It connects: Perfume → attraction (smell = an evolutionary signal)

**Home alarm system:**
- Does NOT create: The fear of invasion/danger (LF3 — an ancestral fear)
- It connects: Alarm system → safety (a solution for a pre-existing fear)

**A copywriting course:**
- Does NOT create: The desire for money/comfort (LF5 — universal, intense)
- It connects: Copywriting (a skill) → money/freedom (a pre-existing desire)

---

**Copy articulates the connection between product → desire.**

**Copy does NOT generate the base desire** (it's biological/cultural, not created by advertising).

---

#### **REASON #2 — Schwartz Tested This Empirically (40 Years, Thousands of Promos)**

**Schwartz's career (1946-1986):**

40 years of writing copy.

Thousands of promotions (newsletters, courses, health products).

Revenue generated: $100M+ (estimated, directly from his promos).

---

**Schwartz tried (experimentally) to sell products that NO ONE wanted:**

Obscure products, tiny niches, without validated market desire.

**Result:** Total failure.

"Brilliant" copy couldn't save them.

"Brilliant" headlines didn't work.

"Unique" mechanisms didn't matter.

"Strong" guarantees didn't help.

---

**Non-existent desire = impotent copy** (no matter the skill of the copywriter).

---

**Then, Schwartz focused ONLY on products that markets ALREADY desired intensely:**

- Financial newsletters (pre-existing desire: money, financial security — LF5, universal)
- Health products (desire: vitality, longevity, avoiding pain — LF1, LF3, intense)
- Self-help courses (desire: success, status, respect — LF6, LF8, strong)

**Result:** Massive success.

**Mediocre** copy sold well (because the market desire was intense — Schwartz just channeled it).

---

**Schwartz's conclusion (40 years distilled):**

> *"I can write copy that channels existing intense desire and generate millions in sales.*
>
> *I CANNOT write copy that creates desire where none exists and make a single dime.*
>
> *This is not modesty.*
>
> *This is a LAW — observed empirically over 40 years and thousands of campaigns."*

---

**Temporal validation:**

1946-1966 (20 years): Schwartz tests creating vs. channeling

1966: Publishes his discovery (*Breakthrough Advertising*)

1966-1986 (20 years): Applies his discovery (only channels, never tries to create)

**Revenue explodes** (his most profitable decades).

---

**This is not theory.**

It is an **observed law** (from decades of market experiments, millions in testing).

---

#### **REASON #3 — Modern Psychology Validates This Scientifically (Kahneman, Ariely)**

**Daniel Kahneman** (*Thinking, Fast and Slow*, 2011):

Humans have **pre-formed preferences** (System 1 — automatic, emotional).

System 2 (rational) can **modify** preferences slightly.

**But:** System 2 is lazy (avoids effort).

**Result:** Most decisions = System 1 (automatic preferences, not deeply thought out).

---

**Implication for copy:**

**Copy can:**
- ✅ Activate a pre-existing preference (System 1 responds automatically)
- ✅ Make a latent preference conscious (bring it to the surface)
- ✅ Amplify a weak preference (make it strong via context, urgency)

**Copy CANNOT:**
- ❌ Create a completely new preference (would require extensive System 2 — deep analysis, a change of values — which people intensely resist)

---

**Dan Ariely** (*Predictably Irrational*, 2008):

Desires are **relative** (in comparison to alternatives), not absolute.

---

**Copy can:**
- ✅ Make a product seem MORE desirable (via context, decoy pricing, framing)
- ✅ Make a benefit more salient (amplify a specific aspect)

**But:** The BASE desire for that **type of thing** must exist.

---

**Example (Chocolate Experiment — Ariely):**

Lindt (premium) vs. Hershey's Kiss.

Pricing was manipulated:
- Scenario A: Lindt $0.15, Kiss $0.01 → 73% chose Lindt
- Scenario B: Lindt $0.14, Kiss $0.00 → 69% chose Kiss

**"Free" reversed the preferences** (Hershey's won when it was $0).

---

**BUT (this is critical):**

The experiment **ASSUMES the desire for chocolate already exists**.

The participants already WANT chocolate (sweetness, pleasure — LF2).

---

**If a participant HATES chocolate** (allergy, diet, personal preference):

Neither a free Lindt nor a free Kiss works.

**Non-existent base desire = impotent copy/pricing.**

---

**Ariely can:**
- ✅ Change WHICH chocolate (Lindt vs. Kiss — a relative preference)
- ✅ AMPLIFY the desire for chocolate (via "free" — zero risk)

**Ariely CANNOT:**
- ❌ Create a desire for chocolate in someone who doesn't want sweets

---

**Scientific conclusion (Kahneman + Ariely converge with Schwartz):**

**Copy can amplify, direct, and make salient an existing desire.**

**Copy cannot generate a non-existent desire.**

---

**Schwartz was right** (science validated it 40+ years later).

---

### **MASS × INTENSITY — The Market Value Formula**

**Schwartz** (*Breakthrough Advertising*, Chapter 1, pages 19-24):

Not all markets are equal.

**Market value = a function of 2 variables:**

```
MARKET VALUE = Mass (size) × Intensity (strength of desire)
```

---

**MASS (Market Size):**

How many people have the desire/problem?

- 100 people = very low mass
- 10,000 people = low mass
- 100,000 people = medium mass
- 1,000,000 people = high mass
- 10,000,000+ people = very high mass

---

**INTENSITY (Strength of Desire — Scale of 1-10):**

How strong is the desire? How much does it hurt? What would they pay? Do they lose sleep over it?

**1-3 (Low):**
- Vague, casual desire, "nice to have"
- Not urgent, not painful
- Would pay very little (<$10)
- Example: "It would be nice to be more organized"

**4-6 (Medium):**
- Moderate desire, a "want"
- Some annoyance, but tolerable
- Would pay a moderate amount ($50-200)
- Example: "I want to lose 10 lbs" (slightly overweight)

**7-9 (High):**
- Intense desire, a "need"
- Acute pain or a strong craving
- Would pay a high amount ($500-5,000)
- They lose sleep, it affects their health/relationships
- Example: "I need to lose 60 lbs" (obesity, risk of diabetes)

**10 (Extreme):**
- Unbearable pain or an obsessive desire
- Maximum urgency, desperation
- Would pay ANYTHING ($10k+)
- Example: "My child has cancer, I need a cure" (life-or-death)

---

### **THE 4 QUADRANTS (Combinations of Mass × Intensity):**

---

**QUADRANT A — HIGH Mass × HIGH Intensity:**

**Example:** "To make money" (billions of people × a strong, universal desire)

**Characteristics:**
- HUGE market (10M+ people)
- INTENSE desire (8-10 on the scale)
- Market Value Score: Very high (10M × 8 = 80M)

**Advantage:** Astronomical revenue potential (giant market + willing to pay)

**Disadvantage:** FIERCE competition (all copywriters want this market)

**Viability:** Difficult (unless you have a revolutionary Big Idea — Doc 3, or a sustainable unique advantage)

---

**QUADRANT B — HIGH Mass × LOW Intensity:**

**Example:** "To be more organized" (billions might be interested × a vague, casual desire)

**Characteristics:**
- Large market (10M+ people)
- WEAK desire (2-4 on the scale)
- Market Value Score: Medium-low (10M × 3 = 30M)

**Problem:** The desire is insufficient to motivate a PURCHASE.

People say "yes, I want that" (in a survey), but they don't ACT (they don't pay, they don't implement).

**Result:** Very low conversion (0.1-0.5% is typical).

**Viability:** POOR (avoid — a lot of effort for little return).

---

**QUADRANT C — LOW Mass × HIGH Intensity:** ⭐ **THE SWEET SPOT**

**Example:** "To cure severe cystic acne in competitive teenagers applying to college (who need photos for applications with a close deadline)"

**Characteristics:**
- SMALL market (hundreds of thousands, not billions — a niche)
- EXTREME desire (9-10 on the scale — acute social pain, a real deadline, critical self-image)
- Market Value Score: High (500k × 9 = 4.5M)

**Advantages:**
- Less competition (niche — most ignore small markets)
- VERY HIGH conversion (acute pain = extreme willingness to pay, 5-15% CVR is possible)
- Premium pricing (a 9-10 pain = they would pay $500-2,000 for a solution)
- High customer lifetime value (problem solved = gratitude, loyalty, referrals)
- Organic word-of-mouth (niches have tight networks)

**Viability:** **EXCELLENT** (a profitable niche — a favorite of Schwartz).

---

**QUADRANT D — LOW Mass × LOW Intensity:**

**Example:** "To collect stamps from Bulgaria issued between 1952-1957"

**Characteristics:**
- TINY market (hundreds, maybe thousands globally)
- WEAK desire (2-3 — a casual, non-urgent hobby)
- Market Value Score: Terrible (1,000 × 2 = 2,000)

**Problem:** The market is too small + the desire is too weak = impossible to scale.

**Viability:** TERRIBLE (avoid completely — not worth the effort).

---

### **The Distilled Wisdom (Schwartz — Prioritization):**

> *"I would rather have a market of 10,000 people with INTENSE desire (a 9-10 pain)*
>
> *Than a market of 10 million people with vague interest (a 2-3 pain)."*

---

**Why Quadrant C > Quadrant B?**

**The math (a real example):**

**Market A (Quadrant B — High Mass, Low Intensity):**
- Size: 10,000,000 people
- Intensity: 3 (vague)
- CVR: 0.1% (weak desire = few buy)
- Price: $10 (commoditization, price war)
- Revenue: 10M × 0.1% × $10 = **$100,000**

**Problems:**
- Extreme competition (commoditization)
- Low pricing (can't charge more)
- High churn (low commitment)
- High CAC (requires massive ads to stand out in a saturated market)

---

**Market B (Quadrant C — Low Mass, High Intensity):**
- Size: 10,000 people
- Intensity: 9 (acute pain)
- CVR: 10% (intense desire = high conversion)
- Price: $100 (premium — acute pain = willingness to pay)
- Revenue: 10k × 10% × $100 = **$100,000**

**SAME revenue, BUT:**
- Low competition (niche is ignored by the masses)
- Premium pricing (defensible via specialization)
- Low churn (problem solved = gratitude)
- Low CAC (word-of-mouth, precise targeting)

---

**Market B is superior** (same revenue, better economics, a sustainable business).

---

Schwartz:

> *"Intensity is more important than Mass.*
>
> *10,000 people in agony will make you rich.*
>
> *10 million people mildly interested will make you poor."*

---

**Prioritize Intensity over Mass** (whenever possible).

**A Starving Crowd** (Halbert — next section) = Intensity 8-10 (not necessarily Mass).

---

### **CRITICAL IMPLICATIONS FOR COPYWRITING:**

---

#### **IMPLICATION #1 — Market Selection > Brilliant Copy (The 80/20 Law)**

**Gary Halbert** (*The Boron Letters*, Letter #2, validating Schwartz):

> *"The best copy in the world, written by the most brilliant copywriter, cannot sell ice to Eskimos.*
>
> *It cannot sell sand to a Bedouin in the desert.*
>
> *It cannot sell what NO ONE WANTS."*

---

And the classic analogy (the hamburger stand):

> *"Imagine: You're going to open a hamburger stand.*
>
> *I give you only ONE competitive advantage (just one, choose wisely).*
>
> *What would you choose?*
>
> *• The best meat? (Wagyu beef, premium quality)*
>
> *• The best location? (A busy corner, high foot traffic)*
>
> *• The best cook? (A Michelin-star chef, perfect burgers)*
>
> *• The best price? (Cheaper than all competitors)*
>
> *• The best service? (Trained staff, 5-star service)*
>
> *• The best marketing? (Brilliant ads, superior branding)*
>
> ***THE RIGHT ANSWER:***
>
> ***A STARVING CROWD.***
>
> *People with INTENSE HUNGER > everything else.*
>
> *A mediocre hamburger (OK meat, OK location, OK price) + starving people = A LINE OUT THE DOOR.*
>
> *A PERFECT hamburger (Wagyu, Michelin chef, prime location) + people who AREN'T HUNGRY = BANKRUPTCY.*
>
> *Hunger (intense desire) decides everything."*

---

**The brutal translation for copywriting:**

**80% of your success = choosing a market with acute pain/intense desire** (A Starving Crowd, Intensity 8-10)

**20% = copy that communicates the solution** (well-executed, clear, persuasive)

---

**Choose the WRONG market** (Intensity <5, vague/non-existent desire):

→ Brilliant copy fails (tries to create a river where there's no water — Schwartz's analogy)

→ Brilliant headlines don't save it (there's no desire to channel)

→ Irresistible offers don't convert (no one wants it, even for free)

---

**Choose the RIGHT market** (Intensity 8-10, validated acute pain):

→ **Mediocre** copy wins (the desire already exists, copy just articulates the obvious solution)

→ Simple headlines work ("Cure [intense pain]" — straight to the point)

→ Basic offers convert (a serious problem = they are willing to pay)

---

**Halbert (hierarchy of importance):**

```
SUCCESS = (List Quality × Offer × Copy) / Competition

List Quality (The Starving Crowd) = 50% of the result
The Offer (Irresistible) = 25%
The Copy (Well-executed) = 25%
```

**The list (the market with desire) is HALF of your success.**

**The copy is only a quarter.**

---

**The shocking implication:**

**Brilliant copy (top 1%) for a bad market (Intensity 3) < Mediocre copy (top 50%) for an excellent market (Intensity 9).**

---

**Schwartz/Halbert (consensus):**

> *"Choose your market wisely. Copy cannot overcome market indifference."*

---

#### **IMPLICATION #2 — Deep Research BEFORE Copy (Not After, Not During)**

Schwartz:

> *"The power, the force, the overwhelming urge to own that makes advertising work, comes from the MARKET ITSELF — not from the copy.*
>
> *Therefore: Study the market BEFORE you write a single word."*

---

**BEFORE you write copy:**

You MUST know (→ REQUIREMENT: a complete Doc 2 = answering these 5 mandatory questions):

---

**QUESTION #1 — WHERE is the desire?**

Which specific market/niche/segment has an intense pain or desire?

**Not enough:** "Everyone wants X" (vague, not targetable, not actionable)

**Sufficient:** "Brazilian digital entrepreneurs, 35-55, making R$10-50k/month for 12+ months (a plateau), frustrated with slow growth, considering giving up or completely changing their business"

**Specific = targetable** (you know where to find them via Facebook groups, Instagram hashtags, forums, events).

---

**QUESTION #2 — HOW intense is the desire?**

A 1-10 scale (Intensity).

**How to find out:**

**Method A — How much have they spent trying to solve it?**
- $0-100: Intensity 2-4 (casual)
- $100-500: Intensity 5-6 (moderate)
- $500-2,000: Intensity 7-8 (strong)
- $2,000-10,000: Intensity 9 (extreme)
- $10,000+: Intensity 10 (desperation)

**Method B — Do they lose sleep over it?**
- No: Intensity ≤6
- Occasionally: Intensity 7
- Frequently: Intensity 8-9
- Every night: Intensity 10

**Method C — Does it affect their health/relationships?**
- No: Intensity ≤5
- Slightly: Intensity 6-7
- Significantly: Intensity 8-9
- Destroying their life: Intensity 10

**Method D — How much would they be willing to pay for a DEFINITIVE solution?**
- <$100: Intensity ≤5
- $100-500: Intensity 6-7
- $500-2,000: Intensity 8
- $2,000-10,000: Intensity 9
- $10,000+: Intensity 10
- "Any price" / "Everything I have": Intensity 10

---

**Target:** Intensity 7+ (minimum).

**Ideal:** Intensity 8-10.

**If <7:** Consider a different market (insufficient desire to motivate action/payment).

---

**QUESTION #3 — WHAT language do they use to describe their pain/desire?**

The EXACT words the avatar uses (not your words, not professional jargon).

---

**Not enough:** Your words ("optimizing the operational flow of recurring revenue")

**Sufficient:** Their words ("I'm SCREWED. I work like a dog and I'm not getting anywhere. I'm sick of it. I need to get out of this crap.")

---

**How to find the exact language:**

**A. Mine Amazon reviews:**
- A product similar to yours (or a competitor's)
- 1- to 3-star reviews (the dissatisfied — the language of pain)
- 5-star reviews (the satisfied — the language of achieved desire)
- Ctrl+F for emotional words: "frustrated," "finally," "changed my life," "disappointed"
- Copy the exact phrases (they describe the problem/solution better than you can)

**B. Lurk in forums/groups:**
- Reddit (r/entrepreneur, r/fitness, r/personalfinance, etc — search for your niche)
- Facebook Groups (for your avatar)
- Quora (questions about the problem)
- Observe: How do they describe their pain? What words do they repeat? What's the emotional tone?

**C. Interview 5-10 people from your avatar group:**
- Open-ended questions: "What's your biggest frustration with [topic]?" "How would you describe the problem?" "What have you tried?"
- **RECORD** (with permission) and **TRANSCRIBE**
- The raw (unedited) language = pure gold

---

**Collier** (*The Robert Collier Letter Book*, 1931):

> *"Enter the conversation already happening in the reader's mind."*

**The conversation = the language they ALREADY USE** (not what you want them to use, not what sounds "professional").

---

**In your copy:**

**Use THEIR words** (transcribed from research).

**Do NOT translate** it into "better" language (polished, professional, edited).

**Raw = authentic** (the prospect thinks: "They speak my language" → instant identification → Cialdini's Liking/Similarity).

---

**QUESTION #4 — What have they ALREADY TRIED (and why did it fail)?**

A list of previous solutions that did NOT work.

---

**Why this matters (dual value):**

**Value A — Identifying future objections:**

Each failed attempt = an **objection** that will come up:

"I've already tried X, it didn't work." (skepticism based on experience)

**You MUST address this** (Doc 11 — handling the objection: "X failed because [reason], Y is different because [differentiation]").

---

**Value B — An opportunity to justify (Warren — One Sentence #2):**

Each failure = a chance to **externalize the blame** (protect their ego).

"It wasn't YOUR fault that X failed. X was a flawed system (or an inferior product, or wrong information). You were a VICTIM."

**Justifying their failures** → Removes shame/guilt → Mental openness to a new solution.

---

**How to find out:**

Interviews: "What have you already tried to solve [problem]?" "Why didn't it work?"

Reviews: Look for "disappointed," "didn't work," "waste of money" (fail stories).

---

**In your copy:**

"You tried a low-carb diet (and gained the weight back).

You tried keto (unsustainable, you quit in week 3).

You tried intermittent fasting (unbearable hunger, irritability).

**It wasn't your fault.**

These diets SHUT DOWN your metabolism (recent science proves it — the body enters starvation mode and burns even FEWER calories).

You were doing the right thing, but with the wrong information (not your fault)."

**Justifies 3 failures** → Prospect is relieved (I'm not a failure, the systems were flawed) → Open to a new solution.

---

**QUESTION #5 — What is the MASS × INTENSITY score of this market?**

A rough calculation (an estimate):

**Mass:** How many people (an estimate based on research — size of Facebook groups, Google Keyword Planner volume, industry reports)

**Intensity:** A score of 1-10 (based on Methods A-D above)

**Score:** Mass × Intensity

---

**Example:**

Market: "Brazilian digital entrepreneurs stuck at R$10-50k/month"

Mass: ~100,000 (estimate — FB groups combined, Google searches, industry)

Intensity: 8 (they've spent R$5k+ trying, they lose sleep, it affects their family, willing to pay R$2-10k for a solution)

**Score:** 100,000 × 8 = **800,000**

---

**Benchmarks:**

- Score >1,000,000 = Excellent (pursue)
- Score 100,000-1M = Good (viable)
- Score 10,000-100k = Questionable (only if Intensity is 9-10)
- Score <10,000 = Poor (avoid or reconsider)

---

**If Mass AND Intensity are both low (<10k Mass, <5 Intensity):**

→ **Choose a different market** (don't force it — success is unlikely).

---

**Compare potential markets:**

List 3-5 markets (different products/niches).

Calculate the score for each.

**Choose the one with the highest Intensity** (if the Mass is sufficient, >10k).

**OR the highest total score** (if multiple have similar Intensity).

### **Practical Implications (How to Apply Axiom 2):**

---

#### **APPLICATION #1 — Never Write Copy Before Validating Desire**

**The correct process (order matters):**

**STEP 1:** Identify a potential market (a rough avatar)

**STEP 2:** Do deep research (→ REQUIREMENT: a complete Doc 2 — the 5 questions above, 3-5 hours)

**STEP 3:** Validate that the desire exists (Intensity 7+, sufficient Mass)

**STEP 4:** **ONLY THEN:** Write the copy (channeling the validated desire)

---

**The WRONG process (common, fatal):**

**STEP 1:** I have a product

**STEP 2:** I write the copy (assuming desire exists)

**STEP 3:** I launch

**STEP 4:** Failure (desire didn't exist — you found out too late, money and time wasted)

---

**Schwartz:**

> *"More money is wasted on great copy for the wrong market than on poor copy for the right market."*

---

**The investment:**

3-5 hours of research (Doc 2) = **prevents** 20-40 hours of writing masterful copy for a market that doesn't want it.

**The ROI of research:** 4-8x (in time) + infinite (in money not wasted).

---

#### **APPLICATION #2 — The Product Must Connect to a REAL Desire (Not an Invented One)**

**The framework:**

**NOT:**

Product X → Invent a desire for X → Write copy to convince

**YES:**

Desire Y (pre-existing, validated) → Product X solves Y → Copy articulates the obvious connection

---

**Real Example (A Copywriting Course):**

**THE WRONG APPROACH (Violating Axiom 2):**

"I'm going to create a desire to 'learn copywriting' with amazing copy!"

**The problem:**

Very few people wake up thinking "Today I want to learn copywriting."

"Copywriting" is a technical word, with no emotional charge for 99% of the population.

**The desire for the word "copywriting" = non-existent** (low Mass, low Intensity).

**Result:** The copy fails (it tries to create desire from scratch — impossible).

---

**THE CORRECT APPROACH (Obeying Axiom 2):**

**STEP 1 — Identify a pre-existing desire (intense, universal):**

"Where are the people who want:**
- **MONEY** (R$10-30k/month, not R$3k)
- **FREEDOM** (to work from home, a flexible schedule, no boss)
- **AUTONOMY** (not depending on a job — fear of being fired)
- **RESPECT** (to be an expert, family is proud, not 'just another person')"

**These desires (money, freedom, autonomy, respect) are UNIVERSAL, INTENSE** (LF5, LF6, LF8).

---

**The answer:** Entrepreneurs, freelancers, people unhappy in their jobs, mothers who want to stay with their children but need an income, retirees who need extra income.

**Mass:** Millions (in Brazil alone = 5-10M potential)

**Intensity:** 7-9 (money is a need, freedom is a strong dream)

**The desire (for money, freedom, autonomy) ALREADY EXISTS** (pre-validated, not invented).

---

**STEP 2 — Connect the product (copywriting) to the pre-existing desire:**

"Copywriting is a high-demand, low-supply skill that allows you to earn R$5-30k/month working from home (remote, freelance, flexible schedule)."

**Copywriting (the product/vehicle) is connected to:**
- Money (R$5-30k/month — LF5)
- Freedom (home, flexible — LF5)
- Autonomy (freelance, no boss — LF6)
- Respect (a rare skill, an expert — LF8)

**The connection is obvious, logical** (copywriting IS a means to achieve pre-existing desires).

---

**STEP 3 — The copy articulates the connection (it doesn't try to create desire):**

**The resulting headline:**

"How to Earn R$10-30k/Month Working From Home (Even With No Experience, Without Selling Face-to-Face, Without Relying on a Boss) Using This High-Demand Skill"

**Analysis:**

✅ It speaks to PRE-EXISTING DESIRES (R$10-30k, from home, no experience, no selling, no boss — validated in research, Intensity 8)

✅ It removes objections inline ("even with no experience" — a low barrier)

✅ It appeals to LF5 (money/comfort) + LF6 (autonomy) + LF8 (high-demand = status)

✅ "Skill" is mentioned (copywriting is implied), but it's NOT the focus

**The focus is the RESULT** (the achieved desires), not the product/means.

---

**The body copy later reveals:**

"The skill is copywriting. Here's how it works: You write copy for clients (emails, sales pages, ads) → You charge R$3-15k/project → 2-4 projects/month = R$6-60k. It's remote (Zoom calls), flexible (you choose clients/schedule), and you have no boss (you ARE the expert)."

**Copywriting (the vehicle) is revealed AFTER capturing them via desire.**

---

**The copy channeled:**

A pre-existing desire (for money, freedom, autonomy — validated, intense) → to the Vehicle (copywriting — the means to achieve those desires)

---

**The copy did NOT try to:**

❌ Create a desire for "copywriting" from scratch (impossible — the word has no emotional charge)

---

#### **APPLICATION #3 — The Drill Analogy (Ladeira — 5 Levels of Depth)**

**Theodore Levitt** (Harvard, 1960s — a marketing classic):

> *"People don't want a quarter-inch drill.*
>
> *They want a quarter-inch HOLE."*

---

**Ladeira (VTSD) expanded on this:**

"People don't want a hole. They want a HUNG PICTURE. They want a BEAUTIFUL HOME. They want to be ADMIRED for their good taste."

**The 5 levels (drilling down from the feature to identity):**

---

**Level 1 — FEATURE (The Drill):**

"A course with 20 modules, 40 hours of video, 15 templates, 200 swipes"

**No one DESIRES** "20 modules" (an abstract feature, no emotion).

---

**Level 2 — FUNCTIONAL BENEFIT (The Hole):**

"Learn to write copy that converts visitors into buyers"

**Better, but still rational** (moderate desire, Intensity 5-6).

---

**Level 3 — EMOTIONAL BENEFIT (The Hung Picture):**

"The freedom to sell without having to sell face-to-face (no cold calls, no uncomfortable pitching, no personal rejection)"

**Emotional** (freedom, avoiding pain — Intensity 7).

---

**Level 4 — ASPIRATIONAL (The Beautiful Home):**

"R$20k/month working 6 hours/day from home (time with family, travel, no commute, no boss)"

**The desired lifestyle** (a complete transformation — Intensity 8-9).

---

**Level 5 — IDENTITY (Pride/Status — Who You BECOME):**

"Become 'The Expert' your family admires, friends consult, and community respects. You HAVE authority, valuable knowledge, and complete independence."

**Identity/status** (LF6, LF8 — Intensity 9-10).

---

**Copy focuses on Levels 4-5** (aspirational/identity — the deepest, most intense desires).

**Not Levels 1-2** (feature/functional — no one DESIRES features, they desire TRANSFORMATION).

---

**The Mechanism (Levels 1-2) connects to the Transformation (Levels 4-5):**

"20 modules, templates, swipes (Level 1) → Write copy (Level 2) → Sell without calls (Level 3) → R$20k, 6h/day, from home (Level 4) → An admired expert (Level 5)"

**The Headline/Lead = Levels 4-5** (captures via deep desire).

**The Body = Levels 1-3** (explains how to achieve 4-5).

---

**APPLICATION #4 — Headlines Talk About DESIRE (Not the Product)**

**The rule (validated by Schwartz):**

**The Headline = the PRE-EXISTING DESIRE** (it captures the attention of those who ALREADY HAVE the desire).

**The Subhead/Lead = THE VEHICLE** (the product/method that achieves the desire).

---

**The pattern:**

❌ **Wrong:** The headline talks about the product (few people desire the product's name)

✅ **Correct:** The headline talks about the desire (many people have the pre-existing desire)

---

**Comparative examples:**

**Product: A Spanish course**

❌ Product headline: "Learn Spanish With Our Revolutionary Method"

Problem: Few people wake up desiring to "learn Spanish" (the words don't have a strong emotional charge).

Intensity: 4-5 (a casual interest for most).

---

✅ Desire headline: "Speak Fluent Spanish in 90 Days (Impress People on Your Trip to Barcelona, Get That Promotion at the International Company, Connect With Your Wife's Family)"

Pre-existing desires:
- To impress on a trip (LF8 social approval, LF6 superiority)
- A promotion (LF5 money, LF6 status)
- To connect with family (LF7 loved ones)

Intensity: 7-8 (concrete, emotional goals).

The headline captures them via desires. The body reveals the vehicle = Spanish.

---

**Product: Productivity software**

❌ "TaskMaster Pro — Revolutionary Task Management"

Few people desire "task management" (a boring phrase).

---

✅ "Work 4 Hours/Day (Not 10) and Produce 2x More — Have Free Afternoons for Your Family/Hobbies While Your Colleagues Are Stuck at the Office Until 8 PM"

Desires:
- To work less (LF5 comfort)
- To produce more (LF6 superiority)
- Time with family (LF7)
- Freedom vs. stuck colleagues (LF6, LF8)

The headline = the desires. The body = TaskMaster Pro is the vehicle.

---

**Schwartz's rule:**

> *"Your headline has only one job: To stop your prospect.*
>
> *It stops them by appealing to their SELF-INTEREST — their existing desire.*
>
> *Not by mentioning your product name."*

---

#### **APPLICATION #5 — Connect Explicitly (A Transition Template)**

In the body copy, DECLARE the connection from product → desire:

**The template:**

"You want [PRE-EXISTING DESIRE, validated].

[PRODUCT/METHOD] is the vehicle that gets you there.

Here's how it works: [a specific MECHANISM, 3-5 steps]."

---

**Example (Copywriting course):**

"You want R$20k/month, working 6 hours/day, from home, without relying on a boss. [The Desire — research validated that 87% of the avatar wants exactly this, Intensity 8-9]

**Copywriting is the skill** that delivers this specific result.

Why? HIGH demand (every company needs copy — emails, ads, pages), LOW supply (few know how to write copy that converts), it's a REMOTE job (all digital, Zoom calls), and you ARE the expert (not an employee — clients pay you, you choose).

Here's how: [The Mechanism — 4 steps]

1. Learn the skill (a 12-week course, part-time, 10h/week is manageable)
2. Get your first clients (cold email templates, a portfolio with samples, strategic networking — taught in modules 8-10)
3. Charge R$3-10k/project (pricing based on value, not hours — module 11)
4. Scale to 2-4 projects/month (systemize, raise rates, get referrals — module 12)

The result: R$6-40k/month (depending on how many projects you accept), working 20-30h/week (the rest is your time), 100% remote, you control everything."

---

**An explicit connection:**

The desire (R$20k, 6h/day, home, autonomy) ← is connected via a clear mechanism → to Copywriting (the vehicle)

**The prospect sees a PATH** (not just a vague "this gives you that" — but a specific step-by-step).

---

**Collier:**

> *"Always enter the conversation already happening in the reader's mind.*
>
> *That conversation is about THEIR desires — not about your product."*

### **The "Create a Need" Fallacy (Addressing the Steve Jobs Objection):**

**A common objection (it always comes up):**

"But Steve Jobs said: 'People don't know what they want until we show them.'

He CREATED the desire for the iPhone (it didn't exist before).

Doesn't that contradict Axiom 2?"

---

**The answer (Schwartz/Axiom 2 remains valid):**

**Jobs did NOT create fundamental desires.**

---

**What Jobs did NOT create:**

❌ The desire for **music** (has existed for millennia — LF2 enjoyment, LF8 social, ancestral song/dance)

❌ The desire for **communication** (ancestral — LF4 companionship, LF7 family, LF8 social; humans are social animals)

❌ The desire for **information/knowledge** (a learned desire, but a strong one — curiosity, a survival advantage)

❌ The desire for **status/the cool factor** (LF6, LF8 — primate hierarchy, millions of years old)

❌ The desire for **beauty/design** (LF8 — aesthetic appreciation is universal)

❌ The desire for **simplicity/ease** (LF5 — effort minimization, evolutionary)

---

**What Jobs DID:**

✅ **CHANNELED** these (pre-existing) desires into NEW products (iPod, iPhone).

---

**The iPod (2001):**

**Pre-existing desire:** Portable music (anywhere, anytime).

**Proof the desire existed BEFORE:**
- The Walkman (Sony, 1979 — 200M+ sold, 22 years before the iPod)
- The Discman (1984 — 100M+ sold)
- MP3 players (1990s — Diamond Rio, Creative Nomad, etc — an already established market)

**Jobs didn't create the desire for portable music** (it had existed for 22 years).

**Jobs channeled it BETTER:**
- "1,000 songs in your pocket" (vs. 10-20 songs on a Discman)
- Superior design (vs. clunky MP3 players)
- The iTunes ecosystem (vs. a fragmented one)

**The base desire (music anywhere) was pre-existing.**

**The iPod was a superior channel** for an existing desire.

---

**The iPhone (2007):**

**Pre-existing desires:**
- Mobile communication (cell phones since the 1980s)
- Mobile internet (BlackBerry, Palm Pilot since the 1990s)
- Mobile music (iPod since 2001)
- Mobile photos (digital cameras since the 1990s)
- Status/coolness (always — LF6, LF8)

**Jobs didn't create any of these desires.**

**Jobs CONVERGED them** (all of these desires into 1 device).

**Separate devices existed** (phone, PDA, iPod, camera).

The desire for each was pre-existing.

**The iPhone was the convergence** of multiple pre-existing desires into a single, superior vehicle.

---

**Even Apple, even Steve Jobs, obeyed Axiom 2.**

**No one escapes it** (not even geniuses).

---

Schwartz:

> *"The unsuccessful advertiser is the one who tries to CREATE desire.*
>
> *The successful advertiser is the one who CHANNELS existing desire."*
