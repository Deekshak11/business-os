## **SECTION 2.1 — THE PERCEIVED ROI FORMULA**

---

**Mark Joyner** (The Irresistible Offer, 2005):

**Formula:**
```
ROI = (Perceived Value - Price) ÷ Price
```

**In simple terms:** Perceived return on investment (not actual accounting ROI — perceived).

---

**Target benchmarks:**

**Minimum:** 3:1 (perceived value is 3x the price — "a good deal")

**Good:** 5:1 (5x — "a great deal")

**Ideal:** 10:1+ (10x+ — "I can't lose, I'd be a fool to refuse")

---

**Example calculation:**

```
Price: R$1,000

Perceived Value: R$10,000 (via value stacking, proof, guarantee)

ROI = (R$10,000 - R$1,000) ÷ R$1,000
    = R$9,000 ÷ R$1,000
    = 9:1 (900% perceived return)
```

**9:1 = "irresistible"** (Joyner's threshold — above 5:1, resistance crumbles).

Prospect: "A R$1k investment, and I get R$10k in value? It's a steal. I'm buying." (overwhelming logic).

---

### **HOW TO INCREASE ROI (2 Levers):**

---

**LEVER A — INCREASE Perceived Value (Numerator ↑):**

**Method #1:** Value stacking (itemize and assign a value to each item).

```
Core product: R$2,000 value
Bonus #1: Templates (R$500 value)
Bonus #2: Swipes (R$300 value)
Bonus #3: VIP Group (R$800 value)
Bonus #4: 1-on-1 Reviews (R$1,200 value)

TOTAL VALUE: R$4,800
```

**Visible stack:** = perceived value of R$4,800 (vs. core alone at R$2,000).

---

**Method #2:** Comparison to an expensive alternative.

"Personal coaching: R$500/hour × 20 hours = R$10,000.

This course: R$997 (same content, self-paced)."

**R$10,000 anchor** (Doc 1 Section 3.4 Anchoring) = R$997 seems like a 90% discount (perceived value of R$10k).

---

**LEVER B — "DECREASE" the Apparent Price (Denominator strategy):**

**No:** Literally lowering the price (R$1,000 → R$500 — revenue loss).

**Yes:** Frame the price to be perceived as lower (same revenue).

---

**Method #1:** Payment plans.

R$997 pay-in-full → seems high.

12 × R$97 installments = R$1,164 total (MORE revenue for you) BUT R$97/month seems SMALLER than a R$997 lump sum (mental accounting — Kahneman).

**Win-win:** They pay more in total (R$1,164), but feel like they're paying less (R$97 monthly is manageable vs. R$997 is scary).

---

**Method #2:** Cost-per-day framing.

R$1,095/year = **"R$3/day"** (less than a coffee).

**Daily:** = trivial (anyone can afford R$3), annual = large (R$1,095 is scary).

**Same price**, different framing.

---

**Method #3:** Comparison to a cheaper per-unit cost.

Consulting at R$500/hour → Course at R$997 (40h of content) = **R$24.93/hour** (95% cheaper per-hour vs. a consultant).

Makes R$997 seem like a BARGAIN (comparative value per hour).

---

### **IRRESISTIBLE THRESHOLD:**

Joyner (tested 1,000+ offers over 10 years):

**ROI 3:1:** Acceptable (buying is considered, not guaranteed).

**ROI 5:1:** Good (they buy if interested, moderate resistance).

**ROI 10:1+:** **IRRESISTIBLE** (logic overwhelms objections, "I'd be stupid not to buy").

---

**Target:** Aim for a minimum of 10:1 (perceived value is 10x the price).

**How:** Value stacking + bonuses + guarantee + high comparison + low price framing = 10:1+ ROI is achievable (even with a high absolute price, the PERCEIVED value is much higher).
