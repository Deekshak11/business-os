## **SECTION 4.0 — ARIELY + HEATH + WARREN + LF8 (Integrated)**

---

**4.1 — RELATIVITY (Ariely):** Decoy pricing (The Economist's 0% for Print increased revenue).

**4.2 — ZERO (Ariely):** $0≠$0.01 emotionally (the Lindt vs. Kiss chocolate experiment was reversed).

**4.3 — NORMS (Ariely):** Social ≠ Market (AARP's free > $50, the daycare fine made it worse).

**4.4 — HOT STATES (Ariely):** Emotion alters decisions (+136% for risky ones).

---

**5.0 — SUCCESs (Heath):**

Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories.

Score headlines/copy (aim for 5-6/6). A memorability framework.

---

**6.0 — ONE SENTENCE (Warren):**

The 5 elements of deep persuasion: encourage Dreams, justify Failures, allay Fears, confirm Suspicions, and throw rocks at Enemies.

All copy should touch on 4-5.

---

**7.0 — LF8 (Whitman):**

The 8 biological impulses: Survival, Food, Freedom from fear, Sex, Comfort, Superiority, Loved ones, Social approval.

Every product appeals to 1-2 of the LF8 (at a minimum). If zero, it's not desirable.
