## **SECTION 7.1 — THE COMPLETE SEQUENCE (Optimal Psychological Flow)**

---

**Brunson** (Dotcom Secrets, Perfect Webinar, 10+ years of testing):

> *"The stack presentation = an ART and a SCIENCE.*
>
> *The order of the slides/sections = CRITICAL (not arbitrary).*
>
> *The wrong sequence (price before value) = conversion -40-60%.*
>
> *The right sequence (progressive value, price afterward, a surprise, urgency) = maximized conversion.*
>
> *We've tested 100+ variations. This sequence won (consistently)."*

---

### **SLIDES 1-9 — PROGRESSIVE VALUE STACK (Build the Anchor):**

---

**SLIDE 1 — THE CORE PRODUCT (The Foundation):**

```
"Here's what you're getting:

[COMPELLING CORE PRODUCT NAME]
[A 2-3 line description — what it is, what it does]

The main components:
• [Specific Component #1]
• [Component #2]
• [Component #3]
• [+ other main ones]

If sold standalone: R$2,500

[Pause for 3-5 seconds — let it sink in]
```

**The strategy:** Establish the value of the CORE first (it's the foundation of everything, not secondary).

**Be specific:** The components are listed (not a vague "a complete course" — WHAT exactly does it include).

**Value assignment:** A comparison (an equivalent coaching would be R$2,500, or a competitor's pricing, or value-based).

**The pause:** Is critical (don't rush — let them absorb each item before adding the next).

---

**SLIDES 2-8 — PROGRESSIVE BONUSES (1 Bonus per Slide):**

```
SLIDE 2:
"PLUS — you're also getting:

BONUS #1: [A Compelling Name — not a generic 'Bonus 1']
[An icon/image representing the bonus]

What it is: [A 2-3 line description]

It solves: [A specific obstacle — "You don't know what to eat? These 50 meal templates solve that"]

The benefit: [The result of using the bonus — "it saves you 10h/week of meal planning"]

If sold separately: R$500

[Pause]"
```

**Slides 3-8:** Repeat the structure (each bonus = its own slide, its own value, with a pause in between).

**Why 1 bonus/slide** (not all on a single slide):

**Psychological:** Each addition = a separate "gift" (reciprocity Doc 1 — each item = a favor, accumulated = a massive obligation).

**Attention:** 1 item at a time = focus (not an overwhelming list of 7 items at once — the brain processes things sequentially better).

**Building:** It grows (excitement builds with each addition — "and more... and more..." = anticipation).

---

**SLIDE 9 — THE TOTAL VALUE (The Anchor Is Established):**

```
"The TOTAL VALUE you're getting:

Core Product: R$2,500
Bonus #1 (Templates): R$500
Bonus #2 (Swipes): R$400
Bonus #3 (VIP Group): R$800
Bonus #4 (Reviews): R$600
Bonus #5 (Masterclass): R$300

TOTAL: R$5,100

[Pause for 5-10 seconds — let the number SINK IN]
[The R$5,100 anchor is mentally established — Kahneman Doc 1]"
```

**Visual:** A summary table (all items listed, their individual values, the total is highlighted/bold).

**A LONG pause:** 5-10 seconds (the longest pause in the sequence — R$5,100 needs to register in their minds as the anchor).

**Don't rush:** The temptation is to speed up (impatience), BUT the pause = allows the brain to absorb it (a strong anchor = maximized contrast later).

---

**SLIDES 10-14 — THE PRICE REVEAL (Build Suspense, Dramatic Contrast):**

---

**SLIDE 10 — THE SETUP:**

```
"Normally, all of this would cost R$5,100 (the total calculated value).

But..."

[Pause for 2-3 seconds — "but" = a pivot, anticipation]
```

**Linguistic:** "Normally" (a future/alternate reality) vs. "today" (the actual reality that's coming).

---

**SLIDE 11 — NEGATION #1:**

```
"You're NOT going to pay R$5,100.

[Pause — a relief that "I'm not going to pay the total," and curiosity about "how much then?"]

Not even R$4,000..."

[Pause]
```

**The strategy:** Progressively negate HIGH numbers (build suspense, each negation = a lower expectation, a bigger surprise when you reveal).

---

**SLIDE 12 — NEGATION #2:**

```
"Not even R$3,000...

[Pause]

Not even R$2,000..."

[Pause — anticipation is at its peak]
```

**Each negation:** Lowers the expectation (it started at R$5,100, now it's "not even R$2,000" — they're expecting maybe R$1,500-1,800).

---

**SLIDE 13 — THE REVEAL (The Climax):**

```
"Your investment TODAY:

Just R$997

[REVEAL — let them gasp, pause for 3-5 seconds]
```

**Visual:** A large number, highlighted, in a contrasting color (green is typical — positive).

**The words:** "Just" (minimizes it), "TODAY" (a hint of it being time-limited).

**The pause:** Allows the shock/delight to register (R$997 vs. the expected R$1,500+ = a pleasant surprise).

---

**SLIDE 14 — THE SAVINGS (Reinforce the Bargain):**

```
"That's right.

R$997 for R$5,100 in total value.

You SAVE: R$4,103 (80% off)

[Visual: The calculation is shown, the savings are highlighted]"
```

**Reinforce:** The math is explicit (R$5,100 - R$997 = R$4,103 — tangible).

**The percentage:** 80% off (perceived as a massive discount — even if the "discount" is vs. the value you assigned, not an original price paid).

**The anchor effect is maximized:** R$5,100 (the anchor) vs. R$997 (the price) = a 5.1:1 ratio (beyond Joyner's 3:1 minimum, approaching the 10:1 ideal).

---

**Why the sequence of slides 10-14 works (a 5-slide price reveal):**

**Suspense:** Is built (10-12), it peaks (13), and it's released (14) — a narrative arc (tension → climax → resolution).

**Maximized contrast:** It started with an expectation of R$5,100 and revealed R$997 (5x lower — a positive shock).

**vs. An immediate reveal** ("The price is R$997" on slide 2): No buildup, no contrast, no surprise — it's flat, forgettable.

---

**SLIDES 15-16 — THE SURPRISE BONUS (Exceed Expectations, a Tipping Point):**

---

**SLIDE 15 — THE SURPRISE (AFTER the Price, Timing is Critical):**

```
"And there's MORE...

[Pause — "more? I'm already getting R$5,100 in value for R$997, what else could there be?"]

IF you act in the next 30 MINUTES:

A SURPRISE BONUS: [A Compelling Name]
[A description — what it is, its value]

(A R$700 value — we normally sell it separately for R$700, but today it's FREE for action-takers in the next 30 minutes)

TOTALLY FREE for you.

[Visual: A 30:00 countdown starts — immediate urgency]"
```

---

**The timing (why AFTER the price reveal):**

**The price is already established** (R$997 — the decision is 70-80% formed, they're comparing the value vs. the price).

**The surprise bonus:** EXCEEDS their expectations (they thought it was done at R$5,100, BUT there's MORE — it's R$5,800 total now).

**A tipping point:** For on-the-fence buyers ("R$997... hmm... I'm thinking...") → the surprise R$700 bonus is FREE = it pushes them over ("OK that's ridiculous value, I'm in").

---

**vs. Before the price:**

A surprise bonus PRE-price → is integrated into the value stack (it becomes expected, not a surprise) → it loses its impact (only +5-10%).

A surprise bonus POST-price → genuinely surprises (their expectations are exceeded) → maximum impact (+30-50% conversions for those on the fence).

---

**The 30-minute timer:**

Creates IMMEDIATE urgency (a visible countdown, 30 minutes is manageable BUT it's ticking).

Temporal scarcity (in addition to the main offer's deadline — a double layer).

---

**SLIDE 16 — REINFORCE (What They're Getting in Total Now):**

```
"A COMPLETE Recap:

Core: R$2,500
Bonuses 1-5: R$2,600
SURPRISE Bonus (30min): R$700

TOTAL VALUE: R$5,800

The investment: R$997

ROI: R$4,803 in savings (a 482% return — a 5.8:1 ratio)

[Pause — let them absorb the total package]"
```

**The updated total:** R$5,100 → R$5,800 (the surprise has been added).

**The ROI is recalculated:** Now it's 5.8:1 (even better).

**Reinforce:** The full package is clear (no confusion about what's included).

---

**SLIDES 17-19 — FINAL URGENCY/SCARCITY (A Strong Close):**

---

**SLIDE 17 — THE PROBLEM (Scarcity Is Introduced):**

```
"But there's a problem...

[Pause — "a problem? What?"]

I can only accept 100 people into this class.

Why? Quality (>100 = individual attention is <20min/person/month, support is overwhelmed, and results drop by 35-40% as previous data shows).

I chose quality over quantity.

The limit: A firm 100.

[Pause]"
```

**"A problem":** A narrative device (it creates tension — solving the problem = acting fast).

**The reason-why:** Quality (a credible logic, the customer appreciates it — they want quality too).

---

**SLIDE 18 — THE CURRENT SCARCITY:**

```
"We already have 73 people.

[Social proof — others have bought, a validation]

There are only: 27 spots remaining.

[Visual: A 73/100 progress bar, the 27 is highlighted]

First-come, first-served (after 27, the class closes — the next one is in March, a 4-month wait).

[Urgency: Limited spots + a long wait if they miss out]"
```

**The current status:** 73 out of 100 (a real scarcity — the majority is gone, a minority remains).

**Social proof:** 73 have bought (Social Proof Doc 1 — others have acted, you should too).

**The consequence:** Missing out = a 4-month wait (a significant time cost).

---

**SLIDE 19 — THE DEADLINE + COUNTDOWN (Final Urgency):**

```
"And this offer (the R$997 pricing + the 30-minute surprise bonus) EXPIRES:

[A VISUAL COUNTDOWN TIMER: 02:47:18]
(Hours : Minutes : Seconds)

On Friday, November 29, 2024, at 11:59 PM, BRT

After the timer hits zero:
• The price: Becomes R$1,997 (+R$1,000 you'll lose)
• The surprise bonus: Is gone (a R$700 loss)
• The spots: Might be sold out (only 27, others are deciding right now too)

The TOTAL loss for waiting: R$1,700+ (the price + the bonus + a potential 4-month wait)

Don't wait. Act NOW.

[A CTA button appears — green, large, "SECURE MY SPOT NOW →"]"
```

**The countdown:** Visual anxiety (each second = closer to the loss).

**A multi-layer loss:** The price + the bonus + the spots (3 stacked losses).

**The total is quantified:** R$1,700+ (tangible — not a vague "you'll lose out").

**The CTA appears:** The timing is perfect (the urgency is established, resistance is lowered, action = the obvious next step).

---

**TOTAL SEQUENCE:** 19 slides (a typical Perfect Webinar — a 60-90 minute presentation).

**For a sales page:** A similar structure (sections instead of slides, BUT the same psychological sequence).

**For an email:** Condensed, BUT the sequence is maintained (a brief value summary → the price → the urgency — the essence is preserved).
