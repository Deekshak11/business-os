```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
POSITIONING DOCUMENT — [PRODUCT NAME]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

PAGE 1 — THE BIG IDEA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Big Idea (1 final sentence): [Your chosen Big Idea, post-testing]

Type: [ ] Discovery [ ] Conspiracy [ ] Mistake [ ] Twist [ ] Shortcut

The 6-Criteria Checklist Score:
☑ Bold ☑ New ☑ Believable ☑ Exclusive ☑ Emotional ☑ Urgent
Total: 6/6 (or 5/6 if that's what was launched with)

Why it was chosen: [The reason — it was the test winner, it had the highest score, it fit the Stage]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PAGE 2 — THE UNIQUE MECHANISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mechanism (how the product delivers): [3-5 specific steps]

The mechanism type: [ ] Ingredient [ ] Process [ ] Discovery [ ] System

Why it's unique: [Competitors don't have it, or the presentation is different]

Defensibility: [A patent, expertise, proprietary, complexity — how long competitors would need to copy it]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PAGE 3 — THE USP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Unique Selling Proposition (1-2 sentences):
"[Your complete USP]"

The 3 validated Elements:
☑ Unique: [How competitors don't say/have it]
☑ Selling: [A real benefit to the customer]
☑ Proposition: [A specific, testable claim]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PAGE 4 — THE TOUCHSTONE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Touchstone (a memorable phrase): "[Your touchstone]"

The formula applied:
• The Benefit: [Specifically]
• The Timeframe: [Or the condition]
• Without a barrier: [The obstacle that's removed]

The memorability test: [Did a person repeat it after hearing it once? YES/NO]

Where to use it: In the headline, the subhead, email subjects, the CTA, the P.S.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PAGE 5 — THE NEW OPPORTUNITY (If Stage 4-5)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[ ] N/A (Stage 1-3, an improvement/mechanism is OK)
[✓] Applicable (Stage 4-5)

If applicable:

The OLD category (that's being made obsolete): [Name]
The NEW category (that you're creating/leading): [New name]

The language: "Not [the old thing], the [NEW thing]"

Exclusive tribal jargon: [The unique terms for your category]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PAGES 6-7 — THE VOICE (The Attractive Character)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

THE 5 ELEMENTS:

1. The Backstory (3-5 sentences):
   [Where you were → The struggles → The turning point → Where you are now]

2. The Parables (recurring story themes):
   • Theme #1: [e.g., Persistence wins]
   • Theme #2: [e.g., Failure before success]
   • Theme #3: [e.g., The underdog wins]

3. The Character Flaws (2-3 admitted):
   [Vulnerabilities — they humanize you, they're not in your core area of expertise]

4. Polarity (strong stances):
   PRO: [What you defend]
   ANTI: [What you're against]
   The Enemies (concepts): [The identified enemies]

5. The Two Journeys:
   Journey #1 (You as the hero): [Your transformation]
   Journey #2 (You as the guide): [Helping others]

THE VOICE SPECTRUM (The 4 Dimensions):

Formality: [ ] Formal [✓] Moderate [ ] Casual
Tone: [ ] Rational [✓] Balanced [ ] Emotional
Language: [✓] Polished [ ] Moderate [ ] Profane
Vulnerability: [ ] Polished [✓] Moderate [ ] Raw

The voice in 1 sentence: [A description]
Example: "An experienced but vulnerable mentor, direct without being arrogant"

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PAGE 8 — INTEGRATION & USAGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

How the elements connect:

The Big Idea (the overarching concept) →
Informs the Headlines (Doc 6 — expressions of the Big Idea)
Permeates the Copy (Docs 5-13 — the angle is present in all messaging)

The Mechanism (HOW it works) →
Is developed in the Body copy (a detailed explanation)
Is highlighted in the Bullets (Doc 7 — fascinations about the mechanism)

The USP (the claim of exclusivity) →
Is used for differentiation (vs. competitors)
In Headlines/subheads (the unique claim)

The Touchstone (the memorable phrase) →
Is repeated (in the headline, CTA, email subjects, P.S.)
For brand association (it's sticky)

The Voice (the personality) →
Sets the Tone of the copy (Doc 9 — language choices)
For Consistency (in emails, ads, pages — all in the same voice)

ALL of them connect (it's an integrated positioning, not isolated elements).

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

THIS DOCUMENT = THE INPUT for Docs 4-13
All subsequent copy USES the positioning defined here.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```
