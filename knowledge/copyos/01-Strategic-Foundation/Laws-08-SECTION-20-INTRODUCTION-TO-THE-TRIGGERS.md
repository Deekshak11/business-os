## **SECTION 2.0 — INTRODUCTION TO THE TRIGGERS**

---

### **THE RESEARCH (1981-1984):**

Robert B. Cialdini, a professor of social psychology (Arizona State University), made an unusual decision:

**To become an undercover "spy" for 3 years.**

---

**The infiltrated organizations:**

**Sales:**
- Car dealerships (Chevrolet, Ford — he observed closers, techniques, scripts)
- Door-to-door sales (Encyclopedia Britannica, Kirby vacuums, Cutco knives)
- Timeshare sales (high-pressure, intense psychological tactics)

**Telemarketing:**
- Credit cards (cold calls, objections, closes)
- Insurance (life insurance — fear-based selling)

**Fundraising:**
- Charities (Disabled American Veterans, Red Cross — guilt/reciprocity)
- Political campaigns (door-to-door, phone banking)

**Recruiting:**
- Religious organizations (Hare Krishna, Moonies — commitment tactics)
- Multi-level marketing (Amway, etc — social proof, authority)

**Advertising:**
- Direct response agencies (mail order houses, copy departments)

---

**The method:**

He worked as a trainee, an observer, an assistant.

He noted EVERYTHING:
- Scripts that worked
- Tactics that failed
- Recurring patterns
- The underlying psychological principles

---

**The objective (his research question):**

"What makes people say 'YES'?

What universal principles of compliance exist (cross-context, cross-cultural)?"

---

**After 3 years** (an estimated 1,000+ hours of observing compliance professionals):

Cialdini distilled **7 principles** that appeared REPEATEDLY in ALL successful organizations (no matter the industry, product, or context).

---

**He published:** *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* (1984)

**The impact:** 5M+ copies sold, translated into 30+ languages, cited 50,000+ times (academically), the basis of professional compliance globally.

---

### **THE 7 TRIGGERS:**

1. **Reciprocity** (to repay favors)
2. **Commitment and Consistency** (to remain consistent with the past)
3. **Social Proof** (to follow what others do)
4. **Liking** (to say yes to those we like)
5. **Authority** (to obey authority figures)
6. **Scarcity** (to value what is rare/limited)
7. **Unity** (to say yes to "one of us" — added in the 2016 edition)

---

### **UNIVERSAL CHARACTERISTICS OF THE TRIGGERS:**

---

**✅ AUTOMATIC (Click-Whirr):**

They activate without conscious thought.

They bypass System 2 (rational analysis).

System 1 responds instantly.

---

**✅ POWERFUL (They Outperform Logic):**

Shocking experiments:
- Milgram (Authority): 65% would apply a fatal shock when obeying
- Asch (Social Proof): 75% would deny their own eyes to conform

**Triggers > Reason** (frighteningly powerful).

---

**✅ PREDICTABLE (They Always Work):**

They are not random or dependent on mood.

Under the right conditions: a **100% activation rate** (all humans respond).

---

**✅ UNIVERSAL (Cross-Cultural, Cross-Temporal):**

Cialdini tested/validated them in:
- North America (USA, Canada)
- Europe (UK, Germany, France)
- Asia (Japan, China)
- Latin America (Brazil included in later studies)

**The same results** (humans are humans, no matter the culture).

---

They work in:
- 1984 (when they were discovered)
- 2024 (they still work)
- 2100 (they will work — they are evolutionary)

---

**✅ EVOLUTIONARY (An Ancestral Basis):**

Each trigger exists because it **increased ancestral survival/reproduction**.

They are not social "bugs."

They are **evolutionary features** (selected over millions of years).

---

**Examples:**

**Reciprocity** → Cooperation → Group survival (shared hunts)

**Social Proof** → Follow the majority → Avoid predators ("If 100 people are running, run too" = you survive)

**Authority** → Obey the leader → Social organization (coordinated tribes > individuals)

**Scarcity** → Value what's rare → Accumulate resources (scarce food = precious)

---

**The genes of those who had these shortcuts survived** (a selective advantage).

**The genes of those who didn't were eliminated** (a fatal disadvantage).

---

**Today (2024):**

**100% of humans have these 7 shortcuts** (hardwired via natural selection).

**They are not optional.** They are not cultural. They are **biological**.

---

### **How to Use Them in Copywriting:**

**Cialdini's rule:**

> *"The truly gifted negotiator, salesperson, or advertiser is one who can put the power of ALL SEVEN principles to work."*

---

**The translation for copy:**

**Use a minimum of 3-4 triggers in ALL copy** (not just 1).

**Ideally:** 5-7 (for maximum power).

---

**A test (Agora Financial, financial newsletters, 2015-2020):**

Copy using **1 trigger** (only Social Proof): 100% CVR (baseline)

Copy using **3 triggers** (Social Proof + Scarcity + Authority): +85% CVR

Copy using **5 triggers** (adds Reciprocity + Unity): +140% CVR

**It's cumulative, not linear** (each additional trigger is an increment).
