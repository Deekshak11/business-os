## **SECTION 2.4 — TESTING BIG IDEAS (Market Validation)**

---

### **THE OBJECTIVE:**

The top 3 Big Ideas (post-refinement, each scored 5-6/6) → TEST them before the final commit.

**Why test:**

**Intuition fails** (you/your team loves an idea ≠ the market loves the idea — a common disconnect).

**Data > opinion** (a small test reveals the real preference).

**It's insurance** (investing $100-500 in a test prevents investing $50k in the wrong campaign).

---

### **METHOD A — A HEADLINE TEST (Facebook Ads, Cheap/Fast):**

**The setup:**

Create a headline for each of the top 3 Big Ideas (1-2 variations per idea = 3-6 headlines in total).

---

**The campaign:**

A Facebook ad (or Google, your choice of platform):
- Budget: $50-100 per headline (a total of $150-600 for 3-6 headlines)
- Audience: Your target avatar (from Doc 2 — age, interests, behaviors)
- Objective: Traffic (to measure the CTR — click-through rate)
- Creative: A simple image + the headline (don't elaborate, you're testing the headline specifically)
- Duration: 48-72h (enough for 500-1,000 impressions each)

---

**Measure:** The CTR (click-through rate)

Headline A (Big Idea #1): 1.2% CTR

Headline B (Big Idea #2): 3.8% CTR

Headline C (Big Idea #3): 2.1% CTR

**The winner:** Headline B (Big Idea #2 — a 3.8% CTR, 3.2x better than A, 1.8x better than C).

---

**Interpretation:**

The CTR = a proxy for interest (the more they click, the more intrigued they are).

The highest CTR = the Big Idea with the most appeal, validated by the market (not just you/your team).

---

**Cost:** $150-600 total (cheap validation).

**Time:** 2-4 days (for setup + run + analysis).

**Decision confidence:** High (real people, real money spent on targeting, real behavior measured).

---

### **METHOD B — A SURVEY (Free, Qualitative Feedback):**

**The setup:**

Create descriptions of the top 3 Big Ideas (2-3 sentences each, or the headlines).

A survey (Google Forms, Typeform):
- Show the 3 Big Ideas
- The question: "Which one makes you want to know MORE?" (a forced ranking of 1-3)
- A bonus question: "Why did you choose [#1]?" (qualitative — what appealed to them)

---

**Distribution:**

Email it to: A segment of your list (100-200 people), friends/family similar to your avatar (20-50), online communities (post the survey, get 50-100 responses).

Target: 50-100+ responses (a minimum for statistical significance).

An incentive: A $50-100 raffle (it increases participation by 3-5x).

---

**Measure:**

Tally the votes for each Big Idea.

Big Idea #1: 23 votes (23%)  
Big Idea #2: 58 votes (58%)  
Big Idea #3: 19 votes (19%)

**The winner:** Big Idea #2 (58% — a clear majority preference).

---

**Bonus — Qualitative:**

Read the "Why?" responses (to identify WHICH element appealed to them — the mechanism? The authority? The urgency? — these insights can be used for further refinement).

Example responses: "I chose #2 because the data (891 tracked) is reliable" → An insight: Data/proof is a key appeal (emphasize it in the final copy).

---

**Cost:** $0-100 (the raffle incentive is optional).

**Time:** 2-5 days (to create the survey, distribute it, collect responses, and analyze them).

**The benefit:** Qualitative insights (not just numbers — you understand WHY there's a preference).

---

### **METHOD C — COMMIT WITHOUT A TEST (Higher Risk, Acceptable If...):**

**When it's acceptable:**

✅ A ZERO budget (a startup, bootstrapped — you can't spend even $100)

✅ A tight timeline (you're launching tomorrow, no time to test)

✅ Extremely high confidence (a 6/6 score, the team is unanimous, the intuition is strong)

---

**The process:**

Choose the highest-scored one (a 6/6 is ideal, a 5/6 is acceptable if there aren't multiple 6/6s).

The tiebreaker: A team vote, the founder's intuition, the Stage fit (from Doc 2).

**Commit:** Launch (a full campaign, without a prior test).

---

**Risk mitigation:**

**A soft launch:** To 100-500 people first (a small email segment, an ad budget of $500).

Measure the CVR.

✅ A good CVR (>50% of the baseline): Scale (with confidence).

❌ A bad CVR (<30% of the baseline): Pivot (test Big Idea #2, or iterate #1).

**Do not:** Launch to 10,000+ people untested (if it flops, it's a massive waste).

**A soft launch = a mini-test** (a lower risk than a full, blind commit).

---

**Empiricus:** "We always test when possible (when the budget/time allow). Intuition's accuracy is ~60% (better than a coin flip BUT not reliable). Data's accuracy is 85-95% (the test's CTR reasonably predicts the CVR). We prefer data."

---

### **THE POST-TEST DECISION:**

**A CLEAR winner (a CTR that's 2x+ higher than the others, or 60%+ of the votes):**

Commit (use the winning Big Idea as the campaign's foundation).

Develop: The headlines (Doc 6), the copy (Docs 5-13), the offer (Doc 4) — everything expresses the winning Big Idea.

---

**A close race (the difference is <20%, e.g., a 3.2% vs. 3.8% CTR):**

Test it deeper: An A/B test on a larger scale (a budget of $500-1k each, measure the CVR, not just the CTR — conversion matters).

The winner (by CVR) = the final choice.

---

**They all performed POORLY (a CTR of <1% for all, or the votes were dispersed at 30-35-35%):**

None of them resonated (a market rejection).

**The action:** Go back to the brainstorm (generate a NEW batch, something is missing in the current batch — maybe the wrong types for the Stage, or the benefit isn't compelling, or there's an avatar mismatch).

**Do not:** Force a launch with a weak idea (it's been validated as weak — it will fail).

**Humility:** The market said "no" — listen, and iterate.
