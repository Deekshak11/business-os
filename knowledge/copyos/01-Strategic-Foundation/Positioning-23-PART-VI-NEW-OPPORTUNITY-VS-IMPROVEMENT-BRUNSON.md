## **PART VI — NEW OPPORTUNITY VS. IMPROVEMENT (BRUNSON)**

**Words:** 3,200

**Section 6.1 — The 3 Levels (1,600w):**

Brunson (*Expert Secrets*): Market evolution = 3 levels of offers.

**Level 1 — AN IMPROVEMENT:** "A better version of what you're already doing"  
Example: "A new, better diet" (it triggers past failures — Warren's anti-justification)  
When to use: Stage 1-2 only (a virgin market, an improvement is an enhancement)  
The problem: It reminds them of their failures (they've already tried diets and failed)

**Level 2 — A NEW MECHANISM:** "A different way to achieve the same result"  
Example: "Lose weight with KETO (not traditional calorie counting)"  
It's better: A new mechanism = renewed hope (it's not associated with past failures)  
When to use: Stage 3-4 (mechanism differentiation)

**Level 3 — A NEW OPPORTUNITY:** "A NEW category, a redefined outcome"  
Example: "Don't just 'lose weight' — TRANSFORM yourself via Biohacking (a new category)"  
It's the best: A total fresh start (zero association with failures, a new identity)  
When to use: Stage 5 (total saturation, you need a new category to cut through)

Each level is developed (why it works, when to use it, examples, and a mapping to the Stages).

---

**Section 6.2 — The Opportunity Switch (900w):**

How to transform an Improvement/Mechanism into a New Opportunity (category creation).

**The process:**

"A better [old product]" (an improvement) →  
"Forget [the old product] (it's dead/obsolete) — [a NEW category] is the future" (an opportunity)

Examples:

An improvement: "A better website builder"  
An opportunity: "Funnels (forget websites — they're dead, their conversion rate is 0.5%, funnels get 15%+)"

Language patterns: "Not [the old thing], the [NEW thing]," "Forget [the obsolete category], the [new category] dominates," "[The old thing] is dead, the [new thing] is the future."

Naming the new category (exclusive jargon, branded terms).

---

**Section 6.3 — When to Use Each Level (700w):**

A Stage-based decision tree:

Stage 1-2: An Improvement is acceptable (or a Shortcut/Mechanism)  
Stage 3: A New Mechanism (not an opportunity yet)  
Stage 4: An elaborate New Mechanism OR the beginning of a New Opportunity  
Stage 5: A New Opportunity is MANDATORY (the only way to cut through total saturation)

Examples for each Stage, and why the level is appropriate.

---

**Application Box Part VI (600w):**

How to create a new category (naming, positioning, tribal jargon), when it's necessary vs. overkill, and the risks (it can be confusing if the category is too different).
