## **SECTION 1.6 — STATE 5: UNAWARE**

---

### **COMPLETE DEFINITION:**

The customer is at the **bottom of the awareness funnel** (furthest from a purchase).

**They DON'T know:**
- ❌ That they have a PROBLEM (zero problem awareness)
- ❌ Or: They know vaguely BUT don't consider it serious/urgent ("it's normal," "everyone has it," "it's not a big deal")

**Mental state:** Living with the problem (it's normalized, part of life, they don't question it).

---

**They need (the complete journey):**

1. **To create AWARENESS** of the problem (a revelation — "you HAVE this, it's serious, it's not normal")
2. **To create urgency** (not just conscious, but MOTIVATED to solve it)
3. **To show that a solution** exists (category education)
4. **To present YOUR product** (the specific one)

**4 transitions:** Unaware → Problem-Aware → Solution-Aware → Product-Aware → Purchase

---

**Distance from purchase:** MAXIMUM (more education needed, more time, more proof).

---

**Schwartz** (p.31):

> *"The Unaware prospect is the hardest to reach and the hardest to sell.*
>
> *He doesn't know he has a problem. Or knows but dismisses it.*
>
> *Your copy cannot start with the problem (he doesn't see it as a problem).*
>
> *It must start with a STORY or a DISCOVERY — something that captures attention WITHOUT relying on pain awareness.*
>
> *Curiosity, not pain, is the hook.*
>
> *Then: Reveal the problem (gently — 'you didn't know, but...'), create urgency, show the solution, pitch the product.*
>
> *This is the longest, most educational copy. The success rate is the lowest (the hardest conversion). But when it works: Loyal customers (you educated them, created category awareness — they see you as an authority)."*

---

**Analogy:**

"A person who snores loudly every night (undiagnosed sleep apnea — a serious medical condition).

**They don't know:** Snoring = a medical problem (they think 'it's normal to snore, everyone snores, it's not a disease').

**They aren't looking for:** A solution (why would they? They don't see it as a serious problem, just an 'inconvenience' for their spouse).

**The reality:** Apnea increases the risk of a heart attack by 4x, a stroke by 3x, and diabetes by 2x (CDC data — BUT the person doesn't know this).

**State 5 copy needs:**

1. A revelation: 'Snoring isn't normal. It's apnea (it's medical). It kills (4x heart attack risk).'
2. Urgency: 'How long have you had apnea? The damage accumulates (every night without oxygen = brain cells die).'
3. A solution: 'A simple test (a 2-min diagnostic questionnaire). An effective treatment (CPAP or an oral appliance, 95% effective).'
4. An offer: 'Our test kit + treatment protocol...'

**It CANNOT start with:** 'Buy our apnea treatment!' (The person: 'Apnea? I don't have it. I just snore.' — a total disconnect)."

---

**Difference between State 4 vs. 5:**

**State 4:** CONSCIOUSLY suffers ("I have chronic pain, it's a problem").

**State 5:** DOESN'T recognize it as a problem ("it's normal," or doesn't even notice).

**Emotional state:** State 4 = suffering. State 5 = neutral/slightly bothered (not acute pain).

**Copy:** State 4 = agitation (they already feel it). State 5 = revelation (to make them feel it).

---

**Overlap:** Both don't know about solutions (education is needed).

**The difference:** State 4 is motivated (the pain motivates them to search). State 5 is NOT motivated (no conscious pain, no active search).

**Implication:** State 5 needs to be captured via **curiosity/story** (not a pain hook — the pain isn't conscious).

---

### **APPROPRIATE COPY:**

**LENGTH:** 8,000-12,000 words (MAXIMUM — the most education of all States)

**Why so EXTREME:**

State 5 = 4 transitions (vs. State 4 = 2 transitions, State 1 = 0).

**Each transition needs words:**

1. **Unaware → Problem-Aware** (revelation, 1,500-2,500w)
2. **Problem-Aware → Solution-Aware** (category education, 1,500-2,000w)
3. **Solution-Aware → Product-Aware** (introducing you, 1,000-1,500w)
4. **Product-Aware → Purchase** (offer, proof, 2,000-3,000w)

**Subtotal of transitions:** 6,000-9,000 words

**Add:** The lead (800-1,500w story/discovery), Benefits (800-1,200w), FAQ (600-900w), CTA (300-400w)

**Total:** 8,000-12,000 words (justified — a complete journey).

---

**HIERARCHICAL FOCUS:**

1. **Problem Revelation** (30%, 2,400-3,600w) — The CORE of State 5, creating awareness
2. **Solution Education** (25%, 2,000-3,000w) — The category, what works
3. **Proof** (20%, 1,600-2,400w) — Zero initial trust
4. **YOUR Product** (15%, 1,200-1,800w) — The specific one
5. **Benefits** (7%, 560-840w)
6. **Offer** (3%, 240-360w) — The smallest proportion (it's not a transaction, it's an education/transformation sell)

**State 5 = 55% of the copy is education** (revelation + category) vs. 45% pitch.

The highest education-to-sales ratio of any State.

---

**TONE:** Storytelling, discovery, revelatory (not salesy)

**Language:**
- Narrative (before → discovery → after — a story arc)
- "You didn't know, but..." (a gentle revelation, not "you're dumb for not knowing")
- "Here's what I discovered..." (sharing a discovery, helpful)
- "This will surprise you..." (curiosity, not fear)
- Educational/helpful (not pushy — State 5 is extremely resistant to a hard sell)

**Avoid:**
- Starting with problem horror ("YOU HAVE A TERRIBLE PROBLEM!" — State 5: "I do? I don't feel it. Get lost." — rejection)
- Sales language ("Buy now!") early on (too soon — they don't even know they have a problem yet)
- Being accusatory ("You're ignoring a serious problem" — they get defensive, it alienates)

---

### **HEADLINE TYPE — Story/Curiosity/Discovery:**

**Templates (10 State 5 variations):**

```
a) "They Laughed When I [Took an Unlikely Action] — But When I [Got a Surprising Result]!"
   (A Collier classic, timeless)

b) "[An Unlikely Person] Discovered [a Surprising Thing] That [Achieved a Result] ([Number] Tested It, [%] It Worked)"
   (Discovery + authority + proof)

c) "What [an Authority/the Industry] Doesn't Want You to Know About [a Common Topic]"
   (A conspiracy — Makepeace style)

d) "The [Adjective: Shocking/Surprising/Hidden] Truth About [a Common Belief]"
   (A truth reveal, controversy)

e) "[Number] Signs You Have [a Hidden Problem] (And 90% Ignore Them Until [a Severe Consequence])"
   (A checklist revelation — a self-diagnostic hook)

f) "A Discovery from [a Location/Institution]: [A Surprising Finding] That [Changed the Category]"
   (An authority discovery — a news angle)

g) "Why [a Common Behavior/a Normal Symptom] Might Be [Causing a Hidden Problem]"
   (A cause-and-effect revelation — they didn't know the connection)

h) "The Story of [a Person]: How [an Accident/Serendipity] Revealed [a Hidden Truth About a Topic]"
   (A story discovery — a narrative hook)

i) "A [Age]-Year-Old [Authority] Never [Has a Problem] — The [Number] of Habits [He/She] Has (And [%] Ignore)"
   (An aspirational figure — reverse engineering success)

j) "Do You [Do a Common Daily Action] Without Knowing It's [Causing a Problem]? [Number] of Studies Confirm It"
   (A behavior revelation — a daily action = the source of the problem, they didn't know)
```

---

**Mandatory elements for State 5 headlines:**

✅ **A story/discovery angle** (not problem horror — they don't see it as a problem, they'll be repelled)

✅ **A curiosity gap** (Heath Doc 1 Section 5.0 — incompleteness, "what don't they want you to know?", "the truth about," "the signs," "the discovery")

✅ **Credibility** (authority, numbers, social proof — you're a complete unknown, the headline needs credentials)

✅ **Intrigue > Promise** (State 5 isn't actively looking for a solution — you need to CAPTURE their attention via curiosity, not by promising a benefit)

✅ **Non-threatening** (not alarmist — "YOU'RE GOING TO DIE!" repels, "the signs" or "a discovery" intrigues)

---

**Examples (5, diverse niches):**

**a) Health/Prevention:**

"A 67-Year-Old Doctor Who Never Gets Sick: The 5 Morning Habits He Has (And 89% of People Completely Ignore) — Validated by a Harvard Study of 12,000 Participants, with Shocking Results"

Analysis: A story (a 67-year-old doctor, never sick — aspirational), curiosity (the 5 habits, what are they?), 89% ignore (are you one of them? Self-diagnostic), authority (Harvard), proof (a 12k study), intrigue (shocking results).

Perfect for State 5: It doesn't say "you're sick" (that repels). It says "a doctor never gets sick, here's how" (aspirational, curiosity).

---

**b) Finance/Retirement:**

"What Your Financial Advisor Doesn't Tell You About Retirement (And How This Silent 'Mistake' Costs You $400,000 Over 20 Years) — Revealed By a Former VP at Morgan Stanley"

Analysis: A conspiracy (an advisor is hiding something — intrigue), a consequence ($400k specific — shocking), authority (a former MS VP — insider credibility), curiosity (what's the mistake?).

State 5: A person doesn't think "I have a retirement problem" (they have an advisor, it's assumed to be OK). The headline reveals "your advisor is hiding something" (a problem they didn't know they had).

---

**c) Productivity/Hidden Health:**

"Do You Sleep 7 Hours But Still Wake Up Tired? 3 Signs of a Hidden 'Sleep Debt' That 78% Ignore (And How Navy SEALs Fully Recover in 72 Hours)"

Analysis: A relatable symptom (sleeps but is tired — common, normalized), a revelation (sleep debt — a concept they didn't know), a checklist (3 signs — self-diagnostic, curiosity), 78% ignore (the majority — you too?), authority (Navy SEALs — aspirational + credible), a timeframe (72h — specific, achievable).

State 5: "Tired after 7h of sleep" = normalized ("it's my age," "stress"). The headline reveals "it's not normal, it's a medical condition called sleep debt" (problem awareness is created).

---

**d) Business/Stagnation:**

"They Called Me Crazy When I Quit My $15k Job to 'Write on the Internet' — Today I Earn $80k/Month Working 20h/Week from Home (And I'll Reveal the Exact System, 89 People Have Replicated It)"

Analysis: A story (they laughed — conflict), a bold action (quit a $15k job), a shocking result ($80k vs. $15k = 5.3x, 20h vs. the typical 40-60h — a better life), a promise (to reveal the system), proof (89 have replicated it — not just him).

State 5: A person in a job doesn't think "I'm stuck" (it's normalized — "everyone works for a company"). The story reveals "there's an alternative" (internet income, freedom) — problem awareness ("wait, AM I stuck too?") is created via contrast.

---

**e) Relationships/Communication:**

"92% of Couples Make This 'Small' Mistake in Arguments (And It Destroys Intimacy Over Years Without Them Realizing) — A Yale Therapist Explains + a Simple 3-Min Fix"

Analysis: A shocking stat (92% — the majority, you too?), a "small" mistake (not obvious — hidden), a consequence (intimacy destroyed, over years — the time dimension is scary), without them realizing (unconscious — a State 5 theme), authority (a Yale therapist), a solution hint (a simple 3-min fix — hope + achievable).

State 5: Couples don't think "we have a communication problem" (fights are normalized — "all couples fight"). The headline reveals "a specific mistake is destroying intimacy" — problem awareness.

---

### **LEAD TYPE — Secret Lead OR Story Lead:**

**→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 6, Sections 6.4 (Story) and 6.5 (Secret)**

**Why these 2 work for State 5:**

**Story Lead:** A narrative = engagement without a pain hook (State 5 doesn't respond to pain — they don't feel it). The protagonist's journey = a revelation via identification.

**Secret Lead:** A conspiracy/discovery = curiosity (not pain). "What are they hiding?" = intrigue (an investigative tone, not salesy).

---

**State 5 Secret Lead Structure** (400-700 words):

```
P1: The secret/truth exists (100-150w)
"There's a [truth/secret] about [a common topic] that [an authority/the industry]
doesn't want you to know (or doesn't know themselves — ignorance, not
necessarily a conspiracy).

Why it's important: [The consequence of not knowing — it's affecting you RIGHT
NOW without you realizing]."

P2: Who's hiding it (or ignoring it) — 100-150w
"The [industry/medical community/financial advisors] either don't know
(an education gap) or don't mention it (cui bono — who profits from
your ignorance).

The more you [continue your current behavior without knowing], the more
the [consequence accumulates — health deteriorates, money is lost, an opportunity is missed]."

P3: What's hidden/ignored — 200-300w
"Here's what [most people don't know]:

[A root cause or a simple solution that is counter-intuitive/overlooked].

Why it works: [The mechanism — scientific if possible].

Why it's ignored: [The reason — complexity, a recent discovery, it conflicts
with conventional wisdom]."

P4: A promise of an exposé — 50-100w
"In the next [X] minutes, I'm going to reveal:
• The [complete secret/truth]
• The [proof — studies, cases]
• [How to apply it — your solution]

Get ready (this will [an emotion: surprise/shock/liberate] you)..."
```

**Total:** 400-700 words (A State 5 Secret Lead).

---

### **BODY — Problem Revelation Is CRITICAL (1,500-2,500 words):**

**Objective:** To make the prospect REALIZE they have a problem (they didn't know, or knew but didn't consider it serious).

**Revelation Techniques:**

**A. A SYMPTOM CHECKLIST** (self-diagnostic):

```
"Do you have 3+ of these 7 signs?

[ ] [Symptom 1 — common, normalized]
[ ] [Symptom 2]
[ ] [Symptom 3]
[...]

IF you have 3+: You have [the problem — a medical/technical name].

It's not 'normal' (even if it's common). It's a [condition] that has [a consequence if not treated]."
```

**B. A STORY REVELATION:**

The protagonist was living with "normal symptoms" (didn't know it was a problem) → they discovered it (a casual doctor visit, a test revealed it, or a crisis event) → "I had [the condition] for years without knowing" → treatment → transformation.

The prospect: "Wait, I have the same symptoms. So... I have [the condition] too?" (self-diagnosis via a story).

---

**C. SHOCKING DATA:**

"[X]% of the population has [a hidden problem] without knowing it (CDC/WHO/a study).

Most only find out when [a severe consequence has already happened].

The signs: [List 5-7 — a checklist format].

Do you have 2+? There's a [Y]% likelihood you have the condition."

---

**D. A CONSEQUENCE REVEAL:**

"Ignoring [a hidden problem] (or not knowing you have it) leads to:

Short-term (6-12 months): [Consequence A — moderate]
Medium-term (2-3 years): [Consequence B — serious]
Long-term (5-10 years): [Consequence C — severe/irreversible]"

Negative future pacing (Doc 9) — BUT after the revelation (not before, or it's confusing).

---

**The tone of the revelation:** Informative, not alarmist.

"You didn't know (it's not your fault). Here's what happens (education). Now that you know, here's what to do (empowerment)."

**NOT:** "YOU'RE AN IDIOT FOR NOT KNOWING!" (accusatory, a defensive reaction).

**NOT:** "YOU'RE GOING TO DIE A HORRIBLE DEATH!" (excessive fear, avoidance).

---

**AFTER THE REVELATION:** THEN agitate (State 4 style) + solution education + offer.

State 5 = revelation FIRST (to create problem awareness), agitation SECOND (once they're aware, then agitate).

---

### **WHEN TO USE STATE 5:**

**CONTEXT #1 — Cold Traffic (Zero Prior Interaction):**

Native ads (Taboola, Outbrain — discovery browsing, not intent).

Facebook ads with broad interests (targeting demographics, not problem keywords).

YouTube pre-roll (interruption — not searching for a solution).

**Why State 5:** They've never heard of you, they're not actively searching (serendipitous discovery).

---

**CONTEXT #2 — Prevention Products:**

The problem is in the FUTURE (not present pain).

Examples: Retirement planning (for young people), health optimization (preventive, not treatment), insurance (to protect against future loss).

State 5: They don't feel any urgency (the problem isn't NOW — it's "someday").

The copy needs: To create urgency via a revelation (the consequences are accumulating NOW, even if they don't feel it).

---

**CONTEXT #3 — Non-Obvious "Improvements":**

Optimization (not fixing what's broken).

Examples: Productivity tools (they're working OK, but could be 2x more efficient — they don't perceive inefficiency as a "problem").

State 5: "It's OK" (satisficing). The copy reveals: "OK isn't great, you're losing [X] without realizing it."

---

**CONTEXT #4 — "Invisible" Problems:**

Sleep debt, postural issues, financial leaks, gradual relationship erosion.

They happen slowly (the boiling frog — they don't notice until it's severe).

The copy: A revelation that deterioration is happening (they don't feel it because it's gradual).

---

### **WHEN NOT TO USE STATE 5:**

**❌ State 1-4 (Already Aware):**

A problem revelation for someone who ALREADY KNOWS = condescending.

"You have problem X!" (The person: "I KNOW. Stop explaining the obvious.")

---

**❌ Acute Pain (Present, Sharp Pain):**

If the pain is acute (happening NOW, 8-10 intensity):

The person is ALREADY aware (it's impossible not to be — severe pain = conscious).

They're in State 4 (problem-aware), not 5.

A State 5 revelation = a waste (they already know).

---

**❌ Intent-Based Traffic (Google Search for Problem Keywords):**

Searching for "[a specific problem]" = ALREADY aware (they're looking for a solution).

State 3-4 (solution or problem-aware), not 5.

State 5 = discovery traffic (not intent-based).
