## **SECTION 5.1 — WHY GUARANTEES WORK (THE PARADOX)**

---

### **THE PARADOX (Counter-Intuitive):**

**Common assumption:** "A strong guarantee (90 days, generous) = MORE refunds (customers will abuse it)."

**Reality (tested by Abraham/Kennedy/Schwartz):** **A strong guarantee = FEWER refunds** (not more).

---

**Test (Agora Financial, newsletters):**

```
30-day guarantee: 8.2% refund rate
60-day guarantee: 4.7% refund rate
90-day guarantee: 2.9% refund rate
1-year guarantee: 1.8% refund rate
```

**A longer guarantee** (stronger) = **LOWER refunds** (an inverse correlation).

---

**Why the paradox (3 mechanisms):**

---

**MECHANISM #1 — CONFIDENCE SIGNAL:**

**Strong guarantee:** "The company offers a 1-year guarantee — they MUST trust their product (otherwise they wouldn't offer such a long-term risk)."

**Perceived Likelihood:** ↑↑ (Value Equation — if they guarantee it for a year, the product WORKS, I will be successful).

**Commitment:** The customer invests effort (uses the product seriously — their belief is high).

**Result:** Higher satisfaction (effort invested + belief = results achieved) → they don't refund (they're happy).

---

**MECHANISM #2 — COMMITMENT EFFECT (Doc 1 Section 2.2):**

Buying = a commitment (psychological).

**A stronger guarantee:** = ironically, a stronger INITIAL commitment (the customer thinks "I'm going to make this work, I have time to test it — 90 days — no rush").

Commitment → effort → results → satisfaction → no refund.

**vs. a weak guarantee** (30 days): A rush mentality ("only 30 days, maybe it won't work, easy to refund") → low commitment → low effort → mediocre results → refund.

---

**MECHANISM #3 — FILTERING:**

**Conditional guarantee** (results-based — Level 4 in the next section):

"If you APPLY the complete method and don't get [result], you get a refund."

It filters: Only the committed buy (not tire-kickers — they know they need to work).

Tire-kickers: Don't buy (they self-select out — "I have to apply it? Too much work, I'll pass").

**Result:** The customer base = committed (they apply, get results, and don't refund).

**Refund rate:** Low (only committed people bought, committed = results, results = happy).

---

**Paradox summary:**

A STRONGER guarantee (90 days, 1 year, results-based, penalty) →

Higher perceived likelihood the product works →

Higher customer commitment →

Higher effort invested →

Higher results achieved →

Higher satisfaction →

LOWER refund rate (not higher).

---

**Abraham:**

> *"The strongest guarantee I ever offered: 1-year, results-based, with a $5,000 penalty if we failed to deliver.*
>
> *Refund rate: 0.8% (8 out of 1,000 clients).*
>
> *Why so low? Only the committed bought (they self-selected), they worked hard (they believed the guarantee = we're confident), they got results (the majority — 92%), and they were happy (they didn't refund despite having a year to do so).*
>
> *A weak guarantee (30-day, vague): 6-12% refund is typical.*
>
> *A strong guarantee (1-year, specific): <2% refund.*
>
> *The math: A strong guarantee converts +40-80% MORE (Doc 1 Section 3.5 — it removes loss aversion).*
>
> *Even with 2% refunds, NET revenue is +35-75% (more conversions >> slightly more refunds).*
>
> *It's counter-intuitive BUT has been validated for decades."*
