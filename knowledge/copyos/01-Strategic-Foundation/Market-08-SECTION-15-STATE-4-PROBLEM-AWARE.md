## **SECTION 1.5 — STATE 4: PROBLEM-AWARE**

---

### **COMPLETE DEFINITION:**

The customer has **problem awareness** (they know they HAVE a problem, they feel the pain).

**They know:**
- ✅ The specific problem (they identify it, name it, feel it)
- ✅ That the problem affects their life (they're conscious of the consequences)
- ✅ That they want to solve it (the motivation exists)

**They DON'T know:**
- ❌ That SOLUTIONS exist (or they think it's impossible to solve)
- ❌ That the problem IS solvable (resignation: "this is just how it is," "I have to live with it")

**Or:** They tried "solutions" (that failed) → They concluded "nothing works" (they're burned out, cynical).

---

**They need:**

1. **To create HOPE** (a solution exists, it's possible to solve it — not impossible)
2. **To agitate the problem** (to make the pain conscious, urgent — they may have normalized it)
3. **To reveal your solution** as the definitive answer (not temporary relief)
4. **Massive proof** (skepticism is high — they've been burned before or are resigned)

---

**Distance from purchase:** Long (the journey: resignation/hopelessness → hope → belief the solution works → YOUR specific product → purchase).

---

**Schwartz** (p.27):

> *"The Problem-Aware prospect is SUFFERING but doesn't believe relief is possible.*
>
> *They've either never heard of solutions, or tried and failed (they're burned, cynical).*
>
> *Your copy must first give HOPE — show that a solution EXISTS and WORKS.*
>
> *Then present your product as that solution.*
>
> *This is delicate: Too much hope without proof = seen as hype. Too much agitation without hope = depression/avoidance.*
>
> *The balance: Agitate (make the pain conscious) + Hope (a solution is possible) + Proof (it's validated) + Offer (your product delivers)."*

---

**Analogy:**

"A person with chronic back pain (for 10 years).

They've tried: 5 doctors (prescription: pills, physical therapy), 3 physical therapists (6 months each, $12k), acupuncture (20 sessions), yoga (1 year), a chiropractor (6 months).

The result: TEMPORARY relief (days, sometimes weeks). Then: the pain RETURNS. Always.

Resignation: 'I'm going to live with this. It's my age. It's genetic. Nothing solves it permanently.' (hopeless)

**State 4:** Knows the problem (pain), DOESN'T believe a permanent solution exists (they've tried everything, it failed).

**The copy needs:** To reveal that a permanent solution EXISTS (hope — a Johns Hopkins study, a new technique, 83% of cases resolved permanently), proof (1,200 patients tested it, 22-month follow-up with no recurrence), and THEN pitch (your specific method/product that implements the technique)."

---

**Difference between State 3 vs. 4:**

**State 3:** Knows solutions exist (optimistic, researching).

**State 4:** DOESN'T know or believe in solutions (pessimistic, resigned, or burned out).

**Emotional state:** State 3 = hopeful. State 4 = hopeless/cynical.

**Copy implication:** State 4 needs to **create hope** BEFORE educating about solutions (State 3 already has hope, so you can jump straight to education).

---

### **APPROPRIATE COPY:**

---

**LENGTH:** 6,000-10,000 words (long, deep agitation + education + hope creation)

**Why SO long:**

State 4 = the **hardest to convert** (hopelessness or cynicism = deep psychological barriers).

**It needs:**
- To agitate the problem (vividly — they FEEL it but may have normalized it, 800-1,500w)
- To create hope (a solution is possible — to overcome resignation, 500-800w)
- To justify past failures (Warren Doc 1 Section 6.0 — it's not their fault, 400-600w)
- To educate about the solution (what REALLY works, why past attempts failed, 1,200-1,800w)
- To introduce the product + credibility (unknown + a burned out audience = trust is difficult, 800-1,200w)
- MASSIVE proof (transformation testimonials, data, case studies — skepticism is extremely high, 1,500-2,000w)
- A STRONG guarantee (total risk reversal — the fear of trying and failing AGAIN, 400-600w)
- An extensive FAQ (objections: "I've tried it," "nothing works for me," 600-900w)

**Total:** 6,000-10,000 words justified (each section is necessary to overcome the psychological barriers).

---

**BREAKDOWN:**

```
Headline: 25-40 words (question/problem, empathetic, not accusatory)

Subhead: 40-60 words (empathy, a hint of hope, a proof element)

Lead (Story Lead preferred): 700-1,200 words
├─ A protagonist (similar to the avatar, suffering from the same problem)
├─ Their attempts (the same failed solutions)
├─ Resignation/hopelessness (an emotional low)
├─ The discovery (they found the solution, were skeptical but tried it)
├─ The result (a transformation — hope is restored for the prospect)

Problem Agitation: 1,200-1,800 words (DEEP — SPIN Implication style)
├─ Current pain (vivid, sensory — Doc 9 Sensory Language)
├─ Consequences if not solved (negative future pacing — it gets worse)
├─ Emotional impact (family, self-image, health — holistic)
└─ NOT: Excessive horror (depression — balance with hope)

Justification Section (UNIQUE to State 4): 500-700 words
├─ "It wasn't YOUR fault that your attempts failed" (Warren — externalize)
├─ Why [Solution A] failed: [A systemic flaw, not you]
├─ Why [Solution B] failed: [A flaw]
└─ You did the right thing with the wrong information (ego is protected)

Hope Creation: 600-900 words
├─ A solution EXISTS (studies, cases, proof a solution is possible)
├─ It's not you (the problem is solvable, others have succeeded)
├─ Here's what REALLY works (introduction to your solution)

Solution Education: 1,200-1,800 words
├─ What it is (the product, who created it, who it's for)
├─ The Mechanism: HOW it works (detailed, 3-5 steps)
├─ Why it's different from previous attempts (avoids past flaws)
└─ Why it will work THIS TIME (logical + emotional reasons)

Benefits: 700-1,000 words
└─ 20-25 bullets (a complete transformation, not just the problem solved)

Proof: 1,800-2,500 words (MAXIMUM for State 4)
├─ Testimonials (4-5): Transformation stories ("I tried everything, only this...")
├─ Data: Track record (X people, Y% success, Z timeframe)
├─ Case studies (2-3 detailed ones)
├─ Authority (studies, experts, institutions)
└─ Guarantee (results-based, strong — Doc 4 Level 4-5)

Offer: 600-800 words
└─ Stack, pricing, emphasis on the guarantee (risk reversal is critical)

FAQ: 700-1,000 words (EXTENSIVE)
├─ "I've tried [X], it didn't work. Why would this be different?"
├─ "Nothing works for me." (extreme skepticism)
├─ "What if it fails AGAIN?" (fear of re-experiencing failure)
└─ 10-15 questions (to address hopelessness, cynicism, and fear)

CTA: 200-300 words
└─ Recap the journey (suffering → hope → solution → action)

P.S.: 50-80 words (guarantee reminder — reassurance)
```

**Total:** 6,000-10,000 words (upper range of copywriting, justified).

---

**HIERARCHICAL FOCUS:**

1. **Agitation + Justification + Hope** (40%) — To overcome psychological barriers (hopelessness, cynicism, fear)
2. **Proof** (25%) — Skepticism is extremely high (they've been burned before)
3. **Solution education** (20%) — What works, the mechanism
4. **Benefits** (10%)
5. **Offer** (5% — a smaller proportion, it's not a transaction yet, it's a transformation sell)

**State 4 = 65% of the copy is psychological** (emotions, beliefs, fears) vs. 35% logical (mechanism, benefits, offer).

---

**TONE:** Empathetic, hopeful (not desperate), validating

**Language:**
- "I understand your frustration..." (deep empathy)
- "It wasn't your fault..." (Warren — to justify failures)
- "A solution exists (it's validated)..." (hope, with proof — not empty)
- "Here's why your previous attempts failed..." (educational, not accusatory)
- "And here's what works..." (solution reveal with confidence)

**Avoid:**
- Blaming ("You failed because you didn't try hard enough" — the ego rejects this, it alienates)
- Horror without hope ("It will get worse, you'll die, you'll lose everything..." without offering a solution — depression, avoidance)
- Overpromising without proof ("A miracle cure!" — State 4 has been burned before, is ultra-skeptical, and rejects hyperbole)

**The delicate balance:** Agitation (real pain) + Justification (not their fault) + Hope (a solution exists) + Proof (it's validated) = State 4 conversion.

**Mandatory elements for State 4 headlines:**

✅ **Identified problem** (specific — they recognize it, "yes, I have this")

✅ **Empathy/validation** (not accusation — "still suffering," "not your fault," "tired of")

✅ **Attempts acknowledged** (validates that they've tried — "even after 5 doctors," "I've already tried")

✅ **Promise of a revelation** (teases the solution, "here's why," "the truth," "what works")

✅ **Credibility/proof hint** (Johns Hopkins authority, a validated number, data — not an empty claim)

✅ **Hope** (implicit — a solution exists, people have succeeded)

### **BODY STRUCTURE (The Balance of Agitation + Education + Hope):**

---

**SECTION A — Deep Problem Agitation** (1,200-1,800 words):

**→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 11, Section 3** (SPIN Selling — Implication questions adapted for copy)

**Objective:** To make the pain CONSCIOUS (they may have normalized it), to create urgency (to solve it NOW vs. continuing to suffer).

**SPIN Implication applied** (agitation via consequences):

```
Structure (4 levels of escalation):

1. PRESENT PAIN (300-400w):
   Describe the current pain (sensory, vivid, specific).
   "You wake up: [symptom]. Throughout the day: [how it affects you]. At night: [the impact on sleep/family]."
   
2. IMMEDIATE CONSEQUENCES (300-400w):
   What you are losing TODAY.
   "Every day with [the problem]: You lose [X — opportunity, time, money, moments]."
   
3. FUTURE CONSEQUENCES (If Not Solved) — 400-600w:
   Negative future pacing (Doc 9 — Section 3.5).
   "In 6 months (if you don't solve it): [A specific worsening].
   In 2 years: [A greater worsening].
   In 5 years: [A severe consequence]."
   
4. EMOTIONAL/HOLISTIC (200-400w):
   The impact beyond the physical problem.
   "How it affects: Your family ([specific]), your work ([specific]), your self-image ([specific]), your overall health ([specific])."
```

**Example (Back Pain — Agitation):**

```
[PRESENT PAIN]
You wake up: Lower back stiffness (you need 5-10 min to "warm up" before getting up — literally rolling out of bed).

Work (sitting for 8h): A constant ache (6/10 — manageable but always there, difficult to concentrate, ibuprofen 2-3x/day).

End of the day: A flare-up (8-9/10 — driving home is agony, every pothole in the road = a spike of pain).

Night: Trying different positions (you change 4-5x looking for comfort, wake up 3-4x, broken sleep).

[IMMEDIATE CONSEQUENCES]
Every day with pain:

Exercise: Impossible (a 15min walk = the pain increases, the gym is out of the question).

Mood: Irritability (constant pain = a short temper, your family suffers).

Cost: Ibuprofen $150/month, occasional massages $200, doctor visits ($500-1,000/year).

Total: $3,000-5,000/year managing (not solving).

[THE FUTURE IF NOT SOLVED]
In 6 months (if it continues like this):

The pain: Worsens (disc degeneration continues, 6/10 → 7/10 daily).

Mobility: Reduced (the activities you avoid increase — less movement = more stiffness = a cycle).

In 2 years:

The pain: 7-8/10 daily (stronger pills, side effects — stomach issues, dependence).

Work: Considering disability (you can't handle sitting for 8h — financial worry).

In 5 years:

Surgery is considered (lumbar fusion — $50-100k, 6-12 months recovery, 60-70% success rate, risks).

The alternative: Living with severe pain (quality of life destroyed).

[EMOTIONAL/HOLISTIC]
Your family sees you suffer (for 8 years now — guilt).

Your grandkids are growing up (you're missing moments — you didn't play, didn't pick them up, didn't make memories).

Self-image: "I'm old, broken, useless" (it's not true, but the pain makes you feel that way).

Work: Performance drops (colleagues notice — "Are you OK?" — embarrassment).

Relationship: Your spouse is worried (or irritated that you're always in pain, always complaining — tension).

Overall health: Forced sedentary lifestyle (the pain prevents exercise) → weight gain → more stress on your knees/back → a vicious cycle.

Mental health: Depression (studies: chronic pain = 3x more likely to have depression — you feel it).
```

**Total agitation:** 1,200 words (DEEP — present, immediate, future, holistic).

**Emotional impact:** HIGH (the prospect FEELS it — vivid, sensory Doc 9, real consequences).

**BUT:** Not pure horror (balance with hope in the next section).

Complete justification (each failed solution = a flaw explained scientifically), it externalizes (the methods were flawed, not you), it validates (you did the right thing, you were committed), the root cause is revealed (psoas — sets up your solution that targets it), authority (Johns Hopkins 2019 — a recent discovery explains it).

The prospect: "IT WASN'T MY FAULT. (Relief — ego protected). The methods were wrong (not me). Now I understand WHY they failed. (Logical closure). Maybe there IS a method that works (hope)." (a mental opening for a new solution).

**SECTION D — Solution Education** (1,200-1,800 words):

Develop the solution (the complete mechanism, why it works, how it's different from previous attempts).

**(The structure is similar to the State 3 Solution section, but with an emphasis on "why it will work THIS TIME" — to address the burned-out experience)**

---

**SECTION E — Massive Proof** (1,800-2,500 words — the HIGHEST proportion of any State):

State 4 = extremely high skepticism (they've been burned before).

The proof needs to be:
- Abundant (4-5 transformation testimonials, not 1-2)
- Specific (numbers, timeframes, before/after)
- Comparative ("I tried A, B, C, D — only this worked")
- Diverse (multiple profiles — if it worked for people aged 52, 61, and 45, it will work for a range)
- Authoritative (studies, experts, institutions — not just "trust me")
- Guaranteed (results-based, with a penalty if it fails — Doc 4 Level 4-5)

**(See Doc 8, Part I — The Proof Hierarchy, 5 Tiers, all applied in State 4)**

---

### **WHEN TO USE STATE 4:**

---

**CONTEXT #1 — Pain Points Research (Forums, Communities):**

**Where State 4 prospects congregate:**

Reddit threads:
- r/ChronicPain: "Has anyone ACTUALLY solved this?" (a hopeless tone)
- r/Entrepreneur: "Stuck for years, has anyone gotten out?" (desperation)

Facebook Groups:
- Chronic pain support groups (shared suffering)
- Business stagnation groups ("Stuck at $X" discussions)

Niche forums:
- Discussions: "I've tried everything, nothing works" (resigned)
- Questions: "Is it possible to [solve it] or do I have to accept it?" (doubt)

**State 4 linguistic markers:**
- "Still" (still suffering)
- "I've tried everything" (exhausted attempts)
- "Nothing works" (cynicism)
- "Has anyone ACTUALLY..." (seeking validation that a solution exists)

---

**CONTEXT #2 — High Pain/Frustration Keywords:**

**Google searches (emotional, not clinical):**

- "Chronic [body part] pain won't go away" (chronic, persistent)
- "Stuck in [situation] for years" (long-term stuck)
- "Tried [solution A, B] didn't work" (failed attempts)
- "Is it possible to [solve problem]?" (doubt + hope)
- "Why won't [problem] go away?" (seeking understanding)

**NOT State 3 searches:**
- "Best solutions for [problem]" (optimistic, solution-aware)
- "How to [solve it]" (confident it's solvable)

**The difference in tone:** State 4 = emotional/desperate. State 3 = analytical/optimistic.

---

**CONTEXT #3 — Long-Suffering Audiences:**

**Temporal markers:**
- They've had the problem for 3+ years (chronic, not acute)
- They've tried 3+ solutions (validated experience)
- They've spent $1,000+ trying (financial commitment shows pain intensity)

**Validation:**
- Interviews: "How long have you had [the problem]?" — If >3 years + they've tried >3 solutions = likely State 4

---

**CONTEXT #4 — Advertorial → VSL (Typical Native Ads):**

**A common State 4 funnel:**

Native Ad (Taboola, curiosity/story headline) → **Advertorial** (editorial format, 800-1,200w, story or discovery) → **VSL** (30-45min, full pitch)

The advertorial = a State 4 bridge (creates hope, introduces the solution, teases) → the VSL develops it completely.

---

**Confirming signs the audience is State 4:**

✅ Forums/groups: A hopeless, resigned tone, "I've tried everything"

✅ Reviews of products in the category: 1-3 stars are common ("didn't work for me," "a waste")

✅ Questions: "Does it REALLY work or is it more hype?" (burned skepticism)

✅ Behavior: Hesitant engagement (they opt in, but are skeptical — protective, not eager)

✅ Anticipated objections: "I've already tried [something similar], it didn't work. Why would this be different?" (explicitly burned out)

---

### **WHEN NOT TO USE STATE 4:**

---

**❌ STATE 1-3 (They Already Know About Solutions or You):**

State 1-3 copy is already long (1k-6kw).

Deep State 4 agitation (1,200-1,800w of just agitation) = OVERKILL.

A State 1-3 audience: "I ALREADY KNOW the problem. I ALREADY KNOW solutions exist. Stop agitating, get to the solution!"

Impatience, bounce.

---

**❌ STATE 5 (They Don't Even Know They Have a Problem):**

State 4 agitation assumes "you FEEL the pain" (conscious suffering).

State 5: "Pain? What pain? I don't feel anything." (they don't recognize it as a problem).

Agitation = confusion ("Why are you talking about suffering I don't have?").

State 5 needs: Problem REVELATION (to create awareness) BEFORE agitation.

---

**❌ Low Pain Intensity (<6 on the Schwartz Scale):**

Deep State 4 agitation (1,200-1,800w, emotional, severe consequences) only works if the pain IS REAL (Intensity 7+).

If the pain is mild (3-5 intensity — an inconvenience, not suffering):

Deep agitation = an exaggeration (not credible, perceived as manipulation).

Better: A State 2-3 approach (educational, less emotional).

---

**❌ A Quick Decision Context:**

State 4 copy = 6-10k words (a 40-60min VSL, a 20-30 page sales letter).

It takes TIME to consume (30-90 minutes).

If the prospect needs to decide TODAY (a real deadline, extreme urgency):

They don't have the patience for long copy.

Better: Short State 1-2 copy (if they're already familiar).
