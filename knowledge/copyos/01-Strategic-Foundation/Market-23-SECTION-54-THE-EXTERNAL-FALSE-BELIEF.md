## **SECTION 5.4 — THE EXTERNAL FALSE BELIEF**

---

**Definition:** "External forces (outside of my control) are preventing my success."

---

**Variations:**
- "The market is saturated" (overwhelming competition)
- "The economy is bad" (macro conditions)
- "I don't have the money to start" (a capital barrier)
- "I don't have the time" (life circumstances)
- "I don't have the connections/network" (social capital)
- "The government/taxes" (regulatory)
- "I don't live in [the ideal location]" (geography)

**The pattern:** Externalization (it's not my fault, it's not the product — it's the WORLD that's stopping me).

---

### **How to Identify It:**

**Interviews:**

Q: "What would prevent you from [achieving the result]?"

If the answer contains external factors (the market, the economy, others, time, money) → An External FB.

---

**Forums:**

Discussions: "The market is saturated, it's impossible for a new player to win" (a market conditions FB).

"How do you start without capital?" (a money barrier FB).

"I need X, Y, and Z before I can start" (a prerequisites FB — believing success requires external factors they don't have).

---

### **Examples:**

**Entrepreneurship:**

The External FB: "The market is saturated (1,000 competitors). I don't have the money ($50k+ is needed to start — I don't have it). I don't have a network (I don't know investors, mentors). The Brazilian economy is bad (instability). It's impossible to succeed NOW (the timing is wrong)."

A **Stack** of Externals (multiple) = paralysis (so many "reasons" not to start).

---

**Copywriting:**

The External FB: "The copywriting market is saturated (everyone is offering it). Brazilian companies don't pay well ($500/project max, not $5k like in other countries). I don't have a portfolio (no one hires without experience — a catch-22). The competition on Upwork = a race to the bottom ($10/hour)."

---

### **How to Break the External FB:**

**A Story (Epiphany Bridge #3):**

The protagonist faced the SAME external forces (a saturated market, no money, no network).

They discovered: The external forces are REAL BUT they don't prevent success (others have overcome the SAME forces).

The approach: [How they got around it — by niching down (to avoid saturation), bootstrapping (without capital), strategic outreach (to build a network), etc.].

The result: Success DESPITE the external forces.

**The epiphany:** "The external forces exist BUT they aren't an excuse. Others have overcome them. I can too (strategy > circumstances)."

---

**Proof:** Testimonials from people with the SAME external barriers (no money, a saturated market, etc.) who succeeded.

**A reframe:** "The obstacles are real. BUT they are surmountable (not impossible). Here's how [X people] overcame them [list specific strategies]."
