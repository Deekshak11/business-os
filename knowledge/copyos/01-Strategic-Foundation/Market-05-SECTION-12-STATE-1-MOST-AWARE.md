## **SECTION 1.2 — STATE 1: MOST AWARE**

---

### **COMPLETE DEFINITION:**

The customer is at the **top of the awareness funnel**.

**They know:**
- ✅ YOUR PRODUCT specifically (name, brand, what it is)
- ✅ The main features (what it does, how it works)
- ✅ The benefits (what they gain by using it)
- ✅ That they WANT to buy it (the purchase decision is already formed)

**They only need:**
- The final trigger: A **Deal** (the best possible offer — discount, bonus, value)
- A **Push** (urgency to act NOW vs. "later" — deadline, scarcity)

---

**Psychological distance from purchase:** MINIMAL

Between "I want it" and "I'm buying it" there is only: The timing decision (now vs. later).

There are no: Doubts about the product, comparisons with alternatives, need for education, or skepticism about it working.

---

**Schwartz** (*Breakthrough*, p.19):

> *"The Most Aware prospect is the easiest to sell to.*
>
> *He knows your product. He knows what it does. He wants it.*
>
> *All that remains is to give him the best deal possible — and a reason to buy NOW instead of later.*
>
> *Your copy can be SHORT. No need for long education. Just offer, value, urgency."*

---

**Analogy:**

"A customer in an online checkout (cart is full, products selected).

They only need: A discount coupon (deal) OR a countdown timer (urgency: 'Stock runs out in 2h').

They don't need: A video explaining what the product is (they already know), reviews (they've already decided), or a comparison with alternatives (they've already chosen).

Education = unnecessary friction (impatience: 'I just want to complete my purchase!')."

---

**Emotional state:** Positive, ready (not in pain/frustration — that's already been mentally resolved, only the transaction is left).

---

### **APPROPRIATE COPY (Complete Characteristics):**

---

**LENGTH:** 300-800 words total (short, direct)

**Typical structural breakdown:**

```
Headline: 10-15 words
Subhead (optional): 15-25 words
Lead (Offer Lead): 100-200 words (3 paragraphs)
Body: 100-300 words (value stack reminder, savings comparison)
Proof: 0-100 words (optional — trust exists, can be omitted)
FAQ: 0-50 words (1-2 main objections if any, or zero)
CTA: 50-100 words (direct, can be repeated 2-3x in short copy)
P.S.: 20-40 words (urgency reminder or bonus mention)

Total: 300-800 words (lower end 300-500 = email, upper end 600-800 = landing page)
```

---

**FOCUS (Hierarchical priorities):**

1. **Price/Discount** (35% of attention) — Maximum highlight (visual emphasis, larger font, contrasting color)
2. **Offer/Stack** (25%) — What they receive (itemized if multiple items, value of each)
3. **Urgency/Scarcity** (25%) — Why NOW (real deadline, scarcity, penalty for delay)
4. **CTA** (15%) — Clear command (specific action, easy, repeated)

**Not the focus:**
- ❌ Problem agitation (already mentally resolved)
- ❌ Education about the product (they already know)
- ❌ Extensive proof (trust is established)
- ❌ Comparison (they've already chosen you over alternatives)

---

**TONE:** Direct, transactional, friendly but brief

**Language:**
- "Hi [Name], a quick offer..." (casual, personal)
- "Today only: 50% off..." (transactional, urgent)
- "Save $X..." (value-focused, specific)

**Avoid:**
- Long storytelling (unnecessary — emotional connection already exists)
- Agitation ("Are you suffering from..." — no, that's resolved in their mind)
- Elaborating on features ("Let me explain how it works..." — they already know)

---

**STRUCTURE:** Linear, simple

Flow: Offer → Value → Urgency → CTA (a straight line, no twists)

**Scannable:** Bullets, minimal subheads, visual hierarchy (easy to read in 30-60 seconds)

---

**PROOF LEVEL:** Minimal or zero

**Why it can be omitted:**
- Trust is already established (existing customers or hot traffic = they've interacted before)
- Decision is already formed (they don't need to be convinced again)
- Extensive proof = overkill (might even cause doubt: "Why are they insisting so much? Is something wrong?")

**If you include proof:**
- Brief (50-100 words)
- Generic social proof ("10,487 customers," logo bar)
- Not long testimonials (unnecessary)

---

### **HEADLINE TYPE — Pure Offer:**

**Base template:**

```
[PRODUCT/BRAND] — [DISCOUNT/VALUE] — Expires [DATE]
```

**Mandatory elements (3):**

1. **Product/brand name** (instant recognition — familiar, trigger: "Oh yes, [product]")
2. **Discount OR value** (specific number — "50% OFF" or "Save $1,000" — tangible)
3. **Urgency** (specific deadline — "Friday" or "Today at 11:59 PM," not a vague "soon")

---

**Variations (additional State 1 templates):**

**Variation A — Benefit + Offer:**

```
[PRODUCT]: [Core Benefit] — [X]% OFF Today Only
```

Example: "ClickFunnels: Build Complete Funnels — 50% OFF Annual Plan (Today Only)"

---

**Variation B — Urgency Emphasis:**

```
Last Chance: [PRODUCT] [Discount] (Expires [Specific Time])
```

Example: "Last Chance: Vitamin D3 — Buy 2 Get 3 (Expires Today at 11:59 PM)"

---

**Variation C — Exclusivity:**

```
[BRAND] — Exclusive Offer for [Segment] — [Value]
```

Example: "Netflix — 3 Months Free — Exclusive Offer for Former Members"

---

**Persuasion elements (always present):**

✅ **Specific** (numbers, dates — not vague: "50% off" not "big discount," "Friday" not "soon")

✅ **Urgency** (deadline or scarcity — Doc 1 Section 2.6 Scarcity)

✅ **Clear value** (quantified savings when possible — "Save $1,000")

---

**Examples (4, diverse niches):**

**a) SaaS/Software (ClickFunnels):**

"ClickFunnels — 50% Discount on Annual Plan — Only Until Friday 11:59 PM ET"

Analysis:
- Recognized product (ClickFunnels = known brand in State 1)
- Clear discount (50% — specific)
- Specific deadline (Friday 11:59 PM ET — timezone included)
- Length: 13 words (concise)

---

**b) E-commerce/Supplement:**

"[Brand] Vitamin D3 — Buy 2 Get 3 — Last 247 Units — Ends Today"

Analysis:
- Specific product (D3, recognized brand)
- Offer (buy 2 get 3 — clear value)
- Scarcity (247 units — specific number, not "few")
- Urgency (today — deadline)

---

**c) Subscription/Streaming:**

"Spotify Premium — 3 Months for $9.90 (Normally $21.90/Month) — Today Only"

Analysis:
- Brand (Spotify — universally known)
- Deal (3 months for $9.90 vs. normal $21.90/month = clear savings)
- Urgency (today only)

---

**d) Info Product/Course:**

"[Your Name]'s Copy Course — Black Friday: $997 → $497 — Expires Nov 27th, 11:59 PM"

Analysis:
- Product (course known to subscribers)
- Event (Black Friday — familiar context)
- Pricing ($997 → $497 — anchoring + discount)
- Deadline (specific date + time)

---

**Why these headlines work for State 1:**

**Recognition** → Product/brand name = triggers memory ("Oh yes, I wanted this")

**Clarity** → The deal is obvious in a 2-second scan (50% off, buy 2 get 3, etc.)

**Urgency** → Deadline/scarcity = immediate action ("I need to act today")

---

**Why they WOULDN'T work for States 2-5:**

A State 2-5 prospect would see the headline and think: "A discount on what? What is [unknown brand]? Why do I need it?" (confusion, skip).

State 1 is the only state where the product name alone is sufficient (recognition exists).

---

### **LEAD TYPE — Offer Lead (Masterson):**

**→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 6, Section 6.1** (Complete Offer Lead structure, multiple examples, when to use it vs. the other 5 lead types)

---

**Structure (3 paragraphs, 100-200 words total):**

---

**PARAGRAPH 1 — State the Offer Directly (30-50 words):**

Template:
```
"Today only: [Product] with a [X]% discount.

Normally [normal price], today it's [discounted price].

Your savings: [$ specific amount]."
```

Example:
```
"Today only: ClickFunnels Annual Plan with a 50% discount.

Normally $297/month ($3,564/year).

Today: $1,782/year.

Your savings: $1,782."
```

Elements: Offer in line 1 (no intro/buildup), pricing (normal vs. today — anchoring Doc 1 Section 3.4), savings ($ specific, not % — perceived as higher impact).

---

**PARAGRAPH 2 — Benefit Reminder (40-60 words):**

Template:
```
"The same [main features they already know].

The same [core benefit].

Only the price is different (a saving of [$ or %])."
```

Example:
```
"The same unlimited funnels, 200+ templates, 40+ integrations, and A/B testing.

The same complete platform you already know.

Only the price is different (half price)."
```

Elements: Feature reminder (brief — they already know, just a refresh), benefit (core value prop), emphasis on "only the price is different" (everything is the same, the deal is the only differential).

---

**PARAGRAPH 3 — Urgency + CTA (30-50 words):**

Template:
```
"Expires on [date] at [time] [timezone].

After that, the price goes back to [normal price].

[Direct CTA — button or link]."
```

Example:
```
"Expires Friday at 11:59 PM ET.

After Friday, it's back to $297/month.

[GREEN BUTTON: LOCK IN 50% OFF NOW →]"
```

Elements: Deadline (specific — date, time, timezone), penalty (price goes back — loss aversion Doc 1 Section 3.5), visual CTA (button, contrasting color, clear action).

---

**Total Lead:** 100-160 words (concise, zero unnecessary elaboration).

**Tone:** Factual, direct (not emotional/story — pure transaction).

---

### **BODY STRUCTURE (The Bare Minimum):**

**For State 1, the body is OPTIONAL or MINIMAL** (100-300 words if included).

---

**If you include a body (for a landing page, not an email), the sections are:**

**A. Value Stack** (if there are multiple items — 100-150 words):

```
"What you get:
• [Item 1]
• [Item 2]
• [Item 3]

Total value: $X
Today's price: $Y
Your savings: $Z"
```

Itemization (bullets) = scannable, clear value.

---

**B. Urgency Reinforcement** (50-100 words):

```
"Why does it expire on [date]?

[Reason-why — if possible: event-based (Black Friday ends),
inventory (last units), cohort (class closes)]

After [deadline]: [Penalty — price goes up, offer unavailable]"
```

Reason-why (Doc 1 Section 1.3 — Langer's "because") + penalty (loss aversion).

---

**C. Repeated CTA** (50 words):

The same CTA (button/link) after the value stack and after the urgency section.

In State 1, you can repeat it 3x in short copy (it's not annoying — it's transactional and expected).

---

**D. P.S.** (20-40 words — always):

```
"P.S. Seriously — this expires [deadline]. Don't miss out on [savings/bonus].
One click. [Repeated link]"
```

Doc 1 validated: The P.S. is read by 80% (even by those who skimmed the body). For State 1, it's a reminder of the urgency or savings.

---

**AVOID in the State 1 body:**

❌ **Problem agitation** ("Are you suffering from..." — no, it's mentally resolved, they want the product)

❌ **Education about the product** ("Let me explain how [product] works..." — they already know, it's tedious)

❌ **Long testimonials** (3-5 testimonials of 100 words each — overkill, trust exists)

❌ **Comparison with competitors** (they've already chosen you, comparison might create doubt)

❌ **Extensive FAQ** (8-10 questions — State 1 has few doubts, 1-2 max or zero)

**State 1 = less is more** (respectful brevity).

---

### **WHEN TO USE STATE 1:**

---

**CONTEXT #1 — EXISTING CUSTOMERS (Email List, Bought Before):**

**Signs:**
- Database: They bought product A (12 months ago), you're offering product B (upsell/cross-sell)
- Email list: Segmented by "customers" (not "leads")
- Relationship: Multiple interactions (emails opened, previous purchases)

**Why State 1:**
- They know the brand (trust is established)
- Previous positive experience (assumed — if it was negative, they wouldn't be on the list)
- Familiar with the product type (they've used something similar)

**Copy:** A 100-200 word email, direct offer, clear CTA.

---

**CONTEXT #2 — HOT TRAFFIC (Retargeting, Behavioral Signals):**

**Signs:**
- Pixel data: Visited the site 3+ times (deep research)
- Added to cart but didn't complete the purchase (almost bought, final hesitation)
- Spent 5+ minutes on product pages (high engagement, not a bounce)
- Watched a full demo/video (interest validated, time invested)
- Downloaded resources (whitepapers, PDFs — engaged)

**Why almost State 1:**
- Multiple visits = they know the product (not their first exposure)
- Time invested = serious interest (not casual browsing)
- Add-to-cart = micro-commitment (Doc 1 Section 2.2 — Commitment)

**Copy:** Retargeting ad (short, 50-100w) or email (150-300w), offer-focused, urgency (deadline or bonus to complete).

---

**CONTEXT #3 — PRODUCT LAUNCHES (Subscribers, Waitlist):**

**Signs:**
- Opted-in to "notify me when it launches" (explicit intent)
- On the waitlist (registered interest, waiting)
- Following updates (opened pre-launch emails, engaged)
- Left comments/questions (active interaction, not passive)

**Why State 1:**
- Total awareness (they know the product, what it does — pre-launch education happened)
- Pent-up demand (they've been waiting for weeks/months — hot)
- Pre-formed decision (a waitlist is a soft commitment)

**Copy:** Launch email (200-400w), offer (launch price, early bird bonuses), urgency (first 48h, limited qty), CTA (buy now).

---

**CONTEXT #4 — REMARKETING (Abandoned Cart, Browse Abandonment):**

**Signs:**
- Added a product to the cart, didn't complete (final hesitation)
- Viewed a product 2-3x in the same session (interest but no conversion)
- Compared pricing tiers (decision mode, not discovery)

**Why State 1:**
- Product selected (add-to-cart = choice made)
- They know the details (saw the product page, pricing, features)
- The barrier isn't knowledge (State 2-5) — it's timing, price hesitation, or distraction

**Copy:** Remarketing email/ad (100-200w), reminder ("You left this in your cart"), sweetener (extra 10-15% discount, free shipping), urgency (cart expires in 24h), CTA (complete your purchase in 1-click).

---

**Confirming signs (the audience is validated as State 1):**

✅ They ask about **price/availability** (not "what is it?", "how does it work?")

✅ They compare **your product vs. nothing** (not vs. competitors — they've already chosen you, they're just deciding whether to buy or not)

✅ They mention the product **by NAME** in communications ("When will [product] be on sale?")

✅ Behavior: Multiple touchpoints (3+ visits, opened emails, engagement)

✅ Speed: Quick decision when an offer appears (hours-days, not weeks — ready to buy)

---

### **WHEN NOT TO USE STATE 1 (Avoid Mismatch):**

---

**❌ COLD TRAFFIC (They've Never Heard of You):**

Facebook ads to broad interests (e.g., "Entrepreneurship," "Fitness").

Google Display Network (cold audiences).

YouTube pre-roll (interruption, not intent).

**Why not State 1:**
- Zero recognition (they don't know who you are, what you sell)
- State 1 copy (direct offer for an unknown product) = confusion
- They need: State 4-5 copy (education, awareness creation)

---

**❌ NEW PROSPECTS (Recently Acquired Leads):**

They downloaded a lead magnet yesterday (first interaction).

They opted-in for a webinar (haven't watched it yet).

**Why not State 1:**
- Nascent relationship (trust-building is in progress)
- Low product awareness (they might not have even seen the sales page yet)
- They need: A nurture sequence (Doc 10 — 5-7 educational emails BEFORE a State 1 pitch)

---

**❌ SKEPTICAL/SATURATED MARKET (Stage 5):**

Even if they know the product (State 1-2 awareness), if they're in Stage 5 (ultra-sophisticated):

Skepticism is extremely high ("I've seen 100 offers, all empty promises").

**State 1 copy** (direct offer, minimal proof) = insufficient (doesn't break skepticism).

They need: Massive proof (video testimonials, case studies, strong guarantee) + Story/identification (Stage 5 approach — Doc 2 Part II).

**Layers cross:** State 1 + Stage 5 = a direct offer BUT with extensive proof (hybrid approach).

---

**❌ COMPLEX/NEW PRODUCT (Innovation):**

Enterprise SaaS (20 features, complex integration).

A product in a new category (they've never seen it before).

**Why not State 1:**
- Even if interested, they don't fully UNDERSTAND (knowledge gap)
- A direct offer = "Buy... what exactly? How does it work?"
- They need: A demo, explanation, education (State 2-3 approach)

---

**Fatal error:**

Assuming State 1 when it's 3-5 (thinking "everyone knows my product" — ego bias).

**Reality:** Most people don't know it (your bubble ≠ the real market).

**Validate** (don't assume): Ask 10 people in your avatar, "Do you know [product]?" If <7 say "yes" → It's NOT State 1.

• Specificity: Numbers ($1,782, 200 templates, 40 integrations, 8 hours)
• Reciprocity: Implicit (an offer "for you," existing customer care)
• Commitment: Existing customer (already committed to the brand)

Expected conversion: 8-15% (existing customers, relevant offer, real
