## **SECTION 5.1 — INTRODUCTION TO THE FRAMEWORK**

---

**Russell Brunson** (*Expert Secrets*, 2017, Chapter 5):

> *"Every prospect has THREE false beliefs preventing them from buying.*
>
> *Not one. Not two. THREE.*
>
> *If you break two but leave one standing, they WON'T buy.*
>
> *All three must be addressed and BROKEN.*
>
> *Break all three = the sale is inevitable."*

---

### **THE 3 FALSE BELIEFS (The Categories):**

---

**FALSE BELIEF #1 — THE VEHICLE (About the Product/Solution):**

"This type of product/solution doesn't work (for me, or in general)."

Example: "Diets don't work" (they've been burned before, are cynical about the category).

---

**FALSE BELIEF #2 — THE INTERNAL (About Themselves):**

"I can't do this / I'm not capable / I don't have the talent."

Example: "I'm not tech-savvy enough to start an online business" (self-doubt, a limiting belief).

---

**FALSE BELIEF #3 — THE EXTERNAL (About External Forces):**

"External forces are preventing my success (the market, the economy, the system, others)."

Example: "The market is saturated, it's impossible to compete" (they externalize, are hopeless about the environment).

---

**The relationship between the 3:**

**They are layers** (layers of resistance):

```
THE SURFACE LAYER (the easiest to break):
↓
The Vehicle (the product) — "Product X doesn't work"
↓
The Internal (their capability) — "I can't use product X"
↓
The External (the environment) — "Even if I can, external forces will stop me"
↓
THE DEEP LAYER (the hardest, the last resistance)
```

**Breaking only The Vehicle:** The customer thinks "OK, the product works... BUT I can't do it" (The Internal isn't broken) → They don't buy.

**Breaking The Vehicle + The Internal:** "OK, it works, AND I can do it... BUT the market is saturated, it's impossible" (The External isn't broken) → They don't buy.

**Breaking all 3:** "It works (The Vehicle ✓), I can do it (The Internal ✓), and the environment allows it (The External ✓)" → **They buy** (no barriers are left).

---

**Brunson:**

> *"Most marketers only address The Vehicle belief ('The product works! Here's proof!').*
>
> *They ignore The Internal and The External.*
>
> *The result: The prospect believes the product works but STILL doesn't buy (The Internal/The External are blocking them).*
>
> *You MUST address all three — explicitly, with stories (the Epiphany Bridge)."*

---

### **The Connection to OBJECTIONS:**

**False Beliefs = the ROOT of objections.**

**Objections = the SYMPTOMS.**

---

Example:

**The False Belief (Internal):** "I'm not tech-savvy enough."

**The derived objections** (the symptoms):
- "I don't have time to learn the tech" (a time objection)
- "It's too complicated for me" (a complexity objection)
- "I need 24/7 tech support" (a capability objection)
- "What if I can't implement it?" (a fear of failure objection)

**If you break the Internal FB** (via an Epiphany Bridge — "you don't need to be tech-savvy, the system is simple, anyone can do it") → **ALL the derived objections disappear** (the root has been removed).

**vs.**

**If you break the objections individually** (time, complexity, support, fear) WITHOUT breaking the root FB → **New objections will appear** (a hydra — you cut off 1 head, 2 grow back).

---

**The strategy:**

**Not:** Breaking 15 individual objections (it's exhaustive, it's whack-a-mole).

**Yes:** Breaking the 3 False Beliefs (the roots) → The derived objections automatically collapse.

**Efficiency:** 3 stories (Epiphany Bridge, Doc 11 Section 1.2) > 15 FAQs.

---

**The implication for Doc 2:**

**IDENTIFY the 3 specific False Beliefs** of your avatar (via research — Sections 5.2-5.4).

**List them in the Campaign Brief** (Part IX).

**Use them in Doc 11** (to break them via Epiphany Bridge × 3, 1 story per belief).
