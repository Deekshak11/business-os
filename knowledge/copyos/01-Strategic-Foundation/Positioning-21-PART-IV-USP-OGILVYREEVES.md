## **PART IV — USP (OGILVY/REEVES)**

**Words:** 2,000

**Section 4.1 — The 3 Elements of a USP (1,200w):**

Rosser Reeves (1960s, *Reality in Advertising*) coined the USP.

Ogilvy popularized it (*Confessions of an Advertising Man*).

**The 3 mandatory elements:**

1.  **UNIQUE:** Competitors don't say it (or can't say it)
2.  **SELLING:** It's a REAL benefit to the customer (it's not just different, it's BETTER)
3.  **PROPOSITION:** A specific claim (not a vague "quality" — it's tangible)

Classic examples developed:

**M&Ms:** "Melts in your mouth, not in your hand" (unique = the chocolate coating tech, selling = convenience/cleanliness, proposition = a specific, testable claim)

**Domino's (original):** "Fresh, hot pizza in 30 minutes or it's FREE" (unique = a speed guarantee, selling = fast/free, proposition = a specific 30min/free)

**FedEx:** "When it absolutely, positively has to be there overnight" (unique = absolute reliability, selling = peace of mind for critical shipments, proposition = overnight is guaranteed)

How to create each element (the process), validation tests (is it REALLY unique? Have you checked competitors?), and examples for various products.

**Section 4.2 — USP vs. Big Idea vs. Mechanism (800w):**

**The differences (a table):**

| Concept    | Definition                           | Example                                         | When to Use                      |
|------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Benefit    | What you get                         | "Lose 20 lbs"                                   | Universal (everyone has it)      |
| Mechanism  | How it works                         | "Via ketosis"                                   | Technical differentiation        |
| Big Idea   | The angle/presentation               | "Eat fat to burn fat"                           | Messaging differentiation        |
| USP        | An exclusive claim                   | "The only one with a 20 lbs or $500 guarantee"  | Transactional differentiation    |
| Touchstone | A sticky phrase                      | "Bacon for breakfast, pounds off"               | A memorable summary              |

**The hierarchy:** The Benefit (the base) → The Mechanism (the HOW) → The Big Idea (the presentation of the HOW) → The USP (the claim of exclusivity) → The Touchstone (the memorable expression).

**They are all connected** (not isolated — the Big Idea informs the USP, the USP uses the mechanism, etc.).
