## **SECTION 3.2 — CALCULATING YOUR MARKET (An Applied Worksheet)**

---

### **THE PROCESS (5 Steps):**

---

**STEP 1 — List Your Potential Markets (3-5 options):**

Don't commit to just 1 yet. Explore your options.

```
Market A: [A specific description — who, the problem, the context]
Market B: [Different from A — could be the same product, a different audience]
Market C: [An alternative]
[...]
```

Example:
```
Product: An Online Copywriting Course

Market A: Brazilian digital entrepreneurs, 35-55, stuck at $10-50k/month
Market B: Beginner freelancers, 22-35, who want to earn an extra $500-2k/month
Market C: Marketing professionals (35-50), who want to add copywriting as a skill, are employed
```

---

**STEP 2 — Estimate the MASS of Each Market:**

**Methods (use 2-3, triangulate):**

a) **Google KW Planner:**
- The market's main keywords
- Searches/month (in Brazil or globally)
- A multiplier: 10-50x (not everyone who has the problem uses Google search)

b) **Facebook Audience:**
- Target interests + demographics
- The "potential reach" number

c) **Groups/Forums:**
- The count of active members
- A multiplier: 20-100x (lurkers, non-members with the same problem)

d) **Industry reports:**
- "[Niche] market size Brazil" (Google)
- Paid (IBISWorld) or free (blogs, studies) reports

**A conservative estimate** (the lowest of the methods).

---

Example calculation:
```
Market A (Stuck Brazilian digital entrepreneurs):

Google KW: "how to scale a digital business" = 8,900 searches/month × 30 (multiplier) = 267,000

Facebook Audience: Interests (entrepreneurship, digital marketing, online business) + Age 35-55 + Brazil + Behavior (small business owner) = 340,000 potential reach

Groups: "Brazilian Digital Entrepreneurs" groups (the sum of the top 10) = 180,000 members × 2 (lurkers) = 360,000

Industry: Digital entrepreneurs in Brazil (SEBRAE 2023) = ~500,000 (total, not just those who are stuck)

CONSERVATIVE ESTIMATE (the lowest): 267,000
LIBERAL ESTIMATE (the highest): 500,000
USED: 300,000 (a mid-range, conservative number)
```

---

**STEP 3 — Score the INTENSITY of Each Market:**

Use the 4 methods (A-D above) and average them.

Example:
```
Market A (Stuck entrepreneurs):

Method A ($ spent): They've tried ads ($3-10k), courses ($1-3k), mentorships ($5-15k) — Avg $5k+ → Intensity 9

Method B (sleep): Researching forums — posts like "I can't sleep thinking about this," "I wake up at 3am worried" — common → Intensity 8

Method C (impact): It affects their family (absence, stress), their health (burnout, anxiety), their self-image (frustration, "am I a failure?") → Severe → Intensity 9

Method D (WTP): 5 people were interviewed: "How much would you pay for a guaranteed solution to scale 3x?" — The answers: $5k, $10k, $8k, $15k, $7k (avg $9k) → Intensity 9

AVERAGE: (9+8+9+9) ÷ 4 = 8.75 → **Intensity: 9/10**
```

---

**STEP 4 — Calculate the MARKET VALUE SCORE:**

```
MARKET VALUE = Mass × Intensity

Market A: 300,000 × 9 = 2,700,000

Market B (Beginner freelancers): 
Mass: 800,000 (larger — more freelancers than entrepreneurs)
Intensity: 5 (moderate — they want extra income but aren't desperate)
Score: 800,000 × 5 = 4,000,000

Market C (Employed marketing professionals):
Mass: 150,000
Intensity: 4 (low — a nice-to-have skill, not urgent)
Score: 150,000 × 4 = 600,000
```

---

**STEP 5 — Compare and Choose:**

**Benchmarks:**

- Score >5,000,000: Excellent (pursue with confidence)
- Score 1,000,000-5,000,000: Good (viable, proceed)
- Score 100,000-1,000,000: Questionable (only if the Intensity is 8-10 — extreme pain compensates for low mass)
- Score <100,000: Poor (reconsider — the market is too small OR the desire is too weak)

---

**Example comparison:**

| Market | Mass | Intensity | Score | Rank |
|---------|------|-----------|-------|------|
| B (Freelancers) | 800k | 5 | 4,000,000 | #1 (score) |
| **A (Entrepreneurs)** | 300k | **9** | **2,700,000** | **#2 (score) BUT #1 (Intensity)** |
| C (Marketing pros) | 150k | 4 | 600,000 | #3 |

---

**The choice:**

**By SCORE:** Market B (4M > 2.7M).

**By INTENSITY (Schwartz's rule):** **Market A** (Intensity 9 > 5).

---

**Schwartz recommends:** **Choose A** (prioritize Intensity).

**Why:**

Market B (Intensity 5): Mild desire = low CVR (1-3%), low WTP ($200-500), risk of commoditization, high churn.

Market A (Intensity 9): Extreme pain = high CVR (5-12%), high WTP ($2-5k), premium positioning, high loyalty.

**Even though Market B's score is higher, A is a better business** (superior economics).

---

**The rule:** If the Intensity difference is >3 points, choose the higher Intensity (even if the score is lower).

**The exception:** If the Mass difference is 10x+ (e.g., 50k vs. 500k) AND the Intensity is similar (7 vs. 8), consider the Mass.

**In general:** **Intensity > Mass** (in most cases).
