## **SECTION 4.2 — PRICE ANCHORING (APPLIED KAHNEMAN)**

---

**Kahneman** (Doc 1 Section 3.4 — Anchoring):

The first number presented = a mental reference point (contaminates subsequent judgments).

**Application in pricing:** Show a HIGHER number BEFORE the real price (a high anchor = the real price seems like a bargain).

---

### **ANCHORING TACTICS:**

---

**TACTIC #1 — VALUE STACK BEFORE PRICE:**

**Sequence (Brunson's Stack Slide — developed in Part VII):**

```
Slide 1: Core Product (R$2,000 value)
Slide 2: + Bonus #1 (R$500)
Slide 3: + Bonus #2 (R$300)
Slide 4: + Bonus #3 (R$800)
[Slides 5-8: Continue stacking bonuses]

Slide 9: TOTAL VALUE = R$4,800 [ANCHOR ESTABLISHED]

[Pause — build expectation]

Slide 10: "Normally this would cost R$4,800..."
Slide 11: "But you're NOT going to pay R$4,800..."
Slide 12: "Not R$3,000... Not even R$2,000..."
Slide 13: "Your investment today: R$997"

Slide 14: "That's right. R$997 for R$4,800 in value.
           You save R$3,803."
```

**Anchor:** R$4,800 (total value stacked BEFORE the price reveal).

**Real price:** R$997 (revealed AFTER the anchor).

**Perception:** R$997 = a steal (a 79% "discount" vs. the R$4,800 anchor).

**vs. Revealing R$997 FIRST** (without an anchor): It might seem expensive (no reference point).

**Contrast effect** (Kahneman): R$4,800 vs. R$997 = a massive difference = perceived as a bargain.

---

**TACTIC #2 — COMPARISON TO AN EXPENSIVE ALTERNATIVE:**

It doesn't have to be YOUR product (it can be a competitor, an equivalent service, the DIY cost).

```
"Personal financial coaching: R$500/hour.
To build a complete plan + implement it: 15-20 hours = R$7,500-10,000.

This course: R$997 (same content in modules, self-paced, lifetime access).

Savings: R$6,500-9,000 vs. hiring a coach."
```

**Anchor:** R$7,500-10,000 (the expensive alternative).

**Your price:** R$997 (the comparison = 90% cheaper).

**Perception:** Massive value (same outcome, 10% of the cost).

---

**TACTIC #3 — "NORMALLY R$X" (Even If You've Never Sold It at X):**

```
"Normal price after launch: R$2,497
Launch price today: R$997
Savings: R$1,500"
```

**Anchor:** R$2,497 ("normal" — the future price).

**Ethical?** If you INTEND to sell it for R$2,497 later (a genuine future plan) = OK.

**Unethical:** If you're NEVER going to sell it for R$2,497 (a fake anchor, dishonest) = fraud.

**Ladeira's emphasis:** "The price increase MUST be real (genuinely raise it after the launch). Fake = brand death."

---

**TACTIC #4 — DECOY PRICING (Ariely — Doc 1 Section 4.1):**

**3 tiers (the decoy makes the middle one irresistible):**

```
BASIC: R$497 (core only, limited)

PREMIUM: R$997 (core + bonuses + support) ← TARGET (you want the majority to choose this)

ELITE: R$997 (same as Premium + 1 group call bonus) ← DECOY (dominated by Premium — same price, less value)
```

The **Elite** is dominated by the **Premium** (same price of R$997, but Premium has more).

The **presence of the Elite:** Makes the Premium seem OBVIOUSLY the best (a favorable comparison).

**Result:** 60-70% choose Premium (vs. 30-40% without the decoy — Ariely tested).

**The decoy:** Is never chosen (0-5%), BUT its presence increases Premium conversions by +100%+ (the asymmetric dominance effect).
