## **SECTION 1.4 — STATE 3: SOLUTION-AWARE**

---

### **COMPLETE DEFINITION:**

The customer has **solution category awareness** (they know that solutions to their problem EXIST).

**They know:**
- ✅ That the problem IS solvable (it's not impossible, they don't have to "live with it")
- ✅ The general category of solutions ("a personal trainer helps with weight loss," "a CRM organizes sales," "a course teaches a skill")
- ✅ That multiple solutions/approaches exist (keto, paleo, IF for weight loss)

**They DON'T know:**
- ❌ YOUR specific PRODUCT (your brand/name = still unknown)
- ❌ The details of specific products (they haven't compared them yet — discovery phase)

**Mental mode:** Research/discovery (seeking information about solutions, not comparing products yet).

---

**They need:**

1. **To identify YOUR product** as THE SOLUTION (not just ONE solution — positioning it as the obvious choice)
2. **An introduction + credibility** (you're unknown — who are you, why should they trust you)
3. **Category differentiation** (why your approach > the generic solutions they know)

---

**Distance from purchase:** Medium-long (more research than State 2, less education than State 4-5).

---

**Schwartz** (p.24):

> *"The Solution-Aware prospect knows solutions exist but doesn't know YOUR specific product.*
>
> *Your copy must introduce yourself, establish credibility quickly, and position your product as THE solution — not just another option in a crowded category.*
>
> *This requires education (what makes you different from generic solutions) but not as much as for the Problem-Aware (they already know the problem and are seeking solutions)."*

---

**Analogy:**

"A person wants to lose weight (problem-aware).

They've researched: Googled 'how to lose weight' (solution-aware — they know diets/exercise work).

They've read articles: 'Keto works,' 'Paleo is effective,' 'Intermittent Fasting results.'

They know: Solutions exist (multiple — diet, exercise, lifestyle).

They DON'T know: Specific program X, Y, or Z (the brands are unknown).

Their Google search: 'Best weight loss programs' (generic, discovery).

**Your product** (a specific Keto program): Needs to appear in THAT search, and be positioned as THE answer (not just be listed)."

---

**Difference between State 2 vs. 3:**

**State 2:** Knows YOUR product (comparing you vs. Competitor A, B).

**State 3:** DOESN'T know you yet (discovering general options, you are new to them).

**Copy implication:**

State 2 = assumes recognition (you can mention your name right away).

State 3 = an introduction is necessary (who we are, what we do, why we exist — don't assume they know).

---

**Overlap with State 4:**

Both don't know you (you're unknown).

**The difference:** State 3 knows SOLUTIONS exist (they're searching). State 4 DOESN'T know (or thinks it's impossible).

State 3 = optimistic ("there's an answer, I'll find it"). State 4 = resigned ("nothing works").

---

**Emotional state:** Hopeful, active (researching, not passively suffering like State 4).

---

### **APPROPRIATE COPY:**

---

**LENGTH:** 3,000-6,000 words (medium-long)

**Range explanation:**

**Lower end (3,000-4,000w):**
- The differentiation is simple (a unique approach, easy to explain)
- Credibility is established quickly (clear authority, recognition exists)
- The category isn't complex (a simple concept)

**Upper end (5,000-6,000w):**
- The differentiation is complex (a deep mechanism, needs analogies, education)
- Credibility is built from scratch (you are a complete unknown)
- The category has nuances (multiple sub-approaches, needs clarification)

---

**STRUCTURAL BREAKDOWN:**

```
Headline: 20-30 words (news/how-to/discovery, solution-oriented)

Subhead: 30-50 words (expands on the benefit, hints at differentiation, adds a proof element)

Lead (Problem-Solution preferred): 400-700 words
├─ P1: Problem identification (100-150w — brief, they already know it)
├─ P2: Acknowledge common solutions (100-150w — validate their research)
├─ P3: Why common solutions are incomplete (150-250w — sets up your differentiation)
├─ P4: YOUR solution introduced (100-150w — positioning it as the answer)

Problem Section: 400-700 words (moderate)
├─ Agitate briefly (not as deep as State 4)
├─ Focus: "Common solutions don't solve it COMPLETELY"
└─ Setup: Why you solve what others don't

Introduction Section (NEW for State 3): 300-500 words
├─ Who you are (brief credentials)
├─ Why you created the product (short origin story)
├─ Track record (social proof — X clients, Y years)
└─ Establish credibility (you're unknown, you need an introduction)

Solution/Differentiation: 900-1,400 words (THE CORE — 25%)
├─ What the product is (name, category, what it does — 100-200w)
├─ The Mechanism: HOW it works (400-600w — step-by-step, analogies)
├─ Category differentiation: Why your approach > generic solutions (300-400w)
└─ Specific benefits (200-300w)

Benefits: 500-800 words
└─ 15-20 bullets (benefits of the unique mechanism)

Proof: 700-1,000 words
├─ Testimonials (3-4 — transformation stories)
├─ Data/track record (aggregated results)
├─ Detailed case study (1)
└─ Authority citations (if applicable)

Offer: 400-600 words
└─ Stack, pricing, guarantee, urgency

FAQ: 400-600 words
└─ 6-8 questions (how it works, why it's different, who it's for)

CTA: 150-200 words

P.S.: 40-60 words
```

**Total:** 3,000-6,000 words (flexible).

---

**HIERARCHICAL FOCUS:**

1. **Solution/Mechanism explanation** (28%) — Core of State 3, introduce and explain deeply
2. **Proof/Credibility** (22%) — You're unknown, establish trust
3. **Differentiation** (18%) — Why your approach > generic ones
4. **Benefits** (15%)
5. **Problem agitation** (10% — less than State 4, just for setup)
6. **Offer** (7%)

---

**TONE:** Educational, helpful, expert (not salesy)

**Language:**
- "There are several ways to solve [problem]. We tested them all. Here's the best one..." (research-based, authoritative)
- "Let me explain how it works..." (educational, patient)
- "3 reasons this approach is superior..." (systematic, logical)
- Not pushy ("Buy now!" — too soon for State 3)
- Not vague ("The best solution" — always be specific)

---

### **HEADLINE TYPE — News/How-to:**

**Templates (8 variations):**

**Template A — "Finally/At Last" (Solution Arrival):**
```
"Finally/At Last: A Way to [Get a Benefit] Without [the Common Pain/Sacrifice of Generic Solutions]"
```

Examples:

a) "Finally: A Way to Lose 20 Lbs and KEEP It Off Without a Gym Membership, Meal Prep, or Counting Every Calorie"

→ Solution (lose + keep), without sacrifices (gym, meal prep, counting — common barriers of generic solutions)

b) "At Last: A CRM That Your Sales Team Actually USES (85% Daily Adoption vs. 40% Industry Avg Shelf-ware)"

→ Solution (a CRM that's used, not abandoned), comparative proof (85% vs. 40%)

---

**Template B — "How-to" (Process-Oriented):**
```
"How to [Get a Benefit] in [Timeframe] Even If [Worst-Case Scenario] (Using a [Mechanism/Approach Hint])"
```

Examples:

a) "How to Scale from $30k to $100k/Month in 90 Days Even If You Have Zero Team (A 3-Pillar System, Validated by 47 Companies)"

→ Benefit ($30k→100k), timeframe (90 days), obstacle removed (zero team), mechanism hint (3-pillar), proof (47 companies)

b) "How to Write a Sales Letter That Converts at 5%+ Even If You've Never Written Copy Before (Fill-in-the-Blank Templates, 340 Beginners Got Results)"

→ Benefit (5% CVR), obstacle (never written), solution hint (templates), proof (340 beginners)

---

**Template C — "New/Discovery" (Innovation Angle):**
```
"New/Discovery: A [Solution Type] That [Achieves a Result] ([Unique Mechanism/Authority])"
```

Examples:

a) "New: A CRM Built FOR WhatsApp (Not 'a CRM + a Zapier Integration' — It's Native, Instant, and Never Breaks)"

→ Innovation (built FOR, not adapted), differentiation (native vs. Zapier), benefits (instant, reliable)

b) "A Johns Hopkins Discovery: The 7-Second Technique That Eliminates Back Pain in 83% of Cases (Without Pills or Expensive Physical Therapy)"

→ Authority (Johns Hopkins), mechanism (7-sec technique), efficacy (83%), without barriers (pills, PT)

---

**Template D — Number-Based (Systematic):**
```
"[Number] Ways to [Get a Benefit] — We Tested Them All, #[Number] Is [Superlative] ([Proof])"
```

Example: "7 Diets For Weight Loss — We Tested Them All in a Study of 1,200 People, #4 (Carb Cycling) Is the Only One With 0% Weight Regain in 12 Months"

→ Comparison (7 diets), authority (we tested, a study), the winner (#4), superlative (0% regain), proof (1,200 people, 12 months)

---

**Mandatory elements for State 3 headlines:**

✅ **Solution-oriented** (focus on "finally there's an answer," "how-to," not problem horror)

✅ **Specific benefit** (quantified: "20 lbs," "$100k," "5% CVR" — not a vague "results")

✅ **Differentiation hint** (mechanism, approach, authority — tease what makes it special)

✅ **Credibility signal** (numbers, authority mention, proof element — you're unknown, you need a credential in the headline)

✅ **Obstacle removal** ("even if," "without" — address the barriers that common solutions have)

---

### **LEAD TYPE — Problem-Solution Lead:**

**→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 6, Section 6.3** (Detailed Problem-Solution Lead)

---

**Structure (4 paragraphs, 400-700 words):**

---

**PARAGRAPH 1 — Identify the Problem (100-150 words):**

Template:
```
"You have [a specific problem].

You ALREADY KNOW that [category of solutions] can help (you've researched — [mention their common research: Google, articles, videos]).

You've seen: [2-3 generic solutions mentioned — keto, paleo, IF / CRMs, personal trainers, apps].

But..."
```

Example:
```
"You want to scale your business from $30k to $100k/month.

You ALREADY KNOW that 'systems' and 'processes' can help (you've researched — you've read articles about scaling, watched YouTube videos on 'how to scale,' maybe you even bought a course or two).

You've seen solutions mentioned: Hiring a team (to delegate tasks), paid ads (to scale traffic), automation (software/tools), outsourcing (VAs, freelancers).

But..."
```

Identifies the problem (scaling), validates their research ("you already know," "you've researched"), lists known generic solutions (hiring, ads, automation, outsourcing), and sets up the "but..."

---

**PARAGRAPH 2 — Acknowledge Common Solutions + Their Flaw (150-250 words):**

Template:
```
"Those solutions [work / partially help].

[Solution A]: [Benefit] BUT [flaw/limitation].
[Solution B]: [Benefit] BUT [flaw].
[Solution C]: [Benefit] BUT [flaw].

Did you try [1-2 of them]? (Many do.)

The typical result: [The problem is partially solved BUT another problem is created / high cost / complexity / not sustainable].

It's not a COMPLETE solution (it solves X, but Y is still a problem)."
```

Example:
```
"Those solutions help (partially).

Hiring a team: Increases capacity BUT it's high cost ($5-15k/month per person, margins get tight), management overhead (you become a manager, not an operator), and training time (3-6 months to be productive).

Paid ads: Generates leads BUT high CAC ($200-500/lead in a B2B niche), requires expertise (or an agency for $3-8k/month), and it's risky (you burn your budget if you don't optimize).

Automation: Saves time BUT the setup is complex (integrations, learning curve), software costs (a stack of $200-800/month), and it doesn't solve everything (sales are still manual).

Did you try 1-2? (67% of entrepreneurs have tried hiring or ads before finding a complete solution.)

The result: Revenue grows 20-40% (not the 3x+ you want), complexity increases (more people, more tools, more stress), and margins fall (costs rise faster than revenue).

It's not a COMPLETE solution for sustainable 3x+ scaling."
```

Validates known solutions (3 are listed), identifies the flaws of EACH ONE (cost, complexity, partial), shows empathy ("did you try 1-2?"), typical result (partial, not complete — sets up "there's a better solution").

---

**PARAGRAPH 3 — Introduce YOUR Solution (120-180 words):**

Template:
```
"Here's what REALLY works for [the complete result]:

[YOUR PRODUCT/METHOD] (its name).

Created by [who — brief credentials] specifically for [a specific avatar / a specific problem].

The approach: [Unique mechanism in 1-2 sentences — HOW it's different from generic solutions].

It's not [common solution A or B] (you've seen they have flaws).

It's a [new/unique approach] — [1 sentence on the core differentiation].

The result (validated): [X people] achieved [a specific outcome] in [a timeframe] using this method."
```

Example:
```
"Here's what REALLY works to scale 3x+ sustainably:

**The Structured Scaling System** (the name of our method).

Created by [Your Name], a former COO of a SaaS company (scaled it from $2M → $50M in 4 years, with zero funding), specifically for digital entrepreneurs stuck at $20-50k who want to get to $100k+ without increasing their hours or exploding their costs.

The approach: 3 Integrated Pillars (Optimized Offer + Automated Funnel + Predictable Traffic) — not "hiring people" (cost), not "just ads" (unsustainable CAC), not "isolated automation" (doesn't solve sales).

It's not generic consulting ($10k/month, you do everything).

It's not a theoretical course (passive videos, you implement alone, you get stuck).

It's a COMPLETE SYSTEM implemented WITH you (12 weeks, hands-on, we do it together, not alone) — coaching + implementation + accountability.

The result (validated by 47 companies in the last 24 months): An average 4.1x revenue scale ($32k → $131k avg) in 8 months, with REDUCED hours (62h → 38h/week avg — automation works), and MAINTAINED or improved margins (costs don't explode)."
```

Introduces the product (name), the creator (brief credentials — former COO, scaled to $50M), the specific audience (entrepreneurs at $20-50k), the approach (3 pillars — differentiation from common solutions), what it ISN'T (consulting, a course — known alternatives), what it IS (a hands-on system), the result (47 companies, 4.1x, 8 months, reduced hours, maintained margins — specific, multi-dimensional).

---

**PARAGRAPH 4 — Tease the Proof (80-120 words):**

Template:
```
"How do I know it works? (You should ask — healthy skepticism.)

[X] companies/people have implemented it in the last [timeframe].

[Y]% achieved [the result] in [a timeframe].

The average: [A specific metric].

It's not theory. It's [data/practice/validated].

Let me show you exactly how..."
```

Example:
```
"How do I know it works?

47 companies have implemented it (January 2023 - October 2024, 22 months).

89% scaled 2x+ in 12 months.

The average: 4.1x revenue ($32k → $131k), 8.3 months to 3x.

The range: 2.1x (minimum, a difficult case) to 9.7x (maximum, an ideal case).

It's not theory (not an empty promise).

It's data (tracked, verified, 47 documented case studies).

Let me show you the complete system, the proof from each company, and how you can replicate it..."
```

Proof preview (47 companies, 89%, 4.1x average, 2.1-9.7x range — specific, comprehensive), transition (will be developed).

---

**Total Lead:** 400-700 words (A complete State 3 Problem-Solution Lead).

---

### **WHEN TO USE STATE 3:**

---

**CONTEXT #1 — Generic Google Search Terms (Intent-Based):**

**Search queries (high volume, generic):**
- "How to [solve problem]" (how-to searches)
- "Solutions for [problem]" (solution discovery)
- "Best way to [achieve objective]" (best practice research)
- "[Category] that works" (solution validation)

**NOT these searches:**
- "[Your product name]" (that's State 1-2 — brand awareness)
- "[Problem] vs. [Problem]" (that could be a State 2 comparison)

**Implication:** SEO/content marketing (blog posts, educational YouTube videos) = natural State 3 traffic.

---

**CONTEXT #2 — Content Marketing (Discovery Phase):**

**Channels:**
- Educational blog posts (SEO-optimized, "[How to do X]," "The complete guide to Y")
- YouTube videos ("Best ways to...", "How to [achieve an objective]")
- Podcasts (educational content, interviews)
- Educational webinars (teach-first, sell-later approach)

**Audience behavior:** Consuming content (learning mode), not buying yet (discovery).

**Copy approach:** A educational hybrid (70% value/education, 30% pitch — lead magnet style).

---

**CONTEXT #3 — Native Ads (Taboola, Outbrain):**

**Typical funnel:** Native Ad (curiosity headline) → **Advertorial** (800-1,200w editorial) → **VSL/Sales Page** (full pitch).

The advertorial = a State 3 piece (introduces the solution, your product, a tease — not the full pitch yet).

→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 5, Part II-B.2 (Advertorial structure for State 3)

---

**CONTEXT #4 — Prospects Ask "How Does It Work?" (Not "How Much Is It?"):**

**Linguistic signs:**

✅ State 3: "How does this work?", "What's the process?", "What's included?" (interest in the mechanism, not the pricing — discovery)

❌ State 1-2: "How much is it?", "Is there a discount?", "When can I start?" (transaction mode)

**Implication:** Questions about HOW = State 3 (education is needed). Questions about the DEAL = State 1-2 (direct offer).

---

**Confirming signs (the audience is State 3):**

✅ Generic research (not brand-specific): Articles about the category, YouTube how-tos

✅ Engagement with educational content (downloads guides, watches tutorials, attends webinars)

✅ Questions about the approach/mechanism (not about your specific product vs. a competitor)

✅ Timeline: Weeks of research (not days — there's a learning curve)

---

### **WHEN NOT TO USE STATE 3:**

---

**❌ STATE 1-2 (They Already Know You or the Specific Category):**

If the customer already knows your product's name (State 1) or is comparing you vs. Competitor A (State 2):

Long, educational State 3 copy = overkill (impatience).

"I ALREADY KNOW what a [category] does. Get to the point: what's your deal/differentiator?"

State 3 copy (3-6k words of education) = unnecessary friction.

---

**❌ STATE 4-5 (They Don't Know About the Problem or Solutions):**

If the customer doesn't know they have a problem (State 5) or knows the problem but not the solutions (State 4):

State 3 copy assumes "they already know solutions exist" (education about the category).

State 4-5: "Solutions? What solutions? I don't even know if it can be solved."

They need: Problem awareness/agitation BEFORE solution education.

---

**❌ Extreme Urgency (A Decision Today):**

State 3 copy = 3-6k words (takes 15-30 minutes to read/watch).

If the prospect needs to decide TODAY (a real deadline, maximum urgency):

They don't have the time/patience for long copy.

They need: A State 1-2 approach (short, direct offer).
