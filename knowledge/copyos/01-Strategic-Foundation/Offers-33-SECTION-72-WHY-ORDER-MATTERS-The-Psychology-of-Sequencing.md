## **SECTION 7.2 — WHY ORDER MATTERS (The Psychology of Sequencing)**

---

**Brunson's test** (ClickFunnels webinars, 2014-2019, 150+ A/B tested iterations):

---

**VERSION A — An Early Price (Slide 5, After 3 Items Are Stacked):**

The sequence:
- Slides 1-3: Stack the core + 2 bonuses (R$3,400 in value)
- Slide 4: A total value of R$3,400
- **Slide 5: The price reveal of R$997** (early)
- Slides 6-10: Continue adding bonuses 3-7 (R$1,700 more in value)
- Slide 11: Urgency/scarcity

Conversions: 100% (the baseline for comparison).

---

**VERSION B — A Late Price (Slide 13, After ALL Items Are Stacked):**

The sequence:
- Slides 1-8: Stack the core + ALL 7 bonuses (a complete R$5,100 in value)
- Slide 9: A total value of R$5,100 (the maximum anchor)
- Slides 10-12: Build suspense ("you're not going to pay R$5,100... not R$3k... not even R$2k...")
- **Slide 13: The price reveal of R$997** (late, after ALL the value)
- Slide 14: Savings of R$4,103
- Slides 15-16: The surprise bonus
- Slides 17-19: Urgency

Conversions: **168%** (+68% vs. Version A).

---

**THE DIFFERENCE:** Just the TIMING of the price reveal (slide 5 vs. slide 13).

**EVERYTHING else was the same** (the same items, the same price, the same urgency).

**The result:** +68% conversion (from timing alone).

---

**WHY sequence B wins (Psychological):**

---

**VERSION A (An Early Price — The Problems):**

**The anchor:** R$3,400 (only 3 items when the price is revealed).

**The mental process:** "R$997 vs. R$3,400 in value... hmm, an OK deal I guess" (a premature comparison, an incomplete anchor).

**Bonuses 3-7 afterward:** Are perceived as "adding justification for a price that's already been revealed" (suspicion: "they're trying to justify the R$997 post-hoc").

**Resistance:** Moderate (the price is anchored low, the additions feel like "upselling extras").

---

**VERSION B (A Late Price — The Advantages):**

**The anchor:** R$5,100 (ALL items are stacked BEFORE the price).

**The mental process:** "R$5,100 in value... it probably costs R$3-5k..." (the expectation is set HIGH).

**The price reveal:** "R$997?!" (a positive shock — 5x lower than expected, a pleasant surprise).

**The anchor effect:** R$5,100 vs. R$997 = a massive contrast (Kahneman Doc 1 Section 3.4 — the first number contaminates, the R$5,100 anchor makes R$997 feel trivial).

**Resistance:** Low (the value is overwhelming vs. the price — the logic: "R$5k worth for R$1k? An obvious steal").

---

**Brunson:**

> *"The timing of the price reveal = the single biggest lever for conversion in a stack presentation.*
>
> *An early price (slide 5): OK conversion.*
>
> *A late price (slide 13): +50-80% conversion consistently.*
>
> *Always: The COMPLETE value before the price.*
>
> *Anchor high (with the value), reveal low (with the price) = maximized contrast."*
