## **SECTION 4.5 — CHARM PRICING**

---

### **THE PRINCIPLE:**

**Odd/charm numbers (9, 7) > even/round numbers.**

**Examples:**

R$997 > R$1,000 (perceived as significantly cheaper, even if the R$3 difference is trivial)

R$47 > R$50 (charm)

R$2,997 > R$3,000 (charm)

---

**Why (System 1 processing):**

**Left-digit effect:** The brain processes the left digit first (R$997 = "in the 900s," R$1,000 = "in the 1,000s" — mentally different categories).

R$997 = "under R$1,000" (a psychological threshold).

R$1,000 = "R$1,000+" (crossed the threshold, feels like a higher tier).

**Even a R$3 difference** (0.3%), the perception = a different tier.

---

**Empiricus/Hodgson tested:**

R$497 vs. R$500: +8% conversions (R$497 wins).

R$997 vs. R$1,000: +12% conversions (R$997 wins).

**Charm pricing = a free boost** (costs nothing, +8-12% conversions).

---

**Application:**

**Use:** R$997, R$1,997, R$497, R$2,997 (charm numbers ending in 7 or 97).

**Avoid:** R$1,000, R$2,000, R$500 (round, feels like an "expensive tier").

**Exception:** Very high-ticket ($10k+) = round is OK (R$25,000 not R$24,997 — professionalism, round is cleaner at the high-end).
