## **SECTION 1.7 — THE 4-STEP CREATION PROCESS (MASTERSON'S SYSTEMATIC APPROACH)**

---

### **A UNIVERSAL PROCESS (Works for All 5 Types):**

Refined by Masterson/Agora over 20+ years and hundreds of launches.

**4 systematic steps** (applicable to a Discovery, Conspiracy, Mistake, Twist, or Shortcut — it's universal).

---

### **STEP 1 — IDENTIFY THE CORE BENEFIT**

**Time:** 15-30 minutes

**Objective:** Absolute clarity — what does the product/method DELIVER (the transformational outcome, not the features).

---

**Clarifying questions:**

1.  **What does the customer HAVE after using the product** (that they didn't have before)?  
    Not: "Knowledge of copywriting" (vague, intangible)  
    Yes: "The ability to write a sales letter that converts at 5%+" (tangible, quantified)

2.  **What problem is SOLVED** or desire ACHIEVED?  
    Not: "It improves marketing" (vague)  
    Yes: "It consistently generates 50-100 qualified leads/month" (specific)

3.  **How is their life DIFFERENT** afterward?  
    Not: "They know more about X" (knowledge gain alone)  
    Yes: "They work 30h/week (not 60), earn R$80k/month (not R$30k), and have time for their family" (a life transformation)

4.  **Why do they WANT this?** (Connect it to the LF8 — Doc 1 Section 7.0)  
    Identify 1-2 primary LF8 (money LF5, freedom LF5, status LF6, etc.)

---

**The complete benefit template:**

```
"The customer achieves [a specific, quantified result: numbers, a timeframe] that allows for [a life transformation: how their daily life is different] by activating [the LF8: 1-2 biological impulses]"
```

---

**A developed example:**

```
PRODUCT: An Online Copywriting Course

THE COMPLETE BENEFIT:

"The customer masters copywriting sufficiently to:

SKILL LEVEL:
- Write a sales letter that converts at 3-8% (the industry avg is 1-3% — above average)
- Create email sequences with a 15-25% open rate and a 3-8% click rate (hitting benchmarks)
- Write bullets that generate curiosity (15-20 per promo, Bencivenga-quality)

BUSINESS OUTCOME:
- Get their first 3-5 paying clients (freelance) in 60-90 days
- Charge R$3,000-15,000 per project (value-based, not the commodity R$50-100/hour)
- Work remotely (Zoom calls, email, total flexibility)
- Have a flexible schedule (they choose when they work, not a 9-to-5 clock-in)
- Have no boss (they ARE the expert, clients hire THEM)

FINANCIAL:
- Earn R$10,000-30,000/month (2-4 projects/month @ R$3k-15k each)
- Within 6-12 months, starting from scratch

LIFE TRANSFORMATION:
- Geographic freedom (they can work from anywhere — home, the beach, while traveling)
- Time freedom (they choose their schedule, it's not imposed)
- Financial freedom (R$10k-30k > most salaried jobs at R$3k-8k)
- Complete autonomy (their decisions are their own, not a boss's)
- Status (an expert, not "just another employee" — their family admires them, peers ask for advice)

LF8 ACTIVATED:
Primary: LF5 (money at R$10k-30k, the comfort of working from home, the ease of flexibility)
Secondary: LF6 (superiority — an expert, not an employee, autonomy)
Tertiary: LF8 (social approval — admired as an expert, peers consult them)
```

**Specific** (numbers, timeframes), **multi-dimensional** (skill + business + financial + life), and **emotional** (the LF8 are identified) = a complete core benefit (not a vague "learn copywriting").

---

**Step 1 output:** 1 paragraph (150-300 words) or a structured breakdown (like the one above) describing the complete transformational benefit.

---

**Benefit validation:**

Show it to 3-5 people from your avatar: "Are you interested in this? Is it worth a [estimated price] investment?"

✅ 4-5/5 "YES, it's worth it": The benefit is strong (proceed).

⚠️ 2-3/5: The benefit is moderate (refine it, make it more compelling).

❌ 0-1/5: The benefit is weak or for the wrong audience (reconsider the benefit's articulation or the choice of avatar).

---

### **STEP 2 — CHOOSE THE BIG IDEA TYPE (A Diverse Brainstorm)**

**Time:** 45-90 minutes (a brainstorm session)

**Objective:** Generate 10-15 ROUGH Big Ideas (2-3 of EACH type — diversity is critical).

---

**Inputs that inform the type selection (from Doc 2):**

**A. The diagnosed Stage:**
- Stage 1-2: Shortcut, Discovery (simple, direct)
- Stage 3: Discovery, Mistake, Shortcut (a mechanism reveal)
- Stage 4: Conspiracy, Twist (elaborate, challenging)
- Stage 5: Any BUT the score must be 6/6 (perfect or it's invisible)

**B. The avatar's characteristics:**
- Analytical (engineers): A Discovery (the appeal of data/science)
- Cynical (burned): A Conspiracy, a Mistake (validates their suspicions, explains their failures)
- Busy (time-scarce): A Shortcut (the appeal of efficiency)
- Conventional (mainstream): A Twist, a Mistake (challenges their comfortable assumptions)

**C. The competitive landscape:**
- All competitors are using a Discovery: You use a Conspiracy (differentiate the type)
- Everyone is using a Conspiracy: You use a Shortcut (contrarian positioning)
- Variation prevents type-specific saturation (even for Big Ideas in Stage 5, diverse types = fresh)

---

**The brainstorm method:**

**SOLO (By Yourself):**

A timer: 60-90 minutes (no interruptions).

The process:
1. Review the 5 types (the templates, the examples — refresh your memory)
2. The core benefit (for clarity — the output of Step 1)
3. Free-write (no filter, no self-criticism, quantity over quality)
4. Force 2-3 of each type (even if some feel "unnatural" — explore them anyway)
5. The output: 10-15 rough Big Ideas (diverse)

---

**TEAM (2-5 People — Better If Possible):**

The participants: Senior copywriters (2-3) + a product expert (1) + an "outsider" with fresh eyes (1-2 — not from the industry, a different perspective).

The process:
1. Align: Explain the core benefit, the avatar, and the State/Stage (so everyone has context)
2. Explain the 5 types (the templates, the characteristics — so everyone understands)
3. **A silent brainstorm** (15-20min — each person writes their own ideas, without talking)
4. **Share round-robin** (each person presents 3-5 ideas, no criticism yet — just sharing)
5. **Build/combine** (riff on others' ideas, combine elements, twist variations)
6. **The final collection** (aggregate them all — typically a team will generate 30-60 Big Ideas vs. 10-15 solo)

Agora's practice: Always a team (3-4 senior copywriters + 1-2 product experts + 1 "outsider").

The output: 40-60 rough Big Ideas (vs. 10-15 solo) — quantity increases the odds of finding a gem.

---

**Quick-fill templates (for each type):**

```
DISCOVERY (Generate 2-3):
1. "[An Institution: Harvard/MIT/Hopkins] discovers [a mechanism/compound] that [a benefit]"
2. "[A Researcher: Dr. Name] finds [a surprising thing] that achieves [a result]"
3. "An ancient [practice] is rediscovered, and a modern study validates [the benefit data]"

CONSPIRACY (Generate 2-3):
1. "[An Industry: Wall St/Pharma/Gov] is hiding [info] because of [a profit motive]"
2. "[An Authority] doesn't want you to know [a secret] (so you stay dependent on them)"
3. "An insider [position] leaks [information] that [the establishment] has suppressed for [years]"

MISTAKE (Generate 2-3):
1. "You're doing [a common action] WRONG — here's the RIGHT way ([data validates it])"
2. "[Conventional wisdom] fails because of [a flaw] — do [the correct approach instead]"
3. "The #1 mistake [a group] makes: [a specific error] (it costs them [a consequence] — the fix: [the correct way])"

TWIST (Generate 2-3):
1. "[A common belief X] is actually [the opposite Y] (science shows [a mechanism])"
2. "[A good thing] is bad / [a bad thing] is good ([recent research reverses it])"
3. "The cause-and-effect is reversed: You thought [A causes B], but in reality, [B causes A]"

SHORTCUT (Generate 2-3):
1. "[A benefit] in [a short time] not [a long conventional time] (the mechanism: [why it's faster])"
2. "[A benefit] with [low effort] not [high effort] (the efficiency: [why less = the same])"
3. "[A benefit] in [simple steps] not [a complex process] (the simplification: [why easier works])"
```

**Fill in the templates** (each person or team member): 5 types × 2-3 each = 10-15 rough Big Ideas.

**DO NOT filter yet** (quantity first, quality in Steps 3-4).

**Step 2 output:** A list of 10-15 (solo) or 30-60 (team) rough Big Ideas (diverse types, not refined).

### **STEP 3 — ADD A MECHANISM (To EACH Rough Big Idea)**

**Time:** 30-60 minutes

**Objective:** For EACH rough Big Idea (10-15 or 30-60), add a mechanism (HOW the product delivers).

---

**Why a mechanism is MANDATORY:**

**Schwartz** (Breakthrough Advertising, p.58 — the golden rule):

> *"The mechanism can be unique even when the benefit is common.*
>
> *Everyone promises to 'lose weight' (a generic, saturated benefit).*
>
> *But YOU can have a unique mechanism: 'via ketosis induced by MCT oil' (specific, differentiated).*
>
> *The Mechanism = your MOAT (it's defensible, hard to copy quickly)."*

---

**A Big Idea WITHOUT a mechanism:**

"Lose weight fast!" (an empty promise — HOW? It doesn't say. Not credible, generic.)

---

**A Big Idea WITH a mechanism:**

"Lose Weight Fast Via Accelerated Ketosis with MCT Oil (It Induces Ketosis in 3 Hours, Not the 14 Days of Adaptation Hell from Normal Keto)" (a specific HOW — the MCT oil mechanism, a time compression of 3h vs. 14d, a clear benefit without the keto flu)

**A mechanism adds:**
- Credibility (logic — it explains HOW)
- Specificity (MCT oil is tangible, not a vague "method")
- Defensibility (a specific concentration of MCT = harder to replicate)
- It's proof-friendly (you can measure ketosis in the blood/urine, demonstrate it works)

---

**For each rough Big Idea, add:**

```
A rough Big Idea: "[The base angle/promise]"

+ A mechanism: "via/using/through [the specific HOW the product works]"

= A Big Idea with a mechanism: "[The Angle] via [the Mechanism] that [an enhanced Benefit]"
```

---

**Examples of the transformation (before/after a mechanism):**

**Example A:**

**Rough:** "Wall Street is hiding a strategy" (a Conspiracy type, vague)

**+ Mechanism:** "Dividend Aristocrats (stocks with 25+ years of consecutive dividend increases) + just a quarterly rebalance (not daily trading)"

**= Complete:** "Wall Street Is Hiding the 'Dividend Aristocrats' (67 Stocks, 25+ Years of Consecutive Dividend Increases, Beat the S&P 78% of the Last 30 Years) — A 10-Min Quarterly Rebalance (Not the Hours of Daily Trading They Sell You) — Why Don't Brokers Tell You? Because There Are Zero Commissions If You Hold Long-Term (They Lose an Average of $4,200/Client/Year)"

The mechanism (the aristocrats' criteria + the quarterly rebalance) transforms a vague conspiracy into a specific one (tangible, actionable, credible).

---

**Example B:**

**Rough:** "Learn Spanish fast" (a Shortcut type, generic)

**+ Mechanism:** "A 20min/day immersion (native audio + forced output speaking drills, not passive listening in classes)"

**= Complete:** "Become Conversationally Fluent in Spanish in 90 Days (Not 3 Years) — An Immersion Method of 20 Min/Day: Native Audio (for Ear Training) + Forced Output Drills (for Speaking Practice, Not Passive Listening) = Learning That's 8x Faster with Active Recall (A Neuroscience Study Validates It) — 3,400 Students, 71% Were Conversational in 90 Days"

The mechanism (a specific immersion: audio + output, not passive) + the science (8x faster with active recall) + the proof (3,400, 71%) transforms a generic shortcut into a credible, specific one.

---

**Example C:**

**Rough:** "You're dieting wrong" (a Mistake type, it's vague about what the mistake is)

**+ Mechanism:** "Extreme restriction (<1,200 cal) → Starvation mode → A metabolism crash (adaptive thermogenesis) — The correct way: Adequate calories (1,800+) BUT a different composition (keto macros)"

**= Complete:** "Why 'Eating Less' Made You GAIN Weight — The Metabolic Mistake: Restriction to <1,200 Cal → Your Body Thinks It's a Famine → Your Metabolism Slows by 40% (The Minnesota Starvation Study, The Biggest Loser 2016) → A Weight Loss Plateau → An Inevitable Regain When You Resume Normal Eating (Your Metabolism Is Still Slow) — The Correct Method: Eat ADEQUATE Calories (1,800-2,200) BUT the Right Macros (Keto: 70% Fat, 25% Protein, 5% Carb) = Ketosis Burns Fat + Your Metabolism is Preserved — 12,000 Tested It, 78% Maintained for 24+ Months"

The mechanism (starvation mode → a metabolism crash, a specific 40%, studies are cited) + the correct mechanism (adequate cal + keto macros, ketosis preserves the metabolism) transforms a vague mistake into a credible scientific explanation.

---

**Mechanism checklist (validate each one):**

✅ **Specific:** Not a vague "system" or "method" — a NAME, identifiable components, and steps

✅ **Logical:** It makes sense (supported by science/reason, not magic)

✅ **Defensible:** Competitors can't copy it easily (a proprietary ingredient concentration, expertise-based, complex to develop, or you are the first to own the mindshare)

✅ **Proof-friendly:** You can demonstrate it (show data, measure the results, testimonials validate that the mechanism works)

✅ **Communicable:** You can explain it in 2-3 sentences (not too complex to explain — if you can't explain it simply, it's too complex to sell)

---

**Step 3 output:** 10-15 (or 30-60 for a team) Big Ideas WITH mechanisms (they are complete, not rough).

---

### **STEP 4 — WRAP IT IN A DISCOVERY/STORY (Polishing the Top 5)**

**Time:** 30-60 minutes

**Objective:** The top 3-5 Big Ideas (chosen after the mechanism step, the most promising ones) → Wrap them in a compelling presentation (a narrative, a discovery, intrigue — not just dry facts alone).

---

**Not enough (dry facts):**

"MCT oil activates ketosis in 3 hours." (the mechanism alone — it's factual, true, BUT it's boring, there's no hook)

---

**Enough (wrapped in a narrative):**

"UCLA scientists discovered something surprising about coconut fat:

MCT oil (medium-chain triglycerides — a specific type of coconut fat with a different molecular structure) activates ketosis in 3 hours (measured with blood ketones, verified).

vs. Traditional keto (just a low-carb diet): 10-14 days to enter ketosis (an adaptation period — the 'keto flu' with headaches, fatigue, and irritability, most people quit).

They tested 340 overweight people (a randomized trial, 12 weeks):

Group A (traditional keto): 63% achieved ketosis, with an avg adaptation of 11 days, and 31% quit (the keto flu was unbearable).

Group B (keto + 2 tablespoons of MCT oil daily): 94% achieved ketosis, with an avg adaptation of 2.8 days (the first ketones were detected in 3 hours, full ketosis in 2-3 days), and only 8% quit.

The weight loss result (at 12 weeks):
- Group A: An avg of 14 lbs (for those who stayed — the remaining 69%)
- Group B: An avg of 19 lbs (the remaining 92%)

MCT = a shortcut to ketosis (it fast-tracks fat burning, you skip the suffering of adaptation).

Published in: The Obesity Research Journal, 2019.

Here's the complete study [link] + how to use the exact MCT protocol..."

**The wrapping adds:**

✅ Authority (UCLA — a credible institution)

✅ A discovery narrative ("they discovered something surprising" — a story hook, not a dry "a study shows")

✅ A dramatic contrast (3h vs. 10-14 days — a difference of 80-110 hours compressed)

✅ Layered proof (340 participants, an RCT, 12 weeks, %, a journal from 2019)

✅ A specific benefit (skip the keto flu, a 94% vs. 63% success rate, 19 lbs vs. 14 lbs)

✅ A mechanism (MCT has a different molecular structure, how it triggers ketosis faster)

✅ It's actionable (an exact protocol — 2 tablespoons daily)

✅ It's verifiable (a link to the study — transparency)

---

**vs. The mechanism alone** (dry): No narrative, no emotional hook, no intrigue of discovery — factual but boring (low engagement).

**Wrapped:** A story (scientists discovered), drama (suffer vs. not suffer), proof (numbers), action (here's how) — engaging (high engagement).

---

**The wrapping template (universal):**

```
"[An Authority: Scientists/Experts/Researchers] [discovered/found/revealed] [a specific Mechanism/Finding — what it is, how it works]:

[A Contrast: the new way vs. the old way — a dramatic difference is shown]

[It Was Tested: X people, for Y weeks/months, where it was published]

[The Results: Z% achieved [the outcome], a comparison of A vs. B groups if applicable, specific numbers]

[A Unique Benefit: without [a common barrier], or [an advantage that others don't have]]

[Validation: Published in [a Journal, in a Year], verifiable]

Here's [the complete study / the full protocol / how to use it]..."
```

**The formula:** Authority + Discovery + Contrast + Test + Results + Benefit + Validation + Action = a complete wrapped presentation (vs. just a mechanism fact).

---

**Apply the wrapping:** To the top 3-5 Big Ideas only (the most promising ones after the mechanism step).

**Don't wrap all 10-15** (a waste of time — the bottom ones aren't worth polishing).

---

**Step 4 output:** 3-5 POLISHED Big Ideas (their presentation is ready, not a raw mechanism, with a compelling narrative/discovery angle).

### **SUMMARY OF THE 4-STEP PROCESS:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STEP 1 — THE BENEFIT (What the Customer Gets)   │
│ • A specific, quantified transformation         │
│ • Multi-dimensional (skill+business+life)       │
│ • The LF8 are identified (1-2 primary appeals)  │
│ • Validation: A "is it worth it?" test with 3-5 people │
│ • Time: 15-30min                                │
│ • Output: 1 paragraph with the complete benefit │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STEP 2 — THE TYPE (Which Angle, A Brainstorm)   │
│ • Based on: Stage, avatar, competition          │
│ • Generate 2-3 of EACH type (10-15 solo, 30-60 for a team) │
│ • No filter (quantity > quality here)           │
│ • Templates: Use the formulas for each type (for speed) │
│ • Time: 45-90min                                │
│ • Output: 10-15+ rough Big Ideas (diverse)      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STEP 3 — THE MECHANISM (How the Product Delivers) │
│ • Add one to EACH rough idea (10-15 or 30-60)   │
│ • Specific (not vague), logical, defensible     │
│ • Checklist: Specific, Logical, Defensible,     │
│   Proof-friendly, Communicable                  │
│ • Time: 30-60min                                │
│ • Output: Big Ideas WITH mechanisms (complete)  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STEP 4 — THE WRAPPING (The Presentation of the Top 3-5) │
│ • Choose the top 3-5 (the most promising after the mechanism step) │
│ • Authority, a narrative, a contrast, a proof preview │
│ • Polish it (a discovery story, not dry facts)  │
│ • The wrapping template (a universal formula)   │
│ • Time: 30-60min                                │
│ • Output: 3-5 POLISHED Big Ideas (ready to be scored) │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

TOTAL PROCESS TIME: 2-4 hours

FINAL OUTPUT OF STEP 4: 3-5 polished Big Ideas

NEXT: Part II (Score each one using the 6-criteria checklist,
refine them using the 5 Empiricus techniques, test the top 3 on the market,
and choose 1 final winner with a 5-6/6 score → the Positioning Document)
```
