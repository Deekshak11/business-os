## **SECTION 4.1 — THE 10x RULE (HORMOZI)**

---

### **THE PRINCIPLE:**

**Hormozi** ($100M Offers, Gym Launch scaling):

> *"Charge a MINIMUM of 10x your cost of delivery (fulfillment cost).*
>
> *Not 2x. Not 5x. A MINIMUM of 10x.*
>
> *Why? You need margins for: Marketing/acquisition (high CAC = scalable), refunds (2-8% is typical), operations, and profit.*
>
> *Below 10x = unsustainable (margin compression, you cannot scale, you're trapped being small)."*

---

### **THE CALCULATION:**

```
Cost to Deliver (total):
• Production (create the course, record videos, build software, manufacture physical)
• Hosting/infrastructure (servers, platform fees, bandwidth)
• Support (customer service, email, chat — time/people)
• Refunds (2-8% avg — budget for this)
• Updates/maintenance (ongoing improvements)

Example (Online Course):
One-time production: R$10,000 (amortize over 100 sales = R$100/sale)
Hosting: R$50/month ÷ 100 students = R$0.50/student
Support: 2h/student avg × R$50/h = R$100
Refunds budget: 5% × price (assume)

TOTAL COST: ~$200/student (fulfillment)

MINIMUM PRICE (10x rule): R$2,000
```

**10x = R$2,000 minimum price** (below this = insufficient margin to scale).

---

**Why 10x (the breakdown):**

Revenue of R$2,000 per customer:
- Fulfillment cost: R$200 (10%)
- Marketing/CAC: R$800 (40% — you can afford ads, scale traffic)
- Refunds: R$100 (5%)
- Operations: R$300 (15% — staff, tools, overhead)
- Profit: R$600 (30% — sustainable margins)

**Total:** 100% accounted for.

---

**Below 10x (ex: 5x = R$1,000 price):**

Revenue of R$1,000:
- Cost: R$200 (20% — doubled proportion)
- Marketing: R$300 (30% — HALF the budget vs. 10x, cannot compete on ads)
- Refunds: R$50 (5%)
- Operations: R$200 (20%)
- Profit: R$250 (25%)

**Problems:**
- 50% lower marketing budget (R$300 vs. R$800) = cannot outbid competitors (lose auctions, lower reach)
- Thinner profit margin (25% vs. 30%) = less cushion (vulnerability)
- Limited scale (low CAC budget = slow growth)

**Hormozi:** "5x = survival. 10x+ = scale (the difference between staying small vs. growing massive)."

---

**Exception (Physical Products):**

Margins are typically lower (3-5x is acceptable, not 10x).

Why: COGS are higher (manufacturing, inventory, shipping, returns logistics).

Example: E-commerce product costs R$20 (to make) + R$15 (to ship) = R$35 total → Sell for R$105-175 (3-5x) = industry standard acceptable.

**Info products/digital/services:** No excuse for being below 10x (marginal cost is near-zero, you should have fat margins).
