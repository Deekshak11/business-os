## **PART V — TOUCHSTONE (JOYNER)**

**Words:** 2,400

**Section 5.1 — The Definition (800w):**

Mark Joyner (*The Irresistible Offer*): A Touchstone is "a single memorable phrase summarizing the core offer benefit."

Characteristics: Specific, unique, emotional, credible, memorable.

The difference between a tagline (a brand slogan) vs. a touchstone (an offer summary).

Historical examples: FedEx's "overnight," Domino's "30 min or free," M&Ms' "melts in your mouth, not in your hand."

**The test:** Can a prospect repeat it after hearing it once? (If YES = it's sticky, If NO = not memorable enough, refine it).

---

**Section 5.2 — The Formula (700w):**

**Joyner's Template:**
```
[A specific, quantified benefit] + [A timeframe OR a condition] + [Without a common obstacle/barrier]
```

**Examples applying the formula:**

a) "Your first copywriting sale in 30 days or your money back + R$500"  
→ The Benefit (your first sale), a timeframe (30 days), without a barrier (the guarantee completely removes the risk)

b) "20 Lbs in 90 Days Without the Gym, Without Starving"  
→ The Benefit (20 lbs), a timeframe (90 days), without barriers (the gym, hunger)

c) "R$100k/Month in 12 Months Working 30h/Week (Not 60h)"  
→ The Benefit (R$100k), a timeframe (12 months), without a barrier (working less, not more)

The formula's breakdown: Each component (the benefit, the timeframe, without an obstacle) is developed (a 200w section for each).

---

**Section 5.3 — The Creation Process (900w):**

**4 Steps:**

1. Write the core benefit (in 1 sentence)
2. Add specificity (numbers, a timeframe — quantify it)
3. Add "without [a common barrier]" (remove an objection/pain point)
4. Test its memorability (can a person repeat it? Refine until they can)

The process is developed, with examples for each step, and refinement iterations (shortening, rhythm, sometimes alliteration — "Bacon for Breakfast, Pounds Off" = B/P alliteration, rhythm).

---

**Application Box Part V (500w):**

When to use a touchstone (in a headline, a subhead, email subjects, a CTA, a P.S. — it's versatile), how to test it (for memorability, stickiness), and how to iterate until it's memorable.

---

**TOTAL PART V:** ~2,900 words ✅
