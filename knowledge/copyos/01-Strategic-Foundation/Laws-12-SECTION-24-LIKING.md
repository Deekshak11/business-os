## **SECTION 2.4 — LIKING**

---

### **THE PRINCIPLE:**

> **"We prefer to say yes to the requests of people we know and like."**

— Cialdini

---

### **THE 6 FACTORS THAT GENERATE LIKING:**

---

**FACTOR #1 — PHYSICAL ATTRACTIVENESS**

**The Halo Effect:**

One positive characteristic (physical beauty) contaminates the overall perception (of intelligence, competence, honesty).

---

**The experiment (Canadian elections, 1970s):**

Candidates (photos were rated by independent judges):

Attractive (top 25% in ratings) vs. Unattractive (bottom 25%)

**The result:** Attractive candidates received **+150% more votes** (2.5x more), controlling for competence/experience.

**Physical beauty = a massive electoral advantage** (it shouldn't matter, but it does).

---

**In copywriting:**

Photos/videos of attractive people in ads: +18-25% CTR

A handsome spokesperson: +12-20% conversion

**Caution:** Incongruity (a fashion model selling enterprise software = a disconnect, can backfire).

---

**FACTOR #2 — SIMILARITY**

We like people who are similar to us (in values, background, interests, language).

---

**The experiment (Mirroring Salespeople):**

Salespeople were trained to mirror the customer:
- Body posture (if the customer crosses their arms, the salesperson crosses theirs)
- Speaking speed (if the customer speaks slowly, the salesperson speaks slowly)
- Keywords (if the customer says "concerned," the salesperson uses "concerned," not "anxious")

**The result:** +20% more sales (vs. salespeople without mirroring).

**Mirroring = artificial similarity** (but it works).

---

**In copywriting:**

Use the **avatar's OWN language** (their exact words, from Doc 2 research).

If the avatar says "I'm screwed" → The copy uses "screwed" (not "facing challenges" — your professional language).

---

Collier (1931):

> *"Always enter the conversation already happening in the reader's mind — using THEIR words, not yours."*

Linguistic similarity = identification = liking.

---

**FACTOR #3 — COMPLIMENTS**

Compliments increase liking (even when they are obviously manipulative).

---

**The experiment:**

Participants received compliments from a salesperson (who was clearly interested in selling — the context was obvious).

**The result:** +15% more purchases (vs. no compliments).

**Even knowing it was a tactic** (the manipulation was obvious), the compliments worked.

---

**The mechanism:** Compliments activate pleasure (dopamine) — overriding rational skepticism.

---

**In copy:**

"Smart people like you..."

"You're smart enough to recognize..."

Kennedy (to the affluent): "For demanding/discerning customers like you..."

---

**FACTOR #4 — FAMILIARITY (The Mere Exposure Effect)**

**Robert Zajonc (a psychologist, 1960s-1990s):**

Repeated exposure → liking increases (with no interaction, just exposure).

---

**The experiments:**

**Songs:** Played repeatedly (5, 10, 25 times) → "How much you like it" ratings increase linearly with exposure (up to ~25, then it plateaus).

**Faces:** Shown repeatedly (subliminally, for <1 second) → Preference increases (even without consciously remembering having seen them).

**Words:** Read repeatedly → Rated as more "beautiful," more "true."

---

**Mere Exposure** (pure exposure, without interaction) is sufficient to increase liking.

---

**In copywriting:**

A **consistent** newsletter/email (weekly, never miss it).

Kennedy: "I sent a newsletter every Monday for 18 years. ZERO exceptions. That's familiarity."

---

**After 10-20 exposures:**

The prospect "knows" you (familiarity).

Familiarity → liking (the Zajonc effect).

Liking → compliance when the pitch comes.

---

**FACTOR #5 — COOPERATION**

Working together for a common goal → liking between the cooperators.

---

**The experiment (Sherif — Robbers Cave, 1954):**

Boys (at a summer camp) were divided into 2 groups.

**Phase 1:** Competition (games, the groups were hostile to each other — rivalry, extreme hostility).

**Phase 2:** Forced cooperation (a problem that ONLY together could they solve — e.g., a truck was stuck, they all had to pull it).

**The result:** The hostility disappeared. Friendships formed between the previously enemy groups.

**Cooperating = liking** (even among the hostile).

---

**In copy:**

Cooperative language: "**We** are going to achieve..." (not "I'm going to teach you...").

"**Together** we'll build..." (not "You'll do it on your own...").

Frame it as a partnership (not a vendor-customer relationship).

---

**FACTOR #6 — ASSOCIATION**

We like people who bring good news (even if they're not responsible for it).

We don't like people who bring bad news (shoot the messenger).

---

**The experiment (A TV Meteorologist):**

Good weather is predicted → The meteorologist is rated as "likable," "competent"

Bad weather is predicted → The meteorologist is rated as "annoying," "incompetent"

**Even knowing that the meteorologist does NOT CONTROL the weather** (they just report it).

**The association of the weather → the person** (it's irrational, but it happens).

---

**The Persian Messenger Syndrome** (an old story):

A Persian king would kill the messengers who brought news of a defeat (the association of the messenger → the bad news → the anger was directed at the innocent messenger).

---

**In copy:**

**Associate the product with POSITIVE emotions/images:**
- A luxury car + a beautiful woman (an association with beauty)
- A fitness product + a happy transformation (an association with joy)
- A course + success/wealth (an association with aspiration)

**Avoid NEGATIVE associations:**
- Don't show the product associated with failure, sadness, or ugliness (even in the "before" of a before/after — the "before" should be relatable, not repulsive)

---

A COMPLETE EXAMPLE — LIKING IN AN OPENING:

✅ An authentic voice + vulnerability + a story:

"I was like you.

In 2015: I worked 12h/day (at an agency, as a junior partner). I earned R$4k/month. I was tired. No time for family. Frustrated.

I tried to 'be an entrepreneur.' I failed 3x (a digital product, e-commerce, consulting — all failed in 6-9 months).

I spent R$22k (on courses, ads, tools). Zero return.

I thought: 'Maybe I'm not cut out for this. Should I go back to a 9-to-5?'

My wife: 'Try one more time. The last one.'

Then I discovered copywriting... [the story continues]

Today: R$60k/month, 25h/week, I work from home (the beach on Tuesdays). My family is happy.

The same frustrations YOU feel — I FELT them.

The same doubts — I HAD them.

I UNDERSTAND you."

→ Similarity (an identical struggle), vulnerability (failures admitted), familiarity (a personal story), deep identification.

The result: Maximum liking (the prospect "likes" them before the pitch).

❌ Corporate/distant:

"Our company, founded in 2015, has consolidated expertise in digital marketing solutions with a proprietary, certified methodology, serving satisfied clients nationwide."

→ Zero personality, zero vulnerability, zero identification.
The result: Indifference (they don't like them, they don't care).
