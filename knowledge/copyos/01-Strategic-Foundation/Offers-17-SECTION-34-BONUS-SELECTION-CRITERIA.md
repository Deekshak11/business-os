## **SECTION 3.4 — BONUS SELECTION CRITERIA**

---

**Bonus checklist (evaluate EACH one before including):**

```
☐ Does it SOLVE a specific obstacle? (not a generic "nice-to-have bonus")
  • Which obstacle exactly? (reference the list from Section 3.2)
  • Would the customer be stuck without it? (critical vs. nice)

☐ Does it have HIGH perceived value? (R$100+ is ideal, R$200-500 is best)
  • What's its standalone worth? (if sold separately)
  • Does the customer NEED this? (pain/desire associated)

☐ Does it have a LOW cost to deliver? (R$50 or less is ideal, R$0 is best)
  • Digital = R$0 (template, PDF, video)
  • Group = split cost (R$50/person for a group of 20-50)
  • Physical = avoid (high cost, logistics)

☐ Is it RELATED to the core? (complements, doesn't distract)
  • Is it a natural next step? (logical progression)
  • Does it help achieve the SAME result? (synergy with the core)
  • Not: Random and unrelated (confuses the value prop)

☐ Is it DELIVERABLE immediately? (instant gratification)
  • Instant digital download? (preferred)
  • Shipped fast? (if physical, express)
  • Access granted automatically? (no delay)

☐ Is it ETHICALLY valuable? (genuinely useful, not fluff)
  • Will the customer USE this? (not ignored shelf-ware)
  • Does it deliver the promised value? (not an exaggerated worth)
```

---

**Score each bonus: __/6**

**5-6/6:** Excellent bonus (include it — high value, low cost, solves a real obstacle)

**3-4/6:** OK bonus (include if it helps diversify the stack)

**<3/6:** Weak bonus (remove it — doesn't add sufficient value, or the cost is too high, or it's unrelated)

---

**Target:** 5-7 bonuses total (the sweet spot — enough to solve most obstacles, not so many that it's overwhelming or "too much, confusing").
