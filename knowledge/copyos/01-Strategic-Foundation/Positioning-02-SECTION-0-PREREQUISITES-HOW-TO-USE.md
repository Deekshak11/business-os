# **SECTION 0 — PREREQUISITES & HOW TO USE**

---

### **CRITICAL Prerequisites:**

**→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 2 complete** (Campaign Brief filled out)

**You MUST have this before starting Doc 3:**

✅ **State/Stage diagnosed** (1-5 each) — determines which type of Big Idea to choose

✅ **Avatar defined** (7 complete dimensions) — informs Voice (tone, language, formality)

✅ **False Beliefs identified** (Vehicle, Internal, External) — the Big Idea can address 1-2, Voice can express related enemies

✅ **Avatar's exact language** (transcripts, quotes) — Voice should mirror it (not diverge)

✅ **Intensity score** (1-10) — correlates with the audacity of the Big Idea (Intensity 9-10 = Big Idea can be bolder)

✅ **Top 5 objections** (listed) — Positioning addresses these via the Big Idea/Mechanism

---

**Optional but helpful:**

Doc 1 (Universal Laws) — understand why differentiation matters (Axiom 2: desire pre-exists, Big Idea = specific channel that directs desire to YOUR product vs. a generic one or a competitor's).

---

### **Time Investment:**

**Complete Read-Through (first time):** 3.5-4.5 hours (Parts I-VII, frameworks)

**Creation (filling out the Positioning Document):** 2-4 hours
- Big Idea: 1-2h (brainstorm 10-15, score, refine top 3-5)
- Mechanism: 30-60min (identify the unique one, articulate it)
- Voice: 1-2h (5 elements, 4 dimensions, calibrate)
- Integration: 30min (fill out the Positioning Document)

**Total:** 6-9 hours (for a complete, defensible strategic positioning).

**ROI:** The right Big Idea vs. a generic one = 200-400% difference in conversion (validated by Agora/Empiricus over 20+ years, hundreds of campaigns). A 2-4h investment in the Big Idea can be worth millions in revenue (Masterson's cases are documented).

---

### **How to Use This Document:**

**FIRST TIME (Creating Positioning From Scratch):**

**Linear flow:**

1.  **Part I** (Big Idea 5 types + process) → Understand the frameworks, generate 10-15 rough Big Ideas
2.  **Part II** (Refinement) → Score with the checklist, refine the top 5, test the top 3, choose a winner
3.  **Part III** (Mechanism) → Identify the product's unique mechanism, articulate it clearly
4.  **Parts IV-VI** (USP, Touchstone, New Opportunity) → Complementary frameworks, create each one
5.  **Part VII** (Voice) → Define the personality, 5 elements, calibrate the spectrum
6.  **Part VIII** (Positioning Document) → Integrate EVERYTHING (Big Idea + Mechanism + USP + Touchstone + Voice), fill out the 8-page template

**Output:** A complete Positioning Document (input for Docs 4-13).

---

**FOR SPECIFIC REFERENCE (Refining Existing Positioning):**

Use the mini-index → Go to the specific framework.

Examples:
- "My current Big Idea scores 4/6, I need to refine it" → Part II (5 refinement techniques)
- "How do I create a Touchstone?" → Part V (formula, process)
- "My voice is inconsistent, need to calibrate" → Part VII (spectrum, guidelines)

---

### **Critical Output:**

**A COMPLETE POSITIONING DOCUMENT** (Part VIII, 8 pages filled out).

**Contains (integrated):**
- Big Idea (1 sentence, scored 5-6/6, type identified)
- Unique Mechanism (3-5 steps explaining how it works)
- USP (3 elements: Unique, Selling, Proposition)
- Touchstone (memorable phrase summarizing the core offer)
- New Opportunity (if Stage 4-5 — new category positioning)
- Voice (5 elements of the Attractive Character + 4 calibrated spectrum dimensions)

---

**This document is a MANDATORY INPUT for Docs 4-13:**

→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 4 (Offers) — The Offer uses the Big Idea as its foundation, Touchstone integrated into the stack presentation

→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 6 (Headlines) — Headlines express the Big Idea (10+ variations tested), Touchstone can be a subhead

→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 7 (Bullets) — Bullets elaborate on the Mechanism, Big Idea permeates

→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 9 (Language) — The Voice defined here is applied to word choices, tone, and sentence structure

→ REQUIREMENT: Doc 11 (Objections) — Polarity (enemies) identified here, broken down via the Epiphany Bridge

Docs 5, 8, 10, 12-13 (Reference) — Use elements when appropriate

**All subsequent copy USES the positioning** (the Big Idea permeates everything — it's not just a headline, it's the messaging's DNA).
