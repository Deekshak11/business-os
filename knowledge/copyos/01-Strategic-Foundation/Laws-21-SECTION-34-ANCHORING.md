## **SECTION 3.4 — ANCHORING**

---

### **THE PRINCIPLE:**

> "The first number presented contaminates all subsequent numerical judgments."

---

### **THE EXPERIMENT (A Wheel of Fortune — Tversky/Kahneman, 1974):**

Participants spin a wheel (it stops on a random number: 10 or 65).

**A completely UNRELATED question:**

"What is your estimate: How many African countries are members of the UN?"

---

**The results:**

An anchor of 10: An average estimate of **25 countries**

An anchor of 65: An average estimate of **45 countries**

The reality: ~54 countries (at the time)

---

**The number from the wheel (100% RANDOM, with zero relation to Africa/the UN) contaminated the estimate.**

System 1 "adjusts" from the anchor (but the adjustment is insufficient — it stays close to the anchor even though it's irrelevant).

---

## **SECTIONS 3.5-3.7 (CONDENSED — High Density):**

---

### **3.5 — LOSS AVERSION:**

**The principle:** Losing hurts 2-2.5x more than gaining pleases.

**The experiment:** A bet ($150 to gain vs. $100 to lose, an EV of +$25) — most reject it.

**Application:** Frame it as "avoiding a loss" > "gaining a benefit." Guarantees reverse loss→gain.

**Power:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### **3.6 — FRAMING:**

**The principle:** The presentation alters the decision (with identical info).

**The experiments:** Meat (75% lean > 25% fat, 3x the sales), Surgery (90% survive vs. 10% die).

**Application:** Generally positive, Price ($3/day < $1,095/year), Time (30 days < 40 hours).

**Power:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### **3.7 — THE ENDOWMENT EFFECT:**

**The principle:** Owning increases value by 2-3x.

**The experiments:** Mugs (selling for $7.12 vs. buying for $2.87, a 2.5x gap), Duke tickets (a 14x gap).

**Application:** Free trials (60-80% CVR), Ownership language ("YOUR new..."), Long guarantees (a 90-day endowment).

**Power:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
